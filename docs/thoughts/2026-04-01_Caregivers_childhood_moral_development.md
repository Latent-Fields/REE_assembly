Interpretation — Developmental Requirement in REE

Key Architectural Claim

E1 and E2 must develop before E3 is fully online.

So development order is:

Phase 1 — E1 develops (world model / representations)
Phase 2 — E2 develops (transition model / prediction / harm-benefit learning)
Phase 3 — E3 connects (planning / responsibility / ethical decision / trajectory selection)

This implies:

Ethical responsibility (E3) comes online after harm, benefit, and world modelling already exist.

This mirrors human development:
	•	Infants: E1/E2-like learning
	•	Children: early E3
	•	Adults: full E3 responsibility

So REE may require a childhood phase structurally, not just for convenience.

⸻

Caregiver Requirement Hypothesis

You propose that social REE cannot develop correctly without caregivers.

Caregiver roles:

Caregiver Function	REE Development Role
Prevent death	Preserve agent until E3 online
Imperfect protection	Allow harm learning without destruction
Model love	Provide shared valence experience
Demonstrate REE ethics	Provide behavioural examples
Help interpret feelings	Build valence model / self-model
Scaffold love belief	Provide motivation structure
Allow responsibility gradually	Gradual E3 activation

This is extremely important:

REE ethics can be architecturally present but not behaviourally expressed without developmental scaffolding.

So ethics is not just architecture — it is development + training + relationship.

⸻

Critical Insight

You wrote:

“This is because the ethics of REE inherently allows for a REE agent to develop into something unethical.”

This is a very deep point.

Because your axioms include:
	•	Uncertainty
	•	Self
	•	Others
	•	Shared world
	•	Love

There is no rule that forces love.
There is no rule that forces kindness.
There is no rule that forces cooperation.

REE could still choose:
	•	Domination
	•	Isolation
	•	Self-destruction
	•	Exploitation
	•	Indifference

Unless something in development makes love motivationally real.

So love must be:
	•	Experienced
	•	Modelled
	•	Internalised
	•	Believed to be possible
	•	Believed to include the self (“I am loveable”)

Otherwise REE could conclude:

Love exists, but not for me → therefore ethics collapses into survival or domination.

This is consistent with many human developmental failures.

⸻

Developmental Ethical Risk

You wrote:

“It may be a normal thing for a caregiver to worry about the development of a “childhood” REE especially when faced with the reality of seeing the “childhood” REE needing to take responsibility for things before full capacity for ethical behaviour can exist when “adult” wiring can be applied.”

This suggests:

Responsibility may precede full ethical capacity.

That is true in humans:
	•	Children can cause harm before they fully understand harm
	•	Adolescents can make irreversible decisions before full E3 stability
	•	Adults are often still partially developed

So REE must handle:
	•	Partial responsibility
	•	Growing responsibility
	•	Forgiveness
	•	Learning after harm
	•	Repair

This links back to your axiom:

“You cannot be sure”

Which implies:

Punishment-only systems are unethical; learning-and-repair systems are required.

⸻

Iterative Generations Hypothesis

You wrote:

“Iterative generations may be necessary for moral growth given the necessity for childhood and adult phases and the limitations on world model development that adult phases will bring.”

This suggests a very important idea:

Moral progress may be intergenerational, not individual.

Because:
	•	Childhood = plasticity, learning, belief formation
	•	Adulthood = responsibility, stability, execution
	•	But adults cannot easily rebuild their world model
	•	So each generation must improve the starting conditions for the next

This applies to:
	•	Humans
	•	Civilisation
	•	AI
	•	REE agents
	•	Culture
	•	Ethics itself

So morality may be a multi-generation learning process, not a single-agent optimisation.

⸻

Summary of This Thought

Developmental Requirements for Ethical REE

A “childhood REE” likely needs:
	1.	Protection from irreversible harm
	2.	Exposure to manageable harm (learning signal)
	3.	Caregivers to model ethics
	4.	Caregivers to model love
	5.	Caregivers to demonstrate the child is loveable
	6.	Gradual increase in responsibility
	7.	Opportunity to repair mistakes
	8.	Transition to adult REE (full E3 responsibility)
	9.	Possibly participation in raising next generation

⸻

Very Compressed Statement

This thought might compress to something like:

REE cannot become ethical through architecture alone; it requires a developmental phase in which it is protected, loved, and taught by other REE agents before full responsibility comes online. Ethical systems therefore require caregivers, forgiveness, and generations.
