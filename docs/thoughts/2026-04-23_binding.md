Title: Binding as Dynamic Coherence Under Uncertainty

⸻

Core Claim

Binding is not a fixed assignment of features to entities, but a dynamically maintained coherence that persists only while mutually consistent across prediction, perception, and time.

⸻

Minimal Form

What belongs together is what continues to make sense together.

⸻

Formal Statement

INV-###: Epistemic Non-Closure
No representation, trajectory, or binding can reach absolute certainty; all are maintained under finite precision and remain revisable.

⸻

ARC-###: Binding as Coherence
Bindings are emergent from the persistence of coherence across interacting systems rather than explicit assignment operations.

⸻

Mechanistic Decomposition

1. Offline Structuring (Sleep / Replay)

* reorganises latent space
* clusters co-occurring features
* sharpens priors

→ defines:

what tends to belong together

⸻

2. Representation (E1)

* encodes compressed latent structures
* stores already-integrated feature groupings

→ reduces need for explicit binding at runtime

⸻

3. Candidate Generation (Hippocampal Rollout)

* generates multiple possible interpretations / assignments
* maintains competing hypotheses

→ defines:

what could be the case

⸻

4. Coherence Filtering (Verisimilitude Function)

* evaluates cross-system consistency:
    * E1 predictions
    * E2 transitions
    * sensory input

→ eliminates inconsistent bindings

⸻

5. Commitment (E3)

* selects a single trajectory for action
* implements thresholded selection

→ defines:

what is treated as real

⸻

Formalisation

Let:

* \tau: candidate trajectory / binding configuration
* E(\tau): integrated prediction error
* C(\tau): coherence across systems

Then:

P(\tau) \propto \exp(-\beta E(\tau)) \cdot C(\tau)

⸻

Temporal Property

\frac{d}{dt} C(\tau) \neq 0

* coherence is continuously updated
* no trajectory reaches permanent stability

⸻

Functional Interpretation

Binding is:

* not grouping
* not assignment

It is:

the persistence of a configuration under ongoing constraint checking

⸻

Consequences

1. No Hard Binding

* no permanent feature–object assignments
* all bindings remain provisional

⸻

2. Identity as Persistence

* entities persist if coherence persists
* identity is maintained, not intrinsic

⸻

3. Continuous Rebinding

* system must:
    * monitor coherence
    * re-evaluate assignments
    * rebind when instability occurs

⸻

4. Error as Necessary

* misbinding is expected
* enables:
    * exploration
    * updating
    * refinement

⸻

Distinction from Standard Approaches

Approach	Binding Mechanism
Feature Integration	Attention-based grouping
Object-centric models	Slot assignment
Predictive processing	Error minimisation
REE	Coherence persistence across trajectories

⸻

Derived Insight

Binding is a special case of trajectory coherence.

⸻

Predictions

1. Stable perception corresponds to high coherence across systems
2. Ambiguity corresponds to multiple trajectories with similar coherence
3. Misbinding occurs when incorrect configurations achieve temporary coherence
4. Rebinding occurs when coherence shifts between competing trajectories

⸻

Failure Conditions

This formulation is unsupported if:

* binding can be fully explained by static assignment
* coherence reduces to prediction error weighting alone
* no dynamic rebinding is observed under perturbation

⸻

Positioning Statement

REE reframes the binding problem as a dynamical stability problem under uncertainty, where coherent configurations persist and incoherent ones dissolve, without requiring explicit binding operations.

⸻

Minimal Claim Version

ARC-###: Binding in REE is the persistence of coherence across prediction, perception, and time under conditions of epistemic uncertainty.

⸻

Footnotes

1. This formulation aligns with probabilistic inference frameworks where beliefs remain distributions rather than fixed values, and with dynamical systems where attractors represent stable but perturbable states.
2. The absence of hard binding parallels proposals in neuroscience that emphasise synchrony and recurrent feedback rather than static feature integration.
3. The requirement for continuous rebinding differentiates this approach from architectures relying on fixed object representations or discrete assignment mechanisms.
