# Dream Phenomenology and Replay Types

**Date:** 2026-04-07
**Source:** Daniel Golden, first-person phenomenological observation
**Status:** Raw thought — three distinct dream types noted as definite and consistent across instances; acknowledged as possibly not exhaustive

---

## The observation

Three structurally distinct dream types, observed consistently:

### Type 1: Stress/escape frustration dreams
Trying to get away from something. Everything that could go wrong does go wrong — not randomly, but systematically. The dreamer has a clear goal (escape, avoidance) and encounters a relentless sequence of obstacles and failures. The affect is frustrated urgency. The dream does not resolve.

### Type 2: Joy/goal frustration dreams
Trying to reach a goal. Again, everything that could go wrong does go wrong. The tone is different from Type 1 — there is something joyful or desired at the end — but the same systematic-failure structure. The dreamer is approach-oriented rather than avoidance-oriented, but encounters the same frustrating obstruction. The dream also does not resolve.

### Type 3: Procedural/perceptual superposition dreams
A single thing experienced from all angles, all scales, and all times simultaneously. Not a sequence — a superposition. Two concrete examples:
- Opening a door — but experienced as all kinds of doors at once, all instances of door simultaneously present
- A cherry blossom tree blooming — experienced from all angles, all scales (petal to whole tree to forest), and all moments of blooming at once

No agent frustration. No goal being pursued. No self trying and failing. The self seems dissolved or absent — the dreamer IS the superposition, not an agent within it. Phenomenologically peaceful.

---

## Initial interpretation

**Types 1 and 2 are structurally mirror images** — harm-avoidance and goal-approach respectively — with the same frustration signature. The systematic failure pattern ("everything that could go wrong") suggests the generative model is exhaustively exploring the failure space, not finding the causal features that distinguish success from failure. The dream continues because the search hasn't converged.

**Type 3 is categorically different.** No agent, no goal, no search. The simultaneous multi-instance, multi-angle, multi-scale, multi-time quality suggests semantic abstraction — the hippocampus rapidly presenting all instances of a concept, and the experiential phenomenology collapsing the sequence to simultaneity. The self-dissolution reflects that episodic context (when, where, who) is being stripped away, leaving only the invariant features of the category.

---

## Why this matters for REE

If dream phenomenology maps onto computational replay type, then:
1. First-person dream reports are evidence about which offline process is running and whether it converged
2. Recurring frustration dreams (Types 1/2) are a clinical marker of convergence failure — the causal features weren't found, the episode wasn't resolved
3. The Type 3 "peaceful superposition" may be the cleanest phenomenological signature of NREM semantic consolidation — and its absence in pathological states (e.g. never having Type 3 dreams) could indicate a failure of episodic→semantic transfer

The anxiety connection (Type 1 and the harm-weighting loop from MECH-208) is also direct: elevated harm-salience → more Type 1 dreams, longer and more intense, because the convergence criterion is set too high.

---

## Notes on scope

Daniel notes these are definite types from his actual experience, but does not claim they are exhaustive. Other dream types may exist. The taxonomy is phenomenological, not theoretical — it was noticed, not derived.
