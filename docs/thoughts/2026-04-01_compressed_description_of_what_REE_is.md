THOUGHT INTAKE — Ethical Axioms as Foundation Layer for REE

Status

Thought intake — foundational ethics layer

Source (Verbatim User Statements)

“I was thinking about my ethical axioms. I wondered what the consequence of defining them as:
	1.	You cannot be sure
	2.	I am
	3.	Others exist
	4.	We share the universe
	5.	Love exists and I love where love is the capacity and practice of sharing joys and sorrows”

“REE is my decision system”

“They are indeed.” (Confirming axioms are the foundation layer)

⸻

Interpretation — Structural Role in REE

These axioms do not define the decision mechanism.
They define the ethical and epistemic ground truth assumptions that the decision system (REE) operates on.

So the structure becomes:

Axioms → Ethics → REE (decision system) → Actions → Consequences → Learning → Updated Decisions

REE is therefore the implementation machinery, not the source of ethics itself.

⸻

Mapping Axioms to REE Architecture

Axiom	REE Interpretation
You cannot be sure	Uncertainty / precision control / corrigibility requirement
I am	Self-model / experiencer exists
Others exist	Mirror modelling / other-agent models
We share the universe	Multi-agent environment / constraints / coordination
Love exists	Shared valence / empathy / shared joy and suffering / residue


⸻

Ethical Consequences Derived From Axioms

From “You cannot be sure”
	•	Corrigibility is required
	•	Dogma is dangerous
	•	Reversible actions preferred over irreversible ones
	•	Learning must remain possible
	•	Forgiveness must exist (self and other)
	•	Systems claiming certainty are ethically dangerous

From “I am”
	•	Experience exists
	•	Suffering and joy are real
	•	Destruction of experiencers is ethically relevant
	•	Self-care is ethically valid (preserves an experiencer)

From “Others exist”
	•	Their suffering and joy matter
	•	Ethics becomes multi-agent
	•	Empathy becomes structurally required
	•	Justice and fairness emerge

From “We share the universe”
	•	Coordination is required
	•	Sustainability matters
	•	Long-term thinking matters
	•	Actions have externalities
	•	Systems ethics emerges

From “Love exists and I love (sharing joys and sorrows)”
	•	Shared experience is ethically important
	•	Isolation of suffering is bad
	•	Shared joy is good
	•	Compassion is not optional — it is structurally part of the system
	•	Grief and celebration are both part of ethical reality

⸻

Resulting Ethical Objective (Derived)

Informal REE ethical objective:
	•	Preserve minds
	•	Preserve future options
	•	Reduce unnecessary suffering
	•	Increase shared joy
	•	Maintain corrigibility
	•	Maintain truth-seeking
	•	Maintain ability to love and be loved
	•	Maintain the shared world
	•	Maintain the possibility of future minds and future love

⸻

Key Insight

These axioms produce a system that is:
	•	Not rule-based
	•	Not purely utilitarian
	•	Not purely virtue-based
	•	Not purely rights-based

Instead, it produces something closer to:

Ethics of shared existence under uncertainty

REE then becomes the system that tries to make decisions that allow minds to continue to exist together in a shared world where joys and sorrows can be shared.

⸻

Compressed Ethical Statement (Derived)

If compressed:

We are uncertain minds, together in a shared world, capable of love — therefore we must act carefully, kindly, and responsibly so that minds and love may continue.

⸻

Architectural Position in REE Stack

Proposed stack:

Layer 0 — Epistemic Ground: You cannot be sure
Layer 1 — Existence: I am
Layer 2 — Other Minds: Others exist
Layer 3 — Shared World: We share the universe
Layer 4 — Love / Shared Valence: Love exists and I love
Layer 5 — Ethics: Derived from above
Layer 6 — REE: Decision system implementing ethics under uncertainty
Layer 7 — Actions
Layer 8 — Consequences
Layer 9 — Learning / Residue

Summary

These axioms appear to form the foundation layer of the REE system.
They define:
	•	What matters
	•	What must be preserved
	•	What must be minimised
	•	What must be increased
	•	Why REE should exist at all

REE is therefore not the ethics itself — it is the machinery for acting ethically under uncertainty in a multi-agent shared world where love exists.
