## Formal candidate for **V(t)**

Let the hierarchy have levels $i = 0 \dots n$, where:

* $i=0$ is the lowest sensory-format level
* $i=n$ is the highest latent / abstract level

At each level and time $t$:

* $a_i(t)$ = ascending, sensory-constrained signal
* $d_i(t)$ = descending, model-based prediction
* $\phi^a_i(t)$ = phase of ascending signal
* $\phi^d_i(t)$ = phase of descending signal
* $\alpha_i(t)$ = precision weight at that level
* $w_i$ = structural importance weight for that level

Then a simple first-pass definition is:

$$V(t) = \frac{\sum_{i=0}^{n} w_i \, \alpha_i(t)\, C_i(t)\, P_i(t)}{\sum_{i=0}^{n} w_i \, \alpha_i(t)}$$

where:

### 1. Content match term

$$C_i(t) = \exp\left(-\frac{\|a_i(t)-d_i(t)\|^2}{\sigma_i^2}\right)$$

This is high when ascending and descending content match.

### 2. Phase alignment term

$$P_i(t) = \frac{1+\cos(\phi^a_i(t)-\phi^d_i(t))}{2}$$

This is high when the two signals are phase-aligned.

So:

* $C_i(t)$ asks: **do they say the same thing?**
* $P_i(t)$ asks: **are they arriving in the same temporal channel?**

And $V(t)$ asks:

> across the hierarchy, how well do descending predictions and ascending sensory-constrained signals match in both content and timing?
