

THOUGHT INTAKE

OCD as Failure Modes of Goal-Field Governance and Termination in REE. a better summary

⸻

Core Claim

Obsessive–compulsive disorder (OCD) can be modelled in REE as a class of failure modes in which:

Goal fields in frontal E1 are over-bound and/or improperly released, while distributed E3 arbitration remains persistently driven by unresolved residue and conflict signals.

This results in:

* repeated trajectory selection toward corrective actions
* failure of termination despite action execution
* persistence of evaluation signals beyond resolution conditions

⸻

Architectural Grounding

E1 (frontal)

* encodes goal fields as latent attractors
* includes safety, correctness, alignment, moral constraints

E3 (distributed process)

* arbitration across:
    * hippocampal trajectory proposals
    * frontal goal fields
    * cingulate conflict/salience signals

Residue

* encodes unresolved mismatch or harm potential in z_world

Cingulate systems

* dACC: conflict / unresolved state detection
* AIC/PCC/PACC: salience, interoception, coherence

Control plane

* modulates:
    * commitment thresholds
    * precision of error signals
    * persistence vs disengagement

⸻

Core Failure Loop

Normal operation:

goal field → trajectory → action → residue reduces → mode exits

OCD regime:

goal field (safety/correctness)
    ↓
trajectory selected
    ↓
action executed
    ↓
residue persists / conflict remains
    ↓
E3 re-selects corrective trajectory
    ↓
loop

⸻

Unifying Mechanism

All OCD subtypes share:

evaluation persists in the absence of a functioning termination/closure mechanism

Formally:

residue > 0
+ conflict signal high
+ no authoritative closure state
→ continued arbitration toward correction

⸻

Parameter-Level Interpretation

Over-binding of goal fields (E1)

* goal attractors too deep
* competing attractors suppressed excessively
* system remains anchored to corrective objective

⸻

Persistent conflict signalling (dACC)

* conflict does not saturate
* remains high despite repeated identical outcomes
* drives continued arbitration

⸻

Residue non-discharge

* corrective action does not sufficiently reduce residue
* or residue threshold for “resolved” is unreachable

⸻

Missing closure representation (SD-033 gap)

* no persistent “done” state
* no enforcement of completion across time
* no suppression of further evaluation

⸻

Control-plane imbalance

* high sensory/error precision (α_S)
* high commitment requirement (κ_commit)
* insufficient mode exit dynamics

⸻

Subtype Mappings

1. Contamination OCD

* interoceptive residue hypersensitivity
* AIC amplification of internal “unclean” signal
* failure of “clean” completion state

⸻

2. Checking OCD

* dACC conflict remains unsaturated
* verification does not bind into persistent state
* repeated identical actions do not reduce uncertainty

⸻

3. Symmetry / “just right”

* continuous-valued residue metric
* no tolerance band (ε)
* E1 completion bias drives infinite refinement

⸻

4. Intrusive thoughts (harm / taboo)

* hippocampal hypothetical trajectories treated as real
* failure of hypothesis isolation
* residue triggered by simulation rather than action

⸻

5. Mental rituals

* internal E1/E2 loops without external grounding
* no sensory update → no residue resolution
* closed internal loop persists

⸻

6. Responsibility OCD

* extended horizon (H) with catastrophic low-probability futures
* overweighting of rare harm in residue calculation
* persistent preventative mode

⸻

7. Relationship OCD

* unstable commitment to goal field (relationship valuation)
* continuous re-evaluation without binding
* absence of persistent “chosen trajectory”

⸻

8. Pure O

* strong hippocampal simulation
* absence of action-based discharge
* evaluation loop without resolution channel

⸻

Axis of Dysfunction

OCD occupies one side of a broader control spectrum:

under-binding ←→ over-binding
(depression)     (OCD)

Where:

* depression → insufficient persistence of goal fields
* OCD → excessive persistence without termination

⸻

Key Insight

OCD is not primarily a disorder of action, but of termination.

More precisely:

A system in which goal-directed correction cannot be turned off because closure is not representable or enforceable.

⸻

Implication for REE Development

To avoid OCD-like dynamics, REE requires:

1. Closure operator
    * explicit “done” state
    * resistant to noise
2. Residue discharge rules
    * action-dependent reduction
    * threshold-based resolution
3. Conflict saturation
    * repeated identical outcomes reduce dACC signal
4. Mode exit dynamics
    * active disengagement conditions
    * not purely decay-based
5. Hypothesis isolation
    * simulated trajectories do not accumulate residue

⸻

Compression

OCD = persistent goal-field activation + unresolved residue + absent closure enforcement
