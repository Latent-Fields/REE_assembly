# Origin and Development of Moral Sense: A Systematic Review — Limone & Toto (2022)

## Relevance to Q-006

The question Q-006 poses -- whether ethics is developmental rather than additive -- invites a straightforward reading: either moral capacity must be built stage-by-stage, or it can be grafted on after a mature cognitive system exists. Limone and Toto's systematic review is useful precisely because it refuses the clean version of either answer, and that refusal carries its own evidential weight.

## What the review establishes

Covering the developmental psychology literature from infancy through adolescence, the review concludes that moral sense is both innate (children show moral discrimination from roughly 8 months) and irreducibly social (peer negotiation and environmental exposure are not optional add-ons but co-constitutive at every stage). Piaget's three-phase sequence and Kohlberg's staged framework are reviewed alongside more recent empirical work, and the synthesis notes that each successive developmental layer -- from automatic avoidance of antisocial agents, to reciprocal fairness in peer play, to principled abstract reasoning -- depends on the preceding one having been established.

The social dimension is worth dwelling on. The review does not say that ethics is added from outside after a complete internal architecture; it says that each internal stage is only accessible through specific forms of social engagement. Peer interaction promotes reasoning that parent-child interaction alone does not. The moral environment is scaffolding, not decoration.

## The complication for a simple REE reading

ARC-015 argues that ethical capacity must be built into REE from the start rather than appended at deployment. Limone and Toto broadly support this. But their emphasis on social co-constitution introduces a complication: if each developmental stage requires a specific kind of social environment to unlock, then the relevant analogue in REE is not just the network architecture but the training curriculum -- the distribution and sequencing of training experiences. A developmentally ordered architecture with a flat training distribution might not show the staged emergence at all.

This is not a rebuttal to ARC-015 but rather a specification of what 'ground-up' must mean: it means both structural readiness (the architecture can support each successive layer) and curricular scaffolding (the training signal promotes the relevant integration at each stage). An ethics module bolted onto a trained general predictor would fail on both counts.

## Calibration note

Confidence is marked as mixed at 0.62 because the review confirms the developmental-stages picture while complicating the simple 'developmental vs. additive' framing. The absence of formal quantitative synthesis is a genuine limitation -- this is a narrative systematic review, and the heterogeneity of included studies (different ages, tasks, and operationalisations) is not formally handled. The strongest claims should be treated as directionally suggestive rather than precisely evidenced.
