# de Waal & Preston (2017) -- Mammalian empathy: behavioural manifestations and neural basis

**Claim tested:** MECH-183 -- z_beta leakage from attributed other-model into self affective processing

## What the paper finds

De Waal and Preston (2017) update their foundational PAM framework in a comprehensive review in Nature Reviews Neuroscience. The key developments since 2002: (1) behavioral evidence for consolation (spontaneous approach and contact with distressed conspecifics) in chimpanzees, corvids, and elephants -- showing active approach toward distress, not just passive resonance; (2) contagious physiological affect -- autonomic synchrony (heart rate, cortisol) in socially bonded pairs experiencing the same stressor -- this is direct state sharing below the level of conscious inference; (3) aid-giving behavior across mammalian species motivated by the other's distress state; (4) mirror neuron and insula/ACC data supporting shared neural representations for self and other states; (5) the key role of past personal experience: having experienced the relevant distressing event oneself increases empathic accuracy.

## The direct-activation evidence

MECH-183 requires that the other's affective state directly activates self's z_beta -- not as inference but as state leakage. De Waal & Preston's strongest evidence for this interpretation is contagious physiological affect: in socially bonded pairs, one individual's autonomic state (heart rate, cortisol) tracks the other's autonomic state. This cannot be explained by conscious inference about the other's emotional state -- it is direct cross-individual physiological coupling. This is the closest behavioral analogue to z_beta leakage that currently exists in the literature. The paper also notes that emotional contagion is the phylogenetically earliest form of empathy, present even in non-primate mammals, consistent with MECH-183's claim that direct state activation precedes more sophisticated mentalizing.

## The role of past personal experience

A key implication for MECH-182 as well: the paper reports that past personal experience with the relevant painful or distressing event directly increases empathic accuracy. This is the PAM's prediction that self-representations ground cross-agent state attribution. For MECH-182, this means the acoustic harm-signal learned from self-experience would specifically enhance recognition of the same acoustic pattern in another agent's vocalizations -- a testable prediction that the comparative vocalization literature has not yet directly examined.

## Novelty implications

The 2017 paper is the most comprehensive current review of direct affective state sharing in mammals. MECH-183 is well-anticipated at the functional/behavioral level by the PAM. The specific REE architectural claim -- that leakage occurs at the z_beta variable level, gated by the OTHER_SELFLIKE attribution tag, mediated by the attribution stream -- is not present in the biological literature. This is the novel REE-specific mechanistic layer on top of an otherwise well-described functional phenomenon.
