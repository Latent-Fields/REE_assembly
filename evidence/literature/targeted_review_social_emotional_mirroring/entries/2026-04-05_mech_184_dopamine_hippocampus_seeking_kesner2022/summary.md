# Kesner, Calva & Ikemoto (2022) -- Seeking motivation and reward: Roles of dopamine, hippocampus, and supramammillo-septal pathway

**Claims tested:** MECH-184 (other-directed hippocampal harm avoidance), MECH-185 (surprise-doubled approach gradient)

## What the paper finds

Kesner et al. (2022) propose and review evidence for a specific neural circuit for curiosity and information-seeking behavior: supramammillary nucleus (SuM) -> hippocampal formation -> medial septum (glutamatergic neurons) -> ventral tegmental area (VTA; dopaminergic neurons). Key features: (1) the hippocampal formation processes the *cognitive* aspects of information-seeking -- salience perception and uncertainty; (2) phasic dopamine responses from VTA signal "potentially important stimuli" rather than primary rewards; (3) the authors introduce "environment prediction error" -- discrepancy between expected and observed environmental states -- as the driving signal; (4) uncertainty and novelty trigger seeking behavior that is distinct from reward-seeking in its neural substrates and motivational character.

## Relevance to MECH-184

MECH-184 claims hippocampal rollout proposals can target harm avoidance for another agent when the harm-gradient source is other-referenced. The Kesner et al. circuit provides a plausible substrate: when the environment contains a distressed other agent (an unexpected, salient event generating prediction error), the SuM-hippocampal-dopamine circuit would trigger seeking/approach toward that stimulus. The hippocampus, as the hub for environment prediction error, would receive the salience signal from the unexpected distress cue and generate prospective processing (approach, investigation). This is not identical to harm-avoidance planning, but it provides the motivational impetus and hippocampal engagement that MECH-184 requires.

## Relevance to MECH-185

MECH-185 predicts that an unexpected acoustic signal from a distressed loved one generates a curiosity-approach gradient via prediction error, in addition to the z_beta leakage approach gradient. Kesner et al. provide the neural mechanism for this: unexpected acoustic stimuli generate environment prediction error -> SuM -> hippocampal engagement -> dopaminergic approach signal. This is the first curiosity gradient in MECH-185's "surprise-doubled" account. The paper does not address the second gradient (z_beta leakage), but it provides the circuit for the first.

## Limitation: non-social domain

All the experiments and examples in Kesner et al. involve spatial or non-social environments. The extension to a social stimulus (distressed other) requires assuming that the hippocampal prediction error computation generalizes from spatial/environmental to social prediction errors. Given that the hippocampus is involved in social cognition (Amft et al. 2014), this is plausible but not directly demonstrated here.
