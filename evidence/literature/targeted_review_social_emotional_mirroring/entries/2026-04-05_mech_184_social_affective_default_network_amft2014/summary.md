# Amft et al. (2014) -- Definition and characterization of an extended social-affective default network

**Claim tested:** MECH-184 -- other-directed hippocampal harm avoidance

## What the paper finds

Amft et al. (2014) define and characterize an extended social-affective default mode network (SA-DMN) using meta-analytic connectivity modeling (seed-based ALE analysis) and resting-state data from a large sample. Their key finding: the SA-DMN consists of distinct functional clusters: (1) amygdala/hippocampus -- associated with emotional processes and memory; (2) vmPFC, ventral striatum, anterior cingulum, subgenual cingulum -- associated with motivation, reward, and cognitive modulation of affect; (3) posterior cingulum/precuneus and dmPFC -- associated with mentalizing, self-reference, autobiographic memory; (4) TPJ and anterior middle temporal sulcus -- associated with language and social cognition. All these clusters are robustly interconnected, forming a network central to "introspective, socio-affective, self- and other-related mental processes."

## The anatomical substrate for MECH-184

MECH-184 claims that hippocampal rollout proposals can be directed toward protecting another agent when the harm-gradient source is other-referenced (via MECH-183's z_beta leakage). For this to work, the hippocampus must be embedded in a network that receives social-affective information about the other and connects it to motivational/planning systems. Amft et al. provide exactly this: the SA-DMN connects hippocampus (via the amygdala/hippocampus emotional-memory cluster) with vmPFC (motivation/reward in social-affective contexts) and with the mentalizing network (TPJ, dmPFC). If z_beta leakage from MECH-183 activates the SA-DMN's affective processing, the hippocampus -- embedded in this network -- would receive social-affective motivational signals and could in principle generate rollout proposals responsive to the other's harm gradient.

## What the paper does not show

The paper characterizes the hippocampus's role in the SA-DMN as "emotional processes and memory" -- not specifically prospective planning for another's welfare. MECH-184 requires a more active function: that hippocampal rollout generation is directed toward the other's harm avoidance. This prospective, other-directed planning function is not demonstrated by connectivity analyses. The SA-DMN structure is a necessary but not sufficient condition for MECH-184. Additional evidence for hippocampal involvement in planning for others (not just remembering about others) would be needed to directly support the claim.

## Novelty implications

The existence of a social-affective DMN that connects hippocampus, vmPFC, and mentalizing regions is well-established. What is not described is the specific MECH-184 claim: that the hippocampal rollout planning architecture is structurally identical for self-preservation and other-directed harm avoidance, with only the harm-gradient source (self vs. attributed other) changing. This specific mechanistic equivalence claim, which is the novel contribution of MECH-184, is not found in the literature.
