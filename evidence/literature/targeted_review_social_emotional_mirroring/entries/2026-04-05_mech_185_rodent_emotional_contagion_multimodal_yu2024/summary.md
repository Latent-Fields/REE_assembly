# Yu, Bao & Yin (2024) -- Emotional contagion in rodents: A comprehensive exploration of mechanisms and multimodal perspectives

**Claims tested:** MECH-185 (surprise-doubled approach gradient), MECH-182 (vocalization timbre), MECH-183 (z_beta leakage)

## What the paper finds

Yu et al. (2024) review the mechanisms of emotional contagion in rodents across four sensory modalities: (1) Olfactory -- two types of pheromones (alarm pheromones and social comfort pheromones) are modulated by oxytocin and directly transmit emotional states to conspecifics; (2) Auditory -- squeaks and ultrasonic vocalizations (USVs) at different frequencies (22 kHz = distress/alarm; 50 kHz = positive arousal) correlate with different emotional states and are essential for social emotional communication; (3) Visual -- facial expression and observational movement cues (less dominant in rodents); (4) Tactile -- allogrooming and socio-affective touch directly modulate emotional states. The core characterization: emotional contagion is "automatic and unconscious." The review highlights multimodal integration as the mechanism for full contagious emotional responses.

## Relevance to MECH-182: the auditory channel

The auditory pathway -- 22 kHz alarm USVs signaling distress -- is the rodent analogue of MECH-182's vocal timbre harm signal. In rodents, these calls are produced during high-arousal distress states and directly elicit defensive/alarm responses in conspecifics. This is consistent with MECH-182's claim that acoustic features of vocalizations carry cross-modal harm signals. However, in rodents, the 22 kHz calls appear to be largely innate signals rather than learned from self-experience -- the learning component of MECH-182 remains unaddressed.

## Relevance to MECH-183: direct-activation channels

The olfactory pathway is the clearest example of direct-state-activation in rodents: alarm pheromones chemically encode the sender's arousal state and directly activate the receiver's autonomic/affective system without requiring inference. This is the rodent analogue of z_beta leakage -- the chemical signal is a direct carrier of affective state rather than a proxy requiring interpretation. The oxytocin modulation is relevant to the coupling mechanism (familiarity and bonding modulate the strength of chemical signal transmission), paralleling MECH-183's OTHER_SELFLIKE coupling requirement.

## Relevance to MECH-185: multimodal convergence

The review's emphasis on multimodal integration is directly relevant to MECH-185's dual-gradient account. In rodents, full emotional contagion involves simultaneous auditory (USV) and olfactory (alarm pheromone) signaling from a distressed conspecific. These are two parallel channels transmitting the same emotional state information, producing a stronger combined response. While Yu et al. do not frame this as "two independent approach gradients," the functional structure is parallel to MECH-185: multiple simultaneous channels converging on a shared behavioral output (approach, affiliation, or defense). The review thus provides indirect support for the dual-channel (not single-channel) account that MECH-185 requires.
