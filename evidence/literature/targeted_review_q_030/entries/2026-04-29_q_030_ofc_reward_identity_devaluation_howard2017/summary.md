# Howard & Kahnt (2017): Identity-specific reward representations in OFC modulated by selective devaluation

## What the paper did

Howard and Kahnt designed a sensory-specific satiety paradigm to dissociate identity-specific from general-value reward coding in the human brain. Hungry participants chose between high- and low-intensity versions of two food-odour rewards. They then ate a meal corresponding to one of the two odours (selective devaluation), and choice and fMRI were re-tested. The crucial methodological move is that the two rewards started with matched value, so any post-meal change must reflect identity-specific updating rather than a general value shift.

## Key findings

In the hungry state, participants chose high-intensity versions of both rewards. After eating one of the meals, choices for the sated odour shifted toward low intensity, while choices for the non-sated odour continued to favour high intensity. fMRI multivoxel pattern analysis localised the identity-specific signature to lateral posterior orbitofrontal cortex (OFC): the pattern coding for the sated-odour identity was specifically altered after the meal, while the pattern for the non-sated odour was preserved. A separate ventromedial prefrontal cortex (vmPFC) region showed general value coding (responsive to how-much-do-I-want-it-now without identity specificity). Functional connectivity between OFC and vmPFC predicted individual differences in how much choice behaviour shifted with satiety.

## Limitations and caveats

OFC function is one of the most contested questions in cognitive neuroscience. The identity-specific coding view (Howard, Kahnt, and colleagues) competes with the cognitive-map view (Schoenbaum, Niv) that OFC encodes more general task-state information including currently-relevant features that are not always identity-bound. If the cognitive-map view is correct, the clean identity-vs-value architecture this paper proposes does not map onto an actual anatomical separation -- OFC and vmPFC may encode different aspects of a more unified task-state representation. The paradigm tests sensory-specific satiety in food odours, which is one operationalisation of identity-specific reward; how well it generalises to the abstract resource representations Q-030 posits (e.g., a token z_resource that encodes "food-type X" invariant to spatial location) is uncertain. The OFC-vmPFC connectivity finding is correlational, so the direction of information flow is inferred rather than directly measured.
