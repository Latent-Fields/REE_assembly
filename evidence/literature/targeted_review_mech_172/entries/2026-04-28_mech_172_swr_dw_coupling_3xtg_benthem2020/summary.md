# Benthem, Skelin, Moseley, Stimmell, Dixon, Melilli, Molina, McNaughton & Wilber (2020) — Impaired hippocampal-cortical interactions during sleep in a mouse model of AD

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cub.2020.04.087); PMID 32470367).

## What the paper did

3xTg-AD mice (amyloid + tau transgenic) and C57 controls were chronically implanted with recording arrays spanning isocortex and hippocampus, then ran a spatial reorientation task across daily rest-task-rest sessions. The dependent variables were sleep architecture, hippocampal sharp-wave-ripple (SWR) density, cortical delta-wave (DW) density, cortical spindles, and the temporal coupling between these events. Behavioural performance the next day was correlated with the night's coupling statistics.

## Key findings

3xTg mice slept *more* than controls but produced *fewer* SWRs per unit time, with the reduced ripple density compensated by extended sleep so total ripple count was similar. The crucial signal was the coupling: SWR-DW coupling and spindle-DW coupling were both impaired in AD mice. In controls, SWR-DW coupling strengthened in post-task sleep and predicted the next day's spatial-reorientation accuracy. In AD mice, this learning-related coupling enhancement and its behavioural correlation were both lost.

## Mapping to MECH-172

This paper supplies the broken-pipeline premise of MECH-172. REE asserts that AD specifically degrades the offline consolidation machinery (hippocampus-to-cortex schema installation), and that this is what selectively damages the contextual attribution system (E3 hippocampal) while leaving the model-free habit system intact. Benthem provides the electrophysiological substrate for the "AD breaks consolidation" half of that claim. SWR-DW coupling is exactly the candidate mechanism for hippocampus-to-cortex transfer in the systems-consolidation literature, and it is exactly what is broken.

The complementary half — that the habit system is preserved under the same pathology — Benthem does not directly test. The behavioural read-out is spatial reorientation, which depends on the hippocampal pipeline that the mice cannot use. So this entry is best read as supporting the broken-pipeline premise specifically, not the dissociation as a whole. The dissociation reading needs to compose Benthem with the AD-procedural-preservation literature (Schmitz 2014 entry).

## Caveats

The 3xTg model bundles amyloid and tau under transgenic promoters and the temporal trajectory of pathology differs from sporadic human AD. The Wei et al. 2025 entry in this same review provides the human counterpart (SO-spindle and SO-theta coupling disruption in MCI/AD humans), which addresses the transfer concern but with weaker mechanistic resolution. Together they triangulate well: mouse-mechanism + human-correlation.

## Confidence reasoning

I land at 0.78. The electrophysiology is high quality and the within-paper correlation between coupling and behavioural performance gives a useful causal-shaped signal even though the design is correlational. The transfer risk is real (3xTg is not sporadic AD) and the paper does not test the habit-preservation half of MECH-172, which keeps the score from going higher.
