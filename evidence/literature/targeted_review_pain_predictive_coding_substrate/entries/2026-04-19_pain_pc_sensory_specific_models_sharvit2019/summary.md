# Sharvit, Vuilleumier, Corradi-Dell'Acqua 2019 -- Anterior insula runs sensory-specific prospective models

**Citation.** Sharvit G, Vuilleumier P, Corradi-Dell'Acqua C. Sensory-specific predictive models in the human anterior insula. F1000Research 2019; 8: 164. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.12688/f1000research.17961.1). (According to PubMed.)

**What they did.** Commentary synthesising two independent cross-modal pain-expectancy fMRI studies (Sharvit 2018 and Fazeli & Buchel 2018). Both studies cued participants for aversive modality (pain vs not-pain) and intensity probabilistically, measuring how cross-modal expectancy violations propagate through insula responses.

**Key finding from the synthesis.** Middle-anterior insula responses to noxious stimuli are modulated only by expectation of pain, not by expectation of other aversive events (loud noises, disgusting images). The authors argue this is strong evidence that the anterior insula maintains multiple, sensory-specific prospective models -- not a single general unpleasantness model. Sub-portions of the middle-anterior insula carry expectancy signals versus prediction-error signals; these are dissociable.

**What this does for the REE question.** This is the clearest conceptual statement in the pull that pain, disgust, etc. each have their own forward model in the same anatomical region. The architecture of choice is therefore "multiple forward models sharing substrate". By extension, it is architecturally coherent in REE to have E2_harm_s and E2_harm_a share substrate (a common trunk) while exposing different heads -- since that is what the biology already does across aversive modalities at a higher level.

**Limitations.** It is a review, not new data. The within-pain question (A-delta vs C-fiber) is outside its explicit scope; the extrapolation rests on the argument that if the brain distinguishes pain-vs-disgust with sensory-specific heads in one region, the same design principle plausibly governs within-pain sub-streams.
