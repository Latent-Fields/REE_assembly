# Fazeli & Buchel 2018 -- Pain PE signals in anterior insula are pain-specific, not general-aversive

**Citation.** Fazeli S, Buchel C. Pain-Related Expectation and Prediction Error Signals in the Anterior Insula Are Not Related to Aversiveness. J Neurosci 2018; 38(29): 6461-6474. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1523/JNEUROSCI.0671-18.2018). (According to PubMed.)

**What they did.** Crossed painful heat with aversive pictures at three intensities each, cueing modality and intensity probabilistically. The question: is the pain-predictive signal in anterior insula actually pain-specific, or does it just reflect aversive-event prediction in general?

**Key finding.** Dorsal anterior insula tracked pain intensity and expectation. Ventral anterior insula tracked the prediction error -- but only when the cued modality matched delivered modality (cue says pain, pain arrives). Under a modality-mismatch (cue says aversive picture, pain arrives), the ventral anterior insula signal collapsed to raw intensity, losing its integration signature. Posterior insula just reported intensity in all conditions.

**What this does for the REE question.** It makes the pain forward model specifically pain-shaped. You cannot build E2_harm_a as a generic "negative valence expected" predictor -- the biology shows that the integration machinery is selective to the modality of the predicted input. This constrains the input ontology of E2_harm_a: its expectation signal must be pain-indexed, not a pooled negative-affect signal.

**Where Fazeli is silent on the sharper question.** The paper dissociates pain from non-pain (pictures). It does not dissociate within-pain sub-modalities (A-delta sensory-discriminative vs C-fiber affective). So it does not directly answer whether E2_harm_a and E2_harm_s share substrate. It argues that both would need to be pain-indexed. It does not argue they must be separate.

**Limitations.** Male-only sample, single pain modality (heat), BOLD resolution, fMRI cannot see the A-delta/C distinction.

**Confidence reasoning.** Strong methodology and clean dissociation. Mapping fidelity is moderate because the stream-within-pain question is not addressed. I read this as weak evidence against "one generic aversive forward model" (shared with emotion/disgust/threat) and no evidence either way on within-pain sub-stream sharing.
