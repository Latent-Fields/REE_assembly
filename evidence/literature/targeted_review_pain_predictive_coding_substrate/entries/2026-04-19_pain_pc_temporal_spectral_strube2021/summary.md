# Strube et al. 2021 -- Pain PE has an inverted gamma signature

**Citation.** Strube A, Rose M, Fazeli S, Buchel C. The temporal and spectral characteristics of expectations and prediction errors in pain and thermoception. eLife 2021; 10: e62809. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.62809). (According to PubMed.)

**What they did.** EEG complement to the Geuter/Fazeli fMRI programme. Same probabilistic heat-pain expectation paradigm, but resolving the millisecond-scale oscillatory signatures of expectation and prediction error.

**Key finding.** Alpha-to-beta activity carried the expectation signal. Gamma-band activity was negatively modulated by the absolute PE -- larger PE produced less gamma, not more. This inverts the canonical predictive-coding signature from visual and auditory cortex, where PE is associated with increased gamma. It resembles the pattern seen in working memory and word matching, where gamma reflects confirmed predictions rather than violations.

**What this does for the REE question.** It says the pain forward-prediction computation is implementationally distinct from exteroceptive predictive coding, even when framed under the same Bayesian scheme. That constrains how you build E2_harm_a or E2_harm_s: you are not just re-using a vision-style predictor with a nociceptive input. This matters architecturally but does not directly settle whether the two harm streams should share a trunk.

**Limitations.** EEG cannot dissociate A-delta from C-fiber contributions; the oscillatory signature is averaged across both nociceptive pathways. The scalp resolution cannot separate dorsal posterior insula from anterior insula signals cleanly.

**Confidence reasoning.** Strong methodology, peer-reviewed eLife. But relevance to the shared-vs-parallel architectural question is indirect. Mapping fidelity 0.55. I include it as supporting material rather than a decisive entry.
