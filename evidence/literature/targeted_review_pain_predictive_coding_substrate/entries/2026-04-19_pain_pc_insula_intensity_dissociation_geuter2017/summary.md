# Geuter et al. 2017 -- Insula intensity vs predictive coding dissociation

**Citation.** Geuter S, Boll S, Eippert F, Buchel C. Functional dissociation of stimulus intensity encoding and predictive coding of pain in the insula. eLife 2017; 6: e24770. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.24770). (According to PubMed.)

**What they did.** Healthy volunteers received probabilistically cued heat-pain stimuli while in fMRI. The cue signalled one of two intensity distributions; the delivered stimulus was sampled from the cued distribution. They asked whether insula sub-regions reflect (i) stimulus intensity, (ii) additive intensity + expectation, or (iii) the predictive-coding signature (expectation + precision-weighted prediction error).

**Key finding.** Posterior insula tracked stimulus intensity. Anterior insula, skin conductance, and pupil dilation tracked the predictive-coding pattern strictly -- responses scaled with expectation and with the mismatch between expected and delivered intensity. Posterior and anterior insula are functionally dissociable: one consumes intensity, the other integrates expectation with sensory input.

**What this does for the REE question.** It answers half of the architectural question cleanly. The consumer side of the pain stream is split: the sensory-discriminative intensity signal (analog of z_harm_s) lands in posterior insula; the affective/predictive-coding integration (analog of what we want z_harm_a to do inside SD-032b) lands in anterior insula. These are not the same region doing both jobs. That is evidence for parallel consumers.

**Where Geuter is silent.** The paper does not test whether there are two independent forward models upstream of these consumers. One forward model with two readouts is fully consistent with the data. So Geuter alone cannot adjudicate between "shared trunk with stream-specific heads" (option a) and "two independent forward models" (option b). It tells us the readout is parallel, not the predictor.

**Limitations.** Healthy young adults; single modality (heat); BOLD-level spatial resolution. The posterior-vs-anterior split is robust but the internal architecture of each region is not resolvable in fMRI.

**Confidence reasoning.** High confidence that consumers are parallel (this replicates across the Buchel group). Moderate confidence for what it implies about the forward model. I would not weight this paper to decide between (a) and (b); I would weight it heavily to rule out a single unified consumer.
