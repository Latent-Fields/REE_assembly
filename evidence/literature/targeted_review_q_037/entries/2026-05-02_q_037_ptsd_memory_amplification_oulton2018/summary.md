# Oulton et al. 2018 -- Memory amplification and reality monitoring in trauma analogue

## What the paper did

Oulton and colleagues used a standard trauma-analogue paradigm: participants viewed highly negative graphic photographs (the analogue stimulus), then logged intrusive memories of the photos over 24 hours. The experimental manipulation was instructive: a subgroup was asked to *elaborate* on their intrusions -- to imagine details about the events beyond what they actually saw in the photographs. After 24 hours, memory for the photos was assessed twice. The question was whether elaboration would amplify or attenuate later memory and trauma endorsement.

## Key findings

The result has two components. First, the elaboration condition reported **fewer** intrusions about the photos than controls, not more. Second, elaboration also produced *less* endorsement of trauma exposure on later assessment. The authors read this as a reality-monitoring story: elaboration enhances conceptual processing of the trauma analogue, which competes with the perceptual/affective intrusion pathway. The blend of imagined and real detail that produces 'memory amplification' in real-world trauma reports is consistent with the same reality-monitoring framework -- imagined details cross the source boundary and become endorsed as witnessed. Elaboration appears to recruit a parallel processing route that gives the content a different memorial signature.

## How this maps to REE

Q-037 asks whether REE's three psychosis substrates are dissociable. MECH-094 is the substrate I'm interested in here -- the categorical hypothesis-tag write gate. The architectural claim is that the tag prevents simulation/replay content from entering the post-commit residue channel; tag loss writes simulated content as if it were committed, producing flashbacks and intrusive vivid memory.

Oulton's finding is direct evidence for the substrate. Imagined elaborations bleed into endorsed exposure -- the architectural definition of source confusion across the simulated/committed boundary. The mechanism is exactly what MECH-094 implements: a tag that distinguishes which content was imagined vs which was perceived/witnessed, whose failure produces the blend. The protective effect of elaboration is the more interesting finding for REE. It suggests that MECH-094's pathology is not simply 'all imagined content leaks' but is specifically about raw, perceptually-rich, low-context replay. Elaboration creates a second, conceptually-rich route that competes for the same memorial slot. In REE terms: the residue field is gated, not all-or-nothing, and conceptual context provides an alternative gating signature that bypasses the broken tag.

This matters for Q-037 because if MECH-094 has this internal structure (raw vs elaborated replay paths), then dissociation tests need to probe the right kind of replay. A test that uses richly elaborated imagery may not reveal MECH-094 pathology even if it is present.

## Limitations and caveats

The paradigm is a trauma analogue, not clinical PTSD. Negative photographs and 24-hour intrusion logs are a pale shadow of what disabling flashbacks actually do to a person's life and physiology. The mechanism that produces a small lab-task shift may not be the same mechanism producing the clinical phenotype. The protective effect of elaboration almost certainly does not generalise to real PTSD -- in clinical practice, asking a trauma survivor to elaborate on intrusions can re-traumatise rather than protect, because the affective load is overwhelming. The mapping to MECH-094's specific 'replay event boundary' locus is also by interpretation; this experiment does not probe replay or boundary signals directly.

## Confidence reasoning

I am holding this at 0.72. The behavioural finding directly demonstrates the substrate-level failure mode MECH-094 predicts -- imagined content blending into committed memory -- which is strong support. But the source-quality is good-not-exceptional (mid-tier specialty journal, lab-task design) and the trauma-analogue paradigm has well-known ecological limits. Useful as part of the evidentiary picture for Q-037, but on its own it doesn't close the substrate-existence question.
