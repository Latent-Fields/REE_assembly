# Corlett & Fraser 2025 -- 20 Years of Aberrant Salience in Psychosis

## What the paper did

Corlett (Yale) and Fraser (Minnesota) wrote a 20-year retrospective on Shitij Kapur's 2003 aberrant-salience hypothesis -- the framework that has dominated lab and clinic talk about delusion formation for two decades. They evaluate the original predictions about striatal dopamine dysfunction against the data accumulated since, and identify which contemporary theories better fit what is actually known about dopaminergic circuits and delusional patients.

## Key findings

The honest summary: the original Kapur formulation has not aged well in its specific neural and behavioural predictions. Newer probes of dopaminergic function (cell-type-specific recordings, optogenetic manipulation, fine-grained behavioural paradigms) have shown that dopamine's role in psychosis is broader and more nuanced than 'inappropriate salience to neutral stimuli'. Contemporary accounts -- Corlett's own predictive-coding framework included -- assign dopamine roles in **belief formation, belief updating under uncertainty, and abductive inference to the best explanation**. Each of these is a distinct computational function with a distinct failure mode.

## How this maps to REE

Q-037 asks whether REE's three psychosis substrates are dissociable. MECH-065 is REE's precision-ratio miscalibration substrate, with a predicted clinical fingerprint of 'paranoid ideation driven by specific external agents, ideas of reference toward external sources.' MECH-223 is the companion bias for animated-agency overweighting. Together they implement REE's version of the aberrant-salience family.

Corlett's review is a useful tonic on this. It supports the broad claim that there *is* a precision-related psychosis substrate distinct from the other two REE substrates (the paranoid-ideation phenotype is real and dopamine-modulated). But it weakens the simpler form of MECH-065 by suggesting that the substrate may itself be multi-process -- belief formation, belief updating, and abductive inference are different computations with different failure signatures. If REE's MECH-065 is being conceived as a single 'RC-conflict threshold too low' parameter, that may be too coarse. The substrate might need to be split into sub-mechanisms, or its behavioural fingerprint reframed as a multivariate signature rather than a single index.

This has direct bearing on Q-037's resolution path. If MECH-065 is a single substrate with one fingerprint, three-substrate dissociation is a tractable test. If MECH-065 is itself a cluster of sub-mechanisms (each with its own fingerprint), then the dissociation experiment needs to be designed to discriminate among five-or-more loci, not three.

## Limitations and caveats

The review is theoretical, not a primary experiment. It synthesises what 20 years of work has produced, but does not directly test any specific computational architecture. Corlett is also one of the major proponents of the predictive-coding-of-psychosis school, so his critique of Kapur is partially a defence of his preferred frame -- the diagnosis of where Kapur fails is more reliable than the prescription of what should replace it. There is no engagement with REE's specific three-substrate framing.
