# Lavalle et al. 2020 -- Dissociable source-monitoring impairments in OCD and SCZ

## What the paper did

Lavalle and colleagues administered a three-condition source-monitoring battery to 32 OCD patients, 38 schizophrenia patients, and 29 matched healthy controls. The three conditions probed (i) internal source monitoring (did *I* say it or did *I* think it?), (ii) external source monitoring (did experimenter A or experimenter B do it?), and (iii) reality monitoring (did this happen externally or did *I* imagine it?). The goal was to test whether the well-known source-monitoring deficits in schizophrenia were a unitary impairment or whether sub-processes carved at different joints.

## Key findings

The clean result: a **single dissociation**. Both OCD and SCZ patients showed deficits on internal source monitoring, but only SCZ patients were impaired on reality monitoring. External monitoring was relatively preserved in both groups. The implication, which the authors state plainly, is that internal source confusion is a transdiagnostic deficit (something OCD obsessions and SCZ delusions may share) while reality monitoring (the self-vs-world distinction) is specifically disturbed in psychosis.

## How this maps to REE

Q-037 asks whether REE's three putative psychosis substrates -- MECH-094 hypothesis-tag failure (reality-monitoring locus, PTSD/replay-driven phenotype), MECH-222 self-attribution overflow (internal-source / referential locus), and MECH-065/MECH-223 precision miscalibration (external-attribution bias / paranoid locus) -- are experimentally dissociable. Lavalle et al. provide an existence proof: the relevant sub-processes in human source monitoring **are** dissociable across populations whose substrate-level pathology differs. That is the architectural form Q-037 calls for.

The mapping is tight on the MECH-094 / MECH-222 axis. MECH-094 is REE's reality-monitoring gate -- the categorical write-protect that prevents simulated/replayed content from entering the post-commit residue channel. Lavalle's reality-monitoring task is the behavioural index of that exact gate. MECH-222 is REE's continuous-z_self residualisation failure -- the mechanism by which self-predicted content fails to be subtracted from z_world. Lavalle's internal-source task is the behavioural index of *that* gate (did this content originate from my own generative process?).

## Limitations and caveats

The mapping is by analogy, not direct test. OCD is not one of REE's three psychosis substrates, and Q-037's payload demand is **triple** dissociation (one fingerprint per substrate), which Lavalle does not provide. They show single dissociation across two disorders; Q-037 wants three dissociations within psychosis. Behavioural source-monitoring tasks also probe recall, not the upstream encoding-time gate -- so a positive result on the task doesn't uniquely identify which substrate is broken upstream. A patient could fail reality monitoring at recall because (a) the tag was never written (MECH-094), (b) the tag was written but lost during consolidation, or (c) the residue channel itself is mis-routed. The behavioural signature is necessary but not sufficient.

## Confidence reasoning

I am holding this at 0.78. The paper directly demonstrates the *form* Q-037 needs (sub-process dissociation), with strong methodology and a clear pattern. But the specific clinical groups don't map onto the three REE substrates, and triple dissociation -- the actual claim Q-037 is asking about -- is a stronger result than this paper provides. It is the cleanest currently-available empirical anchor for the dissociability thesis, and it tells us the kind of paradigms that would work for an REE-specific test (paired source-monitoring tasks in PTSD vs referential delusion vs paranoid ideation populations), but it does not close Q-037 by itself.
