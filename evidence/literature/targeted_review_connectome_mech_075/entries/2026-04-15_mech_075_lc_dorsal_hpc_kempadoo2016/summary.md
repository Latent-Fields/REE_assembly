# Summary: Kempadoo et al. 2016 -- LC dopamine to dorsal hippocampus

**Entry:** 2026-04-15_mech_075_lc_dorsal_hpc_kempadoo2016
**DOI:** [10.1073/pnas.1616515114](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1616515114)
**Claim tested:** MECH-075

---

There is a quiet anatomical paradox buried in decades of hippocampal dopamine research: the dorsal hippocampus is densely carpeted with D1/D5 receptors, yet VTA dopamine axons are remarkably sparse there. Kempadoo and colleagues set out to resolve this paradox using optogenetics in mice. The answer was not that the receptors are waiting for VTA input that rarely arrives -- the answer was that the primary source of dopamine in dorsal HPC is the locus coeruleus (LC), not the VTA at all. Selective photostimulation of LC axons in dorsal CA1 produced a measurable increase in dopamine (confirmed by HPLC microdialysis), while photostimulation of VTA axons had no such effect in that region. This is not a minor quantitative refinement -- it is a categorical re-routing of the dopaminergic gain circuit.

The behavioral consequences followed the anatomy. LC axon stimulation enhanced selective attention and spatial object recognition in a D1/D5-dependent manner. The implication is that the dopaminergic signal gating spatial memory encoding in the dorsal hippocampus is an arousal/novelty signal from LC, not a reward prediction error from VTA. These are not interchangeable: LC is responsive to surprise, arousal, and the unexpected regardless of valence; VTA encodes signed RPE and responds to reward-predictive cues. The dorsal hippocampus -- the substrate most obviously implicated in map-building, spatial planning, and the REE viability map -- is therefore gated by a signal that tracks cognitive novelty and demands attention, not by one that tracks whether outcomes exceeded expectations.

For MECH-075, this paper is foundational in a specific way: it directly licenses the bifurcation of the claim. The original formulation of MECH-075 spoke undifferentiatedly of "dopaminergic gain/threshold setting" mediated by VTA/SNc. Kempadoo et al. force a correction: in the dorsal HPC, the gain operator is LC-mediated and tracks arousal/novelty. VTA operates primarily on ventral HPC where motivational and affective representations live. The MECH-075 bifurcation is not a theoretical elaboration -- it is the empirical default.

The mapping to REE is direct but not without a gap. Kempadoo et al. show behavioral and neurochemical effects; they do not instrument hippocampal attractor dynamics directly. The inference that LC dopamine release modulates the gain or threshold of hippocampal attractor basins -- making certain spatial representations more or less stable -- is a mechanistically plausible but not directly demonstrated step. D1/D5 receptor activation is known to modulate NMDA-receptor-dependent plasticity (see Lemon and Manahan-Vaughan 2006), which is the synaptic mechanism through which attractor thresholds are set. So the chain is: LC activation -> dorsal HPC dopamine release -> D1/D5 -> altered LTP/LTD threshold -> changed attractor basin geometry. Each link is documented; the full chain in one study is not.

**Relevance to EXQ-192a null result:** EXQ-192a probed VTA-like novelty loop gain on dorsal HPC terrain and found a null result. This paper explains why. There are essentially no VTA axons in dorsal HPC. The probe was targeting the wrong source. The null is not a falsification of MECH-075; it is a mis-targeting of the VTA arm on a substrate where the relevant gain operator is LC-mediated.
