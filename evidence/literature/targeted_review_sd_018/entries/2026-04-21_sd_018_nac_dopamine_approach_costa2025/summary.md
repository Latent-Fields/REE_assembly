# Costa et al. 2025 -- NAc Dopamine and ACh Independence During Motivated Approach

**Claim tested:** SD-018 (encoder.resource_proximity_supervision)
**Direction:** mixed | **Confidence:** 0.65

## What the paper did

Costa et al. simultaneously recorded NAc dopamine (using GRAB-DA sensor) and acetylcholine (GRAB-ACh) in rats performing an instrumental motivated-approach task. The central question was whether dopamine ramps during approach are mechanistically coupled to cholinergic interneuron activity -- a key issue given ongoing debate about whether ramps reflect VTA axonal firing or local striatal circuit dynamics. The main finding was that dopamine and ACh signals are largely independent: their correlation changes sign depending on the specific task phase (positive in some phases, negative in others, uncorrelated in others), rather than showing the anti-correlation predicted if they were mechanistically linked through a single circuit.

## Key findings relevant to SD-018

The independence of dopamine ramps from ACh modulation provides mechanistic support for a key implication of SD-018: the approach-proximity signal in NAc is not a derivative of general attentional, motivational, or contextual neuromodulation but reflects a distinct motivational dimension. This parallels the architectural claim that resource proximity encoding in z_world requires its own supervision signal, not a secondary consequence of the world model being generally well-trained.

The paper also directly addresses the "controversy regarding [ramps'] computational role and whether they are driven by dopamine neuron firing or local circuit mechanisms" -- noting this is unresolved. This caveat is important for SD-018: if ramps are primarily generated by local striatal circuits (not upstream VTA projection) then proximity encoding could emerge from NAc network dynamics rather than requiring a separate upstream circuit. In REE terms, this might suggest that proximity sensitivity could emerge from rich enough E3 integration rather than requiring an explicit resource_proximity_head on z_world. This is why the evidence direction is rated mixed rather than supports.

## REE translation

Costa et al. 2025's finding that dopamine ramps are architecturally independent of ACh (the main neuromodulator of striatal interneurons) maps onto SD-018's design choice: the resource_proximity_head loss is computed separately from the E1 world-model loss, with a distinct weight (resource_proximity_weight) that can be tuned independently. The biological and architectural solutions are structurally parallel: both isolate proximity encoding from the main prediction-error or attentional circuit. The remaining question in both biology and REE is whether this separation is mechanistically obligatory or merely a design choice that aids learning efficiency.

## Limitations and caveats

This paper does not directly show that sensory prediction circuits fail to encode reward proximity -- it shows that NAc dopamine and ACh are dissociated. The inference chain to SD-018 requires multiple steps. It is possible to read Costa et al. 2025 as saying the proximity signal is a NAc-local emergent property, which could weaken rather than strengthen the case for an explicit upstream supervised head. The paper also uses an instrumental task (lever-press + approach) rather than a navigation task, which may invoke somewhat different dopamine dynamics than the maze navigation in Howe et al. 2013.

## Confidence reasoning

Rated mixed at 0.65 because the paper's core finding (DA/ACh independence) is genuine mechanistic evidence for the separateness of proximity encoding, but the mechanistic debate it highlights (VTA firing vs. local circuit) introduces uncertainty in the direction the inference goes for SD-018. The paper is a useful complement to Howe et al. 2013 rather than independently decisive.
