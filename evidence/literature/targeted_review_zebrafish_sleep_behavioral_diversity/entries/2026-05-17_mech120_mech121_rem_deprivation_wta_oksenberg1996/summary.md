# REM Sleep Deprivation Amplifies Winner-Take-All Competitive Plasticity

## Source
Oksenberg A, Shaffery JP, Marks GA, Speciale SG, Mihailoff G, Roffwarg HP (1996). Rapid eye movement sleep deprivation in kittens amplifies LGN cell-size disparity induced by monocular deprivation. *Brain Research: Developmental Brain Research*, 97(1), 51-61. DOI: 10.1016/s0165-3806(96)00131-9

---

## What the paper did

During the visual critical period in kittens (approximately postnatal days 28-42), the investigators combined one week of monocular lid suture with a second week of MD plus REM sleep deprivation via instrumental avoidance. The readout was cell soma size in LGN A laminae (non-deprived eye) and A1 laminae (deprived eye). After MD alone, A1 cells are smaller, A cells larger. After MD plus REMSD, this disparity was substantially amplified: deprived-eye A1 cells became even smaller, non-deprived-eye A cells grew even larger.

## Key finding

Sleep deprivation during the critical period does not merely impair plasticity symmetrically. It accelerates the winner-take-all outcome. The proposed mechanism is that REM sleep normally provides symmetric brainstem activation (via PGO waves) to all LGN relay cells regardless of eye innervation, buffering against the asymmetric competitive advantage accumulating during waking. Without it, the dominant pathway captures even greater synaptic territory.

A companion paper (Shaffery et al., 1999) confirmed the PGO mechanism by lesioning pontomesencephalic PGO generators while preserving tonic REM activity, producing the same amplified disparity -- isolating the phasic REM component as the protective factor.

## Translation to REE

The practical question is whether sleep mechanisms (MECH-120, MECH-121) can rescue a substrate with pre-established waking WTA forward model dominance. The Oksenberg data gives a direct biological answer: sleep deprivation makes WTA convergence worse, not merely unchanged. Conversely, normal sleep is actively doing rescue work against waking competitive dynamics. For REE: severe monostrategy lock-in before the offline phase begins may not be fully reversible in a single sleep cycle. The biological precedent suggests a threshold: mild to moderate competitive imbalance can be rescued by sleep; severe pre-established imbalance may require waking-dynamics correction first.

## Limitations

LGN is a thalamic relay, not cortex. The stress confound from instrumental avoidance REMSD is significant. The soma-size disparity is anatomical; its linear translation into competitive advantage in cortical circuits is an additional inference.

## Confidence

Confidence 0.86. Core finding robust and replicated by the same lab across multiple papers. The biological finding is clear; transfer to REE computational architecture requires several inferential steps.
