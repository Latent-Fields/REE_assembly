# Pinheiro-da-Silva et al. 2016 — Sleep Deprivation Abolishes Object Discrimination in Zebrafish

**Source:** Pinheiro-da-Silva J, Silva PF, Nogueira MB, Luchiari AC. "Sleep deprivation effects on object discrimination task in zebrafish (Danio rerio)." *Animal Cognition* 20(2):159-169, 2016. DOI: [10.1007/s10071-016-1034-x](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10071-016-1034-x)

**Claims evidenced:** INV-010

---

## What the paper did

This study examined the dose-dependent cognitive effects of sleep deprivation in adult zebrafish using an object discrimination paradigm -- one of the most widely used behavioural assays for memory consolidation in vertebrates. The task exploits novelty preference: fish that remember the familiar object explore the novel object more. Four conditions were tested: undisturbed controls, partial sleep deprivation (4 hours), total SD by light pulses, and total SD by extended light. Each group was tested the next day on their ability to discriminate between a previously seen (familiar) and a new (novel) object.

## Key findings relevant to REE

Control animals showed robust novelty preference, confirming intact object memory. Partially sleep-deprived fish retained some discriminatory ability, suggesting a graded dose-response rather than an all-or-nothing threshold. Totally sleep-deprived animals -- by either method -- showed equal exploration of familiar and novel objects, indicating complete failure of novelty discrimination: the memory trace for the familiar object was either not consolidated or not retrievable.

This is a direct demonstration in the proposed REE calibration species that one night of sleep loss is sufficient to disrupt the consolidation of recent experience into retrievable memory.

## Translation to REE

At the zebrafish level, the Pinheiro-da-Silva result closes the loop between Yokogawa's demonstration that sleep is present and regulated, and the functional consequence of its absence. Sleep is not merely present in zebrafish as a conserved ritual -- its loss produces measurable representational failure within a single cycle.

For REE, the object discrimination analog would be the ability to distinguish recently-visited grid locations (familiar) from unvisited ones (novel) when constructing or updating the viability map. The graded dose-response (partial SD = partial retention) is worth noting architecturally: it suggests that consolidation is a continuous process, not a binary gate. This complicates any hard threshold implementation of the offline mode switch and suggests that MECH-030's multi-phase architecture (MECH-120 through MECH-123) may represent graded contributions rather than strictly sequential gates.

## Limitations and caveats

The mechanisms underlying the discrimination failure are not identified -- the study does not include neuroimaging, molecular assays, or pharmacological dissections. Whether the deficit reflects failed consolidation during sleep, impaired encoding during the task, or retrieval failure at test cannot be distinguished behaviourally. Light-based sleep deprivation protocols may induce circadian misalignment and stress responses that confound pure sleep loss effects. The zebrafish object discrimination paradigm is less characterised than rodent novel object recognition, and the neural substrates specifically recruited are inferred from mammalian analogy.
