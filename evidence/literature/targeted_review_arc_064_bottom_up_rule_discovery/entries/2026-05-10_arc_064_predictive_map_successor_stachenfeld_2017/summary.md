# Stachenfeld, Botvinick & Gershman 2017 -- Hippocampus as a predictive map

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/nn.4650), PMID 28967910), Stachenfeld, Botvinick and Gershman propose that the hippocampus is not, after all, a geometric cognitive map of space. It is something subtler: a predictive map. Each place cell, on their account, encodes not the agent's current location per se, but a summary of which locations the agent typically reaches in the immediate future from here. Mathematically, this is the successor representation -- the expected discounted future state-occupancy under the current policy. Their reinterpretation explains a sprawl of previously puzzling place-cell findings: the asymmetric tuning where place fields shift toward upcoming locations on the agent's habitual path, the policy-dependence where place fields rearrange when the agent's typical route changes, and the grid-cell structure as a low-dimensional eigenbasis for this predictive map.

For ARC-064 this is a R2 paper that gestures at one specific implementation flavour of bottom-up rule discovery. If we believe Stachenfeld, then the brain's hippocampal-cognitive-map machinery is already running a kind of summary-statistics computation over the trajectory record -- the SR is, in effect, a compressed bottom-up summary of how the agent's behaviour unfolds. ARC-064's bottom-up rule discovery could be implemented as a successor-representation-style mechanism, with rule-equivalents emerging as clusterable structure in the SR.

The architectural read this suggests is interesting and worth flagging in the synthesis. The existing REE hippocampal substrate (MECH-269 anchor sets, MECH-292 ghost-goal bank, MECH-293 awake ghost-goal probes) is episode-anchored: each anchor is tied to a specific past experience, with high pattern separation. The Stachenfeld view is the orthogonal one -- pooling across episodes to produce a continuous predictive summary. These are not the same computation. If ARC-064 is implemented as an SR-analog substrate, it lives in the same neighbourhood as the existing hippocampal substrates but does a fundamentally different thing. That clean architectural separation is worth taking seriously.

The mapping caveat I want to be honest about: the Stachenfeld paper is primarily a theoretical reinterpretation of existing place-cell data, not a primary forward-tested empirical paper. They show that an SR account can reproduce known place-cell phenomena post-hoc, but the field has not yet conducted the discriminative experiments that would distinguish SR from competing accounts (purely-spatial-map, episodic-replay-driven, Bayesian-latent-state). For ARC-064's substrate design, the SR is one candidate implementation among several Niv 2019 names; this paper does not adjudicate which implementation REE should pick. Cross-reference Momennejad 2017 (in this entry set) for the human-behavioural evidence on SR-style computation; that paper supplies more direct empirical support.

Confidence 0.78. Nat Neurosci venue and influential framework. Mapping fidelity moderate because SR is one specific implementation flavour, not the only candidate. Transfer risk a touch elevated because the implementation-granularity choices (over states? over actions? over trajectory segments?) are not pinned down by the paper.
