# Karlsson & Frank 2009 -- Awake replay of remote experiences in the hippocampus

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/nn.2344), PMID 19525943), Karlsson and Frank made a finding that surprised the sleep-replay community when it landed. Hippocampal replay had been characterised in earlier work (notably Wilson & McNaughton 1994) as a sleep-state phenomenon -- place-cell sequences from waking behaviour are reactivated during slow-wave sleep, presumably consolidating the memory traces. Karlsson and Frank showed that the same kind of replay also happens during AWAKE quiet periods, and -- the key result -- it is not limited to the current environment. Rats running on a maze show frequent awake-quiet replay of trajectories from REMOTE environments, past experiences not currently present in sensory input. The replay is internally generated, not stimulus-driven, and it happens often enough to be a normal cognitive operation rather than a curiosity.

For ARC-064 this paper anchors the online-arm of the R3 verdict. The classical sleep-replay literature (Wilson & McNaughton 1994; Stickgold 2013 in this entry set) supports an offline-consolidation-during-sleep account of bottom-up rule discovery. Karlsson and Frank show that the brain has additional online machinery: trajectory data is re-processed during awake quiet periods, available for online extraction of regularities. The R3 verdict on bottom-up rule discovery's timing should be BOTH-CHANNELS-NEEDED: online during awake quiet + offline during sleep, with the two channels likely doing different things (rapid online extraction; slow offline consolidation).

There is also a useful cross-reference to existing REE substrates. MECH-293 (awake ghost-goal probes) and MECH-292 (ranked ghost-goal bank) are partial analogs of the awake-replay machinery Karlsson and Frank describe. They probe candidate goals during awake quiet periods. ARC-064 could extend that substrate's role from goal-probing to rule-extraction -- the same machinery used for proposing future trajectories could also be used for clustering past trajectories into rules. Whether that is the cleanest implementation, or whether ARC-064 needs a separate write path, is a substrate-design call rather than a literature-decidable question. The Pull 1 synthesis flagged MECH-315 (proposal-diversity-channel candidate, hippocampal-trajectory-sampling-analog) as deferred to this pull. Karlsson and Frank's findings reinforce that MECH-292/293 are doing related-but-distinct work from what ARC-064 needs; MECH-315 may be redundant with MECH-293 only if ARC-064 reuses the same substrate, which is possible but not necessitated.

The mapping caveat: rat-maze recordings are a step removed from REE's open-ended-foraging environment. Awake replay events are brief (~100ms) and require quiet pauses. Whether REE has a clean quiet-period analog is task-dependent. The principle (online trajectory re-processing during awake quiet) is general; the specific implementation has to be REE-specific.
