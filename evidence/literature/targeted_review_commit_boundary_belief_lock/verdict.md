## Stream-by-stream classification

### Stream 1: Cognitive dissonance / post-decisional consolidation — **SUPPORTS**

Two entries:

- **Izuma 2010 PNAS** ([10.1073/pnas.1011879108](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1011879108), conf 0.86) — fMRI study with proper Chen-Risen control. Choice-induced preference change is real on both behavioural and neural readouts. Striatum tracks shifted value representation; ACC + dlPFC track dissonance trial-by-trial. This is the load-bearing methodological anchor that rehabilitates the entire 50-year tradition.
- **Colosio 2017 J Neurosci** ([10.1523/JNEUROSCI.3209-16.2017](https://doi.org/10.1523/JNEUROSCI.3209-16.2017), conf 0.78) — EEG showing an ERN-like frontocentral evoked response at ~60 ms post-commit; amplitude predicts subsequent preference-change magnitude; resting-state long-range temporal correlations in frontocentral cortex predict both.

**Stream 1 verdict:** The Festinger-tradition phenomenon survives the methodological scrutiny of the past fifteen years. The neural correlates are reproducible across labs, paradigms, and modalities (fMRI + EEG). The architectural prediction the brief makes — that the typed commit boundary engages performance-monitoring circuitry whose engagement intensity predicts downstream representational shift — is supported. **The empirical foundation for the proposal exists.**

### Stream 2: Insight in psychosis — **MIXED (supports broad shape, does not directly test)**

One entry:

- **Saravanan 2010 BJP** ([10.1192/bjp.bp.109.068577](https://doi.org/10.1192/bjp.bp.109.068577), conf 0.68) — prospective cohort of n=131 first-episode schizophrenia patients in Vellore, India. DUP and changes in insight during the first 6 months strongly and independently predict 1-year outcome, controlling for baseline. Anthony David is senior author.

**Stream 2 verdict:** The DUP-insight relationship is robust and replicable across contexts. It is consistent with the proposed mechanism (more time during which the delusion could drive committed actions = harder later revision) but cannot isolate the mechanism from severity-only or prior-strength accounts. The clinical literature does not, to the best of my reading, separate enacted from non-enacted delusional content systematically. **A direct test of the architectural prediction (matched-severity + matched-DUP cohort differing in extent of delusional enactment) does not yet exist.** This is a gap, but it is a gap in clinical research design, not a refutation of the proposal.

The right next step is **either** a formal clinical-design lit-pull looking for cohort studies that have done the enactment-ideation separation, **or** acknowledgement at registration that Stream 2 evidence is indirect and the mechanism awaits a clinical study designed for it.

### Stream 3: Forensic / internalized false confession — **SUPPORTS**

One entry:

- **Gudjonsson 2016 Cortex** ([10.1016/j.cortex.2016.06.013](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cortex.2016.06.013), conf 0.80) — canonical review of memory distrust syndrome by the originator of the construct, with a detailed diary case of pressured-internalized false confession.

**Stream 3 verdict:** The pressured-internalized type of false confession is the closest naturalistic instance of the proposed mechanism. The forensic confession is a typed commit boundary in the legal-architectural sense; under conditions of isolation, sustained interrogation, and individual vulnerability, crossing this boundary leads to durable belief alignment with what the commit asserted. The "delayed rather than immediate" character of the suggestibility — the belief continues to rearrange itself over hours and days after the commit — is exactly the architectural signature the brief proposes.

The phenomenon is rare in absolute terms, which constrains the strong-form prediction. The proposed mechanism cannot claim that any commit produces lock; it must specify the conditions under which commit-boundary crossing converts to durable belief alignment. Gudjonsson lists those conditions for the forensic case but does not generalise. The two-mechanism framing the brief proposed (commit-boundary belief lock + attribution-rigidity setpoint) is consistent with this — the lock requires both the commit and a high enough rigidity setpoint, and the latter is what is rare.

**A subtler issue:** Gudjonsson categorises pressured-internalized confession under "confabulation" alongside MDS, which puts the phenomenon adjacent to MECH-094 territory. The boundary between the proposed mechanism and the existing MECH-094 (simulation/real distinction failure) needs careful articulation if the claim is registered. The two-mechanism dose-response framing handles this elegantly: same parameter (attribution rigidity), opposite settings, opposite pathologies (confabulation = too low; delusion-with-action-history = too high; pressured-internalized confession sits somewhere in between because the commit is real but external coercion is the proximate cause).

### Stream 4: Predictive-coding delusion accounts (orthogonality cross-check) — **SUPPORTS ORTHOGONALITY**

One entry:

- **Sterzer 2018 Biol Psychiatry** ([10.1016/j.biopsych.2018.05.015](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.biopsych.2018.05.015), conf 0.84) — canonical ten-author review (Adams, Fletcher, Frith, Corlett et al.) of where predictive-coding accounts of psychosis stand. Explicitly states that "canonical theories do not highlight active inference — the process through which the effects of our actions on our sensations are anticipated and minimized." Explicitly flags the emergence-vs-persistence asymmetry of delusions as unresolved. Cites ketamine-induced memory reconsolidation as candidate persistence mechanism.

**Stream 4 verdict:** Orthogonality holds. The canonical predictive-coding account does not predict the action-history asymmetry that the proposed mechanism targets. The reviewers themselves flag the gap and propose three alternative directions for filling it (hierarchical predictive coding, social priors, beliefs-as-utility), none of which specifically predict the action-history asymmetry either. **The proposed mechanism is not redundant with existing predictive-coding accounts of delusion.** It is a candidate fifth direction, alongside the three the reviewers list, for filling the persistence + active-inference gap.

The asymmetric note: the proposed mechanism is genuinely orthogonal, but it is not the only candidate for the gap. Registration should include explicit discussion of how the proposed mechanism does work that the alternatives do not — specifically, the action-history dependency that is the mechanism's diagnostic signature.

### Stream 5: Neural substrate of revising acted-upon vs non-acted-upon beliefs — **SUPPORTS (strongly)**

Two entries:

- **Voigt 2018 J Neurosci** ([10.1523/JNEUROSCI.1681-18.2018](https://doi.org/10.1523/JNEUROSCI.1681-18.2018), conf 0.88) — fMRI + eye-tracking + memory probe. Choice-induced preference change becomes behaviourally relevant **only for choices that were remembered**, and remembering is associated with hippocampus activity. dlPFC + precuneus predict preference change online during decision-making.
- **Tandetnik 2021 Cortex** ([10.1016/j.cortex.2021.02.018](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cortex.2021.02.018), conf 0.82) — frontal-lobe lesion study. Dysexecutive lesion patients **fail to show CIPC even with intact memory of choices**. Non-dysexecutive lesion patients perform like controls. Memory and executive substrates dissociate cleanly.

**Stream 5 verdict:** The architectural prediction of the proposed mechanism — that revision cost depends on the durable record of the commit — is empirically supported and refined. Voigt's data show that the commit-record substrate (hippocampus) is necessary; Tandetnik's data show that an additional executive substrate (frontal lobe / ACC) is also necessary. **The mechanism is at least two-substrate, not one-substrate.** This is unexpected good news — the brief proposed a single attribution-rigidity setpoint, but the empirical data suggest at least two parameters (commit-record strength + executive-conflict-resolution gain).

Pathological lock requires both substrates engaged at high gain. Pathological permeability (confabulation territory, MECH-094) requires commit-record weakness regardless of executive engagement. This decomposition gives the registration cleaner diagnostic predictions than the brief's single-parameter framing.

## Cross-stream synthesis

The five streams converge on a coherent picture:

1. **The empirical foundation exists** (Stream 1) — choice-induced preference change is real, neurally measurable, and reproducible.
2. **The clinical signature is consistent** (Stream 2) — DUP predicts insight trajectory, though the cleaner enactment-ideation dissection has not been done.
3. **The naturalistic strong-form instance exists** (Stream 3) — pressured-internalized false confession is the proposed mechanism observed in the forensic wild.
4. **The orthogonality holds** (Stream 4) — canonical predictive-coding accounts of delusion do not predict the action-history asymmetry, and explicitly flag the active-inference / action-loop gap as an unresolved challenge.
5. **The architectural substrate is identifiable** (Stream 5) — hippocampal commit-record + frontal-executive conflict-resolution, dissociable in lesion data.

The mechanism is therefore **plausible, orthogonal to existing claims, and substrate-grounded**. The brief's single-parameter framing should be **refined to a two-parameter framing** based on Stream 5: commit-record strength × executive-conflict-resolution gain. Pathological lock occurs when both are high; confabulation occurs when commit-record strength is low; healthy belief revision occurs when both are moderate.

## Headline recommendation: **REGISTER WITH MODIFICATION**

**Provisional registration target:**

- **MECH-X commit_boundary_belief_lock** (candidate, v3_pending=true, implementation_phase=v3): durable belief representations that motivated typed commit-boundary crossings carry attribution-load that resists revision; pathological setting produces delusion-resistant-to-argument signature. depends_on: INV-021, MECH-061, MECH-094 (orthogonality reference), ARC-003, SD-026, MECH-Y.
- **MECH-Y attribution_rigidity_setpoint** (candidate, v3_pending=true, implementation_phase=v3): two-parameter substrate setting how strongly downstream belief revision must respect upstream commit history; commit-record strength (hippocampal, INV-021 substrate) and executive-conflict-resolution gain (frontal/ACC, MECH-061 substrate) combine multiplicatively to set rigidity. Too low (commit-record weak) → confabulation territory (overlaps MECH-094). Too high (both substrates strongly engaged) → delusion-resistant-to-argument territory. depends_on: INV-021, MECH-061, MECH-094, MECH-X.

**Modifications from the brief:**

1. The single attribution-rigidity setpoint becomes a two-parameter substrate (Stream 5 finding).
2. MECH-Y is registered explicitly with both pathological tails named — confabulation (MECH-094 territory) at the low end, delusion-with-action-history at the high end. This makes the relationship between the proposed mechanism and existing MECH-094 a registered architectural fact, not an interpretive add-on.
3. Architectural predictions to register on MECH-X:
   - Patients matched on baseline severity but differing in delusional enactment should differ in insight trajectory (testable with cohort design).
   - Frontal-executive impairment should reduce delusion rigidity (paradoxically — predicts that frontal lesions in delusional patients should improve insight).
   - Hippocampal impairment should also reduce delusion rigidity but predicts confabulation drift (different clinical signature from frontal-lesion case).
   - Pharmacological hypothesis: agents that disrupt memory reconsolidation post-commit (e.g. propranolol post-traumatic-event protocols) should be testable on delusion rigidity.

**Cautions:**

- Stream 2 evidence is indirect. The registration should carry an explicit `evidence_quality_note` flagging that no clinical study has yet done the enactment-ideation dissection the mechanism predicts. A follow-up clinical-design lit-pull (or research proposal) is needed.
- Stream 4 confirms orthogonality but does not make the proposed mechanism the only candidate filler for the predictive-coding gap. Registration should include explicit discussion of the discriminative work the proposed mechanism does that hierarchical predictive coding, social priors, and beliefs-as-utility do not (specifically: the action-history dependence).
- Tag inflation risk: the 7 entries in this pull are tagged with placeholder `MECH-CBBL-PROPOSED` to avoid inflating the literature confidence of any registered claim. On registration of MECH-X / MECH-Y, the entries should be re-tagged via the same cross-tagging procedure used for MECH-302 / MECH-303 (relief-completion pull, 2026-05-03). The placeholder tag should be retired at that time.

## Suggested experimental successors (not in scope for this pull, flagged for the governance plan)

If the claim is registered, the following experiments would be the natural next step:

1. **Two-substrate dissociation** — V3 experiment varying (a) memory-of-commit retention via inter-trial interval and (b) executive-conflict-resolution gain via cognitive load, and measuring downstream belief rigidity. Predicts: rigidity is multiplicative, not additive.
2. **Confabulation/delusion axis traversal** — V3 experiment driving the architecture across the proposed two-parameter space and identifying the parameter regions corresponding to confabulation, healthy revision, and lock. Anchors the dose-response framing.
3. **Action-history asymmetry probe** — V3 experiment comparing belief revision under counter-evidence for matched-strength beliefs that did vs did not motivate prior committed actions. Predicts: revision cost increases with commit count.

These would belong in a follow-up `commit_boundary_belief_lock_governance_plan.md` mirroring the SD-033 structure.
