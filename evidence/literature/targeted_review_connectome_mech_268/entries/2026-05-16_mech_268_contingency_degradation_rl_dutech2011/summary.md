# Dutech, Coutureau & Marchand (2011) — A reinforcement learning approach to instrumental contingency degradation in rats

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jphysparis.2011.07.017)), Dutech and colleagues took the behavioural phenomenon of instrumental contingency degradation — and the well-known fact that medial-prefrontal-lesioned rats fail to adapt to it — and asked whether a plain temporal-difference learner could reproduce both the normal and the lesioned trajectory with shared parameters. It could. A SARSA model over event-sequence states, with a low learning rate and a softmax policy, captured the time course of learning and the *differential* adaptation of the two groups. The diagnostic detail, the one worth carrying into the env spec, is where the lesioned animals actually broke: not in gross flexibility (they still stopped pressing when the action actively prevented reward) but specifically in an *inability to initially detect weak contingency changes*.

That weak-versus-strong asymmetry is, almost line for line, the dissociation EXP-0164 is built to measure. MECH-268 commits the dACC prediction-error signal to cap or habituate so that the SD-034 closure operator does not release on weak, local, contradicted outcomes, yet still responds to genuine sustained change. Dutech 2011 is evidence that this asymmetry is real and that it is the fragile computation — the first thing a prefrontal lesion takes away. It also delivers an uncomfortable but useful warning. Their model produces the weak-change blindness with a *low learning rate*, not with a saturating prediction error. Behaviourally these look identical over short windows. They come apart only under sustained contradiction: a low-gain integrator eventually catches up; a hard-capped pe never does.

The consequence for the env primitive is concrete. If we build counter-evidence injection as a single perturbation, or run it for too short a horizon, EXP-0164 cannot distinguish "PE correctly saturated, closure correctly withheld" from "low-gain integrator simply hasn't gotten there yet." So the env must deliver counter-evidence as *graded contingency degradation* and must expose contradiction dose and duration as first-class, sweepable parameters. The measured quantity is the location of the weak/strong boundary, not the response to one contradicting event.

Confidence is 0.68. The paper is a solid specialist computational-behavioural account, and it pins the asymmetry MECH-268 leans on; it does not directly evidence a dACC saturation cap (its mechanism is low-gain TD) and the locus is rat medial PFC. I am treating it, honestly, as support for the *phenomenon* and as a design constraint on the experiment, not as confirmation of the implementation.
