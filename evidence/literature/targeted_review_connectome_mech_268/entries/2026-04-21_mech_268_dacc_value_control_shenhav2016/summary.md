# Shenhav, Cohen, Botvinick 2016 — dACC and the value of control (MECH-268)

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/nn.4384)), Shenhav, Cohen, and Botvinick's Nature Neuroscience review updates the EVC framework with additional evidence on dACC function and contrasts it with foraging-theory accounts. The paper reinforces the claim that dACC specifies optimal control allocation by integrating expected payoff, control investment, and effort cost into a unified value signal. It also discusses dACC's involvement in decisions about when to alter behaviour — a direct link to the MECH-268 governance function where pe-signal dynamics drive mode transitions.

For MECH-268 this is a supporting but indirect entry. The review reinforces that dACC signals adjust over time as task demands shift. In REE terms, "outcome_history becomes repetitive" is precisely the kind of task-demand change that should trigger an adjustment — specifically, a downward shift in the control-allocation signal because the expected value of further control is low under repetitive outcomes. This direction-of-adjustment claim is well-supported by the review. What is less directly supported is MECH-268's specific implementation: bounded pe with outcome-identity-dependent habituation. The review discusses adjustment without specifying its mechanism, and multiple implementations (internal saturation, external modulation, downstream allocation-drop without signal-drop) are all EVC-compatible.

The review's contrast with foraging accounts (Kolling, Rushworth) is worth flagging for MECH-268. Foraging theories of dACC frame the signal as value-of-switching rather than value-of-control. For MECH-268's governance function — prompting mode transitions when the current mode is stuck — the foraging framing is arguably more direct: "value of switching away from this stuck mode" is exactly what should rise as pe becomes saturated on the current mode. The EVC framework can accommodate this through the payoff-of-alternative-control channel, but foraging theories place it more explicitly. MECH-268 is agnostic between EVC and foraging framings; both support the direction of the saturation claim.

The governance coupling of MECH-268 with SD-034 is consistent with both EVC and foraging. MECH-268 provides implicit closure (pe saturates when outcomes are repetitive, mode register relaxes spontaneously); SD-034 provides explicit closure (a detected completion releases the commitment latch and resets saturation). Together they cover two failure modes: stuck-on-repetitive-stuff (MECH-268 rescue) and failed-to-notice-completion (SD-034 rescue). Both rescues correspond to adaptive dACC signal adjustment in the EVC sense.

The failure signature worth recording is that if biological dACC implements adjustment without internal signal saturation — for example, by receiving an external expected-payoff modulation that scales down a fundamentally unbounded raw pe — then MECH-268's specific commitment (bounded pe with habituation) is not the right mechanism even though the direction of the effect is correct. The V3-EXQ-463 experiment is the arbiter; this lit-pull establishes the EVC-compatible direction without resolving the mechanism.
