# Piquet, Faugère & Parkes (2023) — A hippocampo-cortical pathway detects changes in the validity of an action as a predictor of reward

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cub.2023.11.036)), Piquet, Faugère and Parkes asked a deceptively narrow question with broad consequences for how we should build a "counter-evidence" primitive: when an instrumental contingency degrades — when pressing the lever no longer predicts the pellet — what does the brain actually have to compute to notice? Their answer, assembled from excitotoxic lesions, DREADD chemogenetic inhibition, and a circuit-specific silencing of ventral-hippocampus terminals in medial prefrontal cortex, is that the animal must learn the *relative predictive validity of the action versus the context*. Disrupt the ventral hippocampus (but not the dorsal), or just its mPFC projection, and the animal keeps pressing as if nothing had changed. Crucially, the deficit tracked context-outcome learning: degradation detection was parasitic on an intact representation of what the context, independent of the action, predicts.

The reason this matters for MECH-268 is not that it measures dACC prediction-error saturation — it does not — but that it tells us what "counter-evidence against a committed action" *is*, biologically. It is contingency degradation: the committed action progressively loses its standing as a predictor of the committed outcome, against an unchanged background. It is not a single signed jolt of negative reward at the target, and it is not a contingency reversal (the target now reliably yielding the opposite). Those three manipulations are dissociable in this literature and recruit partly different machinery. EXP-0164 is built to dissociate the SD-034 closure operator from MECH-268 dACC pe-saturation under *sustained* contradiction; if the env injects the wrong kind of contradiction, the experiment will resolve an artefact rather than the dissociation we care about.

So the translation I am willing to defend is a constraint on the env, stated with more confidence than the dACC-saturation mapping itself deserves: the counter-evidence injection hook should degrade the action→outcome contingency for the persistent rule_state while leaving the context intact, rather than perturb or reverse it. The honest caveat is that this paper localises the *detector* of degradation to a vHPC→mPFC validity computation. If REE's dACC pe-saturation sits downstream of, or parallel to, that validity computation, then the paper supports the env-design choice strongly while supporting the specific dACC-cap commitment only by adjacency. There is also the usual rodent-operant-chamber-to-grid-world transfer risk, which here is structural rather than parametric: we are borrowing the *form* of the manipulation, not its magnitudes.

I have set confidence at 0.70 — high enough to act on for the env-design decision (it is the clearest biological steer we have on Q-2a), low enough to flag that it informs the operationalisation more than it tests the saturation cap. The failure signatures record the two ways this could mislead us: building the hook as a bare perturbation, or conflating degradation with reversal.
