# Holroyd and Yeung 2012 — ACC and motivation of extended behaviours (MECH-268)

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2011.12.008)), Holroyd and Yeung's TiCS review proposes that the anterior cingulate cortex supports the selection and maintenance of "options" — extended, context-specific sequences of behaviour directed toward particular goals — learned through hierarchical reinforcement learning. The paper reconciles two previously competing views of ACC: one emphasising cognitive control and monitoring, another emphasising motivation and energising. The options framing is that ACC simultaneously maintains the motivational context for an extended behavioural option while also monitoring and learning about its execution.

For MECH-268 this is a complementary rather than primary entry. MECH-268's central commitment is that the dACC-analog pe signal saturates under repeated identical outcomes. Holroyd and Yeung's options framework does not directly predict saturation, but it does provide an adjacent architectural claim that speaks to MECH-268's governance function: if ACC maintains the motivational context for an option, and the option is producing no value (repeated identical outcomes), the motivational context for that option should decline. Saturation of the pe signal is one plausible implementation of that decline, although the options framework is silent on the specific mechanism.

The interesting contrast is with the EVC framework (Shenhav 2013, 2016; see separate entries). EVC frames dACC activity as value-of-control signalling; options frames it as maintenance of goal-directed motivational context. These are not identical theories, and part of the field-level debate Holroyd and Yeung engage is whether EVC's control-allocation framing subsumes the motivational / energising phenomena or leaves them to a separate mechanism. For MECH-268 specifically, both frameworks support the direction of the saturation claim (signal declines when value-of-continuation drops) without directly prescribing the bounded-pe-with-habituation mechanism.

The options framework has one specific advantage for REE's governance architecture: it natively contemplates the completion / withdrawal of an extended behavioural option, which maps onto SD-034's closure operator. Closure in SD-034 is the explicit release of the motivational context for a completed option; MECH-268 is the implicit decline of that context under repetitive non-productive outcomes. Both are option-framework-consistent operations on the ACC context signal. The options framework does not discriminate between explicit and implicit withdrawal, but it frames them as two aspects of a single option-maintenance function, which is close to how MECH-268 and SD-034 pair in REE.

The failure signature I record is the most honest caveat: the options framework is silent on the specific pe dynamics MECH-268 commits to. If biological ACC maintains option context with a non-saturating drive signal that is withdrawn cleanly on option completion (SD-034-style only), MECH-268's implicit-closure saturation is redundant. Conversely, if pe saturation is the primary implicit-closure mechanism and explicit closure is a special case, MECH-268 is the more fundamental claim. The V3-EXQ-463 and V3-EXQ-468 experiments will test this; the options framework provides one more architectural lens through which to interpret the results.
