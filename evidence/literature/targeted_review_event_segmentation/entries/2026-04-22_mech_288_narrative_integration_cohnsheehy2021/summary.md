# Cohn-Sheehy, Delarazan, Reagh, Crivelli-Decker, Kim, Barnett, Zacks & Ranganath 2021 -- The hippocampus constructs narrative memories across distant events

According to PubMed, this fMRI study showed that hippocampal patterns at event boundaries integrate distant events into coherent narratives. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cub.2021.09.013)

## What the paper does

fMRI during encoding of fictional stories where some events were narratively coherent (formed one larger story) and others were unrelated. Subjects then completed a one-day-delayed recall test. The authors asked whether hippocampal patterns at event boundaries reflected narrative coherence -- and whether boundary-pattern reinstatement predicted subsequent recall.

## Findings relevant to MECH-288

Hippocampal activity patterns at event boundaries were MORE similar between distant events forming a coherent narrative than between unrelated events. Hippocampal pattern reinstatement at boundaries predicted detailed recall of narrative-coherent events one day later. This establishes that boundary-driven hippocampal activity is not just a separator but an INTEGRATOR -- it links the new segment to prior narrative context.

## Mapping to REE

The substrate-level recommendation: event boundaries are not just separators, they are integration points. MECH-288 should expose boundary events to MECH-269 anchor-set so the new segment can either start fresh OR be linked to existing anchor memory based on narrative coherence. This is the "anchored vs probe" distinction in MECH-269 / MECH-272: at a boundary, the system decides whether the new segment is a continuation of an existing narrative anchor (link) or a new exploration (fresh anchor). MECH-288 is the substrate that emits the decision moment; MECH-269 / MECH-272 do the routing.

## Limitations and caveats

Cohn-Sheehy stands in for the spec's "Reagh & Ranganath 2018" citation -- Reagh is co-author and the paper is on event-boundary hippocampal mechanisms, more directly testing the integration role than Reagh 2018. The narrative-coherence framing is more specific than the original Reagh 2018 work but more directly evidences the integrative role of boundary HC activity, which is the substrate-relevant claim. The mapping caveat is that "narrative" in REE means something simpler than human story comprehension.
