# Mansouri, Buckley, Mahboubi & Tanaka 2015 — Causal lesion phenotype of frontal pole cortex

According to PubMed, Mansouri, Buckley, Mahboubi & Tanaka (2015, [PNAS 112:E3940-9](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1422629112)) report behavioural consequences of bilateral selective lesion to frontal pole cortex (FPC) and posterior cingulate cortex (PCC) in macaques tested on a Wisconsin Card Sorting Test analog and related cognitive tasks. This is one of the very few primate lesion studies specifically targeting FPC, and it provides causal rather than correlational evidence on FPC function.

The findings produce a paradoxical phenotype that is the most informative part of the paper for SD-033e. Compared to controls, FPC-lesioned animals (a) showed no impairment at following frequent rule switches *within* the WCST task, (b) showed *augmented* post-conflict cognitive control adjustment, and (c) showed *better* memory for the relevant rule across experimentally-imposed distractions (an intervening secondary task or surprising free reward delivery). PCC-lesioned animals did not show this pattern. The authors propose that FPC's normal function is therefore not to enhance current-task focus — quite the opposite — but to **disengage executive control from the current task and redistribute it to novel sources of reward** (new opportunities, counterfactual options, prospective planning). Removing FPC removes the disengagement pressure, which paradoxically *improves* current-task focus and distractor-resistance.

For SD-033e Prong D, this is direct causal support for the architectural commitment underlying MECH-264 (counterfactual-value tracking) and MECH-265 (competing-goal monitoring). The FPC analog should function as a disengage-and-redistribute module, not as a current-task focus enhancer. The implementation implication is concrete: the FPC analog's `score_bias` projection should *reduce* the current-candidate weight in proportion to counterfactual evidence, not increase it. If the V3 implementation makes the FPC analog augment current-task focus (e.g., by adding a positive bias on the currently-committed trajectory), it would be implementing the opposite of what the lesion phenotype suggests the biology actually does.

This paper also informs the resolution of one of the SD-033e governance plan's open questions: is FPC counterfactual-value tracking pairwise (MECH-264) or K≥2-way (MECH-265)? The lesion phenotype is consistent with both — FPC removes the disengagement pressure regardless of whether the alternative was a single foregone option (Boorman 2009 paradigm) or a set of alternatives (the WCST has 4 simultaneously-available card stacks). The lesion result therefore does not discriminate between MECH-264 and MECH-265 but is consistent with both.

I want to flag the interpretation caveat. The lesion phenotype is causal and dissociable — that part is empirically clean. But the interpretation as "disengagement and exploration" is post-hoc theoretical synthesis rather than direct empirical measurement of counterfactual value or exploration drive. Alternative interpretations are not strictly excluded: FPC could be a generic conflict / novelty detector with downstream explore/exploit-orienting effects; FPC could be modulating a specific kind of attention rather than executive control per se; FPC could be primarily implicated in the rule-conditional vmPFC↔FPC connectivity network demonstrated in Boorman 2009 rather than acting as a stand-alone disengagement module. The disengagement interpretation is the most coherent and most explanatory, but it is theoretical synthesis, not measured.

A second caveat: the species transfer is from macaque to REE's abstract action space. The mapping is by structural analogy — the disengage-and-redistribute function is what the FPC analog should implement, regardless of whether the substrate is biological FPC or REE's lateral-PFC-analog-extension SD-033e specifies. This is acceptable transfer for an architectural commitment but should be flagged.

Confidence components: source quality very high at 0.92 (PNAS 2015, Buckley + Tanaka groups, primate selective lesion is the highest-evidence-grade design for causal-localisation claims). Mapping fidelity high at 0.85 — causal lesion phenotype directly supports the architectural commitment that FPC operates as a disengage-and-redistribute module. Transfer risk low at 0.25; species-transfer to REE abstract action space is the main flag. Net direction is strongly **supports** SD-033e Prong D, with the implementation-level recommendation that the FPC analog's score_bias should reduce current-candidate weight in proportion to counterfactual evidence rather than augment it.
