# Burgess, Dumontheil & Gilbert 2007 — The gateway hypothesis of rostral prefrontal cortex (BA 10)

According to PubMed, Burgess, Dumontheil & Gilbert (2007, [Trends Cogn Sci 11:290-8](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2007.05.004)) propose the **gateway hypothesis** of rostral prefrontal cortex (Brodmann area 10) function: BA 10 implements mechanisms that enable attention to switch between **environmental stimuli** (external engagement) and **self-generated or maintained representations** (internal cognition — "the thoughts in our head"). The architectural decomposition has a medial-lateral structure: medial BA 10 supports external engagement; lateral BA 10 supports internal cognition.

For SD-033e Prong D, Burgess 2007 functions as the architectural anchor establishing that rostral PFC has a *gateway* role — it does not just hold representations but implements the switching machinery between externally-driven and internally-driven cognitive modes. The relevance to SD-033e is that the FPC analog the design doc commits to should support precisely this kind of switching: between processing the current task (external engagement, attended to environmental input) and processing counterfactual / prospective alternatives (internal cognition, self-generated representations of foregone options). Translated to MECH-261 operating modes, the medial-lateral dissociation suggests that some modes (current-task-execution) recruit the medial BA 10 mechanism while others (planning, counterfactual evaluation, default-mode-like internally-directed cognition) recruit the lateral BA 10 mechanism.

I want to flag two caveats. The first is that the gateway hypothesis specifies a **continuous-modulatory** account — attention switches between external and internal as a graded shift rather than as discrete mode-state transitions. SD-033e's V3 implementation question is whether the FPC analog enters and exits operating modes via discrete transitions (Schmitt-trigger-style) or via graded shifts. Burgess 2007's mechanism is more compatible with the graded version. This is one ARC of indirection from what SD-033e currently commits to (which inherits the discrete-mode framing from MECH-261).

The second caveat is that the medial vs lateral BA 10 dissociation has been refined in subsequent work — notably the Mansouri 2017 review (also indexed in this Prong D pull) which emphasises a more graded functional specialisation rather than a clean medial/lateral split. SD-033e's V3 implementation should not lock to the strict medial/lateral split as if it were a decided architectural fact; the safer commitment is that the FPC analog supports both external-engagement and internal-cognition processing without strict spatial localisation.
