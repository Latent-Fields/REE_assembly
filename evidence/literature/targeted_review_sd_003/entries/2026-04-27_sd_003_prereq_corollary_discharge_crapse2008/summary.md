# Crapse and Sommer 2008 -- Corollary discharge circuits in the primate brain

According to PubMed: Crapse, T.B., & Sommer, M.A. (2008). Corollary discharge circuits in the primate brain. *Current Opinion in Neurobiology*, 18(6), 552-557. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.conb.2008.09.017)

## What the paper did

Crapse and Sommer review the primate corollary-discharge (CD) literature across four sensorimotor systems -- saccadic eye movements, vocalisation, reaching, and locomotion -- and ask a unified question: how does the brain compensate for the sensory consequences of self-movement? Their answer, drawn together from single-unit recordings, microstimulation, lesion and inactivation studies, and circuit tracing, is that copies of motor commands (CDs, often used interchangeably with the older term "efference copy") are routed from motor structures to sensory and parietal regions, where they support prediction of the expected reafferent signal. The clearest case is the saccadic system: the superior colliculus sends a CD via mediodorsal thalamus to the frontal eye field, where it is used to update the spatial map across saccades. Inactivation of MD thalamus abolishes this updating and the monkey behaves as if the saccade had never occurred.

## What it tells us about SD-003-prereq

SD-003-prereq is the architectural-prerequisite claim: before the REE comparator pipeline (MECH-256, SD-029) can be tested cleanly, the substrate has to exist -- an efference-copy distribution of `a_actual` to a forward model whose output is then differenced against the realised reafferent stream. Crapse and Sommer give us the biological grounding for that substrate. Across multiple primate systems, exactly this architectural motif recurs: the brain copies the action command, routes it to a predictive structure, and uses the prediction to interpret incoming sensory data as either self-caused (matching) or world-caused (deviating). That is, in plain terms, what SD-003-prereq has to instantiate computationally.

The mapping is cleanest at the substrate level. `a_actual` in MECH-256 corresponds to the CD signal; `E2_x` corresponds to the predictive structure receiving it; the residual `z_x_observed - E2_x(z_x_{t-1}, a_actual)` corresponds to the unexplained-by-self signal that drives external attribution. The paper does not formalise the residual the way MECH-256 does, but it documents the circuit architecture that any such residual computation requires. It also undercuts a possible monolithic-E2 framing: CD signals are stream-specific, with different latencies and circuit motifs in vision versus audition versus proprioception. That fits MECH-256's stream-agnostic single-pass framing better than SD-003's older two-pass counterfactual proposal.

## Limitations and caveats

Three caveats matter for governance use. First, the review supports the *single-pass* comparator -- predicted-minus-observed -- not the two-pass counterfactual `E2(z_t, a_actual) - E2(z_t, a_cf)` that the original SD-003 proposed. This is consistent with the supersession decision (SD-003 -> MECH-256/SD-029) and with the existing Frith 2000 and Shergill 2003 entries in this directory, but it should not be cited as evidence for the two-pass version. Second, the paper works at the level of raw sensory afferents and motor commands, not the latent-stream abstraction REE uses; the leap to `z_x` and especially to a harm-stream `z_harm_s` is by analogy and depends on whether interoceptive streams have CD pathways of comparable structure. The interoceptive case is mentioned only in passing. Third, the paper is mostly macaque, so the human-clinical extension (which Frith 2000 and Shergill 2003 cover) is needed to complete the chain.

## Confidence reasoning

I have set confidence at 0.78. Source quality is high (0.88) -- this is a canonical, NIH-funded, peer-reviewed review from the lab that did the formative saccadic CD work. Mapping fidelity is high-moderate (0.78): the substrate-level claim maps cleanly onto MECH-256's `a_actual`-to-`E2_x` route, but the paper does not formalise the residual-as-agency-signal step. Transfer risk is moderate (0.30) -- primate motor CD circuits are well-conserved, but the extension to an interoceptive harm-stream comparator (the SD-029 instantiation) is not directly evidenced here. On balance, this is the best single citation for the *substrate* component of SD-003-prereq: it tells us that the architectural pattern MECH-256 names is biologically real, primate-general, and circuit-resolved.
