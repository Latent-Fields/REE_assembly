# Synofzik, Vosgerau and Newen 2008 -- Beyond the comparator model

According to PubMed: Synofzik, M., Vosgerau, G., & Newen, A. (2008). Beyond the comparator model: a multifactorial two-step account of agency. *Consciousness and Cognition*, 17(1), 219-239. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.concog.2007.03.010)

## What the paper did

Synofzik, Vosgerau, and Newen argue that the comparator model -- the Frith/Wolpert account in which a forward-model mismatch suffices for sense of agency -- is empirically inadequate, and they propose a two-step replacement. In their account, agency has two distinct levels: a non-conceptual *feeling* of agency that integrates many cues (forward-model match, but also intentional-binding, sensory feedback, contextual priors, environmental cues) via a multifactorial weighting process; and a separate *judgement* of agency, which sits on top of the feeling and is shaped by conceptual modules (beliefs about one's intentions, attribution heuristics, social context). They argue this two-step structure is needed to explain (a) cases where the comparator should fire but agency is preserved (e.g. compensated motor noise), (b) cases where the comparator should be silent but agency is disrupted (e.g. some schizophrenia passivity phenomena), and (c) the sense of agency for thoughts, where no motor efference copy exists at all.

## What it tells us about SD-003-prereq

This is the most important *cautionary* citation for SD-003-prereq: it tells us that even with a correctly implemented forward-model comparator substrate, a residual signal alone may not be enough to produce reliable self-attribution. For the SD-003-prereq architectural work this is a both-and verdict. On the supports side, the paper does not deny that the forward-model comparator is necessary -- it is one of the cues feeding the multifactorial weighting in step one. So the substrate work (efference copy + E2_x + residual) is still required. On the weakens side, the paper directly contests the assumption that a single comparator suffices: cases of intact agency under high residual, and disrupted agency under low residual, mean that downstream weighting and conceptual modules cannot be wired out of the architecture.

For SD-029 specifically, this means the z_harm_s comparator residual should probably be treated as one input into a broader self-attribution computation rather than the self-attribution signal. The implication for the REE prereq plan is that a clean comparator residual is necessary but not sufficient -- additional REE machinery (intentional-binding-style temporal contingency, contextual prior, perhaps E3-level evaluation) will need to combine with the residual to yield a robust agency signal. Mechanistically, this paper supports the existing strategy of treating MECH-256 as a *building block* of self-attribution rather than as the whole story; pragmatically, it warns that experiments testing only residual-vs-no-residual will under-determine the agency claim.

## Limitations and caveats

Three caveats. First, this is a theoretical/conceptual paper; its empirical evidence is drawn from existing studies it reinterprets, not new data. The strongest claim is conceptual coherence with a wide pattern of clinical and behavioural findings, not a direct experimental contrast. Second, the two-step structure is itself underspecified at the computational level -- the weighting process in step one is described qualitatively, with no commitment about how cues are combined. So while the paper tells us *that* a comparator alone is insufficient, it does not give us a formal target architecture for what to add. Third, the paper's strongest negative case is from schizophrenia, where the forward-model account has known limitations (predictions can be locally accurate even when global agency is disrupted), but other clinical populations (alien hand, anarchic hand) are arguably better explained by comparator-only accounts.
