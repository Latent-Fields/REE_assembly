# Summary: On the computational architecture of the neocortex. II. Cortico-cortical loops — Mumford (1992)

**Claim tested:** ARC-022 (Hierarchical effect-object abstraction pipeline)
**Evidence direction:** supports
**Confidence:** 0.78

The question that matters for ARC-022 is not whether typed predictions flow downward in the face-processing hierarchy — Schwiedrzik & Freiwald (2017) confirmed that they do — but whether predictive coding theorists are justified in generalising that result to any cortical hierarchy. The "license" they invoke has to come from somewhere. Mumford (1992) is where it comes from.

The argument Mumford makes is architectural and mathematical, not empirical. He proposes that top-down cortico-cortical feedback connections carry the higher area's prediction of lower-area activity, while bottom-up feedforward connections carry the residual — the part of lower-area activity not accounted for by the higher-level prediction. This is stated as a functional proposition about what the connections are *for*, derived from information-theoretic considerations: if a higher-level model has formed a representation of the world, the efficient thing to send downward is the prediction, and the efficient thing to send upward is what the prediction got wrong. The result is that feedback carries interpretation; feedforward carries surprise.

The key consequence for typed-prediction descent follows immediately from the architecture: because predictions are generated by the higher-level model, they are necessarily formatted in the higher level's representational space. There is no separate inference needed. The higher area has more abstract, invariant representations than the lower area — this was already known from Hubel and Wiesel's hierarchy and the decades of visual physiology that followed. If predictions are generated by that more-abstract model, those predictions carry the abstraction properties of the higher level. Typed prediction descent is a theorem of this architecture, not an empirical observation waiting to be confirmed.

This is the argument Schwiedrzik and Freiwald are implicitly using when they describe their face-processing result as consistent with a "general cortical principle." The generalisation is licensed because the principle is architectural, not domain-specific. Any system where higher-level models generate predictions for lower levels will exhibit this property, because predictions inherit the representational format of the model that generates them. The face-processing hierarchy is one instantiation. The E1→E2 interface in ARC-022 would be another.

The implications for how to read the ARC-022 evidence base are substantial. The Schwiedrzik and Freiwald result is not the foundation for ARC-022's typed-object pipeline — it is an empirical confirmation of a theoretical proposition that was established in 1992 and formalised computationally by Rao and Ballard in 1999. ARC-022's reliance on typed-object descent at the E1→E2 interface draws on a 25-year theoretical tradition with independent confirmation from two directions: the statistical inference tradition (Mumford → Rao/Ballard → Friston) and the anatomical specificity evidence (Felleman & Van Essen 1991 → Bastos et al. 2012). Schwiedrzik and Freiwald add the electrophysiological content of the prediction signal itself. Together these are three independent lines of evidence for a single architectural principle, not a single specialized result being generalised.

What Mumford (1992) does not provide is any specificity about what the typed objects actually are at each interface. The framework says predictions carry the higher level's abstraction properties; it does not say that at the E1→E2 interface those objects are z_gamma transitions, or that at the DMN layer they are narrative trajectory bundles, or that the pipeline has five stages rather than two. Those remain ARC-022's specific architectural claims. The Mumford framework is the theoretical foundation on which those claims stand; it is not a validation of the particular objects and layer count that ARC-022 proposes.
