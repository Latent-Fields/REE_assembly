# Palagi & Mancini 2011: Gelada Play Face Ontogeny and Signal Modulation

## What the paper did

Palagi and Mancini studied play face variant usage in zoo-housed geladas (Theropithecus gelada) across different age groups. They documented two play face variants: PF (standard play face, mouth open with lower teeth only) and FPF (full play face, with additional retraction of the upper lip exposing upper teeth and gums). They examined whether age predicted which variant was used, whether adults and immatures differed in their sensitivity to others' play face displays, and whether the two variants occurred differentially in social vs. solitary play contexts.

## Key findings relevant to the claim

In immatures, PF dominated; in adults, FPF became the primary variant and PF was virtually absent. Adults were significantly more sensitive than immatures to conspecifics' play face displays -- adults responded to partners' play faces with appropriate facial matching more reliably. The FPF was extremely frequent during mother-offspring play (an intimate, asymmetric dyad), suggesting it carries an additional emotional/affiliative signal beyond pure play-frame maintenance. The ontogenetic trajectory from PF to FPF parallels the inferred phylogenetic sequence, with FPF considered a derived form.

## Translation to REE

This is the most direct evidence on developmental timeline of play signal competence available for a non-canid species. For REE's DEV-NEED-009 (play-dominant childhood), the key finding is that sensitivity to partners' background signals is developmentally acquired through play experience and neural maturation -- it is not innate. An agent trained without a play-dominant developmental phase would use the simpler, less-socially-precise signal variant and would have reduced sensitivity to partners' frame state signals. INV-059 (mutual frame maintenance necessity) is supported by adults' superior response sensitivity: the bilateral frame maintenance that INV-059 posits requires both parties to be able to produce and decode background signals reliably. Developmental experience installs this capacity. DEV-NEED-014 (cooperative play with diverse partners) is the mechanism: playing with adults as an immature, and with immatures as an adult, provides the calibration training for the graded signal system.

## Limitations and caveats

Cross-sectional design (different animals at different ages) cannot directly measure within-individual developmental change. Zoo housing may alter signal repertoire. The intimate mother-offspring FPF pattern suggests the FPF carries functions beyond pure play-frame maintenance (possibly affiliative bonding), which complicates the mapping to REE's level-2 background signal. The emotional vs. strategic distinction in FPF use requires further investigation.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence is 0.80. Good behavioral methodology, published in a respected comparative psychology journal, directly addresses the developmental timeline question. Confidence below 0.85 due to cross-sectional design and zoo housing limitations.
