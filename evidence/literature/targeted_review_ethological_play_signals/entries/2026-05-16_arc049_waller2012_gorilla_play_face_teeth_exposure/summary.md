# Waller & Cherry 2012: Gorilla Play Face -- Teeth Exposure and Play Facilitation

## What the paper did

Waller and Cherry used focal animal sampling of 10 captive Western Lowland gorillas to compare two variants of the gorilla play face: the standard relaxed open mouth (ROM/PF, lower teeth only exposed) and the full play face (PF with upper teeth additionally exposed). They measured when each variant occurred relative to play intensity and what happened after each variant in terms of bout duration and post-play affiliative behavior.

## Key findings relevant to the claim

The full PF was significantly more common during intense play than gentle play. Intense play bouts that contained the full PF were longer than those with only the standard PF. Both expressions were associated with increased affiliative behavior after play, but the full PF was associated with a larger increase. The authors interpret the full PF as having an additional role in coordinating and maintaining play -- possibly by "reducing uncertainty in the receiver and confirming that play is only play." Both expressions are associated with the relaxed open mouth display (ROM) seen across primates and carnivores.

## Translation to REE

This paper demonstrates that the background signal (level-2 in the three-level architecture) carries graded intensity information, not just binary play/not-play status. The standard play face = 'frame open, current intensity moderate'; the full play face = 'frame open, intensity elevated, this is still play even though it seems rough'. This graded modulation within level-2 is important for REE's ARC-049 implementation: the frame context tag may need an intensity component, allowing the partner to calibrate expectation and harm-assessment thresholds during play. The full PF's association with longer bouts supports INV-059 (mutual frame maintenance necessity): active background signaling sustains the episode; episodes without adequate signaling terminate earlier. This also partially resolves Q-035's sub-question about the background signal's necessity: the background signal carries information that shapes episode duration and post-play social outcomes.

## Limitations and caveats

Captive setting (N=10 gorillas) limits ecological validity. The mechanism -- whether the full PF functions as an escalated background signal or as a within-level repair -- is not fully separable with behavioral data alone. The gorilla is a single species; the cross-species review requires the other entries for generalization. The full PF appears absent in other apes studied to date, suggesting it may be a derived gorilla-specific signal variant rather than a universal primate pattern.
