# Targeted Review: Ethological Play Signals and Three-Level Signal Architecture

## Overview

This review targets the empirical basis for ARC-049's three-level play signal architecture (frame-open, background signal, explicit repair signal), originally described by Bekoff (1995) in canids. Eight entries cover canids, gorillas, geladas, hyenas, dolphins, and ravens, spanning from the foundational 1995 paper to the most recent cetacean study (Palagi 2024, iScience).

## Summary Verdict

The three-level architecture generalizes robustly beyond canids. Key results:

1. **Background signal (level-2) is a conserved mammalian mechanism.** Open-mouth play expressions in primates and carnivores are phylogenetic homologues, not analogues (Davila-Ross & Palagi 2022, Phil Trans Roy Soc B). Dolphins independently evolved the same mechanism at 95 million years phylogenetic distance with the same functional properties (Palagi 2024, iScience). This convergent evidence across such phylogenetic distance argues for the architecture as an adaptive solution to a universal problem, not a phylogenetic accident.

2. **Background signal is sustained by rapid facial mimicry (RFM).** RFM -- automatic sub-500ms replication of a partner's play face -- is the neural mechanism of level-2 frame maintenance. RFM predicts episode duration and balance (Davila-Ross & Palagi 2022). The 13x odds ratio for OM replication in dolphins when the signal is visible vs. invisible is an operationalizable metric for frame maintenance integrity.

3. **Level-3 explicit repair signals fire at ambiguity junctures, not uniformly.** Bekoff 1995 established that play bows in canids cluster before/after ambiguous actions (bite-shakes). Nolfo et al. 2022 dissociated two hyena signals: ROM (metacommunicative, attention-sensitive, anticipates offensive actions, increases during asymmetric play) from HB (head-bobbing, not metacommunicative). This ROM/HB dissociation in a single species is the cleanest evidence for distinct level-2 vs. level-3 channels.

4. **Background signal is graded, not binary.** Gorilla full-PF vs. standard PF (Waller 2012) and gelada FPF vs. PF (Palagi 2011) both document intensity-modulated variants of the background signal that carry contextually specific information (roughness, intimacy). Level-2 is a continuous channel, not a flag.

5. **Developmental trajectory: signal competence is acquired through play experience.** Gelada data shows immatures use simpler variants (PF) and have lower response sensitivity; adults use the more precise variants (FPF) and are better calibrated to partners' signals (Palagi 2011). This directly supports DEV-NEED-009's claim that play-dominant childhood builds the signal architecture.

6. **Non-mammalian extension.** Ravens show emotional play contagion at 3 months (Wenig 2021, Animal Cognition) -- mood-transfer without specific facial signals, suggesting the level-2 function (sustaining mutual play state) can be implemented through channels other than facial RFM.

## Answers to Specific Questions

**Does background signal sustain the frame (necessary, not just convenient)?** Yes. RFM frequency predicts episode duration (Davila-Ross 2022). Gorilla PF presence associated with longer intense play (Waller 2012). Background signal is functionally necessary.

**What determines when explicit repair signal fires?** Ambiguity triggers: presence of cross-context actions (bite-shakes: Bekoff 1995), pause/disengagement risk (Byosiere 2017), play asymmetry (Nolfo 2022 hyenas). All are variants of 'current play frame status uncertain.'

**Does the architecture generalize beyond canids?** Yes: gorillas, geladas (primates), hyenas, meerkats, sun bears (carnivores), dolphins (cetaceans), ravens (corvids). The core mechanism is conserved or convergently evolved.

**Developmental timeline of signal levels?** Level-2 background signal competence (production and response sensitivity) develops across juvenile period; immatures use simpler variants and have lower sensitivity (geladas). Level-3 explicit repair signals may be present earlier (Bekoff 1995 infant dogs) -- potentially appearing before full adult signal calibration.
