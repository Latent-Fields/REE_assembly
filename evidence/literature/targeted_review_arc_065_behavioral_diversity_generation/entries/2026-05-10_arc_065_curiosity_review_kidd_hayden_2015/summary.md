# Kidd & Hayden 2015 — The Psychology and Neuroscience of Curiosity

[DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2015.09.010) · PMID 26539887 · *Neuron* 88(3):449–60

## What the paper argues

Kidd & Hayden survey the curiosity literature spanning behavioural psychology, developmental psychology, and primate neurophysiology. They argue against treating curiosity as a single unitary construct with a single neural signature. The behavioural phenomena that get labelled "curiosity" — perceptual curiosity, epistemic curiosity, diversive exploration, specific information-seeking, learning-progress maximisation — have overlapping but distinguishable neural correlates spanning OFC, ACC, dopaminergic midbrain, and hippocampus. The paper recommends that the field stop trying to define curiosity in the abstract and instead study information-seeking in its ethological context.

## Why this matters for ARC-065

For the V3 cluster scoping, this paper is a guardrail against a tempting but wrong simplification: registering MECH-314 as a single "curiosity bonus on E3 score_bias" knob with a single threshold. Kidd & Hayden's heterogeneity argument predicts that single-channel implementation will fail to reproduce the context-dependent flavour of biological information-seeking. REE's existing MECH-216 (E1 predictive wanting / schema readout) partially captures the novelty-driven flavour by reading schema recognition out as a wanting bonus. But two other flavours have no substrate yet: uncertainty-driven exploration (Daw 2006 frontopolar substrate), and learning-progress-driven exploration (the Schmidhuber/Pathak computational tradition).

The cluster registration recommendation that follows: ARC-065 anchors the pathway, MECH-314 is the structured-curiosity-channel umbrella, with at least three candidate sub-mechs to break out (MECH-314a novelty / MECH-314b uncertainty / MECH-314c learning-progress) once the next pull resolves which biological substrate carries each. We don't need to lock the sub-MECH structure now; the cluster anchor leaves room for it.

## Limitations and confidence

The paper is a Perspective rather than empirical work; its load-bearing contribution is conceptual framing. Kidd & Hayden explicitly resist single computational definitions, which makes the paper less directly actionable than Wilson 2014's clean two-channel decomposition. Used here mainly to justify the heterogeneity caveat in the R1 verdict and to motivate leaving sub-MECH room under MECH-314 for the curiosity-flavour distinctions. Confidence aggregate 0.78 — high venue and synthesis quality, moderate mapping fidelity because the paper resists committing to a substrate-level mechanism.

## Failure signature it would falsify

A single-knob MECH-314 implementation that treats curiosity as a temperature parameter or single bonus magnitude — and consequently fails to reproduce the context-dependent flavour shifts (novelty-driven vs uncertainty-driven vs progress-driven) the paper documents.
