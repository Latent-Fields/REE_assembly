# Laukkonen, Friston & Chandaria 2025 — A beautiful loop: An active inference theory of consciousness

**Source:** Laukkonen R, Friston K, Chandaria S. *Neuroscience and Biobehavioral Reviews* 176:106296 (2025). [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neubiorev.2025.106296). PMID 40750007. According to PubMed.

## What the paper did

This is a recent (2025) Friston-coauthored paper proposing an active-inference theory of consciousness. The relevant architectural commitment for REE's MECH-204 question is in the formal proposal: "We propose a hyper-model for precision-control, whose latent states (or parameters) encode and control the overall structure and weighting rules for all layers of inference. These globally integrated preferences for precision enact the epistemic agency and flexibility reminiscent of general intelligence." The Bayesian beliefs about precision are recursively shared throughout the system in a hierarchical loop — what the authors call a "beautiful loop."

The hyper-model is proposed as the architecture by which a conscious system maintains and broadcasts global preferences for precision across all layers of its hierarchical inference structure. This is structurally distinct from the simpler model where each layer has its own local precision parameters and inference proceeds purely by local update.

## Why it matters for MECH-204

The hyper-model proposal directly supports the architectural shape that Phase 7 / Option B (broadcast read at action selection consuming the captured precision) would instantiate in REE. If biology has a hyper-model that broadcasts global precision preferences through the inference hierarchy — and if that hyper-model consumes REM-captured zero-points as part of its global precision state — then F3 / Option B is biologically motivated. The hyper-model is the active-inference framing of "precision is broadcast at choice time, separately from the underlying parameter updates that happen post-outcome (Iglesias 2013) or during sleep (this lit pull's Hobson-Hong-Friston 2014 entry)."

But the paper does NOT specifically address REM-driven recalibration. The hyper-model is proposed in the context of waking conscious experience integration. Whether the hyper-model consumes REM-captured zero-points (which would license F3 / Option B for REE's MECH-204) or operates only on within-waking precision dynamics (which would leave MECH-204 in the F1-sufficient regime per Hobson-Hong-Friston 2014) is an inferential mapping step the paper does not directly resolve.

This is why the evidence direction is **mixed**. The paper supports the existence of an explicit precision-broadcast system in biology — that's the F3 / Option B hypothesis. But it does not specifically connect that broadcast system to REM-captured zero-points. The most defensible reading is that biology may run BOTH F1 (the cumulative parameter refinement Hobson-Hong-Friston 2014 + Walker-Stickgold 2006 support) AND F3 (the hyper-model broadcast Laukkonen-Friston-Chandaria 2025 supports), as a sleep-extension of the dual-arm pattern Q-042 already established for general waking precision-update timing.

## Mapping to REE

For REE's Phase 7 / Option B decision, this paper TILTS the reading toward "F3 is biologically motivated and may be needed in addition to F1 (just landed)." That is the dual-arm pattern: F1 absorbs precision recalibration through the parameter refinement pathway (the model is updated during REM and waking inference uses the updated model); F3 / hyper-model broadcasts the captured precision globally at action-selection time, providing a second-order signal that complements the first-order parameter consumption.

Concretely: if V3-EXQ-541b's step-size sweep finds that no step in the defensible band {0.05, 0.10, 0.25} clears C3 (cross-arm divergence), this paper supports queueing Phase 7 / Option B implementation as the next architectural lever, consuming `_persistent_zero_point` (or the moment-snapshot `_precision_at_rem_entry`) as a broadcast signal at `select_action()` time. The broadcast read would not REPLACE the F1 cumulative reference; it would COMPLEMENT it — gating action choices against the reference in the same tick that the reference value is updated.

## Caveats

(1) The hyper-model is proposed in the consciousness / general-intelligence framing, not specifically as a REM-recalibration consumer. The mapping to REE's MECH-204 is one architectural step removed. (2) Lower confidence than Hobson-Hong-Friston 2014 because the F3-supporting reading requires inferring that the hyper-model consumes REM-captured zero-points, which the paper does not directly state. (3) The 2025 Friston paper postdates the Hobson-Friston 2014 framework by 11 years; the hyper-model framing represents an evolution of the active-inference architecture toward more explicit precision-control machinery. The two are compatible (both Friston-authored) but differ in emphasis. (4) No new empirical data — purely theoretical.
