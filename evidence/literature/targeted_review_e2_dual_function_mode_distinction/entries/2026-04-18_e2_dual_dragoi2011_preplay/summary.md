# Dragoi & Tonegawa 2011 -- Preplay of Future Place Cell Sequences

The Dragoi and Tonegawa Nature paper is, to my reading, the single most useful piece of biological evidence for the question we are trying to settle about E2. The authors record from hippocampal place cells during rest and sleep that precede a novel spatial experience, and then again during the experience itself. They show that temporal sequences of firing -- the same kind of structured order that, after exploration, is known to be expressed in replay -- are present before the animal ever enters the novel environment. The assembly that will later encode the experience has already rehearsed it, in a statistical sense, during the preceding quiescent period. This is preplay.

What makes this relevant to the E2 mode question is that preplay and replay share substrate. They are not two different hippocampal populations. The same cellular assembly expresses sequences in a prospective mode (before experience) and in a retrospective mode (after experience). The distinction between the two modes is not which neurons fire, but when, under what behavioural state, and with what downstream consequences. This is the cleanest instance I can find in the neuroscience literature of a predictive substrate operating bimodally -- prospective for anticipation, retrospective for consolidation -- without architectural duplication.

For E2 the implication is straightforward. If hippocampal sequence machinery evolved to support one population reading in two modes, the structural default for a forward-model substrate like E2 is the same. Attribution (after-the-fact comparison of predicted vs actual harm outcome) and evaluation (before-the-fact rollout scoring over candidate actions) can plausibly share a single E2, with mode determined by behavioural state and downstream readout. That is an important nudge away from registering MECH-E2-DUAL-FUNCTION as two disjoint E2 substrates.

Two caveats deserve to be stated plainly. First, preplay is not a forward-model prediction in the E2 sense. It is a structured sequence of place cell activations, probabilistically matched to future exploration. Calling it prospective rollout is generous; it is predictive in that it anticipates, but it is not necessarily running the kind of f(z_t, a_t) rollout that E2 implements. So the mapping from hippocampal sequence to E2 action-consequence prediction is principle-level, not implementation-level. Second, the preplay literature itself has acknowledged that the pre-configured subset of cells expressing preplay is not exactly coextensive with the set of cells that replay the experience afterwards. There is substantial overlap, but there is also partial specialisation. That slight asymmetry is exactly where a dual-substrate reading can slip in.

On balance, I would weight this entry at 0.70 confidence -- the strongest of the five in this pull. It is direct, it is from a canonical lab (Tonegawa), and the methodology is the sort of thing one can build on. It supports the single-substrate-plus-readout framing of MECH-E2-DUAL-FUNCTION, and by extension supports ARC-018 (hippocampal rollout + viability mapping) on the same mechanism. What would push this higher still is evidence that the same preplay/replay cells participate in both an attributional comparison circuit and an evaluative rollout-scoring circuit within the same behavioural session -- and that, so far, is not a published result I have seen.
