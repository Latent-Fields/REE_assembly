# Diba & Buzsaki 2007 -- Forward and Reverse Place-Cell Sequences

Diba and Buzsaki report, in this short but remarkably influential Nature Neuroscience paper, that hippocampal place cells on an elevated track express temporally compressed sequences in two directions, timed to behavioural phase and riding on sharp-wave ripple events. At the end of a run, sequences play backwards -- reverse replay. In anticipation of a run, sequences play forwards. Both use the same cell population. The vector distance between place fields is preserved in the temporal structure of the compressed sequences, suggesting that the hippocampal assembly is not just replaying recent activity but re-expressing a spatial trajectory with directional metadata.

For the E2 dual-function question, this is about as close to a direct biological analogue as one can hope for. Forward sequences before action behave, computationally, like a prospective rollout: the assembly projects the upcoming trajectory in a temporally compressed preview. Reverse sequences after action behave like a retrospective attribution step: the assembly replays the just-completed trajectory in reverse, which is exactly the computational shape of credit assignment (walk back the trajectory to compute which steps produced the outcome). The crucial empirical point is that both modes use the same underlying substrate. The cell population is not duplicated; only the direction of temporal compression and the behavioural phase change.

This maps onto MECH-E2-DUAL-FUNCTION about as cleanly as a biological result can map onto an architectural design question. If one were going to assert that E2 must have two disjoint substrates -- one for evaluation, one for attribution -- the Diba/Buzsaki result is the kind of finding that asks you to reconsider. The brain's own forward-trajectory-capable substrate (the hippocampus on this paradigm, at least) is shared across modes. The mode is gated by behavioural phase and by some controller signal that flips the direction of sequence expression.

There are two caveats worth pausing on. The first is that the result lives inside sharp-wave ripple events. SWRs are a specific brain state -- typically offline, consummatory, post-run immobility, slow-wave sleep -- and not the moment-by-moment online regime in which E2 is proposed to operate during action selection. If attribution and evaluation in E2 need to happen during ongoing behaviour, the Diba/Buzsaki precedent may or may not transfer. The second caveat is that subsequent literature has partially dissociated forward and reverse replay in terms of their downstream function (reverse replay has been more closely tied to credit assignment, forward replay/preplay to planning). If the downstream circuits that consume forward vs reverse sequences turn out to be substantially disjoint, the single-substrate reading weakens, and a partial dual-substrate account becomes defensible.

With those caveats in place, this is the strongest entry in the pull, which is why I rate it at 0.72. The combination of the Diba/Buzsaki forward/reverse result and the Dragoi/Tonegawa preplay/replay result gives a coherent picture: the hippocampal sequence substrate is shared across prospective and retrospective modes, with directional and temporal structure encoding the mode. Imported into REE, this strongly suggests MECH-E2-DUAL-FUNCTION should be framed as a single E2 substrate read bimodally under controller/behavioural-phase gating, and not as two separate E2 networks.
