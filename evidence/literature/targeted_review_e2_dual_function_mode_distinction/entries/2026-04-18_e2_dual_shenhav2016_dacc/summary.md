# Shenhav, Cohen, Botvinick 2016 -- dACC and the Value of Control

The 2016 Nature Neuroscience follow-up sharpens and defends the expected value of control account of dACC. The authors take direct aim at foraging-value accounts, arguing that the EVC framework better explains why the same region responds to such a diverse array of experimental variables -- reward prediction, conflict, effort, switching, surprise, error. On their reading, one unified control-allocation function explains the region's apparent promiscuity; distinct sub-regions for distinct task modes are not required.

This is precisely the structural point that matters for our E2 dual-function question. If the brain has elsewhere decided to economise -- one region, one integrative function, many downstream readouts -- then the a priori default for E2 is that its apparent bimodality (attributional after-the-fact comparison vs. evaluative pre-action scoring) is likely to be readout-driven rather than substrate-driven. The paper's argument against decomposition of dACC is structurally identical to the argument one would have to mount against decomposition of E2: the diverse functional signatures do not require a multi-module architecture; they can emerge from a single module read under different controllers.

Two caveats are important. First, the subject matter is cognitive control, not forward-model reuse. The transfer is architectural reasoning by analogy. Second, Shenhav and colleagues are themselves in an ongoing argument against decompositional accounts; the field has not settled, and at least some evidence (e.g. specific dACC responses to foraging value) is consistent with a more modular organisation. If that decompositional view turns out to be correct for dACC, the single-substrate precedent for E2 weakens.

For registry purposes, this entry strengthens the same direction as the 2013 entry: a single-process-plus-readout framing of MECH-E2-DUAL-FUNCTION is more consistent with prior cortical architectural preferences than a dual-substrate framing. But the evidence is indirect, so I hold confidence around 0.55. The combination of the 2013 Neuron paper and this 2016 Perspective is not a proof; it is a pattern of prior that biases us toward the parsimonious claim.

What would settle the question is not another theoretical defence of EVC but a direct single-unit or two-photon demonstration that the same forward-model-coding population is read in two computational modes within the same session. The Shenhav papers tell us the shape of the answer the cortex prefers; they do not, by themselves, answer the E2 question.
