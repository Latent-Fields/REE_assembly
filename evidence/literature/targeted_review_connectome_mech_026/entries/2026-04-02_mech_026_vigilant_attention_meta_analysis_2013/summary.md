# Langner & Eickhoff (2013) — Sustaining Attention: Neural Mechanisms of Vigilant Attention

## What the paper does

This is a comprehensive meta-analysis in Psychological Bulletin covering 100+ neuroimaging studies of vigilant attention. Langner and Eickhoff synthesize the neural architecture underlying the ability to maintain readiness to detect rare signals over extended periods. The key finding: vigilant attention recruits a right-lateralized frontoparietal network (dorsolateral PFC, anterior insula, inferior parietal lobule) sustained by thalamic and brainstem arousal circuits. The neural state is one of high cortical arousal with suppressed motor output — the organism is maximally alert but behaviorally quiescent.

## Relevance to MECH-026

MECH-026 claims that ready vigilance primes restraint under high sensitivity without action. This is precisely what the vigilance literature describes at the neural level: a dedicated brain state for being maximally sensitive while producing no motor output. The right PFC plays a dual role — both sustaining attention and actively inhibiting premature motor responses. This is not passive waiting; it is active restraint coupled with heightened monitoring.

For REE, the mapping is: elevated E3 sensitivity (high precision weighting on harm and goal channels) with suppressed action selection. The system is in a state of readiness — monitoring the environment with full sensitivity but not committing to any action. The biological precedent suggests this is a genuine, distinct neural mode, not merely "doing nothing."

## The vigilance decrement problem

The meta-analysis documents the vigilance decrement — performance declining over time as right PFC and parietal activation wane. This is a well-established phenomenon in human sustained attention. For REE, this raises an interesting question: does computational ready vigilance suffer an analogous decrement? If E3's sensitivity parameter is static, there would be no decrement — but biological vigilance appears to require active metabolic maintenance. This could be a feature (REE doesn't fatigue) or a fidelity gap (biological vigilance has costs that REE doesn't model, and those costs may be informationally important).

## The arousal-inhibition architecture

The cingulo-opercular network (anterior insula, dorsal ACC) and the locus coeruleus-norepinephrine system jointly maintain the arousal component. The LC-NE system operates on an inverted-U curve: too little norepinephrine and the system is drowsy; too much and it becomes distractible. Optimal vigilance sits at the peak. In REE, this suggests that ready vigilance may need a precision parameter that is neither too low (insensitive) nor too high (triggering false alarms). This connects to ARC-016's dynamic precision claim — the precision setting for ready vigilance may need to be actively regulated, not just set high.

## Confidence reasoning

Excellent meta-analytic source in a top-tier venue. The "high arousal + suppressed motor output" finding maps directly onto MECH-026's core claim. Confidence moderated by the substantial gap between perceptual vigilance (detecting rare signals in monotonous tasks) and ethical vigilance (maintaining moral sensitivity without acting). The cognitive content differs even if the arousal/inhibition architecture is shared.
