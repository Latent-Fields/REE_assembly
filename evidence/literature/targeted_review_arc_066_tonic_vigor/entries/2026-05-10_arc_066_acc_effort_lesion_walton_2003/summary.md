# Walton, Bannerman, Alterescu & Rushworth 2003 -- ACC lesion reverses effort-cost preference

**Citation:** Walton ME, Bannerman DM, Alterescu K, Rushworth MFS. Functional specialization within medial frontal cortex of the anterior cingulate for evaluating effort-related decisions. *Journal of Neuroscience*. 2003;23(16):6475-6479. PMID: 12878688. DOI: 10.1523/JNEUROSCI.23-16-06475.2003.

## What the paper does

The canonical rodent ACC lesion study on effort-cost decision making. Long-Evans rats trained on a T-maze cost-benefit task: one arm required climbing a 30 cm barrier to obtain a large quantity of reward, the other arm was a level path leading to a smaller reward. Sham-lesioned controls reliably chose the high-effort high-reward arm; rats with excitotoxic ACC lesions nearly always chose the low-effort low-reward option, even though they remained physically capable of climbing (verified by forced-choice trials). The interpretation: ACC is necessary for integrating effort cost into the value computation that drives action selection.

## Why this is a mixed-direction entry

The R1 verdict needs to attribute ARC-066's substrate. The candidate substrates were DA-vigor (Niv / Salamone / Beierholm), LC-NE-direction (Aston-Jones / weakened by Kane), BAS abstract (Depue & Collins), and ACC effort/vigor (Walton). The synthesis verdict is that DA-vigor is load-bearing; ACC's role is contributory rather than load-bearing for ARC-066 specifically.

The reason: Walton 2003's evidence is for ACC's role in COST-BENEFIT WEIGHING GIVEN A SPECIFIC GOAL (high-reward arm vs low-reward arm). The paper does not directly evidence the TARGET-FREE function ARC-066 names. The closer REE relatives for the ACC-effort-cost machinery are:

- **SD-032b (dACC adaptive control / foraging value):** the substrate-landed dACC machinery in REE that handles cost-benefit-given-goal computation. Walton 2003's lesion pattern is the kind of effect SD-032b was designed to reproduce.
- **ARC-068 (opportunity cost no-op penalty):** the family-companion slot to ARC-066. ARC-068 is about cost-of-passivity, which is closer to the "passive arm has lower expected utility than active arm" computation Walton 2003 reveals.

ARC-066 specifically is about a capacity-keyed POSITIVE bias toward action over no-op, target-free. Walton 2003 supports the broader architectural commitment that a cost-benefit weighing happens (which ARC-066 + ARC-068 together compete on), but doesn't directly evidence ARC-066's positive-side function. This is why the entry is mixed-direction rather than supports.

## What this paper is good for in the synthesis

The synthesis uses Walton 2003 as a BOUNDARY anchor, clarifying which existing REE substrate handles which biological function:

- ACC effort cost: SD-032b dACC adaptive control covers it; Walton 2003 is the validation anchor for SD-032b.
- DA vigor scalar: Niv / Salamone / Beierholm cover it; this is ARC-066 territory.
- LC-NE noise floor: MECH-313 covers it; Aston-Jones / Kane are the substrate-level anchors (with Kane disambiguating LC-NE tonic as noise-not-direction).
- Capacity-keyed action bias (target-free): ARC-066 (currently empty); substrate attribution per R1 is mesolimbic DA-vigor.
- Opportunity cost on passive: ARC-068 (currently empty); substrate attribution overlaps Niv 2007 and Kolling et al. 2015.

Walton 2003 lives at the SD-032b boundary, not the ARC-066 substrate-attribution centre. The entry is included for completeness (because the user briefing pre-registered it) and to make the substrate boundaries explicit.
