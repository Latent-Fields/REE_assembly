# Salamone & Correa 2012 -- Mesolimbic DA encodes activational, not hedonic, motivation

**Citation:** Salamone JD, Correa M. The mysterious motivational functions of mesolimbic dopamine. *Neuron*. 2012;76(3):470-485. PMID: 23141060. DOI: 10.1016/j.neuron.2012.10.021.

## What the paper does

Salamone & Correa give the canonical modern statement of the activational-vs-hedonic dissociation in motivational neuroscience. Their thesis is that decades of pharmacology data have been mis-summarised as "DA = reward signal", when the more accurate reading is that mesolimbic / accumbens DA encodes the *activational dimension* of motivation -- behavioural activation, exertion of effort, approach behaviour, sustained task engagement -- and is mostly dissociable from hedonic / consummatory aspects of reward. The supporting evidence cluster: DA antagonism and accumbens DA depletion impair instrumental / preparatory behaviours while leaving consumption preserved; effortful task selection is selectively reduced; the wanting-liking dissociation (Berridge & Robinson) is grounded in this substrate. They explicitly cite clinical anhedonic / abulic phenotypes (depression, Parkinson's) as the human anchor.

## Why this matters for ARC-066

The R1 verdict (substrate attribution) needed to choose among DA-vigor (Niv 2007), LC-NE-direction (Aston-Jones 2005), and the BAS-abstract (Depue & Collins 1999). Kane et al. 2017 (separate entry) weakens the LC-NE-direction substrate by showing that LC-NE tonic effects are noise-mediated. That leaves DA-vigor and BAS as the surviving R1 candidates, and Salamone & Correa is the load-bearing biological-substrate paper for the DA-vigor side.

The functional shape Salamone & Correa describe is exactly what ARC-066 needs: a behavioural-activation gradient that is target-INDEPENDENT (about acting at all, not about pursuing a specific target). They explicitly contrast this with consummatory / hedonic functions, which is the boundary REE needs to draw between ARC-066 (target-free action bias) and MECH-216 / MECH-295 (target-conditioned wanting / hedonic-bias). The dissociation gives ARC-066 a clean substrate-level identity that does not collide with existing substrate-landed mechanisms.

## Boundary work this paper does

The paper helps draw three boundaries the synthesis depends on:

1. **vs MECH-216 (predictive wanting):** MECH-216 fires when a specific target is in scope. Salamone & Correa's DA-activation function is target-free (general behavioural activation). ARC-066 inherits the target-free reading.
2. **vs MECH-295 (incentive liking-bias):** MECH-295 is hedonic; Salamone & Correa's substrate is activational. ARC-066 is not a re-implementation of MECH-295.
3. **vs ARC-068 (opportunity-cost no-op penalty):** Both relate to behavioural activation, but the implementations differ in shape. Salamone & Correa support the broader architectural commitment without committing to the additive vs multiplicative formalism. R3 leans on Niv 2007 for the formalism; Salamone & Correa give the substrate identity.

## Caveats

The paper says less than one would like about how DA-vigor relates to average-reward-rate (Niv 2007) formally. The two accounts overlap conceptually -- both describe a tonic DA scalar that drives behavioural activation -- but they have different theoretical apparatus. Niv 2007 derives the scalar from optimal control under average-cost RL; Salamone & Correa observe the dissociation empirically without committing to a formal model. The synthesis treats them as complementary: Niv 2007 supplies the formalism, Salamone & Correa supply the substrate identity, and they converge on the architectural commitment ARC-066 names.

A second caveat: the substrate is rodent / primate. Human evidence for the same dissociation is in companion papers (Beierholm 2013 with L-DOPA, Treadway et al. 2012 EEfRT task, Chong et al.) which the synthesis would expand into in a follow-up cycle. The human anchor in this pull is Beierholm 2013 (separate entry).
