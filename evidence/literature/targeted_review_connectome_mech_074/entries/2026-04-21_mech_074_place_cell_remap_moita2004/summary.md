# Moita, Rosis, Zhou, LeDoux & Blair 2004 -- Putting fear in its place: remapping of hippocampal place cells during fear conditioning

## What the paper establishes

Rats were implanted with tetrodes in dorsal CA1 and recorded in two arenas -- a "training box" and a "control box" -- across three sessions: pre-conditioning baseline, fear conditioning (16 tone-shock eyelid pairings in the training box), and post-conditioning. The question was whether fear conditioning would reshape the spatial map of the environment where the shock was delivered. It did. In the training box, the correlation between each place cell's pre- and post-conditioning spatial firing pattern fell from a within-session stability baseline of about 0.57 to a post-conditioning value of 0.39. In the control box -- which the same animals explored but where nothing aversive happened -- the correlation stayed at 0.59. The within-session stability of both arenas, measured pre- and post-conditioning separately, did not change. So the remap is not recording drift, not arena re-exposure, not time passing: it is a specific response to the arena having acquired threat-predictive status.

## The contextual-vs-auditory dissociation

This is the finding that matters most for MECH-074. The authors ran two fear-conditioning protocols: contextual (the context itself predicts shock, no discrete cue) and auditory (a tone predicts shock, context-independent). Contextual conditioning produced the big remapping effect described above; auditory conditioning produced almost none (normalised Z-scores: -1.36 vs -0.34, p=0.02 for the difference). This is exactly the pattern MECH-074 predicts. The amygdala is not broadcasting a "threat happened, remap everything" signal. It is routing the remap toward whichever representation the animal's causal model identified as the PREDICTOR of the threat. When the context is the predictor, CA1 spatial maps remap. When a tone is the predictor, CA1 spatial maps stay largely stable because space is not the relevant causal variable. The BLA->HPC write-gate has structure: it fires at predictor-identified features, not at everything active at the time of harm.

## What the paper does not isolate

Moita used a 16-trial conditioning block, measured the remap after the full block, and compared pre vs post. This is well above any plausible one-shot threshold, so the paper cannot distinguish whether the remap is a single-trial step-function response or a graded accumulation across 16 trials. A few later papers get closer (Dupret 2013, Wang 2015 on trial-by-trial map tracking) but Moita's contribution is the aggregate geometry -- how much of the map changes, and where. For BLAAnalog implementation purposes, this is the right thing to take from the paper: set the remap amplitude based on the correlation drop, set the routing rule based on the predictor-identification principle, and defer the per-trial dynamics to a follow-up literature pull.

## The partial-remap finding

One more detail is worth flagging: Moita observed that some fields within a single animal remapped while others did not, even within the training box. This is NOT a wholesale map replacement. It is a sparse, field-specific rewrite. The implication for BLAAnalog is that the remap_signal should not reinitialise the HPC latent stack; it should perturb a selected subset of codes identified as predictors. A default of ~30-40% of codes perturbed is consistent with the observed 0.6 -> 0.4 correlation drop.

## Quantitative defaults for BLAAnalog remap

- **PE threshold (in ||z_harm_a - pred|| units):** NOT isolated by this paper -- the 16-trial block is supra-threshold by design. Use the Nader 2000 retrieval-gated principle as the temporal trigger and leave the PE magnitude as a free parameter. Suggested starting value for REE: the remap fires when the harm-PE exceeds ~1 SD of the running PE distribution AND the predictor-identification head flags a candidate feature.
- **Time constant of remap decision (window duration):** The remap is observable within ~1 hour of the last conditioning trial (the post-conditioning session followed conditioning with a 10-minute rest). Combined with Nader's 4-6 h labile window, the remap DECISION fires fast (minutes) and the plasticity GATE stays open for hours.
- **One-shot vs graded:** Moita cannot distinguish. The partial-remap finding (only a subset of fields remap) argues for a sparse step-function -- at the level of individual codes, a code either remaps or it does not, but the POPULATION looks graded because the gate fires on only a fraction of predictor-candidate codes. Default: step-function at the code level, with the fraction of codes remapped set by the predictor-identification head (default ~30-40% of affected codes).
- **Remap amplitude:** Training-box correlation dropped from ~0.6 to ~0.4, a ~30-35% reduction in spatial information. In REE terms, the perturbation injected by the remap_signal should overwrite roughly one-third of the selected latent codes, not replace the whole map.
- **Routing specificity:** Remap fires at the feature that PREDICTED the threat, not at everything active. For REE, this means BLAAnalog must consult a predictor-identification step (which latent codes have non-zero attribution to the harm-PE) before emitting remap_signal. A broadcast architecture is ruled out by the contextual-vs-auditory dissociation.
