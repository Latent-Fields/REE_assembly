# An integrative theory of prefrontal cortex function
**Miller & Cohen (2001), Annual Review of Neuroscience** | DOI: 10.1146/annurev.neuro.24.1.167

## What the paper did

Miller and Cohen synthesised neurophysiological, neurobiological, neuroimaging, and computational evidence to propose a unified account of PFC function. Their core claim: PFC does not compute responses directly but maintains active representations of goals and task rules that send top-down bias signals to downstream regions, shaping which input-output mappings are active for a given context.

## Key findings

Active maintenance of task-relevant representations in PFC biases activity in sensory, motor, and associative areas toward context-appropriate mappings. This bias is implemented via direct PFC projections to posterior cortical areas, with influence persisting over the behavioural timescale needed to complete a task. The model explains attention, working memory, inhibitory control, and task switching as different instances of the same underlying bias mechanism.

The review documented that PFC neurons fire selectively for task rules, not just sensory features, and that this activity is maintained across delay periods -- a defining feature of working memory that distinguishes PFC from posterior cortex.

## REE translation

Miller & Cohen 2001 provides the mechanistic vocabulary for ARC-016: PFC does not select actions directly but biases downstream circuits toward context-relevant response mappings. In REE, 'downstream circuits' include the hippocampal trajectory store. When PFC is in a goal-state, its bias signal shifts hippocampal retrieval toward trajectories that serve that goal. This is exactly the 'frontal cue -> hippocampal pattern completion -> sequence retrieval' circuit that Topic 3 requests. The subsequent Jadhav work provides the specific hippocampal evidence; Miller & Cohen provide the PFC side of the story.

## Limitations

The 2001 formulation emphasises sensory-motor routing through cortical pathways; hippocampal trajectory retrieval requires extending the model by one synaptic step. The NHP working memory tasks used are much simpler than naturalistic navigation.
