# Increasing muscle activity correlations during spontaneous movements in the first six months of life
**Dolinskaya et al. (2021) -- Neuroscience Letters**

## What the paper did

Dolinskaya, Solopova, Zhvansky, Keshishian, and Ivanenko recorded surface EMG from 12 muscles (upper and lower limb, bilateral) in 40 full-term healthy Russian infants at intervals from 1 week to 6 months of age. They measured the correlation structure of ipsilateral and contralateral limb muscle activity during spontaneous movement and during passive leg movement paradigms, treating this as an index of neuromuscular network maturation.

## Key findings

A progressive, age-linked increase in ipsilateral and contralateral limb muscle activity correlations across both flexors and extensors. The increase was gradual, not abrupt. Correlations between arm and leg muscle responses also increased during passive leg movements. The authors interpret this as the emergence of coordinative neuromuscular behaviour underlying the transition from spontaneous-like to voluntary goal-directed movement.

## REE translation

This paper documents the structural scaffolding underlying the random-to-structured transition. Structure does not appear de novo at the onset of goal-directed behavior -- it accumulates continuously during the spontaneous phase. The inter-limb correlation gradient is an observable index of this accumulation.

For REE's infant substrate, this suggests that a metric of cross-module prediction consistency would be an appropriate developmental progress marker. An agent in which E1 predictions for one action class are highly correlated with those for another class has begun to build the coordinative scaffold; an agent where all action-class predictions are uncorrelated is still in the noise phase. Tracking latent-prediction cross-correlation is a concrete infant-stage progress metric.

## Limitations and caveats

The study is purely observational; the correlation increase could reflect pure maturational processes (myelination, synaptic pruning) with no causal role for sensorimotor experience. Cross-limb coupling in the neuromuscular system is mechanistically different from cross-module representational coupling in a neural network, so the transfer requires a conceptual bridge.

## Confidence reasoning

Solid EMG study with 40 infants and a longitudinal design. Neuroscience Letters is not top-tier but the methodology is sound. Confidence 0.75, reflecting moderate source quality and the moderate conceptual gap between neuromuscular and REE latent-space coordination.
