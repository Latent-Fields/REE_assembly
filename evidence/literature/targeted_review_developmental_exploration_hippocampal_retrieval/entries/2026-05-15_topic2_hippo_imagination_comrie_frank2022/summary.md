# Imagination as a fundamental function of the hippocampus
**Comrie, Frank & Kay (2022), Phil Trans R Soc B** | DOI: 10.1098/rstb.2021.0336

## What the paper did

Review arguing that the fundamental function of the hippocampus is generating hypothetical experiences and thoughts -- a broader framing than either spatial mapping or episodic memory retrieval. The paper surveys rodent and human hippocampal firing data documenting sequences corresponding to paths not taken, locations not occupied, and scenarios with no overt reference to past or future.

## Key findings

Hippocampal firing patterns regularly correspond to alternatives to actual experience during active behaviour, during rest/sleep, and at decision points. These hypothetical-experience events go beyond replay of actual paths: they include novel path combinations, remote location coding, and content-free sequence generation. The review proposes that episodic memory and spatial navigation are special cases of a more general imagination system.

## REE translation

The imagination framing is the ARC-007 claim stated at the level of the underlying functional architecture. If hippocampus generates hypothetical trajectories that need not reference actual past experiences, it functions as a cue-queryable option space. This supports the claim that the trajectory library is not a fixed archive of experienced paths but a compositional generator that can produce novel combinations -- which is what enables novel-combination planning without requiring a new path to have been previously experienced.

## Limitations

Many imagination-event firing patterns may be epiphenomenal; functional role in guiding specific choices not always established. The cue-trigger specificity (which PFC input triggers which imagination sequence) is not addressed.
