# Dynamics of Awake Hippocampal-Prefrontal Replay for Spatial Learning and Memory-Guided Decision Making
**Shin, Tang & Jadhav (2019), Neuron** | DOI: 10.1016/j.neuron.2019.09.012

## What the paper did

Simultaneous recording from 200+ neurons in hippocampal CA1 and mPFC in rats during learning of a spatial alternation task. The study tracked awake hippocampal replay (sharp-wave ripple-associated sequences during immobility pauses between laps) continuously throughout the entire learning trajectory.

## Key findings

During pauses, hippocampal replay performs an internal search of available past and future paths. Crucially: reverse replay (evaluating past paths) and forward replay (planning future paths) have opposing learning gradients -- reverse replay peaks early in learning, forward replay peaks as the animal becomes proficient. Coordinated hippocampal-prefrontal replay during these pauses specifically distinguished correct future paths from alternative choices, suggesting that PFC reads out hippocampal trajectory content to guide prospective planning.

Quantitative: by the end of learning, forward replay of future paths accounted for the majority of SWR events at the decision point, and PFC theta coherence during forward replay predicted subsequent correct choice.

## REE translation

This is the most direct experimental evidence available for the ARC-007 claim in a decision-making context. The forward replay events are precisely 'hippocampus retrieving a trajectory template toward a remembered goal' -- and the prefrontal theta coherence result supports the claim that PFC is reading hippocampal sequence content to guide the upcoming action. The learning shift from reverse to forward replay also mirrors the REE expectation: early learning is error-corrective (retrospective), late learning is plan-guided (prospective).

## Limitations

Spatial alternation task constrains trajectory space; open-field generalisation (as in Pfeiffer & Foster 2013) is an extension. The causal role of PFC readout in guiding choices is correlational.

## Confidence reasoning

Neuron 2019; in-vivo electrophysiology; high mapping fidelity to the cue-indexed retrieval model. 0.84.
