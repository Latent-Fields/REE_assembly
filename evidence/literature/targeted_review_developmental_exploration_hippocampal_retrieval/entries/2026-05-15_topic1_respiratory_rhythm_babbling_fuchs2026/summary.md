# Toward a Fuller Integration of Respiratory Rhythms Into Research on Infant Vocal and Motor Development
**Fuchs et al. (2026), Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences** | DOI: 10.1111/nyas.70197

## What the paper did

Review proposing that respiration functions as the temporal scaffold integrating vocalisation and bodily movement during infant development. During canonical babbling, repetitive adult-like syllables emerge from rhythmic motor actions that require co-ordinated respiratory-motor-vocal alignment.

## Key findings

Canonical babbling is a multimodal milestone: it reflects three-way coordination (motor, respiratory, vocal), not just vocal maturation. The respiratory rhythm provides the temporal framework within which articulation and motor actions progressively align. The advent of canonical babbling marks the point at which this three-way coordination is sufficiently developed for adult-like syllable production.

## REE translation

The three-way coordination argument implies that the exploration epoch must be broad enough to allow cross-system calibration -- piecemeal exploration of one subsystem at a time leaves the multi-modal representation incomplete. In REE, this maps to the requirement that behavioral option exploration must be sufficiently diverse to populate the trajectory store with options that combine multiple action types, not just single-modality actions.

## Limitations

Perspective/review; mechanistic claims are proposals awaiting primary data. Specific to vocal system; transfer to spatial motor systems requires inference.
