# Chronic administration of clozapine alleviates reversal-learning impairment in isolation-reared rats
**Li, Wu & Li (2007), Behavioural Pharmacology** | DOI: 10.1097/FBP.0b013e3280d3ee83

## What the paper did

Post-weaning isolation-reared and socially-reared adult rats were tested on visual discrimination acquisition and reversal learning in a rotating T-maze. The authors additionally tested whether chronic clozapine administration (5 or 10 mg/kg) could remediate any deficit in isolation-reared animals.

## Key findings

Isolation-reared rats performed significantly worse than socially-reared controls on reversal learning, specifically in the new-strategy acquisition phase. Initial acquisition of the visual discrimination was unimpaired -- the isolation-reared animals could learn a strategy, they simply could not update it. Clozapine (an antipsychotic with D4 and 5HT2A antagonism) significantly improved the reversal learning in isolation-reared animals, specifically in the new-strategy phase, not the extinction/inhibition phase.

## REE translation

This is the behavioral signature of monostrategy: the isolation-reared animal acquires one behavioral option but cannot switch to another when contingencies change. The developmental impoverishment (insufficient exploration during the critical post-weaning window) produces a permanently restricted behavioral repertoire in adulthood. For ARC-065, this is the negative-space prediction: when the developmental exploration epoch is insufficient, the outcome is not merely reduced behavioral diversity but behavioral rigidity -- the existing option is overused and the new option cannot be retrieved.

The clozapine remediation suggests the mechanism involves prefrontal dopamine (D4 receptors are highly PFC-concentrated), which maps back to Miller & Cohen's claim that PFC bias signals are required for context-appropriate strategy selection.

## Limitations

Social isolation confounds social and environmental exploration deprivation. The locus of the deficit (hippocampal trajectory store? PFC dopamine? neither?) is not established -- the pharmacology points to dopamine/PFC but does not exclude a hippocampal mechanism. Small sample size typical of pharmacological behavioural studies.
