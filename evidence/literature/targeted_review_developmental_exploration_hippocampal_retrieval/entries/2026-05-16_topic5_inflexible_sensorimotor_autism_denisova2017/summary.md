# Inflexible neurobiological signatures precede atypical development in infants at high risk for autism
**Denisova & Zhao (2017) -- Scientific Reports**

## What the paper did

Denisova and Zhao at Columbia University used Motion Capture to record spontaneous head movements in 71 infants (high-risk [HR] and low-risk [LR] for ASD by familial history) at 1-2 months of age, measuring movement during language listening and sleep conditions. The central analysis compared within-condition variability with between-condition variability (the context-flexibility index). They validated their main findings against a cohort of 1,445 infants from representative infant-sibling studies.

## Key findings

The critical finding is a dissociation: it is not raw variability level that predicts atypical developmental trajectory -- it is context-inflexibility. Healthy LR infants show significantly different movement patterns during language listening versus sleep at 1-2 months. HR infants who showed delayed development had movements that were similar across both conditions -- not lower variability, but context-invariant variability. This context-inflexibility at 1-2 months predicted delayed developmental milestones through toddlerhood.

## REE translation

This paper makes a precise and REE-relevant distinction: the pathological failure mode is not entropy collapse but context-rigidity. An agent that produces the same distribution of actions regardless of context is behaviourally impaired even if its action entropy is locally high.

This directly informs REE's infant substrate diversity criteria. The Denisova finding suggests that REE's Rung 2 (context-sensitive switching) is not a later luxury but a prerequisite for healthy development from the very start. An infant REE substrate that does not modulate its exploration amplitude in response to contextual signals would be replicating the ASD-risk pattern.

The practical corollary: the environmental context signal in the REE infant substrate must be active and legible to the exploration mechanism from the earliest stages. Simply injecting noise into the CEM will not produce context-appropriate variability.

## Limitations and caveats

The HR group does not uniformly develop ASD; causal inference is limited. Head movement may not generalize to limb or locomotor variability patterns. The ASD pathway may be atypical in ways specific to social development, potentially limiting transfer to a non-social agent architecture.

## Confidence reasoning

Scientific Reports with 71-infant primary cohort validated against 1,445 infants. The effect is robust and the distinction between context-inflexibility and raw variability deficit is conceptually important. Confidence 0.79 -- moderate deduction for the ASD-specificity caveat and the head-movement proxy.
