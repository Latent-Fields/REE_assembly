# The role of replay and theta sequences in mediating hippocampal-prefrontal interactions for memory and cognition
**Zielinski, Tang & Jadhav (2018), Hippocampus** | DOI: 10.1002/hipo.22821

## What the paper did

Review paper from the Jadhav lab explicitly framing hippocampal-prefrontal interactions as the mechanism for context-dependent memory retrieval and behavioral flexibility. Analyses the complementary and overlapping roles of awake SWR replay and theta sequences across different stages of learning (encoding, consolidation, retrieval, planning).

## Key findings

During early learning, awake reverse replay (retrospective evaluation) is dominant; during late learning, forward replay (prospective planning) takes over -- a learning-stage dissociation that mirrors the retrieval requirements. PFC reads hippocampal sequence content: coordinated hippocampal-prefrontal SWR activity during inter-trial pauses predicts upcoming correct choices. Theta sequences provide a fast deliberation mechanism at decision points, encoding alternatives for comparison within the hippocampus and broadcasting the selected option to PFC.

The paper explicitly proposes that PFC provides context that gates which hippocampal sequences are replayed -- though this claim is flagged as requiring closed-loop causal evidence.

## REE translation

This review provides the most complete picture of the hippocampal-PFC interaction that ARC-007, ARC-016, and MECH-025 collectively require. The forward replay / theta sequence dissociation maps directly onto the REE planning horizon: theta sequences handle local deliberation (which immediate step), forward SWR replay handles longer trajectory retrieval (which full path to the goal). The PFC-gating hypothesis is the cue-differentiation requirement: different PFC context states produce different hippocampal replay content, so behavioral diversity at inference time depends on which PFC cues are active.

## Limitations

The 'PFC gates hippocampal replay content' claim is a proposal awaiting closed-loop intervention. The readout mechanism from hippocampal sequences to PFC action selection is not established.
