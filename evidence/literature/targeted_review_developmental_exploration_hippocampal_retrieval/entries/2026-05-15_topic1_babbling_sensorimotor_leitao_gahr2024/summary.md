# Babbling opens the sensory phase for imitative vocal learning
**Leitao & Gahr (2024), PNAS** | DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2312323121

## What the paper did

Leitao and Gahr administered testosterone to juvenile zebra finches ~16 days post-hatch to induce premature motor vocal activity, asking whether premature babbling altered the sensory learning phase. The traditional model held that sensory template acquisition precedes babbling onset.

## Key finding

Premature babbling triggered the onset of the sensory phase rather than merely initiating the sensorimotor phase. Motor vocal exploration is causally upstream of perceptual template encoding: birds that babbled early memorized the tutor song earlier and more faithfully. The motor exploration epoch cannot be bypassed or passively substituted.

## REE translation

The ARC-065 relevance is direct: an agent that does not execute diverse behavioral candidates during an early developmental window fails to form the sensory context-templates that later serve as retrieval cues for hippocampal trajectories. Babbling is not noise -- it opens the perceptual learning window. This makes the 'sufficiently long/broad exploration epoch' requirement mechanistically precise.

## Limitations

Highly songbird-specific neural circuit; transfer to limb-based or spatial motor exploration in mammals is plausible but not established. Critical period biology may differ.
