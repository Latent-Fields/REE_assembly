# Multiple time-scales of decision-making in the hippocampus and prefrontal cortex
**Tang, Shin & Jadhav (2021), eLife** | DOI: 10.7554/eLife.66227

## What the paper did

Simultaneous hippocampal-prefrontal ensemble recording during spatial working memory. The study explicitly characterised theta sequences (the fast ~100-200ms sweeps of place cell sequences within individual theta cycles) in both structures and asked how they coordinate during deliberation and navigation.

## Key findings

PFC exhibits theta sequences nested within its slower behavioural sequences -- the first demonstration of theta-timescale prospective coding in PFC. Hippocampal theta sequences at decision points encoded *alternatives* for deliberation (not just the current path), and were coordinated with PFC theta sequences that predicted upcoming choices. This coordination was present even on error trials -- the hippocampal alternative-encoding and PFC prediction were preserved -- but awake SWR replay during inter-trial periods was impaired before error trials relative to correct ones.

## REE translation

The theta-sequence result is the mechanistic resolution of the 'query-retrieval' picture at the fast timescale. The hippocampus sweeps through alternative trajectories within each theta cycle; PFC theta sequences simultaneously encode the predicted upcoming choice. This is the cue-differentiation mechanism at the neural implementation level: PFC provides a bias that steers which hippocampal theta sweep is selected. The inter-trial replay impairment before errors also supports the claim that retrieval quality (not just encoding) gates behavioral flexibility.

## Limitations

Causal direction (PFC -> hippocampus vs. hippocampus -> PFC) is unresolved from correlation. Theta timescale is sub-second; full trajectory retrieval for complex paths may require integration over multiple theta cycles.
