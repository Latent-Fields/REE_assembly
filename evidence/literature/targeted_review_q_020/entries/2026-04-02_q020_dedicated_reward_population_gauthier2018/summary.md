# Gauthier & Tank (2018) — A Dedicated Population for Reward Coding in the Hippocampus

**Claims tested:** Q-020, MECH-073, ARC-007
**Evidence direction:** weakens (ARC-007 strict no-value position) | **Confidence:** 0.79

---

## What the paper did

Gauthier and Tank developed a virtual reality task in which head-fixed mice ran on a treadmill through a linear virtual environment with a reward zone. The critical manipulation was to change the visual environment periodically — creating entirely new spatial contexts — while keeping the reward location stable (or moving it). This design dissociates place coding (which remaps across environments) from reward coding (which should be stable if a dedicated channel exists). They performed large-scale electrophysiological recordings across CA1 and subiculum simultaneously.

The central finding: most CA1 cells showed the expected pattern of complete remapping between environments — their place fields were at essentially random locations across contexts, consistent with existing literature. But a small, identifiable subpopulation did not remap. These cells fired near the reward zone across all environments tested. When reward contingencies were shifted (reward moved to a new location), most of this population's response shifted with it. The cells were not place cells; they were reward-zone cells. And they formed a stable, identifiable subpopulation that persisted across the complete remapping of the spatial map.

## Key findings relevant to Q-020

This is the most challenging paper for ARC-007 in the current Q-020 literature set. The existence of a *dedicated*, *stable*, *reward-selective* population inside CA1 and subiculum anatomy means hippocampal circuitry contains a functionally distinct value-coding channel that is not reducible to spatial map distortion. It is not that place cells are slightly biased toward reward zones (Dupret 2010); it is that a separate population exists *specifically for* reward zone encoding, maintained stably even as the entire spatial map reorganizes around it.

This is exactly the kind of finding that MECH-073 predicts: valence is not merely overlaid on a neutral structure, it is structurally integrated into hippocampal circuitry. Gauthier & Tank's reward-selective cells are the anatomical substrate of MECH-073's "reward and aversive cells within hippocampal circuitry" evidence anchor.

## REE translation and Q-020 implications

The Q-020 stakes are highest here. A dedicated reward channel inside hippocampal anatomy is difficult to reconcile with ARC-007's strict characterization of hippocampal function as "orthogonal to valuation and control." Three readings are available:

**Resolution A (preferred):** The reward-selective population is a stable anatomical relay for subcortical value signals — not a computational locus, but a labeled line carrying persistent BLA or VTA input. These cells compute nothing; they express a stable projection bias. The hippocampus in this reading stores geometry that includes a pre-wired value channel, written by external anatomy, not computed internally. This is structurally compatible with ARC-007's "no value *computation*" constraint as long as the constraint is read narrowly (no online evaluation) rather than broadly (no value-related structure whatsoever).

**Resolution B (strong):** The dedicated reward population is doing value computation — integrating task context, behavioral state, and reward history to produce a reward signal that influences hippocampal map organization downstream. This reading would require revising ARC-007 significantly.

**Resolution C (pragmatic):** The distinction between "relay of external value" and "value computation" is architecturally load-bearing in REE but biologically underspecified. REE should accept MECH-073 as describing a structural fact (value-relevant geometry exists in the hippocampus) while maintaining ARC-007's constraint on what the hippocampus is permitted to *do with* that geometry (no new value computation, no online evaluation).

The paper favors Resolution A/C over Resolution B: the cells track reward zone location but show no evidence of predictive or evaluative processing — they are simply present near reward. The computational work of evaluating trajectories is assigned to downstream structures by the authors.

## Limitations

Head-fixed mouse virtual reality; spatial task with discrete reward zones. The paper does not characterize the input projections to the reward-selective cells — whether they receive direct BLA or VTA input (Resolution A) or are driven by local hippocampal computation (Resolution B) is not established. The subiculum component is significant: subiculum is a primary hippocampal output stage and receives strong amygdala input, so reward-selective subiculum cells may reflect output-projection labeling rather than hippocampal computation per se.
