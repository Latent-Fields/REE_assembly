# Dupret, O'Neill, Pleydell-Bouverie & Csicsvari (2010) — Reward Reorganizes the Hippocampal Map

**Claims tested:** Q-020, MECH-073, ARC-007
**Evidence direction:** weakens (ARC-007 strict no-value position) | **Confidence:** 0.74

---

## What the paper did

Dupret and colleagues recorded from bilateral CA1 tetrode arrays in freely-moving rats learning a reward-location task in an open field. The experimental design was elegant in its simplicity: rats had to find a hidden reward at a fixed location within a familiar environment. Before and after learning, place cell activity was recorded. During rest periods after successful learning, sharp wave-ripple activity was captured. The question was whether the hippocampal map reorganized around the reward location, and whether that reorganization mattered for subsequent memory performance.

It did. Place fields were not randomly distributed before and after learning. After reward-location learning, the proportion of place fields centered on or near the reward location increased substantially — the hippocampal map geometrically over-represented the rewarded zone. Furthermore, the extent of SWR reactivation of the remapped (reward-adjacent) place fields during post-task rest was the single best predictor of subsequent spatial memory performance measured the following day.

## Key findings relevant to Q-020

This paper is direct evidence for MECH-073: valence is intrinsic to hippocampal map geometry. The map does not merely record spatial experience — it reorganizes its geometric structure around locations that carry behavioral relevance. A place field near the reward location is not just a spatial representation; it is geometrically amplified by reward history. The map is warped by value.

The SWR reactivation finding adds a second dimension: the reward-reorganized geometry is then selectively consolidated via SWR. This means that not only does the map encode value geometrically, but the offline replay mechanism (MECH-092 in REE) preferentially reinstates the value-shaped geometry. These two findings together are the biological substrate of what MECH-073 is claiming.

## REE translation and Q-020 implications

For Q-020, this paper weakens ARC-007's strict no-value-computation position, but does not demolish it outright. The crucial ambiguity is mechanistic: does hippocampal circuitry actively compute reward (which would require value computation), or does it passively receive value signals from VTA/amygdala and store their geometric imprint?

The Dupret result is consistent with both readings. Resolution A (hippocampus embodies geometry written by external systems — amygdala as write head, VTA as novelty/salience gate) can accommodate the remapping: BLA→CA1 projections and VTA dopamine together could drive the plasticity that warps the map. The hippocampus in this reading is not computing reward; it is expressing the geometric result of plasticity events triggered by external value signals. The BTSP paper (Bittner et al. 2017) provides the mechanism for this write process.

This paper should be read as establishing *what the output looks like* (value-warped geometry), while leaving open the question of *who is doing the warping*. That question — the Q-020 crux — requires the circuit anatomy (amygdala projections, VTA loop) and the plasticity mechanism (BTSP) to resolve.

## Limitations

Rodent open-field spatial task; the mapping to abstract non-spatial domains (REE's z_world terrain) requires inference. The paper uses tetrode recordings from dorsal CA1 only — ventral CA1, which is more strongly connected to amygdala and more strongly implicated in valence encoding, was not recorded. The reward-zone over-representation effect is real but modest in magnitude. The replication record is strong.
