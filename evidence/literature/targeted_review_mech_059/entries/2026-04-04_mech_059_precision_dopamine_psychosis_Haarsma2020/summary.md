# Haarsma et al. (2020/2021) — Precision weighting, dopamine, and psychosis

**Citation:** Haarsma J et al. Precision weighting of cortical unsigned prediction error signals benefits learning, is mediated by dopamine, and is impaired in psychosis. *Mol Psychiatry*. 2021;26:5808-5818. DOI: 10.1038/s41380-020-0803-8. PMID: 32576965.

**Relevance to MECH-059:** Direct empirical evidence. Precision weighting and prediction error encoding are dissociable — one can be selectively disrupted while the other remains intact.

---

This study is notable for providing what amounts to a natural dissociation experiment. The research team manipulated dopaminergic tone pharmacologically in healthy volunteers (sulpiride, a D2 antagonist; bromocriptine, a D2 agonist; placebo) while participants performed a learning task with variable environmental reliability. They also scanned patients with first-episode psychosis and people with at-risk mental states. The result is a remarkably clean pattern: sulpiride impairs precision weighting of unsigned prediction errors in superior frontal cortex (F = 4.025, p = 0.023) without eliminating the prediction error signal itself. First-episode psychosis patients show the same selective impairment — worse precision weighting, intact error encoding.

The double dissociation logic here is the key contribution from the perspective of MECH-059. If precision weighting were simply a rescaling of prediction error magnitude, you could not pharmacologically target one while leaving the other intact. The two signals are computed by different molecular machinery, modulated by different aspects of the dopaminergic system, and their functional separation maps onto real-world consequences: when precision weighting is disrupted, learning degrades even though prediction errors are still being generated. The error signal is there; the system just cannot correctly assess how much to weight it.

Unsigned prediction errors — signals of surprise or mismatch without sign — were encoded in superior frontal cortex and dorsal anterior cingulate. Precision weighting scaled these signals according to environmental reliability, with better precision weighting associated with better task performance (Rho = -0.45, p < 0.001). Psychosis patients showed reduced precision-weighted responses correlating with symptom severity and schizotypy. The authors frame this as a disruption in the mechanism through which dopamine normally amplifies precise (reliable) prediction errors and attenuates imprecise (unreliable) ones.

For REE, this paper provides several things. First, it confirms the neuroscience consensus that precision and prediction error are separated at both the computational and neurochemical level. Second, it shows that the consequences of conflating them are not subtle — disrupted precision weighting produces a systematic learning deficit and, in extreme cases, psychosis-spectrum symptoms. This is consistent with the REE prediction that corrupting the confidence channel (treating z_beta as a transform of residual error) would impair the architecture's ability to calibrate commitment appropriately. The ARC-016 circuit validated in V2 (E3-derived variance driving commit threshold) is the REE implementation of the healthy precision weighting demonstrated here.
