# Tononi & Cirelli 2014 -- Sleep and the Price of Plasticity (SHY)

**Claim tested: MECH-120**
**Evidence direction: supports**

## What the paper claims

The Synaptic Homeostasis Hypothesis (SHY): waking Hebbian plasticity broadly strengthens synapses, increasing metabolic cost and saturating signal-to-noise. During NREM slow-wave sleep, spontaneous activity renormalises net synaptic strength via down-states, restoring cellular homeostasis and the capacity for subsequent learning. The paper frames SHY as the core function of sleep: it is the price the brain pays for waking plasticity. Without downscaling, runaway potentiation would degrade signal-to-noise and saturate the weight landscape.

## Mapping to MECH-120

MECH-120 claims that slow-wave sleep performs synaptic weight normalisation that prevents dominant high-salience experiences from monopolising the weight landscape. This is precisely SHY. Experiences that drove strong Hebbian potentiation during waking (high-salience, high-prediction-error events) will have more strongly potentiated their associated synapses. SHY-type proportional downscaling during NREM renormalises the weight landscape: dominant traces are reduced, less-potentiated traces are preserved, and the relative encoding of diverse experience is restored.

The MECH-120 failure mode -- replay into non-normalised weights reinforces dominant traces rather than consolidating diverse content -- is the direct prediction of SHY. Without downscaling, replay-driven re-potentiation preferentially strengthens already-dominant weights. In REE terms this produces the monostrategy pattern: the behavioral strategy associated with the highest-salience waking experience crowds out diverse alternatives.

## Key SHY mechanism

- Wake: Hebbian potentiation of synapses associated with experienced events
- NREM down-states: global synaptic downscaling (proportional, not random) renormalises net strength
- Result: restored signal-to-noise, restored LTP capacity, renewed ability to encode diverse content
- Downstream: normalised weight landscape is permissive for NREM replay (MECH-121) to write diverse schema content

## What the paper does not say

SHY is primarily a NREM theory. The paper explicitly acknowledges that REM sleep's relationship to SHY is an open question: "how REM sleep may or may not fit with the hypothesised core function of sleep, and whether the regular alternation of NREM/REM periods in most mammals and birds may serve a particular function." This is relevant to MECH-121 (NREM replay requires normalised weights) but the ordering constraint between MECH-120 and MECH-121 is implied, not stated.

## Evidence quality note

SHY is the most evidenced mechanistic account of NREM function, with ultrastructural evidence from serial electron microscopy in rodents, molecular evidence for sleep-dependent synaptic protein changes, and electrophysiological evidence for slow-wave-associated firing rate decreases. However, the completeness of the proof at the molecular level, and the question of selective vs. global downscaling (are ALL synapses downscaled, or are consolidated memories partially spared?), remain active research questions.
