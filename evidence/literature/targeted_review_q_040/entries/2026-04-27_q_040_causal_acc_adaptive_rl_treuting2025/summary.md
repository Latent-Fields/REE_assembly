# Treuting et al. 2025 -- Causal evidence that ACC supports adaptive RL specifically under uncertainty

Treuting and colleagues (Womelsdorf lab) published a causal study in Neuron in 2025 that does the perturbation work the field has wanted for a long time. Macaques performed a feature-based attention learning task at varying levels of feature uncertainty and motivational saliency. Brief, gaze-contingent electrical stimulation was delivered either to ACC or to striatum, and the effect on adaptive reinforcement learning was measured under each uncertainty regime. The key finding has two halves: (1) ACC stimulation impaired learning *specifically* under high feature uncertainty, while leaving low-uncertainty performance largely intact; (2) striatal stimulation under the same high-uncertainty conditions improved learning. The two structures are doing opposite things, and computational modelling localised the ACC effect to "optimising exploration and the use of prediction errors to reduce uncertainty" -- not to value updating per se.

The single-unit complement is consistent: ACC neurons fired more strongly during more uncertain choices and tracked error history, while striatal neurons fired more strongly during more certain, higher-value choices. ACC is an uncertainty-tracking consumer of PE; striatum is a value-updating consumer. This division of labour matters because Q-040 is asking specifically about the uncertainty-tracking consumer.

For REE, the translation is to SD-032b. The dACC adaptive-control output is, in this picture, an uncertainty-weighted prediction-error consumer. Q-040's architectural prediction is that if V_s gating only reaches the hippocampal proposer (current MECH-269 scope) and not the E1/E2 forward predictions, the uncertainty estimate that feeds into dACC is mis-shaped: E2 keeps grounding harm-rollouts on stale streams, the precision-weighted PE looks self-consistent, and ACC has no genuine uncertainty signal to act on. The Treuting result fits this story in a particular way -- it shows that disrupting ACC under uncertainty breaks the PE-to-policy-revision link, which is the exact link Q-040 says is currently broken in EXQ-483/483a even with all SD-032b machinery wired.

The mapping caveat is that Treuting et al. perturb the *consumer* (ACC), not the *upstream anchor* (analogous to V_s gating of E1/E2). The inference is that if ACC's uncertainty-weighted PE function can be broken downstream, then a mis-formed upstream uncertainty estimate would also break it -- different lever, same expected behavioural signature. This is a reasonable architectural inference but is not a direct test. The substrate gap is also real: macaque feature-attention task is very far from the REE harm-navigation substrate where Q-040 lives.
