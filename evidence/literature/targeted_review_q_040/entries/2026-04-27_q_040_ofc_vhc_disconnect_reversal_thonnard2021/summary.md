# Thonnard et al. 2021 -- OFC <-> hippocampus disconnection produces wired-but-inert reversal-learning deficits

Thonnard, Callaerts-Vegh, and D'Hooge ran a clean disconnection study in C57BL/6JRj mice. They produced excitotoxic lesions of orbitofrontal cortex and ventral hippocampus in either contralateral (cross-hemisphere) or ipsilateral (same-hemisphere) configurations. Contralateral disconnection breaks cross-hemispheric communication between OFC and ventral hippocampus while leaving each region individually functional. Ipsilateral disconnection disrupts each region within the same hemisphere but preserves the homologous regions in the other hemisphere. This factorial lets them ask whether OFC and hippocampus need to be in functional contact, or merely both intact, for behavioural flexibility.

The result is exactly what Q-040 needs. Spatial acquisition was preserved across all lesion conditions. Unilateral OFC lesion alone did not impair reversal learning. Unilateral ventral hippocampus lesion alone did not impair reversal learning. Contralaterally lesioned mice -- where OFC and ventral hippocampus could no longer communicate via cross-hemispheric routes -- were significantly impaired during the early phase of reversal. The deficit was specific to flexibility; spatial mapping per se was intact. The two regions had to be in functional contact for behavioural revision to fire.

For REE, this is the cleanest causal analogue I have found for the wired-but-inert architectural pattern Q-040 articulates. The hippocampus carries upstream state/anchor information; OFC consumes that information to revise value estimates and drive behaviour. With each region individually intact but the upstream-to-downstream pathway cut, the downstream consumer cannot revise behaviour -- not because the consumer is broken, but because the upstream input is no longer arriving in a usable form. Q-040 says exactly this about V3: SD-032b is wired, MECH-269 is wired for the proposer, but if V_s gating does not reach E1/E2 forward predictions (MECH-269b scope), the architectural equivalent of the cross-hemispheric pathway is functionally cut. The override fires, PAG releases up, dACC is in principle ready, but approach_commit stays at zero. Thonnard et al. show the exact behavioural signature in mice.

The mapping caveat is twofold. First, OFC is not dACC. OFC is more naturally implicated in value flexibility and stimulus-outcome remapping; dACC is more naturally implicated in conflict, error-likelihood, and adaptive control. Both are downstream consumers of upstream state, but mapping the OFC <-> hippocampus pathway onto the dACC <-> V_s-gated-E1/E2 pathway is an architectural inference rather than a direct homology. Second, rodent spatial reversal is not REE harm-navigation. The substrate gap is real even though the architectural logic is the same.

Confidence is 0.70. Source quality is solid (peer-reviewed, well-controlled disconnection design with appropriate unilateral and ipsilateral controls). Mapping fidelity is the highest of the four entries because the experimental signature -- intact components, broken pathway, intact basal performance, broken flexibility -- is exactly what Q-040 predicts will be observed when MECH-269b is OFF and SD-032b is otherwise wired.
