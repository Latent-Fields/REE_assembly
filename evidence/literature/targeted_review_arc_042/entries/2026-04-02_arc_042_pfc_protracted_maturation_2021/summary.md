# Chini & Hangya (2021) — Development of Prefrontal Cortex

## What the paper does

This review covers the developmental trajectory of the prefrontal cortex from prenatal neurogenesis through adolescent maturation to adult function. Key findings: PFC neurons are generated before birth, but synaptic connections develop over decades. Synapses and neurotransmitter systems are initially overproduced then selectively pruned. Functional connectivity between PFC and other regions (hippocampus, amygdala) continues developing into the mid-20s. Higher cognitive functions — decision-making, behavioral inhibition, planning — emerge gradually as circuits mature.

## Relevance to ARC-042

ARC-042 claims that E3's ethical selection machinery is architecturally present from initialization but functionally dark until E1 and E2 substrates develop. This maps directly onto the neurodevelopmental finding: PFC circuits for ethical evaluation (vmPFC, OFC) are structurally present early in development but only become functionally effective after prolonged maturation of upstream systems.

The staged dependency in REE — E1 ContextMemory requires supervised training (Phase 1), E2 needs E1 informative priors (Phase 2), E3 ethical selection can only amplify signal that exists (Phase 3) — parallels the biological observation that complex PFC functions depend on the prior maturation of sensory processing, memory consolidation, and emotional regulation circuits.

## The vulnerability parallel

The review notes that the protracted developmental window makes PFC vulnerable to disruption — early life stress can alter trajectory. In REE, this has an interesting parallel: if E1 training goes wrong (e.g., EXQ-181b showed unsupervised training is insufficient), E3 ethical selection has nothing to work with. The system is vulnerable to developmental failure in upstream components, just as biological PFC function is vulnerable to disrupted input from immature subcortical systems.
