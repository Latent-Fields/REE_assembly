# Craig (2009) — The Anterior Insula and the Posterior-to-Anterior Interoceptive Gradient

## What the paper does

This is Craig's landmark review (Nature Reviews Neuroscience, 6000+ citations) establishing the anterior insular cortex as a neural correlate of awareness. It synthesises converging evidence from human neuroimaging, intracranial recordings, and lesion studies to propose that the AIC contains a meta-representation of the body's physiological state — a "global emotional moment" that integrates interoceptive signals into subjective awareness.

The crucial move for SD-011 is the posterior-to-anterior gradient Craig documents within the insular cortex itself.

## Key findings relevant to SD-011

1. **Posterior insula (PIC)**: Contains a primary interoceptive representation — "distinct highly resolved feelings from the body that include pain, temperature, itch, sensual touch, muscular and visceral sensations, vasomotor activity, hunger, thirst, and air hunger." This is a high-resolution, modality-specific, somatotopically organised map. For nociception, PIC encodes stimulus location, intensity, and quality with minimal temporal integration.

2. **Anterior insula (AIC)**: Re-represents the primary interoceptive activity as a "subjective image of the material self as a feeling (sentient) entity." This is not a smoothed version of the PIC signal but a qualitatively different representation — integrated across modalities, temporally extended, and contextualised by cognitive and motivational state. The AIC contains von Economo neurons, large projection neurons connecting to ACC and frontal areas, enabling rapid broadcast of integrated state information.

3. **The gradient is functional, not just anatomical**: Craig documents that the mid-insula serves as a transition zone, and that information flows from posterior (sensory-discriminative) to anterior (affective-integrative) with progressive abstraction at each stage.

## Translation to REE

This maps onto SD-011 with striking directness. The posterior insula's primary interoceptive representation is z_harm_s: immediate, high-resolution, forward-predictable harm proximity/intensity. The anterior insula's meta-representation is z_harm_a: accumulated, integrated, temporally extended homeostatic deviation. The step from PIC to AIC is not merely temporal smoothing (an EMA) — it is a qualitative transformation from resolved sensation to integrated body-state. This is exactly the architectural distinction SD-011 claims: z_harm_a carries fundamentally different information than z_harm_s, not just a low-passed version of the same signal.

The von Economo neuron projection from AIC to ACC provides the anatomical substrate for z_harm_a feeding directly into E3's commit gating (via the ACC's role in action evaluation and conflict monitoring). The PIC's somatotopic organisation provides the substrate for z_harm_s feeding into E2_harm_s forward model (precise, spatially resolved, action-contingent).

## Limitations and caveats

Craig 2009 is about interoception and awareness broadly — pain is one of several interoceptive modalities discussed, and the AIC's function is framed as subjective awareness rather than motivational urgency. The step from "awareness substrate" to "motivational drive input for E3 commit gating" requires additional mechanistic argument that Craig does not provide. The review also does not address nociceptive-specific dual-stream anatomy (Adelta vs. C-fiber pathways) — it works at the cortical level, not the spinal/brainstem level where the two streams originate.

Craig 2002 and 2003 (already in this review series) are more directly about interoception as homeostatic afference. This 2009 paper adds the meta-representation and awareness dimensions, which strengthen the case that z_harm_a is qualitatively distinct from z_harm_s but also introduce broader philosophical claims about consciousness that go beyond what SD-011 requires.

## Confidence reasoning

Exceptionally high source quality — Craig is the authority on insular interoception, and this is his most cited work. Mapping fidelity is good but not perfect: the posterior/anterior gradient is compelling for SD-011, but the AIC function (awareness) is broader than z_harm_a's function (motivational urgency). The transfer risk is low given this is directly about human pain/interoception neuroanatomy.
