# Summary: Salamone & Correa 2012 -- The Mysterious Motivational Functions of Mesolimbic Dopamine

**Claim tested:** ARC-030 (symmetric Go/NoGo sub-channels required; pure NoGo produces behavioral flatness)

**Source:** Salamone JD, Correa M. "The mysterious motivational functions of mesolimbic dopamine." Neuron 2012;76(3):470-85. DOI: 10.1016/j.neuron.2012.10.021

## What they did

Comprehensive review synthesizing decades of lesion, pharmacological, and behavioral-economic studies of nucleus accumbens dopamine and motivation. Key animal models: rats with accumbens 6-OHDA lesions or dopamine antagonist infusions tested on effort-choice tasks (concurrent chow vs. high-effort high-reward lever-pressing; T-maze with barrier; progressive ratio schedules).

## Key findings

- Accumbens DA depletion selectively impairs effortful approach (cost-based decisions) while leaving basic food intake and hedonic liking intact.
- DA is required for behavioral activation, exertion of effort, and approach behavior -- not for primary appetite or pleasure per se.
- DA participates in both appetitive and aversive motivational processes but is specifically critical for the Go/approach channel.
- Without accumbens DA, animals prefer low-effort low-reward options even when they previously preferred high-effort high-reward -- this is a selective approach-activation deficit, not a reward-value deficit.

## REE relevance

This review provides the behavioral-economic evidence that the Go (D1/direct) channel is specifically required for effortful approach. ARC-030 claims that the BG loops require both Go and NoGo sub-channels. This paper shows what happens when only avoidance signaling (NoGo) remains active without adequate Go-channel drive: the agent becomes behaviorally flat with respect to effortful approach goals while retaining aversive sensitivity. The behavioral profile matches precisely what ARC-030 predicts as the failure mode of pure-NoGo architecture.

The effort-choice framework is also directly relevant to the REE benefit_ratio computation: Go channel activation is what energizes the agent to pursue z_goal against cost, producing the benefit_ratio lift. Without it, the benefit_ratio cannot rise above the cost baseline.

## Limitations

This review discusses mesolimbic DA broadly rather than D1 vs. D2 sub-channels specifically. Pharmacological DA depletion affects both D1 and D2 targets. The mapping from accumbens DA to the specific Go/D1 sub-channel of ARC-030 requires an additional inferential step (covered by Kravitz 2010/2012).
