# Central neuroplasticity and pathological pain
**Melzack, R., Coderre, T. J., Katz, J., & Vaccarino, A. L. (2001). *Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences*, 933, 157-174.**
DOI: [10.1111/j.1749-6632.2001.tb05822.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1749-6632.2001.tb05822.x)

## What the study did

This review by Ron Melzack (co-originator of gate control theory) synthesises evidence that the CNS does not passively relay nociceptive signals -- it actively transforms them through processes of sensitisation, wind-up, and cortical reorganisation that can be induced by peripheral injury and can persist long after the peripheral source is removed or blocked. The review covers three converging lines of evidence: (1) spinal wind-up and central sensitisation induced by C-fiber repetitive stimulation (NMDA-receptor-dependent, producing long-lasting hyperexcitability); (2) phantom limb pain as the clearest human demonstration of pain persisting entirely within the CNS after peripheral deafferentation; (3) preemptive analgesia, showing that blocking the afferent barrage at the time of injury can prevent later central sensitisation from developing -- proving the central state is written by injury experience and persists as a CNS memory.

## Key findings

The central argument is that "the perception of pain does not simply involve a moment-to-moment analysis of afferent noxious input, but rather involves a dynamic process that is influenced by the effects of past experiences." Persistent pain in phantom limb patients is not generated by ongoing peripheral signals (which cannot exist from an absent limb) but by central circuits that were sensitised during the original injury period and continue to generate pain-representing activity autonomously. Wind-up -- the progressive increase in dorsal horn neuron firing to repeated C-fiber inputs -- is the canonical mechanism for how temporal accumulation of nociceptive signals leads to a state change that outlasts the input. In chronic pain states, these central changes mean that mild or absent peripheral stimuli can produce full pain percepts, demonstrating the decoupling of current afferent input from current pain experience.

## Relevance to SD-022

SD-022 is explicitly designed to produce a form of exactly this dissociation in simulation: an agent that has accumulated limb_damage (from traversing hazards) maintains elevated z_harm_a even after leaving the hazard zone, where z_harm_s returns to near-zero. Melzack 2001 provides the biological proof of principle that such a dissociation is not only possible but is a well-documented property of biological nociceptive systems. The phantom limb analogy is direct: the absent limb corresponds to the absent hazard, and the persistent phantom pain corresponds to the elevated z_harm_a retention measured in EXQ-319. The experimental evidence for preemptive analgesia further suggests that the key property is the injury-time accumulation of afferent load -- precisely what limb_damage encodes in CausalGridWorldV2.

## Limitations and caveats

The timescale mismatch is the primary proxy risk here. Phantom limb pain and central sensitisation develop over days to months and can persist for years. The SD-022 model heals at 0.002/step, meaning even severe limb damage is largely resolved within 500-1000 training steps. Whether this is sufficient to produce the representational persistence that makes phantom limb pain informative is empirically uncertain -- it is part of what EXQ-319 was designed to test. More fundamentally, the biological mechanisms involve NMDA-receptor potentiation, glial activation, and cortical map reorganisation -- none of which are explicitly modelled in REE. The SD-022 substrate uses an explicit damage register that decays geometrically, which is a behavioural proxy for the computational consequence (harm signal persists after the hazard is gone) without implementing the underlying mechanism. This is acceptable engineering but should not be taken as a mechanistic validation of the biological pathway.

## Confidence reasoning

Melzack 2001 is among the highest-authority papers available for the biological principle that SD-022 instantiates. The mapping confidence is somewhat attenuated by the timescale and mechanism gaps described above, but the conceptual-level validation -- that a body-state harm signal can remain elevated in the absence of a current peripheral stimulus, through a plausible biological mechanism -- is robustly supported. Confidence is set at 0.81 reflecting high source quality and good conceptual mapping against moderate proxy risk.
