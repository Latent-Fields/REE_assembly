# Summary: Geuter, Boll, Eippert and Buchel (2017) -- Functional dissociation of stimulus intensity encoding and predictive coding of pain in the insula

**Entry:** 2026-04-09_sd_020_anterior_posterior_insula_pe_geuter2017
**Claim tested:** SD-020
**Evidence direction:** supports

## What the study did

Geuter and colleagues used a probabilistic heat pain paradigm in 30 healthy adults to ask whether pain responses in the insula follow the predictions of a predictive coding model or a simple intensity-encoding model. Participants received painful thermal stimuli preceded by probabilistic cues that predicted either high or low pain intensity with varying reliability. fMRI BOLD responses were decomposed using a model that separates expectation signals (cue-locked) from prediction error signals (stimulus-locked, proportional to outcome minus prediction). Skin conductance and pupil dilation were simultaneously recorded as peripheral autonomic indices of prediction error.

## Key findings

The anterior insula showed response patterns consistent with the predictive coding model: activity reflected the sum of expectation and PE rather than raw stimulus intensity. Posterior insula, by contrast, encoded stimulus intensity irrespective of expectation context. Skin conductance and pupil dilation followed the same predictive coding pattern as anterior insula -- an important convergent finding, as it connects the fMRI signal to physiological arousal responses that are not confounded by motor responses. The authors interpret this dissociation as suggesting that anterior insula implements inference (integrating prior expectations with incoming harm signals), while posterior insula provides the raw nociceptive data on which that inference operates.

## REE translation

This paper is the clearest pre-Chen fMRI evidence for SD-020. z_harm_a is anchored in anterior insula, and this study shows that anterior insula encodes PE (surprise), not raw harm magnitude. The SD-020 redesign -- replacing the raw harm accumulation training target with a PE-based target -- directly implements what anterior insula actually computes. The posterior insula finding is equally important for REE: it confirms the z_harm_s vs z_harm_a distinction (SD-011), with posterior insula as z_harm_s (raw nociceptive intensity) and anterior insula as z_harm_a (precision-weighted PE). A single paper thus supports both SD-011 and SD-020 simultaneously.

## Limitations and caveats

The PE signal is constructed indirectly via cue reliability and outcome rather than from explicit prediction measurements. Skin conductance and pupil dilation serve as PE proxies rather than independent PE measurements, which introduces circularity risk if these autonomic signals are themselves modulated by expectation. The sample is 30 adults -- adequate but not large by contemporary standards. Transfer to REE requires assuming that the anterior insula PE response generalises beyond heat pain to the broader harm signals REE encounters, which subsequent cross-modal work (Horing 2022) provides.
