# Malik, Galang & Finger (2022) — The sense of agency for brain disorders: a comprehensive review and proposed framework

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neubiorev.2022.104759); PMID 35780975).

## What the paper did

A comprehensive integrative review of sense-of-agency (SoA) findings across schizophrenia, autism spectrum disorders, obsessive-compulsive disorder, and cortico-basal syndrome. The authors note that despite extensive empirical work, no robust treatment for aberrant agency exists, in their view because the literature has not been adequately synthesised. They propose the Agency in Brain Disorders Framework (ABDF), which crosses prospective (pre-action) versus retrospective (post-action) agency cues with top-down versus bottom-up information sources. Different disorders disturb different cells of this matrix.

## Key findings (theoretical)

The framework's main commitments: agency is built compositionally from at least four sources (prospective top-down, prospective bottom-up, retrospective top-down, retrospective bottom-up). Schizophrenia disrupts retrospective top-down inference (over-attribution of internally generated thoughts to external agents). OCD shows hyperactive prospective top-down monitoring with preserved retrospective binding. ASD has reduced multisensory binding affecting retrospective bottom-up cues. Cortico-basal syndrome shows the alien-hand pattern where motor commands disconnect from agency representations.

## Mapping to MECH-025b

This review is most useful as a refinement constraint on MECH-025b rather than as direct confirmation. MECH-025b in its current form proposes a single bridge: action precision determines responsibility attribution. Malik's framework forces a more careful version of that bridge — at minimum, it has to be specified whether the precision-responsibility coupling lives at the prospective stage (planning, action selection, comparator-model formation) or the retrospective stage (outcome monitoring, comparator-error processing) or both. The disorder dissociations Malik catalogues suggest these can come apart: OCD looks like prospective-precision hyperactivity with intact retrospective binding, schizophrenia looks like the inverse.

For REE, the practical implication is that MECH-025b's bridge should be modelled at a particular pipeline stage, not as a global property. The most natural anchor is prospective: the precision regime *under which the action is committed* (REE's commit-boundary) is what carries the responsibility weight, with retrospective comparator-error processing then modulating attribution after the fact. Malik's review supports separating those clearly. ARC-016's commitment→behavior wiring (in V3) is the locus where this distinction would have to be wired.

## Caveats

This is a theoretical synthesis, not a primary empirical test. The unifying framework is the contribution; direct confirmation of MECH-025b's specific bridge would require new experiments designed against ABDF predictions. The cross-disorder aggregation also runs the risk of over-fitting a single framework to heterogeneous disturbances.
