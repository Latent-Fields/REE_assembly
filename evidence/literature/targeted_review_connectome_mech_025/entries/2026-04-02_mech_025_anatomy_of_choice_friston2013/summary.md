# Friston et al. (2013) — The Anatomy of Choice: Active Inference and Agency

## What the paper does

Friston and colleagues formalize agency within the active inference framework. They show that optimal decision-making can be cast as variational Bayesian inference, where the agent infers policies (action sequences) that minimize expected free energy. A key result is that the precision of beliefs about policies — how confident the agent is in its chosen course of action — has a unique Bayes-optimal solution. When goals are attainable through the agent's own actions, policy precision is high; when the environment is uncontrollable, precision drops. Expected utility theory, softmax choice rules, and optimism bias all emerge as special cases.

## Relevance to MECH-025

MECH-025 claims that the action-doing mode prioritizes high-precision, short-horizon, responsibility-linked control. Friston et al. provide the theoretical foundation for the precision component: in active inference, the very act of recognizing oneself as a causal agent (agency) is linked to elevated precision over action policies. When you are the one doing the thing, and your actions have predictable consequences, the system correctly upweights policy precision.

This maps directly onto REE's E3 dynamic precision parameter (ARC-016). In action-doing mode, the agent detects that it is the causal source (SD-003 self-attribution) and E3 precision increases — concentrating trajectory scoring on high-confidence, immediately executable action sequences.

## What the paper does NOT address

The paper treats precision as continuous and context-dependent, not as a discrete mode. MECH-025 proposes a more categorical distinction — action-doing is a recognizable regime with characteristic properties. The short-horizon aspect (prioritizing immediate control over long-range planning) is not addressed. Nor does the paper connect precision to moral responsibility; agency in this framework is about causal efficacy, not ethical accountability.

## Confidence reasoning

High source quality (Friston lab, well-cited). The precision-agency link is directly relevant to MECH-025. Confidence moderated because only one of three aspects of the claim (high precision) is supported — short-horizon and responsibility-linked are not addressed.
