# Wen & Haggard (2020) — Prediction Error and Regularity Detection Underlie Two Dissociable Mechanisms for Computing the Sense of Agency

## What the paper does

Wen and Haggard investigate the computational mechanisms underlying sense of agency (SoA) — the feeling of being the author of one's own actions. They demonstrate that SoA is computed by two independent mechanisms: (1) a comparator mechanism that detects mismatches between predicted and actual sensory outcomes (prediction error), and (2) a regularity detector that learns statistical regularities between actions and effects. These two mechanisms combine additively and can be experimentally dissociated.

## Relevance to MECH-025

MECH-025 claims that action-doing mode is responsibility-linked — the agent must detect that it is the causal source of outcomes. This paper provides human behavioral evidence for how such causal self-detection works: the comparator mechanism generates agency signals by comparing motor predictions against sensory feedback. When the prediction matches (low prediction error), agency is attributed to the self.

In REE, SD-003 implements a computational analogue: E2 generates predictions for the actual action taken versus a counterfactual, and the difference constitutes the agent's causal signature. When this signature is strong (the agent's action made a difference), MECH-025 predicts the system enters action-doing mode with elevated E3 precision.

The paper also supports the claim that responsibility-linked control is mechanistically distinct from passive perception: the two agency mechanisms operate only during action, not during mere observation, consistent with MECH-025's assertion that action mode is a separable control regime.

## Mapping caveats

The study measures subjective experience of agency, not a computational control mode. The intentional binding paradigm used is specific to human temporal perception and has no direct analogue in REE. The mapping from "feeling of agency" to "action-mode precision elevation" requires assuming that the computational mechanism underlying subjective agency also drives precision modulation — a reasonable inference but not empirically established.

## Confidence reasoning

Solid behavioral study from a leading agency research group. Supports the responsibility-linked component of MECH-025 via the comparator mechanism. Confidence moderated by the subjective-to-computational mapping gap.
