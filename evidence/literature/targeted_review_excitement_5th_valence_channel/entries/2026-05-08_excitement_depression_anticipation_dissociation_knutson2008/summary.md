# Neural responses to monetary incentives in major depression (Knutson et al. 2008)

## What the paper did

Knutson, Bhanji, Cooney, Atlas and Gotlib (Biol Psychiatry 2008) compared 14 unmedicated currently-depressed participants with 12 never-depressed controls during an fMRI MID task. The contrast of interest was anticipation and outcome processing for monetary gain and loss across the two groups. The construct anchors were behavioural performance, self-reported affect, and the standard MID neural readouts (NAcc anticipation, ACC anticipation, vmPFC outcome discrimination).

## Key findings relevant to the SD-014 question

The headline finding is a clinical dissociation. Depressed and never-depressed participants did *not* differ in NAcc activation during gain anticipation, or in self-reported affective responses, or in behavioural performance during anticipation. Where they differed was in two specific places: depressed participants showed *increasing ACC activation during anticipation of increasing gains* (controls showed ACC activation during anticipation of loss); and depressed participants showed reduced discrimination of gain-vs-nongain *outcomes* (post-receipt). The interpretation: anhedonia in unmedicated MDD does not blunt the basic NAcc-anticipation signature; it alters anticipation-stage conflict signalling in ACC and outcome-stage hedonic discrimination in vmPFC.

For SD-014 this is clinical-dissociation evidence that NAcc anticipation is separable from related affective constructs. If excitement were just an amplitude-component of VALENCE_WANTING, it should covary with the motivational deficits typical of MDD. It does not, in this sample. NAcc anticipation can be intact while outcome discrimination is reduced — a dissociation that is hard to parsimoniously explain without granting NAcc-anticipation its own channel-like status.

## How this maps to REE

The architectural implication: VALENCE_EXCITEMENT could in principle be selectively preserved or altered in REE, distinct from VALENCE_WANTING amplitude reduction or VALENCE_LIKING capacity reduction. This buys phenomenological flexibility — REE could model depression-like phenotypes that involve altered conflict signalling (SD-032 cingulate cluster) and altered outcome discrimination (vmPFC-equivalent / VALENCE_LIKING) without forcing the agent to also have blunted excitement at cues. Whether REE *will* model depression is a separate question, but having the representational expressiveness is the architectural payoff.

## Limitations and caveats

The clinical-to-RL translation is loose. REE has no current depression-class anchor — there is no MDD phenotype that has been engineered or observed in V3. The dissociation is interesting but its REE relevance is forward-looking. The Knutson 2008 sample is also small (n=14 / n=12) and unmedicated MDD is heterogeneous; the result has been refined in subsequent work and meta-analyses.

## Confidence reasoning

0.72. Clinical-dissociation evidence is moderately informative for the architectural-separability question. The transfer risk is elevated because of the clinical-to-RL gap and the absence of a REE depression model. But the dissociation pattern adds independent support for the channel-separability principle established by the Knutson 2001 papers.
