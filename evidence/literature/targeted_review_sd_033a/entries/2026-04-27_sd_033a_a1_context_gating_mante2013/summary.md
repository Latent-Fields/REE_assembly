# Mante, Sussillo, Shenoy & Newsome 2013 — Context-dependent computation by recurrent dynamics in prefrontal cortex

According to PubMed, Mante, Sussillo, Shenoy & Newsome (2013, [Nature 503:78-84](https://doi.org/10.1038/nature12742)) recorded from approximately 727 single units in macaque dorsolateral prefrontal cortex during a perceptual decision task with two interleaved contexts. On each trial, the animal saw a noisy moving-coloured-dot stimulus carrying both motion and colour information; a context cue indicated which dimension was task-relevant. The animal had to integrate the relevant dimension and ignore the irrelevant one. The headline finding is that PFC implements selection and integration as two aspects of a *single* dynamical process at the population level — and a trained recurrent neural network with task structure as input reproduces the population dynamics with high fidelity. This is the modern population-dynamics-level account of context-dependent computation in PFC.

For SD-033a's A1 question — per-candidate bias projection versus uniform scalar gain — Mante 2013 provides the closest empirical analogue available. The mechanism the paper exposes is **input-selective gating**: the same physical sensory inputs (motion and colour) are routed differently as a function of context. When motion is task-relevant, the population dynamics integrate the motion dimension; when colour is task-relevant, the population integrates colour. This is not a uniform multiplicative gain that scales all inputs identically — it is a context-conditional, dimension-specific gating. Translated to SD-033a's selection problem, this favours the per-candidate landing: the rule_state should differentially weight candidates rather than scale them uniformly.

The translation requires a step of analogy that I want to flag explicitly in the mapping caveat. Mante 2013 measured input selection — choosing among two input dimensions on each trial. SD-033a's A1 lives at the output side: choosing among multiple trajectory candidates within E3. Generalising "input-selective gating in PFC" to "per-candidate gating in PFC's projection to a downstream selection circuit" is a one-step generalisation across the input/output asymmetry. It is plausible — there is no obvious reason the PFC selection machinery should change character at the projection step — but it is not strictly demonstrated.

A second caveat that matters more for the V3-vs-V4 reconsideration of SD-033a is that Mante 2013's mechanism is fundamentally **dynamical and recurrent**. Selection and integration are not implemented as a static lookup or a single Linear projection; they emerge from population dynamics that the RNN reproduces only when the network has the right recurrent structure and context input. SD-033a's V3 abstraction is much more static — a held rule_state, a single Linear bias projection, a frozen-random head. If the population-dynamics-level account is the right level of description for the biological mechanism, the V3 SD-033a may be capturing only the surface signature (rule-conditional bias exists) rather than the generative mechanism (recurrent dynamics gated by context). For V4 this would be one of the strongest reasons to elevate SD-033a from a static head to a recurrent module — and Mante 2013 is the canonical anchor for that elevation.

Confidence components: source quality is very high (Nature 2013, large primate single-unit dataset, RNN modelling that validates the dynamical interpretation, foundational paper for the context-dependent-computation literature). Mapping fidelity is good but not perfect — 0.72 — limited by the input-to-output generalisation. Transfer risk is moderate because the dynamical-process mechanism may not map cleanly onto SD-033a's static abstraction. Net direction is **supports** for the per-candidate A1 landing, with a flag that V3 SD-033a may be the wrong abstraction level for what the biology is actually doing.
