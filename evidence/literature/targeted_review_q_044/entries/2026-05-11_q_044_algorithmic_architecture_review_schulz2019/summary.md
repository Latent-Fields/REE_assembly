## What this review contributes to Q-044

Schulz & Gershman 2019's primary dichotomy is between random exploration (decision noise) and directed exploration (uncertainty-driven information seeking). That dichotomy maps cleanly onto MECH-313/MECH-314 — Q-043's territory — and only indirectly onto MECH-314a/b/c, which is Q-044's territory.

The Q-044-relevant content sits in two places. First, the review discusses developmental dissociations: random exploration peaks in adolescence; directed exploration is adult-stable. If MECH-314 sub-flavours dissociate at all, the developmental literature is one place where the dissociation might surface — the same kind of cross-sectional natural-experiment evidence that has worked for the random/directed split. Second, the review discusses structure-aware exploration: humans exploit correlations between options to generalise from one explored option to similar unexplored ones. Structure-aware exploration is closely related to MECH-314b (uncertainty-driven curiosity over structured state spaces) but distinguishable from raw novelty (MECH-314a) which would not generalise across correlated options.

So the review weakly supports the architectural premise that MECH-314's content can be decomposed along multiple axes (random/directed, structure-blind/structure-aware) without committing to the specific three-way decomposition Q-044 asks about.

## Caveats

Schulz & Gershman 2019 do not directly address the novelty/uncertainty/learning-progress three-way distinction. For that distinction, the load-bearing anchors are Wittmann 2007 (novelty), Daw 2006 (uncertainty), and Kaplan & Oudeyer 2007 (learning progress) — the three sibling entries in this lit-pull. The review is here primarily as synthesis context, not as direct evidence for any specific Q-044 outcome.
