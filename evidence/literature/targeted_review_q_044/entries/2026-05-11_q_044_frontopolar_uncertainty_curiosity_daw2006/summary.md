# Daw et al. 2006 — Cortical substrates for exploratory decisions (Q-044 role)

**Citation.** Daw ND, O'Doherty JP, Dayan P, Seymour B, Dolan RJ. (2006). Cortical substrates for exploratory decisions in humans. *Nature* 441(7095):876-9. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/nature04766). PMID 16778890.

## What this paper contributes to Q-044

Of the three MECH-314 sub-flavours that Q-044 asks about — striatal novelty (314a), frontopolar uncertainty-driven curiosity (314b), and learning-progress curiosity (314c) — Daw 2006 anchors the substrate identity of MECH-314b. In the four-armed bandit task, exploratory choices were modelled with a softmax over Kalman-filter uncertainty estimates; the BOLD correlate of those choices localised to right frontopolar cortex and intraparietal sulcus. The "directed" or "uncertainty-bonus" family of curiosity, which underlies the algorithmic role of MECH-314b in REE, finds its primary neural anchor here.

Combining the three sub-flavour anchors gives Q-044 three architecturally distinct substrate loci to test: SN/VTA + striatum (Wittmann 2007, MECH-314a), frontopolar cortex (Daw 2006, MECH-314b), and a more distributed cortical microcircuit involving tonic dopamine (Kaplan & Oudeyer 2007, MECH-314c). If all three are mechanistically independent, REE's three-arm ablation should produce three distinguishable failure signatures.

## Caveat — uncertainty/novelty confound in Daw 2006

Daw's task uses arms whose payoff distributions drift slowly; an arm becomes "uncertain" exactly when it has not been sampled recently, which means novelty and uncertainty are essentially the same operational variable in the task. The frontopolar BOLD effect could therefore reflect novelty as much as uncertainty. The Wittmann 2007 paradigm (cues predicting novel vs familiar images) pulls these apart more cleanly and assigns the novelty-anticipation signal to SN/VTA, suggesting that the Daw frontopolar effect is more uncertainty than novelty. But the design confound is real and should be acknowledged when Daw 2006 is used as a MECH-314b anchor.

For Q-044's three-arm ablation, this matters because the predicted MECH-314b-OFF failure signature (degraded uncertainty-driven choice) should be tested in a setting where novelty and uncertainty are deconfounded. SD-054 reef may not have that property unless the experiment is designed to separate them — possibly by varying the cue-validity of state-features that signal upcoming uncertainty.
