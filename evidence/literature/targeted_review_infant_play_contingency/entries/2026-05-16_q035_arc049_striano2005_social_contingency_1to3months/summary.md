# Summary: Striano, Henning, Stahl (2005) -- Social Contingency 1-3 Months

**Source:** Striano, Henning, Stahl (2005). Sensitivity to social contingencies between 1 and 3 months of age. Developmental Science, 8(6), 509-518. doi:10.1111/j.1467-7687.2005.00442.x

**Claim IDs:** Q-035, ARC-049, MECH-197

**Evidence direction:** supports | **Confidence:** 0.80

## Key Finding

One-month-olds do not discriminate Normal, Non-Contingent, and Imitation interaction conditions with their mothers. Three-month-olds do discriminate all three: they gaze longer in the Imitation condition and smile reliably more in Normal vs. Non-Contingent and Imitation conditions. This marks a developmental transition in social contingency sensitivity onset between 1 and 3 months.

## REE Relevance

Pins the emergence of continuous social contingency monitoring to a narrow developmental window (1-3 months), before any sophisticated cognitive capacity. This is the onset boundary.

For MECH-197 (play type progression): if the monitoring architecture is operational by 3 months, then the sensorimotor -> functional -> constructive -> pretend -> games-with-rules progression does NOT switch on monitoring -- it escalates what monitoring tracks.

For Q-035: the heartbeat signal is present in the simplest play by 3 months; what grows across development is the semantic complexity of what is checked.

For ARC-049: confirms that even primitive sensorimotor play (face-to-face interaction at 3 months) requires an active monitoring system.

## Limitations

- Single lab, relatively small N per age group.
- Three conditions conflate contingency and timing -- hard to isolate the critical dimension.
- Does not track within-episode dynamics.

## Action

Supports Q-035, ARC-049, MECH-197. Refines MECH-197 by providing the developmental onset boundary for the monitoring architecture. The 1-month null result is as informative as the 3-month positive: monitoring emerges, it is not present from birth.
