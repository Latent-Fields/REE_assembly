# Summary: Rochat, Querido, Striano (1999) -- Protoconversation Timing

**Source:** Rochat, P., Querido, J.G., Striano, T. (1999). Emerging sensitivity to the timing and structure of protoconversation in early infancy. Developmental Psychology, 35(4), 950-957. doi:10.1037//0012-1649.35.4.950

**Claim IDs:** MECH-197, ARC-049, Q-035

**Evidence direction:** supports | **Confidence:** 0.78

## Key Finding

Cross-sectional study across 2-, 4-, and 6-month-olds in organized vs. disorganized peekaboo with an adult stranger. Two-month-olds show no differential response to organized vs. disorganized structure -- they respond only to the presence of a social partner. Four-month-olds and 6-month-olds show differential responding: they smile more and gaze differently in organized peekaboo, suggesting sensitivity to the narrative envelope (timing and structure) of social exchanges.

## REE Relevance

Provides the critical developmental sequence for what monitoring tracks across development:

- At 2 months: monitors 'is a partner present and active?' (partner presence monitoring)
- By 4 months: monitors 'is the partner following the expected timing/structure of exchange?' (narrative envelope monitoring)

This is the content-escalation pattern that MECH-197 (play type progression) predicts: the monitoring architecture is constant across these ages, but what it registers grows in complexity.

For ARC-049: the frame state at 4 months includes information about turn timing and game structure, not just partner responsiveness. The bilateral frame tag must carry sufficient state to represent this narrative structure.

For Q-035: independent evidence that continuous monitoring is present by 2-4 months; the developmental change is in what is monitored, not whether monitoring occurs.

## Limitations

- Cross-sectional design; individual trajectories not captured.
- Organized/disorganized peekaboo confounds timing with overall predictability.
- The 2-month null result could be a floor effect; monitoring may exist but not at a level that differentiates these conditions.

## Action

Supports MECH-197 (content-escalation interpretation), ARC-049, Q-035. Refines MECH-197: the transition from sensorimotor to functional play may correspond to the onset of timing/structure sensitivity at 4 months. Suggests implementation metric: frame state complexity in REE should index not just 'is the agent responding?' but 'is the response occurring at the expected time in the interaction sequence?'
