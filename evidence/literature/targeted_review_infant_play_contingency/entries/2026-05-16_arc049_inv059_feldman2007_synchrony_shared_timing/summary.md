# Summary: Feldman (2007) -- Parent-Infant Synchrony and Shared Timing

**Source:** Feldman, Ruth (2007). Parent-infant synchrony and the construction of shared timing; physiological precursors, developmental outcomes, and risk conditions. Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry, 48(3-4), 329-354. doi:10.1111/j.1469-7610.2006.01701.x

**Claim IDs:** ARC-049, INV-059, DEV-NEED-010, DEV-NEED-015

**Evidence direction:** supports | **Confidence:** 0.76

## Key Finding

Parent-infant synchrony -- the temporally-matched coordination of affective behavior -- has physiological precursors in neonatal oscillator systems (sleep-wake cyclicity, cardiac vagal tone) and predicts self-regulation, symbol use, and empathy across childhood and adolescence. Synchrony is a continuous temporally-matched process grounded in biological rhythms (cardiac pacemaker, biological clock). Different synchrony patterns with mother vs. father vs. family describe relationship-specific coordination modes. Synchrony failure in risk conditions (preterm birth, postpartum depression) predicts psychopathology trajectories.

## REE Relevance

Provides biological grounding for continuous frame monitoring: the capacity to engage in temporally-matched social interaction is grounded in the same oscillatory systems that regulate sleep and cardiac rhythms. This connects the REE play frame monitoring directly to the sleep/oscillator architecture (INV-049 thread).

For ARC-049 (bilateral play frame): bilateral frame monitoring may be implemented as a mutual synchrony computation -- each agent's monitoring heartbeat entrains to the other's biological rhythm. The frame is maintained when synchrony is maintained.

For INV-059 (mutual frame maintenance necessary): synchrony breakdown predicts psychopathology, establishing that ongoing temporal coordination is constitutive of healthy development, not optional. The biological grounding makes the necessity claim mechanistically tractable.

For DEV-NEED-010 (sensorimotor play): sensorimotor play is the earliest developmental context in which synchrony is trained. The neonatal oscillator foundation means the monitoring capacity is being built from the first days of life.

For DEV-NEED-015 (caregiver protection function): the caregiver's contingent responding provides the oscillatory input that regulates infant arousal. Loss of synchrony is one mechanism through which caregiver absence impairs infant development.

## Limitations

- Review paper -- individual study methods vary widely; effect sizes heterogeneous.
- Synchrony is a population-level temporal correlation, not the same as moment-by-moment frame monitoring.
- The physiological precursor story (neonatal oscillators -> synchrony) is correlational.
