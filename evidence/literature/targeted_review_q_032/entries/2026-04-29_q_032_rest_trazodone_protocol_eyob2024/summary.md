# Eyob et al. 2024 -- REST trial protocol: trazodone for SWS preservation in early AD

[DOI: 10.3233/JAD-230635](https://doi.org/10.3233/JAD-230635)

## What they did

This is a study-design and rationale paper for the REST trial (Randomized-Controlled Trial Targeting Cognition in Early Alzheimer's Disease by Improving Sleep with Trazodone, NCT05282550). REST is a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, crossover trial of trazodone in 100 older adults with amnestic mild cognitive impairment and sleep complaints. The paper presents the scientific rationale -- linking sleep disturbance to AD risk via glymphatic amyloid clearance, identifying SWS as the offline phase most relevant to neuroprotection, and arguing that most older-adult sleep medications (benzodiazepines, Z-drugs) have safety problems and either suppress or fail to enhance SWS, leaving trazodone as one of the few candidate agents that preserves SWS architecture in older adults. Outcomes are PSG-measured sleep architecture and cognitive function over the trial period.

## Key findings relevant to Q-032

The paper provides no outcome data -- this is a protocol description, not a results paper. What it provides is institutional and field-level evidence that PSG-measured SWS is treated as a legitimate trial endpoint by major sleep-AD research groups, and that pharmacological preservation of SWS architecture is being operationalised as a testable disease-modifying strategy. The REST trial design directly mirrors what Q-032's pharmacodynamic-biomarker proposal would require: an active drug expected to preserve a specific architectural feature, a placebo control, dual outcomes covering both the architecture metric (PSG SWS) and the cognitive endpoint, and a crossover structure that controls for inter-individual variability.

## How this maps to REE

Q-032's three sub-questions are: (a) does PSG architecture predict dementia, (b) do drug-class differences in SWS preservation produce differential cognitive outcomes, and (c) is the resulting metric a validated PD biomarker for trial design. Sub-questions (a) and the architectural-signature half of (c) are addressed by Pase 2017, Himali 2023, Suh 2019, Winer 2020, and D'Rozario 2020. Sub-question (b) -- the drug-class differential question -- is exactly what REST is designed to test. Trazodone is the canonical test agent because, unlike benzodiazepines/Z-drugs/SSRIs which suppress or distort SWS, trazodone has shown SWS-preserving or SWS-enhancing properties in older adults at sleep-promoting doses.

A positive REST outcome -- trazodone improves SWS and improves cognition relative to placebo, with the cognitive benefit mediated by the SWS preservation -- would be the canonical proof-of-concept for Q-032 limb (b) and would substantially raise the prior on PSG SWS as a regulatorily-acceptable PD biomarker. A null or negative REST outcome would weaken the architecture-specific hypothesis and force a more skeptical reading of the cross-sectional architectural-signature work.

## Limitations and caveats

The hard caveat: there are no outcome data yet. As an REE evidence record, this entry cannot count as outcome-supportive evidence for the PD-biomarker proposal. What it does support is the secondary claim that the field broadly treats Q-032's premise as live and worth testing -- the protocol's IRB approval, funding, and publication establish that.

There are also deeper architectural caveats embedded in the trial design itself. Trazodone alone cannot adjudicate architecture-specificity. A positive trazodone result could reflect (i) genuine SWS-mediated cognitive protection (the Q-032 reading), (ii) general sleep quality improvement that happens to correlate with SWS but acts via other mechanisms, or (iii) trazodone's primary antidepressant or sedative actions. To distinguish (i) from (ii)-(iii), a follow-up trial would need a non-SWS-promoting active comparator with similar sedative profile. The crossover design at n=100 will also have limited power for distal cognitive endpoints; the primary outcomes are likely to be PSG-measured SWS and short-term cognitive proxies, not incident MCI conversion or biomarker progression.

## Confidence reasoning

I rate this 0.45. Source quality is moderate because protocol papers are descriptive rather than evidentiary; until outcomes arrive, the only "evidence" is field acceptance of the design as fundable and IRB-approvable. Mapping fidelity is good because the trial directly operationalises Q-032 limb (b). Transfer risk is moderate because architecture-specificity cannot be adjudicated from trazodone alone. The right reading is "REST shows the field treats Q-032's premise as live and is actively testing it; the actual trial-level evidence on PD-biomarker validity is still pending."
