# Hobson, Hong & Friston 2014 — Virtual reality and consciousness inference in dreaming

**Evidence direction: mixed**

## What the paper claims

The brain is a generative model whose function during waking is to predict sensory input and minimise prediction error. During REM sleep, the generative model is freed from sensory input and can minimise model complexity -- pruning unnecessary parameters and maximising statistical efficiency. Dreaming is the phenomenological correlate of this offline model optimisation. The authors frame this within the free-energy principle (Friston): sleep (specifically REM) minimises variational free energy in the absence of sensory constraints.

## Does it make the full derivation argument?

No. The paper focuses entirely on REM sleep as a model-optimisation phase. It does not address NREM sleep's preparatory role, nor does it argue that REM model optimisation must follow NREM consolidation as a computational necessity. The ordering question -- why NREM precedes REM -- is not asked or answered. The paper establishes what REM does (Phase 4 in INV-045: precision recalibration / model complexity reduction) but not why that phase must be last.

## Relevance to INV-045

Partial support for Phase 4 mechanism. The free-energy account of REM directly instantiates the precision-recalibration function of Phase 4: the generative model's prior weighting is reset during offline optimisation. The logical dependency INV-045 claims -- that recalibration must follow consolidation because you cannot recalibrate priors that don't yet reflect consolidated schemas -- is implied by the paper's logic but never stated. The convergence between Friston's free-energy account and INV-045's Phase 4 is genuine and noteworthy, but it is the user's derivation, not the paper's.

## Key gap for INV-045

No paper in the active-inference / free-energy tradition (Friston or otherwise) has made the full ordering-derivation argument for INV-045. Friston's framework provides the vocabulary (free energy, model complexity, prior weighting) but does not apply it to derive the sequence.
