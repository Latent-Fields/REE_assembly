# Tononi & Cirelli 2014 -- Sleep and the Price of Plasticity (SHY)

**Evidence direction: mixed**

## What the paper claims

The Synaptic Homeostasis Hypothesis (SHY): during waking, Hebbian plasticity strengthens synapses broadly, increasing metabolic cost and saturating signal-to-noise. Slow-wave sleep (NREM) renormalises synaptic strength via down-states, restoring the signal-to-noise ratio and cellular homeostasis. The paper explicitly acknowledges that this is primarily a NREM theory; the role of REM sleep within SHY is left as an open question. The authors ask: "how REM sleep may or may not fit with the hypothesised core function of sleep, and whether the regular alternation of NREM/REM periods in most mammals and birds may serve a particular function" -- but do not answer this.

## Does it make the full derivation argument?

No. SHY provides the mechanism for Phase 1 (synaptic homeostasis before replay) and implies -- but does not state -- that this normalisation must precede effective hippocampal replay (Phase 2). The logic is: saturated synapses cannot undergo further potentiation, so replay-driven Hebbian learning requires a cleared, normalised synapse population first. This implies Phase 1 must precede Phase 2. However, the NREM-to-REM transition (Phases 2-3-4) is entirely outside SHY's scope. The paper explicitly acknowledges REM as an unresolved question.

## Relevance to INV-045

Direct support for Phase 1 mechanism and an implied (not stated) functional ordering between Phases 1 and 2. The signal-to-noise restoration argument is the most natural reason why SHY-type downscaling must precede replay: it is not enough to replay if the target synapses are already saturated. This is the kind of failure-mode dependency that INV-045 requires. But the paper provides only two of the five phases' mechanisms, and even those two are connected by implication rather than explicit derivation.

## Key gap for INV-045

SHY is the most cited mechanistic account of NREM's function, but it explicitly leaves the NREM-REM ordering unresolved. No paper building on SHY has filled this gap by deriving the ordering from the functional dependency between downscaling (Phase 1), replay (Phase 2), and precision recalibration (Phase 4).
