# Yoshida & Toyoizumi 2023 -- Computational role of sleep in memory reorganization

**Evidence direction: mixed**

## What the paper claims

A computational review surveying recent theoretical models of sleep's role in memory. The key functional claims: (1) NREM sleep selectively consolidates memory through slow-wave up-state/down-state gating -- slow oscillations create competition among memory traces, with down-states suppressing weak memories and up-states allowing selective reactivation; (2) REM sleep reorganises memory representations through dreaming -- the semi-random activation during dreaming allows new neuronal representations to develop that were not directly encoded during waking. The review explicitly notes that the computational role of REM is less well-developed in models than NREM.

## Does it make the full derivation argument?

No. The paper is the most recent computational review found and it explicitly does not derive the NREM-before-REM ordering from first principles. The complementary roles are described as distinct computational operations, but the sequencing is treated as a biological given. The note that REM is "less well-developed" in models is itself a form of acknowledging the gap: if REM's computational function were as well-understood as NREM's, the ordering argument might follow more naturally.

## Relevance to INV-045

The complementary-roles framing (NREM consolidates/selects, REM reorganises) directly supports the functional distinction between INV-045's NREM and REM phases. The down-state suppression of weak memories maps onto MECH-165 (replay diversity: the dominant trace must not suppress the less-frequent traces needed for schema generalisation). The REM reorganisation account maps onto Phase 4 (precision recalibration as a form of representational reorganisation). However, the gap between "these phases do different things" and "the sequence is a necessity" is explicitly the gap that INV-045 claims to fill, and this paper confirms that the scientific literature has not filled it as of 2023.

## Key gap for INV-045

The most current computational review of sleep phases concludes that the roles are distinct and complementary but does not derive the ordering. The gap is acknowledged implicitly (REM models are underdeveloped). This is evidence that the INV-045 ordering-derivation argument represents a genuine novel contribution -- the biological literature provides the mechanistic components but has not assembled them into a necessity proof.
