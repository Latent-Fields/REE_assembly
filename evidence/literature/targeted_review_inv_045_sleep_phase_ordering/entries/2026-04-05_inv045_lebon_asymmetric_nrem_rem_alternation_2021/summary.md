# Le Bon 2021 -- An Asymmetrical Hypothesis for the NREM-REM Sleep Alternation

**Evidence direction: mixed**

## What the paper claims

The NREM-REM cycle is asymmetrical: REM sleep episodes generate a proportional post-REM refractory period (PRRP) during which REM cannot recur; NREM episodes do not generate an equivalent refractory constraint. The observed NREM-before-REM pattern is a direct consequence of this asymmetry. Since REM cannot begin a new cycle in adults (it is forbidden to initiate), and since NREM creates no self-inhibitory period, the default pattern is NREM -> REM -> PRRP -> NREM -> REM. The ordering emerges from pressure-convergence and semi-random timing rather than from a logical dependency between what each phase accomplishes computationally.

## Does it make the full derivation argument?

No -- and this paper is notable for engaging most directly with the ordering question while giving a non-computational answer. Le Bon's account is a homeostatic/refractory mechanism at the brainstem level: the sequence is maintained by inhibitory timing circuits, not by the computational content of each phase. This is the most direct challenge found to INV-045's framing: Le Bon shows that the ordering can be explained without appealing to functional dependencies between phases. The accounts are not mutually exclusive (the brainstem circuit could implement a computationally necessary order without 'knowing' why), but Le Bon's paper highlights that INV-045 has an additional explanatory burden: it needs to show not just that the ordering is computationally optimal, but why biology chose to implement this ordering through a refractory mechanism rather than through phase-content handshaking.

## Relevance to INV-045

The paper is a useful contrast: it is the most direct attempt in the published literature to explain why NREM precedes REM, and it does so without any reference to computational function. The brainstem refractory mechanism it describes is entirely compatible with INV-045 (biology could use this mechanism to enforce a computationally necessary sequence), but it also shows that the sequence can be maintained without any representational dependency. This is relevant to INV-045's claim that the ordering is not a contingent biological fact: Le Bon's account suggests it IS maintained by a biological mechanism, even if that mechanism happens to implement the correct computational order. INV-045 needs to engage with this: the claim is not that biology has no mechanism for the ordering, but that the mechanism converges on the computationally necessary order -- and the convergence is itself evidence.

## Key gap for INV-045

Le Bon's paper reveals a gap in INV-045's framing: the claim "not a contingent biological fact" needs to specify what it means by contingent. The sequence is maintained by a biological mechanism (PRRP). INV-045's claim is stronger: that this biological mechanism implements a sequence that is derivable from computational necessity, and that the convergence is evidence for the account. This framing is not present in the biological literature.
