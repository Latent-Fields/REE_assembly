# Palmiter 2018 -- PBN CGRP neurons as general alarm

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tins.2018.03.007)), this review by the Palmiter lab consolidates the case for PBN CGRP neurons as a general alarm system: a population of pontine neurons that integrates interoceptive distress signals across modalities (visceral malaise, pain, itch, temperature, taste aversion, threat-related stimuli) and relays them via extended amygdala, hypothalamus, and thalamus to drive defensive responses including hypophagia, anxiety-like behaviour, and conditioned aversion.

For REE, this is the substrate description of the threat-side broadcast that the hypothesised override layer must oppose. PBN CGRP -> central amygdala -> PAG is one of the canonical pathways driving freeze, and is therefore the upstream input to MECH-279 (PAG GABAergic freeze gate). Notably, PBN CGRP activation directly suppresses appetite -- this is the substrate analogue of "threat shuts down feeding" that the de Araujo Salgado 2023 paper showed is mediated by PMd-CCK -> AgRP. The architectural picture is that hunger override has to overcome at least two parallel inhibitory drives on AgRP/feeding (PBN CGRP from interoceptive distress, PMd-CCK from explicit predator detection).

The architectural implication for V3 is significant: both the threat and the drive sides are broadcast systems competing for arbitration weight at multiple downstream targets simultaneously. SD-036's GABAergic decay handles the slow recovery from threat-side broadcast lock. The new override SD must handle the fast active push from drive-side broadcast against the threat side. The V3-EXQ-471 catatonia represents threat-side broadcast lock without enough drive-side counterforce -- and the substrate analogue would be a mouse with intact PBN CGRP but lesioned AgRP-LH-BNST output: sustained predator-driven freeze with no override even as energy depletes.

Caveat: the review is from the lab that defined the circuit, with the inevitable emphasis on CGRP-neuron centrality. Subsequent work (Arthurs et al 2023, also Palmiter lab) shows that Tac1+/CGRP- neurons in the same PBN can suppress some functions of the CGRP population, complicating the simple "CGRP = general alarm" picture. The override question is not directly addressed -- the review documents what activates the alarm, not how it is normally overridden.

Confidence 0.78. High-quality review, characterises the substrate the override layer must oppose, mapping fidelity high for the threat-side picture but lower for the override per se.
