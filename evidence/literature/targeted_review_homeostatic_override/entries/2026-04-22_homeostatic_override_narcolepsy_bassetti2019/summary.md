# Bassetti et al. 2019 -- Narcolepsy as the human orexin knockout

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41582-019-0226-9)), this consensus review by 19 international experts consolidates the current view that narcolepsy type 1 is a selective loss of orexin (hypocretin) neurons in the lateral hypothalamic area, biochemically confirmed by CSF orexin deficiency in untreated patients. The phenotype is multi-system. Excessive daytime sleepiness and cataplexy (sudden loss of postural tone triggered by strong emotion) are the diagnostic core. Around them cluster disturbances of motor, psychiatric, emotional, cognitive, metabolic, and autonomic function.

For REE, this paper functions as the negative-evidence test of the broadcast architecture. If a single broadcast gain-modulator fails, the prediction is multi-system dysregulation rather than focal symptoms. If override were instead implemented by parallel point-to-point projections each carrying their own copy of the homeostatic signal, single-receptor loss should produce restricted deficits. The narcolepsy phenotype is dramatic confirmation of the broadcast prediction -- the breadth of involvement is precisely what loss of a small, broadly-projecting cell population produces. About 50,000 cells, lost selectively, and the result is a syndromic disorder.

Cataplexy is the most diagnostically specific feature and the most informative for REE. It is a failure of the coupling between emotional input and motor maintenance: strong emotion (positive or negative) collapses postural tone instead of being processed as motivational signal. In REE terms, this is a clean prediction of broadcast-coupling failure -- the override pathway that normally translates emotional state into appropriate motor preparation has gone offline, so the emotion arrives but cannot recruit the motor-arousal coupling. This is exactly the failure mode the V3 spec hypothesises for the missing override layer.

The clinical detail that I find most relevant to V3-EXQ-471: narcolepsy patients also show altered metabolic regulation, with increased BMI and glucose dysregulation despite normal endocrine function. This suggests orexin's metabolic-arousal coupling is functionally important outside the sleep-wake axis -- the same broadcast that couples emotion to motor preparation also couples metabolic state to behavioural arousal. The clinical literature on whether narcoleptics show specific deficits in foraging-under-threat or in starvation-driven food approach is thin and would be a good follow-up pull.

Mapping caveat: narcolepsy is the loss of orexin specifically. SD-036's GABAergic decay and the broader broadcast-override system involve at least three regulatory layers (5-HT MECH-186/187/188, GABA SD-036, hypothesised orexin-analog new SD). Their relative contributions cannot be inferred from a single-channel knockout.

Confidence 0.8. High-quality consensus paper, broadcast architecture clearly supported, specific override-of-defense interpretation is translation rather than direct.
