# James, Mahler, Moorman & Aston-Jones 2017 -- Orexin and motivational activation

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/7854_2016_57)), this review by James, Mahler, Moorman, and Aston-Jones consolidates a decade of orexin/addiction research into the unifying claim that orexin neurons coordinate "motivational activation" -- recruited specifically when behaviour requires effort against an obstacle, when external cues drive craving, or when stress-related signals push for state-dependent escalation. Orexin is not part of the primary reinforcement signal. Behavioural-economic analyses (demand-elasticity assays) consistently show that orexin antagonism reduces willingness to work for a given reward without reducing the reward's hedonic value.

The convergence between this review and Bjorness & Greene 2024 is informative for REE because the two evidence bases are largely independent. Bjorness/Greene synthesise the arousal and sleep literature; James/Aston-Jones synthesise the addiction and motivation literature. Both arrive at the same architectural picture: small broadcast, gain modulation under demand, integrates internal-state with external situation. Independent convergence raises confidence that the architectural pattern is real rather than an artefact of a particular research community.

For REE's V3 implementation, the demand-elasticity framing is the most directly translatable. SD-012's drive_level should not scale primary value (z_goal seeding amplitude) but should scale the cost the agent will tolerate to obtain the goal. In V3-EXQ-471 terms: when drive_level is high enough to override z_harm-driven avoid mode, the agent does not necessarily start to value food MORE, but should be willing to PAY MORE in expected harm to reach it. This is a different parameter from "amplification of z_goal" and might be a cleaner injection point for the SD-036 / new-override-SD cluster.

Caveat: the evidence is largely from rodent drug self-administration. The translation from drug-cue-driven motivation to homeostatic-need-driven motivation is plausible but not direct. Some of the strongest claims in the review depend on dual orexin receptor antagonist studies whose specificity is debated.

Confidence 0.7. Independent convergence with Bjorness/Greene 2024 raises confidence in the architectural picture without requiring either review to be definitive on its own.
