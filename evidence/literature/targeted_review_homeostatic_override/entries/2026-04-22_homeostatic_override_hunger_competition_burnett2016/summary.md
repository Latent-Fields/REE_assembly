# Burnett et al. 2016 -- Hunger-Driven Motivational State Competition

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2016.08.032)), Burnett, Krashes, and colleagues at the NIDDK and NIDA used optogenetic activation and inhibition of arcuate AgRP neurons in mice to assay how hunger reorders the priority of competing motivational systems. They tested four systems against feeding -- thirst, anxiety-related behaviour in the open field, innate fear (predator odour TMT), and social investigation of a conspecific -- and asked, in each case, whether AgRP activity could suppress the competing drive in favour of food approach.

The result is the strongest available demonstration that a single hypothalamic cell type can reorganise the behavioural hierarchy in favour of one drive at the expense of all others. Optogenetic activation of AgRP neurons (mimicking endogenous hunger) suppressed thirst, anxiety, innate fear, and social interaction, and the suppression was contingent on food being accessible. Real-time fibre photometry showed AgRP activity locked not just to food presentation but also to social investigation, suggesting the cells themselves are integrators rather than pure caloric error detectors. The hierarchy is flexibly computed at the level of the AgRP population, with internal state, external affordance, and anticipated yield converging.

For REE, this paper is the central biological exhibit for the override layer that SD-036 (GABAergic decay) cannot supply on its own. Decay reduces a stuck z_harm passively over time; AgRP-style override actively suppresses competing arbitration weights and does so contingent on the presence of an affordance. The contingency on food access maps directly to SD-012's benefit_exposure gate -- drive amplification only converts to action when there is a route to satisfy the drive. Equally important, the paper supports a "broadcast" reading: one cell type, one neurotransmitter signature (GABA + AgRP + NPY), many downstream targets, simultaneous suppression of multiple drives. This is the architectural pattern the spec hypothesised.

The caveat is that the paper documents suppression of competing drives but does not directly distinguish whether the underlying mechanism is (i) AMPLIFICATION of food-seeking signal at multiple downstream sites, with relative weighting shifting against z_harm, or (ii) literal SUPPRESSION of z_harm-like signals. The clinical phenomenology Daniel describes -- where dread and craving co-amplify under starvation -- favours model (ii) for some streams and model (i) for others. Burnett et al cannot adjudicate this, and the architectural question of single-broadcast vs multi-target competition remains partly open. Confidence is held at 0.85: high empirical quality, slightly lower mapping fidelity for the specific competition-vs-amplification distinction.

Mapping caveat: AgRP itself does not project to all the suppressed targets in a single hop. The broadcast is at minimum two-stage (AgRP -> PVH, BNST, PBN, LH; LH/BNST/PBN -> downstream defensive and reward circuits). For REE, this means the override mechanism may be best modelled as a single high-level "drive_emergency" tag that downstream regulator layers consume, rather than as direct z_harm modulation by AgRP-analog activity itself.
