# de Araujo Salgado, Burnett, Krashes et al. 2023 -- Toggling between food-seeking and self-preservation

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2023.06.006)), this NIDDK/NIDA group built directly on Burnett 2016 to ask the architecturally sharper question: when a real predatory threat is in the environment, what circuit mechanism reorganises the food-seeking vs self-preservation tradeoff in real time, and how does internal caloric state modify that tradeoff? The behavioural paradigm -- mice food-seeking under controlled predator exposure -- is the closest published analogue I have found of the V3-EXQ-471 fishtank catatonia scenario.

The central findings: (i) predator exposure rapidly suppresses food consumption by activating cholecystokinin (CCK) neurons of the dorsal premammillary nucleus (PMd), which in turn inhibit hunger-sensitive AgRP neurons; (ii) increasing caloric need re-engages food seeking under threat by amplifying AgRP activity enough to overcome PMd-CCK suppression; (iii) the AgRP-driven override operates through projections to the bed nucleus of the stria terminalis (BNST) and the lateral hypothalamus (LH), rather than through a single broadcast pathway.

For REE, this is the most direct mapping yet onto the SD-036 / SD-012 cluster. The PMd-CCK -> AgRP suppression is a substrate analogue of the V3 catatonia: a hazard event installs an active inhibition on the drive system, not just an arbitration tilt. Recovery then requires drive (AgRP analog) to scale up enough to override the inhibition. SD-036's GABAergic decay would let PMd-CCK influence wane over time; the missing piece this paper supplies is the active fast-override channel via AgRP. The two-target output (BNST and LH) is informative: BNST likely modulates affective arbitration weights upstream of action selection (z_harm vs z_goal), while LH directly drives consummatory motor output. So the broadcast is at least bipartite, with distinct downstream consequences for arbitration vs action.

Crucially, this paper supports a graded rather than threshold override. The authors show that calorie deprivation continuously scales AgRP activity, and the override emerges as soon as AgRP outputs exceed PMd-CCK inhibition rather than at a discrete threshold. This contradicts the cleanest "snap-on" architecture and matches neuroeconomic graded-value accounts more than the clinical "I just couldn't keep refusing food" phenomenology. The clinical snap may reflect a higher-order narrative reinterpretation of an already-graded substrate signal -- worth noting for the V3 implementation.

Mapping caveat: the paper does not test whether AgRP override AMPLIFIES z_harm-analog activity in parallel with restoring feeding. The user's clinical observation that dread and craving co-amplify under starvation predicts a parallel signal not investigated here. The paper is also silent on whether failure of this override circuit could produce sustained immobility (catatonia) or only transient hypophagia -- the V3-EXQ-471 phenotype where energy depletes to zero is more extreme than anything tested.

Confidence 0.88: highest of any paper in this pull because the architectural question being asked maps almost one-to-one onto SD-036/SD-012/MECH-279.
