# Care Ethics (Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry, summarising Gilligan, Noddings, Tronto, Held, Kittay)

[Care Ethics — Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy](https://iep.utm.edu/care-ethics/), Sander-Staudt (entry author), drawing on Gilligan (1982), Noddings (1984, 2013), Tronto, Held (2006), Kittay (1999).

## What the source covers

INV-043 is an explicitly philosophical claim: REE architecture is necessary but not sufficient for ethical development; without a developmental phase of received caregiving and felt loveability, the architectural capacity may resolve to survival, domination, or indifference instead. PubMed evidence on this claim is largely indirect (developmental psychopathology, attachment, deprivation). The most direct philosophical support comes from care ethics, which the IEP entry consolidates.

The tradition begins with Gilligan's 1982 *In a Different Voice* (challenging Kohlberg's universal-justice developmental scheme by arguing for an alternative care voice) and Noddings's 1984 *Caring* (developing care ethics as ethics grounded in maternal-style relations of care and being cared for). It extends through Tronto's political ethics of care (attentiveness, responsibility, competence, responsiveness), Held's *The Ethics of Care* (2006), and Kittay's *Love's Labor* (1999) on dependency and equality. The IEP entry summarises these and the major critiques (essentialism, parochialism, "slave morality").

## Key philosophical claim

The core thesis most relevant to INV-043 is Noddings's: ethical action originates in *the memory of being cared-for*, which generates an ideal self that motivates moral response. Ethics is not derived from autonomous independent agents reasoning their way to abstract principles; it is grown from concrete relationships of dependency and care during development. The architecture-providing-capacity-but-not-content distinction INV-043 draws is exactly the distinction care ethics drew first, in philosophical terms.

## Caveats

Care ethics is contested within philosophy itself — major critiques (essentialism, parochialism, "slave morality") are real and INV-043 should not absorb them. The translation from philosophical claim to REE substrate is non-trivial; the strongest version of INV-043 will articulate the substrate-level mechanism (what kind of memory anchor, written when, with what content shape) rather than just inheriting the philosophical claim.
