# Sutton, Precup & Singh 1999 -- The options framework

Sutton, Precup & Singh's 1999 Artificial Intelligence paper ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0004-3702(99)00052-1)) is the foundational computational-theory anchor for what 'temporal abstraction' even is in reinforcement learning. The contribution is a clean mathematical extension: where standard MDPs only support primitive single-step actions, the semi-MDP framework supports options -- temporally-extended actions defined by a triple of an initiation-set (the states from which the option can be invoked), an intra-option policy (the closed-loop behaviour during execution), and a termination-condition (a probability distribution over states defining when the option ends). The mathematical payoff is that all the standard RL machinery (value iteration, policy iteration, Q-learning) lifts unchanged to options operating over semi-MDPs. Hierarchical agents can plan over options rather than primitive actions, with provable convergence guarantees.

For MECH-317 this is the canonical computational target. If REE wants to claim it instantiates 'behavioural pattern compression', what the substrate should produce IS options in the Sutton-Precup-Singh sense -- chunks represented as <initiation-set, intra-chunk policy, termination-condition> triples. The three-field structure is not optional decoration; it is what makes hierarchical planning over chunks well-defined. Without explicit termination-conditions, planning over chunked units becomes ill-defined: how does the agent know when the chunk has finished and the next planning step should occur? Without an explicit initiation-set, transfer across contexts becomes ill-defined: how does the agent know whether a learned chunk is applicable in a new state?

The alignment with the Martiros et al. 2018 biology (also in this pull) is striking. Striatal SPNs fire at chunk start and end -- that is the initiation-set + termination-condition signal in the biology. Striatal interneurons fire during mid-chunk -- that is the intra-option policy signal. The two-population coordination structure Martiros et al. recorded is a clean biological instantiation of the abstract three-field option structure Sutton-Precup-Singh defined 20 years earlier. The agreement between the foundational theory and the recent biology is reassuring -- it suggests that the options framework is not just a mathematical convenience but tracks something the substrate is actually doing.

For REE's MECH-317 implementation (and for MECH-323, the ARC-071 first child mechanism registered today as the chunk-formation operator), the substrate-design implication is to commit to the option-triple representation explicitly. Chunks should carry:
- An initiation-set: a learned predicate over state-features that defines what state-conditions activate the chunk.
- An intra-chunk policy: the stereotyped action-sequence (or closed-loop micro-policy) executed during the chunk.
- A termination-condition: a learned predicate over state-features (or a learned scalar threshold on chunk-progress) that defines when the chunk ends.

The MECH-323 registration today (parallel session) explicitly mirrors this structure (sequence + initiation_set + termination_condition fields plus a value_tag and replay_origin audit flag). The architectural commitment is therefore already in place at the claim-registry level; what remains is the substrate-implementation pass and a discriminative-pair validation experiment.

The mapping caveat I would not want to bury: Sutton, Precup & Singh 1999 is a foundational computational-theory paper. It establishes that the options framework IS mathematically well-defined and what guarantees it provides, but it does not prescribe which substrate should implement the option-discovery process. Many implementations are possible: hand-engineered options (the framework's original target), gradient option-discovery (Bacon, Harb & Precup 2017, also in this pull), diversity-driven skill discovery (Eysenbach et al. 2018, also in this pull), or hierarchical-Bayesian option-inference. The framework anchors the COMPUTATIONAL TARGET for MECH-317; choosing among substrate implementations of the target is a separate decision the substrate-design pass has to make. The other entries in this pull are the implementation-candidate adjudication.
