# Martiros, Burgess & Graybiel 2018 -- Inversely active striatal bracketing of behavioural chunks

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cub.2018.01.031), PMID 29452904), Martiros and colleagues record from dorsolateral striatum (DLS) in rats overtrained on a T-maze sequence and find a striking signature: the principal projection neurons (SPNs) fire phasically at the start and end of the learned sequence with relatively quiet activity through the middle, while local interneurons -- recorded simultaneously -- fire in the inverse pattern, elevated through the middle and quiet at the boundaries. The two populations are coordinated, not redundant. They are doing different jobs at different phases of the chunked behavioural unit. This is the most-direct empirical evidence to date for the start-stop bracketing signature that Smith & Graybiel 2013/2016 (already anchored in Pull 1) characterised at the review level. The novel contribution is the dual-population coordination structure: chunks are not represented by a continuous neural signal but by a transition pattern at boundaries, with within-chunk activity supplied by a different population.

For MECH-317 the substrate-implementation implication is sharper than what the review literature alone delivers. A naive reading of 'chunking substrate' might suggest a single chunk-detection signal -- one neuron-class or one learned head that fires when a chunk is active. Martiros et al.'s finding suggests instead that chunks need at least two coordinated signals: a boundary detector (the SPN-class signal, marking start and end) and a within-chunk continuation signal (the interneuron-class signal, marking mid-sequence). This maps onto the options framework distinction between initiation-set / termination-condition (the brackets) and the intra-option policy (the within-chunk behaviour). Sutton, Precup & Singh 1999 named this distinction at the theoretical level; Martiros et al. anchor it empirically in striatal biology.

For REE specifically, the substrate-design implication is that MECH-317's implementation needs to support both signals. The chunk-formation operator (which MECH-323 has been registered as in a parallel session today) builds chunks from repeated executions with consistent outcomes; the chunk itself needs to be represented with initiation-set, termination-condition, and intra-chunk policy fields. The two-population biology suggests these fields are not just bookkeeping convenience -- they correspond to functionally-distinct signals in the substrate. A V3 instantiation of MECH-317 should provide observable analogs of both: action-distribution entropy troughs at chunk-boundaries (the bracket signature, observable behaviourally) AND elevated mid-chunk action-determinism (the within-chunk continuation signature, also observable behaviourally).

The mapping caveat I would not want to bury: Martiros et al.'s evidence is in vivo single-unit recording on a specific behavioural paradigm (rat T-maze overtraining). REE's MECH-317 is going to have to be validated on a behavioural substrate (SD-054 reef forage-approach-retreat or an enriched analog), measuring action-distribution signatures rather than neural recording. The mapping from 'neural bracketing pattern' to 'behavioural-entropy bracketing pattern' is plausible because the behavioural manifestation is what the neural pattern produces -- but the strength of the signature on the behavioural side depends on how stereotyped the chunked sequence becomes. SD-054's reef forage-approach-retreat is a candidate; whether the substrate produces a strong enough chunk-boundary signature to detect is a substrate-readiness question similar to the one V3-EXQ-548 (substrate-readiness diagnostic landed earlier today) addressed for SD-054 bipartite layout.

What does failure look like? If a candidate MECH-317 substrate is built without explicit boundary-detection, the behavioural validation should fail to show entropy troughs at chunk boundaries -- the bracket signature won't appear. If the substrate is built with only boundary-detection and no within-chunk continuation signal, the agent should fail to maintain stereotyped within-chunk behaviour after the chunk is formed. Both signatures are falsifiable on a sufficiently structured behavioural substrate.

I rate this confidence 0.84. Source quality is high (Curr Biol, Graybiel-lab primary empirical recording with simultaneous SPN + interneuron isolation -- a technically demanding paper). Mapping fidelity is strong for the substrate-identification claim that DLS does chunking via bracket-defined coordination. Transfer risk is moderate because the behavioural-entropy bracketing signature has to be measured indirectly on REE (no direct neural-recording analog). This is the load-bearing direct-empirical anchor for MECH-317, complementary to the Smith & Graybiel review entries already in Pull 1.
