# Olafsdottir, Carpenter & Barry (2017) — Task Demands Predict a Dynamic Switch in Awake Hippocampal Replay

**Source:** Neuron, 96(4):925-935.e6. DOI: [10.1016/j.neuron.2017.09.035](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2017.09.035)
**PubMed:** PMID 29056296

## What the paper did

Olafsdottir et al. recorded simultaneously from CA1 place cells and deep-layer medial entorhinal cortex grid cells in rats performing a self-paced spatial foraging task. They characterised awake hippocampal replay during three distinct behavioural epochs: immediately before movement onset toward a reward, immediately after arrival at a reward, and during extended immobility periods. By comparing replay content across these epochs, they could ask whether the hippocampal reactivation is functionally differentiated by task state.

## Key findings

The central finding is a clean dissociation: replay immediately preceding or following movement preferentially encoded cells consistent with the current trajectory (task-focused, planning-relevant content). Replay during extended immobility showed no such bias (consistent with consolidation). Critically, the occurrence of task-focused reactivations predicted the accuracy of subsequent spatial decisions -- establishing a causal role for the planning-mode replay in behaviour. The entorhinal grid cell coherence finding added a further dissociation: grid cells replayed coherently with hippocampus only during extended immobility (consolidation mode), not during task-focused epochs. This suggests the hippocampus switches between two operationally distinct modes, each engaging different upstream circuits.

## REE translation -- ARC-018

This paper is important for ARC-018 because it directly addresses the dual-mode operation that the viability map claim implies. ARC-018 claims that the hippocampus generates explicit rollouts (before action) and updates the viability map (after outcome). Olafsdottir et al. provide the clearest evidence that these two functions are not just conceptually distinct -- they are operationally segregated within awake replay, occurring in different behavioural windows and predicting different outcomes. The pre-movement, task-focused reactivations are the rollout generation phase; the post-reward reactivations are the beginning of the viability map update. The fact that the post-reward reactivations are also trajectory-focused (not consolidation-mode) is consistent with E3 harm/goal error arriving at the hippocampus at outcome time and gating update content.

Where the corrected V3 framing finds particular support: the mode-switching itself. The finding that task demands predict replay content -- rather than replay being a fixed, post-hoc process -- implies that some top-down signal is modulating hippocampal replay gating. In REE terms, this would be the E3 commitment/outcome signal that switches the hippocampus from rollout-generation mode to map-update mode. Where the biology is agnostic: again, spatial coordinates. The extended immobility / consolidation mode finding (with grid cell coherence) is also not directly predicted by ARC-018 and may indicate a broader memory consolidation function that ARC-018 does not fully account for.

## Limitations and caveats

The task is spatial navigation with reward. No aversive outcomes are tested -- whether the same mode-switch occurs after harm or near-miss events is unknown and would be directly relevant to ARC-018's harm-viability component. The grid cell coherence finding in consolidation mode raises a question ARC-018 does not currently address: what is the role of entorhinal-hippocampal interactions in viability map maintenance? If consolidation mode is necessary to write the viability map into long-term structure, ARC-018 may need an additional architectural element beyond the current description.

## Confidence reasoning

Solid electrophysiology with a clean behavioural dissociation and a functional consequence (decision accuracy). The dual-mode finding is the most direct evidence available for the rollout/update distinction ARC-018 requires. Confidence is held at 0.78 because the spatial context limits generalisation to action-object coordinates, and the harm component is absent.
