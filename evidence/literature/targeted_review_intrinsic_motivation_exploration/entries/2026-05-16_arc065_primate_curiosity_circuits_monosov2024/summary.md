## What the paper did

Monosov reviewed primate (macaque and human) neural circuit data on curiosity, distinguishing two separable components: novelty-based exploration (which is driven by an animal's detection that a stimulus is new relative to memory) and uncertainty-based information seeking (which is driven by a desire to reduce prediction uncertainty, even when the information obtained has no immediate reward value). The review synthesised single-unit recordings, fMRI, and lesion/pharmacology studies, and included a section on how curiosity-related behaviours emerge during postnatal development.

## Key findings relevant to the claim

The most important finding for REE's MECH-314 sub-flavour question is that novelty-based and uncertainty-based curiosity are anatomically and functionally dissociable in primates. The amygdala, anterior cingulate, and ventral striatum contribute to novelty detection (a stimulus-is-new signal, tied to the MECH-314a striatal novelty sub-flavour). The frontopolar cortex and basolateral amygdala contribute to uncertainty-driven information seeking -- the desire to explore options that are imperfectly known, regardless of novelty per se (MECH-314b). Dopaminergic projections carry both types of signal but with different patterns of modulation.

This anatomical dissociation provides the strongest available biological support for maintaining the MECH-314a/MECH-314b sub-flavour split rather than collapsing them (which is what Q-044 asks). A novelty-only curiosity mechanism would miss the uncertainty-tracking component; an uncertainty-only mechanism would miss the rapid novelty detection that uses memory to flag unfamiliar stimuli without full uncertainty computation.

The developmental section notes that curiosity-related behaviours emerge robustly during postnatal development, are shaped by early environmental experience, and persist across the lifespan. This provides biological context for why an infant-stage training substrate might show different curiosity profiles than a mature agent: the circuit is developing its calibration during early experience.

## REE translation

For ARC-065, this review confirms the two-substrate architecture that the ARC-065 synthesis recommended: MECH-314a (novelty-based, striatal) and MECH-314b (uncertainty-based, frontopolar) are genuine distinct biological mechanisms. Q-044 is partially answered by the anatomy: the sub-flavours are not three readings of one mechanism -- at least two of the three (314a/314b) have separate anatomical substrates in primates.

For the infant-stage design question: the review's note on early environmental shaping of curiosity circuits supports the view that environment richness during the infant stage shapes the calibration of the curiosity machinery, not just the coverage achieved by exploration.

## Limitations and caveats

This review focuses on detection/computation mechanisms rather than on the consequences of curiosity for learning outcomes. It does not directly address when curiosity bonuses fail to produce useful world models (the design question). The developmental content is brief and does not provide quantitative guidance on how much environmental richness is necessary for curiosity circuits to calibrate well. The primate-to-REE mapping involves the usual computational-to-biological abstraction step.
