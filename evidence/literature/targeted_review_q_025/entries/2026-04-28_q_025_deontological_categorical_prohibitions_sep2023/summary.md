# SEP: Deontological Ethics -- bracket on Q-025

## What the entry says

The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry on deontological ethics is the
canonical academic reference for the genre of answer Q-025 expects: a short
list of acts that are categorically forbidden, regardless of consequences,
because the wrongness inheres in the act-type rather than the outcome.

The entry organises the canonical prohibitions under two structural framings.
The agent-centered framing focuses on the agent's own conduct -- "keeping
one's own moral house in order" -- and produces prohibitions like "do not
intend evil as means or ends," supported by the doctrine of double effect.
The patient-centered framing focuses on the rights of those acted upon and
yields the Means Principle: persons must not be used merely as means for
producing good consequences without their consent. Either framing produces
overlapping prohibitions: killing innocents, torture, using persons as mere
means, lying, breaking promises.

Threshold deontology then complicates the picture: most contemporary
deontologists allow that at some level of catastrophic consequence the
categorical norms are overridden, though the threshold itself can be fixed
or sliding.

## Why this matters for Q-025

Q-025 asks what acts are strictly forbidden by REE's five axioms (INV-025
through INV-029, plus INV-042 as the derived-objectives summary). The SEP
entry does not answer that question directly -- REE's axioms are not the
standard deontological starting points. But it supplies three useful
bracketing structures.

First, it confirms that "acts forbidden by structural prerequisites of
agency" is a coherent and well-developed philosophical tradition, so Q-025
is a well-posed question. Second, it provides candidate prohibitions whose
mapping onto REE is plausible: prohibitions on destroying other minds map
onto INV-028 (shared-world ethics); prohibitions on instrumentalising
persons map onto INV-028 and INV-029 (love as care-investment treats the
beloved as end, not means); prohibitions on acts that undermine one's own
agency map onto INV-026 (self as prerequisite). Third, it raises the
threshold question: is REE's list absolute, or threshold-sensitive? That
itself is a separable open question that Q-025 should disentangle from the
list-question.

## Where the mapping is honest about its limits

The mapping is indirect. REE's axioms include premises that have no clean
analogue in standard deontology: INV-025 (irreducible uncertainty as
epistemic constraint), INV-027 (external world as structural prerequisite
for grounded representation), and INV-029 (love as long-horizon
care-investment, framed as structurally coherent rather than sentimental).
Whatever falls out of these is not derivable from the SEP entry. So this
paper is a bracket, not a direct hit.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence is held at 0.45 -- below the 0.5 threshold for "moderate support"
-- to reflect that the mapping_fidelity is genuinely partial. The source
quality is high (authoritative encyclopedia) and transfer risk is moderate,
but for a question whose answer requires deriving a specific list from a
specific non-standard set of axioms, an entry that establishes only the
genre and supplies candidate items cannot be counted as strong evidence.
This is appropriate for a low-priority "no_evidence_for_open_question"
backlog item: it documents what the philosophical neighbourhood looks like
without overclaiming.
