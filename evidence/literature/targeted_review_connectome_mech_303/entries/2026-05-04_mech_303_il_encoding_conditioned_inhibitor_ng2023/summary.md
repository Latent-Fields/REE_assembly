# IL Single-Unit Encoding of Conditioned Inhibitors (Ng & Sangha 2023)

**Source:** Ng KH, Sangha S. "Encoding of conditioned inhibitors of fear in the infralimbic cortex." *Cerebral Cortex* 33(9):5658-5670, 2023. DOI: [10.1093/cercor/bhac450](https://doi.org/10.1093/cercor/bhac450). From PubMed (PMID 36411540).

**What the study did.** Single-unit recordings in the IL of male Long Evans rats performing a three-cue Pavlovian conditional discrimination: fear cue (tone->shock), reward cue (tone->sucrose), safety cue (light, no shock). Compound cues were also presented (fear+safety simultaneously). The study characterised IL firing responses to each cue type and examined correlations between neural activity and freezing behaviour.

**Key findings.** A large proportion of IL neurons showed excitatory responses specifically to the fear+safety compound cue. Three functionally distinct subpopulations emerged: (1) neurons responding exclusively to the fear+safety compound (safety-specific); (2) neurons responding to both the fear+safety compound and the reward cue (shared); (3) bidirectional neurons, excited by fear+safety and inhibited by the pure fear cue. Crucially, IL neural activity was negatively correlated with freezing during the fear+safety compound: more IL firing meant less fear expression. Safety-cue-alone responses were rare; IL encoding appeared to require the conflict context (safety in the presence of threat).

**REE translation.** This is the electrophysiological complement to the IL->CeA circuit paper (Ng & Sangha 2023 Neuropsychopharmacology). Together they show that IL encodes safety-cue information as an active representation (not merely the absence of fear-cue responding) and that this representation drives the output that suppresses fear commitment. MECH-303 requires the IL-equivalent to hold 'this context predicts suffering absent' as an active predictive state. The single-unit data confirm that the IL contains neurons that encode this prediction, with activity anti-correlating with the avoidance behaviour that MECH-303's commitment-release gating should suppress.

The partial overlap with reward-responding neurons is a direct qualifier on MECH-303's claim that the safety-prediction store is distinct from the 'things liked' store. Subpopulation (2) -- neurons responsive to both fear+safety and reward -- suggests the stores are not fully orthogonal; some neurons participate in both. This is an honest complication the claim should acknowledge. Whether this shared subpopulation reflects convergent output routing (separate stores, shared downstream node) or genuine store mixing is not resolvable from these data.

**Limitations and caveats.** The compound-cue context conflates 'safety prediction' with 'conflict resolution'. Most recorded neurons responded to the compound but not safety-alone, suggesting what is being encoded is primarily the cognitive conflict signal -- safety overriding fear -- rather than the standalone safety prediction that MECH-303 targets. Only IL was recorded; the vHipp-PL pathway posited by MECH-303 and Meyer 2019 is not assessed. Male rats only.

**Confidence: 0.84.** Best available direct electrophysiology of IL safety encoding in a well-matched paradigm. The partial reward-neuron overlap is a genuine qualifier but not a falsification.
