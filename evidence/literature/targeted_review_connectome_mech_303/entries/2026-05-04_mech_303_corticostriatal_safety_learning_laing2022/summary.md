# Cortico-Striatal Safety Learning via Conditioned Inhibition (Laing et al. 2022)

**Source:** Laing PAF, Steward T, et al. "Cortico-Striatal Activity Characterizes Human Safety Learning via Pavlovian Conditioned Inhibition." *J Neuroscience* 42(25):5047-5057, 2022. DOI: [10.1523/JNEUROSCI.2181-21.2022](https://doi.org/10.1523/JNEUROSCI.2181-21.2022). From PubMed (PMID 35577553).

**What the study did.** 49 healthy human participants underwent ultra-high field (7T) fMRI while performing a Pavlovian safety-learning task. Two types of safety signal were contrasted within-subjects: (1) a conditioned inhibitor (CI), which actively predicted threat omission in the presence of a known threat signal (A+/AX-) -- this is the 'earned' safety signal; (2) a standard safety signal (SS), which was passively unpaired with threat (BC-) -- this is the 'unearned' contextual safety signal. Both produced equivalent autonomic and subjective safety learning, but diverged strongly in brain activation.

**Key findings.** The conditioned inhibitor drove dorsal striatum, anterior insula, and dorsolateral PFC activation. The standard safety signal drove vmPFC, posterior cingulate, and hippocampus. Further analysis of the CI showed initial learning was characterised by dorsal striatum, midbrain, thalamus, premotor cortex, and PFC engagement -- a full cortico-striatal learning loop. The SS exclusively engaged the cortical safety-related regions (vmPFC/hippocampus). Both signals evoked equivalent behavioural outcomes but via neurally separable routes.

**REE translation and the MECH-303 complication.** This paper creates a fork in the MECH-303 claim that is worth naming explicitly. MECH-303 posits: (a) a predictive encoding store for 'this context predicts suffering absent'; (b) that this store routes through prefrontal-hippocampal substrate rather than ventral-striatal; (c) that the teaching signal is the MECH-302 relief-completion event.

Criterion (c) makes MECH-303's store most analogous to the conditioned inhibitor in this study: the safety prediction is actively learned from a teaching signal. But the conditioned inhibitor here engages the *dorsal striatum*, not vmPFC/hippocampus. If that mapping holds, MECH-303's substrate claim is wrong in the direction.

The standard safety signal (passively unpaired) does engage vmPFC/hippocampus -- consistent with MECH-303's anatomical claim -- but this corresponds to a passively-acquired contextual safety representation, not one driven by an explicit teaching signal.

Whether MECH-303 should be mapped to the CI pathway or the SS pathway is a genuine architectural question this paper surfaces. One resolution: the initial *encoding* of a teaching-signal-driven safety prediction may use cortico-striatal circuits (CI pathway), while the subsequent *retrieval/expression* uses vmPFC/hippocampal circuits (SS pathway). If so, MECH-303 is describing the retrieval/expression substrate, not the encoding loop -- which would need to be specified in the claim.

**Limitations and caveats.** No single-unit resolution; encoding vs. retrieval confounded. Healthy adults only. The CI vs. SS paradigm distinction may not map cleanly onto the MECH-302 teaching-signal concept -- the CI in this study is driven by threat-signal pairing, not by relief-completion.

**Confidence: 0.76.** High-quality human 7T fMRI with a principled within-subjects safety-signal dissociation. Confidence docked for mapping-fidelity uncertainty -- the CI/SS distinction cuts across MECH-303's substrate claim in a way that requires architectural resolution.
