# IL->CeA Circuit for Safety Expression (Ng & Sangha 2023)

**Source:** Ng K, Pollock M, et al. "Suppressing fear in the presence of a safety cue requires infralimbic cortical signaling to central amygdala." *Neuropsychopharmacology* 49(2):359-367, 2024. DOI: [10.1038/s41386-023-01598-0](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41386-023-01598-0). From PubMed (PMID 37188848).

**What the study did.** Ng and Sangha used an intersectional chemogenetic approach to silence projection-specific populations of IL neurons in male Long Evans rats. Animals learned a three-way conditional discrimination: reward cue predicts sucrose, fear cue predicts footshock, and safety cue co-presented with the fear cue predicts *no* footshock. Once this discrimination was acquired, chemogenetic inhibition was applied to either IL->BLA neurons or IL->CeA neurons during recall sessions. The key question was whether one of these two projection targets could account for the previously-established necessity of global IL activity for safety expression.

**Key findings.** Silencing IL->CeA neurons abolished safety expression: freezing to the fear+safety compound rose to the level of fear-cue-alone, erasing the suppression. Silencing IL->BLA neurons had no effect on safety expression -- animals continued to show normal fear suppression during the compound cue, and reward-port behaviour was unaffected in both conditions. Virus-free CNO controls and histological misses confirmed that the CNO itself had no non-specific effect. In vivo recordings confirmed the peak inhibition window (40-80 minutes post-injection) was correctly timed with behavioural assessment.

**REE translation.** MECH-303 posits a prefrontal (IL-equivalent) substrate that encodes 'this context predicts suffering will be absent' and uses that prediction to gate commitment-release thresholds and shape approach toward safety-associated stimuli. This paper provides the downstream output circuit: the IL->CeA projection is the necessary path by which the encoded safety prediction is converted into the fear-suppression behaviour. In REE terms, the CeA is the immediate effector of the commitment-release gate -- when the IL predicts safety and signals via this projection, avoidance commitment threshold drops. The clean BLA null result rules out the simpler story (IL->BLA driving extinction consolidation) and isolates a distinct recall pathway.

**Limitations and caveats.** The study tests expression (recall) of learned safety, not acquisition. Whether IL->CeA is also required to initially build the safety-prediction store is not tested. Female rats in this paradigm typically fail to suppress fear to the safety compound -- a sex difference the authors attribute to differential IL function -- raising questions about whether the mechanism generalises. Most importantly, this paper establishes the output limb of the circuit (IL->CeA for expression), not the input/encoding limb (what writes into the safety-prediction store). MECH-303's most novel claim is about the parallel encoding store and its teaching signal, which this paper does not directly address. That said, a clean projection-level dissociation from the BLA pathway in a well-controlled chemogenetic study is a high-quality substrate marker for the MECH-303 IL node.

**Confidence: 0.82.** High methodological quality and direct circuit relevance. Docked for the expression-vs-encoding ambiguity and the rodent-male-only scope.
