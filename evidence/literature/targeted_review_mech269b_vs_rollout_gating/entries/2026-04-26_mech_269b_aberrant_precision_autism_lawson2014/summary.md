# Lawson, Rees & Friston 2014 -- An aberrant precision account of autism

## What the paper did

Lawson, Rees & Friston extended the precision-pathology framework, previously applied to psychosis, to autism. They argued that many features of autistic perception and behaviour can be attributed to imbalanced precision in hierarchical predictive coding -- specifically, sensory-evidence precision being too high relative to the precision of prior beliefs. Sensory hypersensitivity follows because sensory channels are over-weighted; intolerance of unpredictable environments follows because the system cannot rely on priors to absorb sensory variance; repetitive routines follow because behaviour can fix the prior at high precision when the sensory channel will not yield. The paper engages with empirical findings -- electrophysiology, psychophysics, behavioural observations -- and interprets them under this precision-imbalance account.

## Key findings relevant to MECH-269b

Two relevant signals for MECH-269b. First, the failure-mode argument (a companion to Adams 2013 in a different clinical context): when precision is mis-set, behavioural revision is impaired in characteristic, predictable ways, which inferentially backs MECH-269b architectural claim that precision is the relevant gating signal in the first place. Second, and more interestingly, Lawson et al. note that different sensory modalities can show different precision profiles in autism -- some patients are hypersensitive in audition but not vision, or vice versa. This is the closest available clinical correlate of MECH-269b per-stream V_s assumption: precision is not a single global parameter, it is set independently for different anatomical streams.

This is tag (c) inferential support plus tag (d) failure-mode evidence, with a small but useful gesture toward (a) per-stream precision in the per-modality observations.

## How the findings translate to REE

The translation parallels Adams 2013. REE wired-but-inert hypothesis predicts that an architecture without V_s gating on cortical rollouts will exhibit autism-analog as well as psychosis-analog failure modes: behavioural rigidity, intolerance of variability, over-reliance on routines that fix priors at high precision. The per-modality precision observation in Lawson et al. is the most relevant additional signal -- it suggests that the brain already computes precision at the per-stream rather than per-system level, which is the empirical move MECH-269b extends to the planning loop.

## Limitations and caveats

Three caveats. First, the paper is theoretical -- it interprets existing empirical findings under a precision framework rather than measuring precision directly. Competing accounts (weak-central-coherence, enhanced-perceptual-functioning, predictive-impairment) make overlapping predictions and are not directly adjudicated. Second, the per-modality precision observation is suggestive but not a definitive demonstration of per-stream gating. The clinical heterogeneity that supports it could equally be explained by per-region rather than per-stream differences in precision. Third, hippocampal involvement is not a focus of the paper, so the symmetric-application question (does autism manifest in both hippocampal proposer and cortical world-model precision?) is unaddressed -- tag (d) measurement gap.
