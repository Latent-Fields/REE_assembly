## Source Wording

Sharp-wave ripples (SWRs) are the most synchronous population pattern in the mammalian brain. They are generated by CA3 recurrent excitatory dynamics during offline/quiescent states (rest, slow-wave sleep, inter-trial intervals). SWRs replay compressed (~10-20x faster) sequences of recent experience, transferring them to distributed cortical circuits for consolidation. Disrupting SWRs during rest impairs subsequent memory performance. SWRs require no external input — they are internally generated, consistent with their occurrence during quiescence. Longer or artificially prolonged SWRs improve memory; truncated SWRs slow learning.

## Caveat

Biological SWR replay operates over seconds-to-minutes timescales during rest or sleep; REE micro-DMN replay is designed for within-session inter-action gaps (sub-second to seconds). This timescale compression is a REE architectural choice with no direct biological precedent at the same timescale. The CA3 recurrent dynamics implementing SWRs are not currently present in REEAssembly's HippocampalModule — V3 will require an architectural analog, not a direct implementation.
