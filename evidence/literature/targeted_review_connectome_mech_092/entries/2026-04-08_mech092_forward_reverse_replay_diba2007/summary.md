# Diba & Buzsaki 2007 -- Forward and Reverse Hippocampal Place-Cell Sequences During Ripples

**Source:** Diba K, Buzsaki G. *Forward and reverse hippocampal place-cell sequences during ripples.* Nature Neuroscience 10(10):1241-1242, 2007. DOI: 10.1038/nn1961

## What was done

Diba and Buzsaki recorded from ensembles of hippocampal CA1 place cells in rats running back and forth on an elevated linear track. They analyzed the temporal structure of place cell firing sequences during active running (under theta oscillations) and during periods of immobility at the track ends (associated with sharp-wave ripple events in the local field potential). By computing pairwise temporal offsets between cell pairs during running and comparing these to offsets during SWR-associated bursts, they could identify whether replay sequences preserved, reversed, or scrambled the spatial ordering observed during behavior.

## Key findings

The central finding is that awake SWR events contain both forward and reverse replay of recently experienced place cell sequences, and the direction depends on the behavioral context. At the end of a completed run, place cells replay the just-traversed trajectory in reverse temporal order. Before the start of the next run, cells fire in forward order, effectively "pre-playing" the upcoming trajectory. Critically, the spatial distances between place fields are preserved in the temporal structure of these compressed sequences -- the replay is not merely sequential reactivation but a faithful, temporally compressed reconstruction of spatial experience. The temporal compression factor is substantial (on the order of 10-20x relative to behavioral timescale), consistent with the timescale MECH-092 specifies.

## Mapping to MECH-092

MECH-092 asserts that during quiescent E3 heartbeat cycles, the hippocampal module performs SWR-equivalent replay, replaying compressed recent trajectory experience for viability map consolidation. Diba & Buzsaki 2007 provides direct empirical evidence for two of this claim's core mechanistic features: (1) replay occurs during awake quiescent periods (immobility at track ends, coinciding with SWR events), and (2) replay is temporally compressed to approximately 10-20x real-time speed while preserving the spatial structure of the original trajectory.

The bidirectional nature of replay deserves careful consideration for MECH-092's mapping. Reverse replay of just-completed trajectories maps straightforwardly onto viability map consolidation -- the hippocampal module recapitulates recent experience to strengthen or update map representations. Forward replay (preplay) of upcoming trajectories adds a planning dimension that MECH-092 does not explicitly address but which is not inconsistent with it. One might argue that forward replay represents the hippocampal module "testing" candidate trajectories against the viability map during quiescent periods, effectively combining consolidation with prospective evaluation. This dual function would be an enrichment of MECH-092's scope rather than a contradiction.

## Limitations

This study is correlational -- it characterizes the content and structure of awake replay but does not test whether replay serves a consolidation function (as opposed to retrieval, reward evaluation, or planning). The linear track paradigm is considerably simpler than a full viability map, with only two possible trajectory directions. The study cannot determine whether replay occurs specifically during "quiescent" states in the sense MECH-092 intends (no salient incoming event, low-demand period) versus simply during any period of immobility -- the distinction matters because immobility at track ends may reflect deliberation or reward consumption rather than quiescence per se.

The forward replay component complicates a pure consolidation narrative. If replay serves planning as well as consolidation, then MECH-092's framing as purely consolidation-oriented may be incomplete. This is not a weakening of the claim so much as a suggestion that the mechanism may be functionally richer than MECH-092 currently specifies.

## Confidence reasoning

Source quality is high (Nature Neuroscience, rigorous methods from the Buzsaki lab, foundational paper in the field). Mapping fidelity is good for the temporal compression and awake-SWR association, but the study does not test the consolidation function specifically. Transfer risk is moderate given the simplicity of the linear track relative to viability map navigation. Overall confidence: 0.78.
