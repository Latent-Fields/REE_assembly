# Jadhav et al. 2012 -- Awake Hippocampal Sharp-Wave Ripples Support Spatial Memory

**Source:** Jadhav SP, Kemere C, German PW, Frank LM. *Awake Hippocampal Sharp-Wave Ripples Support Spatial Memory.* Science 336(6087):1454-1458, 2012. DOI: 10.1126/science.1217230

## What was done

Jadhav and colleagues performed a causal disruption experiment in rats learning a spatial alternation task on a W-shaped maze. Rats were implanted with tetrodes in hippocampal CA1 for recording place cell activity and local field potentials, plus stimulation electrodes in the ventral hippocampal commissure for SWR disruption. An online detection system monitored ripple-band power and, upon SWR detection, delivered single-pulse electrical stimulation within 25 milliseconds -- terminating the ripple and transiently inhibiting CA1 spiking for approximately 100ms. Three groups were compared over 8 days of learning: SWR disruption (n=6), yoked control stimulation (n=4), and unstimulated controls (n=4). The task required rats to alternate between the two outer arms of the W-track, with outbound trials (choosing the correct arm from center) requiring working memory and inbound trials (returning to center from a side arm) requiring only reference memory.

## Key findings

Disrupting awake SWRs produced a persistent, specific learning deficit in the outbound (working memory) component of the task. Control animals reached criterion performance on outbound trials; SWR-disrupted animals never fully acquired the rule despite 8 days of training. Critically, inbound (reference memory) performance was unaffected by SWR disruption. Place field representations were preserved, and sleep-associated replay remained intact -- the deficit was specifically attributable to awake SWR replay, not to degraded spatial coding or sleep consolidation processes. The authors emphasize that outbound performance requires linking immediate and more remote past experience to current reward contingencies, precisely the kind of integration that compressed awake replay is well positioned to support.

## Mapping to MECH-092

MECH-092 claims that during quiescent E3 heartbeat cycles, the hippocampal module performs SWR-equivalent replay to consolidate the viability map. Jadhav 2012 provides perhaps the strongest available causal evidence for this claim's biological plausibility. The finding that awake SWR disruption specifically impairs working memory -- the ability to integrate recent trajectory experience into ongoing decisions -- maps directly onto viability map consolidation. If the hippocampal module cannot replay recently traversed trajectories during quiescent periods, it cannot update its model of navigable space, and trajectory selection degrades. The preservation of reference memory under disruption is also informative: it suggests awake replay is not needed for well-consolidated spatial knowledge, only for the ongoing integration of new trajectory information -- exactly the role MECH-092 assigns to quiescent replay within session.

## Limitations

The disruption targeted all detectable awake SWRs, not specifically those occurring during quiescent/immobility periods. MECH-092 specifically ties replay to quiescent E3 heartbeat cycles (no salient incoming event). However, awake SWRs occur predominantly during periods of quiet wakefulness and immobility, so the functional overlap is substantial. The W-track alternation task, while requiring trajectory-based spatial reasoning, is considerably simpler than full viability map navigation in a complex environment. The study also cannot distinguish whether the deficit arises from impaired consolidation per se or from impaired online retrieval/evaluation during the replay event itself -- a distinction relevant to whether MECH-092's consolidation framing is correct versus a more retrieval-oriented account.

## Confidence reasoning

Source quality is excellent (Science, well-controlled causal manipulation with appropriate yoked controls, adequate sample sizes). Mapping fidelity is strong: the specific working memory deficit maps cleanly onto viability map consolidation. The main transfer risk is the non-selective disruption of all awake SWRs rather than quiescence-specific ones, and the simplicity of the task relative to MECH-092's viability map scope. Overall confidence: 0.82.
