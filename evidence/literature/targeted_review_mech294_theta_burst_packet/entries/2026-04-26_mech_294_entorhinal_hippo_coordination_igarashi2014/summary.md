# Igarashi et al. 2014 -- Coordination of entorhinal-hippocampal ensemble activity during associative learning

## What the paper did

Igarashi and colleagues (Moser lab) recorded LFP and ensemble activity simultaneously from lateral entorhinal cortex (LEC), medial entorhinal cortex (MEC), and CA1 of rats learning an olfactory-spatial associative task. They tracked the development of ensemble representations and inter-region oscillatory coupling across days of learning. The headline result: as rats learned to use an odour cue to guide navigational behaviour, 20-40 Hz oscillatory coherence emerged specifically between distal CA1 and LEC -- and only between these subregions (not MEC-proximal-CA1). The coherence was time-locked to the learning of the cue-context association, not to general task engagement.

## Key findings relevant to MECH-294

This is the closest result in the systems-neuroscience literature to MECH-294's joint-binding hypothesis. Two semantically-distinct content streams -- olfactory cue (LEC) and spatial context (CA1) -- become phase-coherent in the same theta-organised window precisely as the learning of their conjunction takes place. That is direct empirical support for the substrate MECH-294 needs: the brain can develop, on a learning timescale, joint binding of distinct content streams within a phase-aligned window.

For MECH-294, this paper supplies the strongest "the architecture exists in biology" evidence, with the caveat that the demonstrated case has two streams, not four, and the streams are encoding-side (cue + context) rather than planning-side (goal + action + risk).

## How the findings translate to REE

MECH-294 extends Igarashi in two directions. First, two-stream to four-stream: where Igarashi demonstrated cue + context joint binding, MECH-294 needs goal_latent + action_proposal + risk_estimate + state_summary jointly bound. Whether the LEC-CA1 circuit (or any other theta-organised circuit) has channel capacity for four heterogeneous streams without interference is an open empirical question. Second, encoding-side to mixed encoding/planning: MECH-294's action and risk streams are sourced from BG and amygdala/ACC respectively, not LEC, and the cross-region theta-coherence relationships of those source regions are less well-characterised.

## Limitations and caveats

Three caveats. First, two-stream binding does not licence four-stream binding without further evidence; channel capacity is an open question. Second, the bound streams here are encoding-side; MECH-294's planning streams arrive from regions not recorded in this paper. Third, the demonstrated coherence is at 20-40 Hz, which spans the high-theta / low-gamma boundary, and the paper does not directly resolve the within-cycle gamma sub-slot structure -- the joint-binding architecture is inferred from the coherence pattern rather than directly observed at sub-cycle resolution.
