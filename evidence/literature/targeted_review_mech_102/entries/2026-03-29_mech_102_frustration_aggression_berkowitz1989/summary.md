## What the paper did

Berkowitz's 1989 paper in *Psychological Bulletin* is a reformulation of the original Dollard et al. (1939) frustration-aggression hypothesis. The original hypothesis was blunt: frustration always causes aggression, and aggression is always caused by frustration. Empirical inconsistencies accumulated over fifty years forced a more careful analysis. Berkowitz's revision holds that frustration generates aggressive inclinations *to the degree that it arouses negative affect* -- a weaker, more defensible claim. The cognitive-neoassociationistic model he introduces treats frustration as one instance of aversive stimulation, capable of generating fight-or-flight responses via negative affect as an automatic mediator. Interpretations and attributions modulate the intensity of the aversive experience but do not determine the core mechanism. The paper also reviews evidence that frustrations generate aggressive inclinations even when they are not arbitrary or personally targeted -- the structural blockage matters independently of its social meaning.

## Key findings

The central empirical claim is that blocked goal attainment -- specifically, interference with an expected reward -- generates negative affect, which in turn generates aggressive inclination as an automatic, associatively mediated response. This is strongest when the agent appraises the blockage as aversive and the aggressive response as capable of removing or retaliating against the block. Berkowitz also notes that competitive encounters are inherently frustrating because contestants mutually block each other's goal attainment, and that escalation in competitive contexts follows from accumulated mutual frustration. The reformulation preserves the core directional relationship (blocked path -> violence tendency) while weakening the strict determinism of the original.

## REE translation: grounding MECH-102

The most important structural parallel to MECH-102 is this: Berkowitz locates the trigger for violence in the closure of the expected pathway to a goal, not in the mere existence of a goal. An organism pursuing a goal by a preferred (low-energy) route that is blocked does not automatically escalate -- it first tries alternative paths, displaces the goal, or resigns. Violence-directed aggression is the response when those alternatives are also unavailable or when the blockage is appraised as sufficiently aversive. This is directionally consistent with MECH-102's claim that violence is selected after low-energy coordination pathways have been exhausted or destroyed.

The mapping challenge is real, however. MECH-102 describes an architectural selection mechanism in an error-correction system: the agent's forward model projects that no coherent future is reachable via low-energy paths, so a high-energy corrective is selected. Berkowitz describes an affective-motivational process in individual humans: blocked goals generate negative affect, which predisposes toward aggression via associative networks. The mechanisms are different. MECH-102 is computational and structural; Berkowitz is emotional and associative. REE does not claim that violence is selected because the agent feels bad -- it claims violence is selected because the trajectory space accessible through coordination has collapsed.

## Limitations and caveats

The most important caveat is that Berkowitz's model does not require exhaustion of alternatives before aggression is triggered. Frustration can induce aggressive inclinations even when non-violent alternatives remain open -- if the blockage is sufficiently aversive, the fight response can override the exploration of alternatives. This is a partial disconfirmation of MECH-102's 'terminal' framing: MECH-102 claims violence occurs only after pathway exhaustion, while Berkowitz's evidence shows it can occur earlier. This reflects a real asymmetry: Berkowitz is describing what humans actually do under frustration (often selecting aggression before alternatives are exhausted), while MECH-102 is describing what a well-designed error-correction system should do (select violence only when lower-energy corrections have failed). The former is descriptive; the latter is architectural and normative.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence sits at 0.70. Source quality is high -- Berkowitz's paper is a landmark reformulation in a high-impact journal, methodologically sound, and empirically well-grounded. The directional support for MECH-102 is genuine: blocked pathway -> violence tendency is the core claim of both. The confidence ceiling is imposed by the mechanism mismatch (affective vs. architectural) and the missing 'terminal' constraint in Berkowitz's model. This paper provides good grounding for the individual-level phenomenology of MECH-102 but cannot alone establish the architectural claim.
