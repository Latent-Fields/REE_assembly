## What the paper did

Anderson and Bushman's 2002 review in *Annual Review of Psychology* presents the General Aggression Model (GAM) as a unifying theoretical framework for human aggression research. The GAM integrates previously domain-limited theories -- cognitive neoassociation, social learning, script theory, excitation transfer -- into a single episodic and developmental model. At the episodic level, the GAM posits that situational inputs (provocation, frustration, pain, aggressive cues) and person variables (trait anger, aggressive scripts, desensitisation) jointly determine an internal state of cognition, affect, and arousal. This state is then subject to appraisal and reappraisal, producing either immediate (impulsive) or considered (thoughtful) action. The developmental component explains how chronic aggression-related experiences build knowledge structures that lower the threshold for aggressive responses over time.

## Key findings

The GAM's most important contribution for MECH-102 is the appraisal/reappraisal cycle. Before aggression is enacted, the model allows for a period of evaluation in which the agent considers available response options. Thoughtful appraisal -- when time and cognitive resources permit -- involves considering alternatives to aggression. Impulsive action bypasses this and executes the dominant activated script. The gap between these two modes is significant for MECH-102: thoughtful appraisal corresponds to the full trajectory evaluation that REE requires before violence is selected; impulsive action corresponds to commitment-architecture failure where the agent cannot model consequences beyond the immediate step.

The GAM also explicitly models the escalation of chronic aggression through feedback loops: aggressive encounters generate hostile attribution biases that further lower the aggression threshold, consistent with MECH-102's scale-invariance claim. At the individual level, patterns established through repeated aggressive episodes generalise to institutional and civilizational dynamics through the same knowledge-structure mechanisms.

## REE translation: grounding MECH-102

The GAM provides useful scaffolding for the REE architecture of violence selection, but the mapping is selective. The 'thoughtful action' pathway -- where the agent appraises all available responses before selecting violence -- is consistent with MECH-102's requirement that low-energy pathways be evaluated and found unavailable before violence is selected. The 'impulsive action' pathway corresponds precisely to MECH-102's condition (c): commitment architecture failure. Where the GAM enriches MECH-102 is in its developmental feedback loop: chronic exposure to violent contexts degrades the appraisal mechanism itself, raising the probability of impulsive action and lowering the threshold for violence. This maps onto institutional and civilizational dynamics where the coordination infrastructure has been eroded by prior conflict.

The complication is sharper here than with Berkowitz. The GAM's most empirically well-supported phenomena -- hostile attribution bias, aggressive script activation, desensitisation -- are precisely the failure modes MECH-102 is designed to prevent. These are conditions where violence is selected before pathway exhaustion because the appraisal mechanism has been corrupted. From an architectural standpoint, this suggests that MECH-102 is describing the ideal function of the error-correction system, while the GAM's empirical findings are descriptions of common architectural failures.

## Limitations and caveats

The GAM does not require prior exhaustion of non-violent alternatives before aggression is triggered. Indeed, the model's most robust empirical findings concern conditions where aggression occurs without exhaustion: hostile attribution, script activation, dehumanisation. This is not a weakness in the GAM -- it is an accurate description of human psychology -- but it means the GAM provides only partial support for MECH-102. The paper also does not use 'energy cost' reasoning or explicitly distinguish coordination-preserving from coordination-destroying interventions. The translational work required to get from the GAM to MECH-102 involves conceptual additions not present in Anderson and Bushman's framework.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence is 0.62, reflecting mixed evidence. The GAM's thoughtful-appraisal pathway and the impulsive/thoughtful distinction are genuine supports. The GAM's developmental feedback loops provide a useful model for how pathway-exhaustion conditions are generated over time. However, the dominant empirical findings in the GAM concern conditions where violence occurs without pathway exhaustion, which is a counter-signal. The GAM is better understood as providing a descriptive framework that helps explain MECH-102 violation mechanisms (how violence gets triggered before pathways are exhausted) than as direct support for MECH-102's core claim.
