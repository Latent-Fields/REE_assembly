# Liu et al. (2024) — Replay-Triggered Brain-Wide Activation in Humans

## What the paper does

Liu and colleagues use simultaneous EEG-fMRI to detect hippocampal replay events during rest and demonstrate their brain-wide consequences. They find that transient replay events (~50ms sequential reactivation of learned spatial patterns) trigger increased BOLD activity in hippocampus and medial prefrontal cortex, and strengthen functional connectivity between hippocampus and the default mode network. This provides the first direct evidence in humans that replay engages the DMN, bridging the gap between rodent replay electrophysiology and human DMN neuroimaging.

## Relevance to MECH-029

MECH-029 claims that the reflective/DMN mode supports moral simulation via gated replay. This paper provides the biological mechanism: during quiescent states, hippocampal replay drives DMN activation. In REE, this maps onto the E3 planning loop entering simulation mode (MECH-092 quiescent heartbeat cycles) and replaying trajectories through the residue-field terrain for moral evaluation.

The coupling between replay and DMN is not random — it occurs during cognitive-map-based computation, which is exactly the kind of structured simulation MECH-029 proposes for moral reasoning. The hippocampus replays possible trajectories; the DMN (particularly mPFC) evaluates their significance.

## What the paper does not address

The paper studies spatial sequence replay, not moral simulation. The leap from "replaying a learned route" to "replaying a counterfactual moral scenario" requires assuming that the same replay infrastructure supports both spatial and evaluative simulation. This is plausible (the DMN is known to support moral reasoning) but not tested by this paper.

Additionally, the paper does not address "gating" — replay appears to occur spontaneously during rest. MECH-029 requires a gating mechanism that selectively triggers moral simulation. The BreathOscillator / MECH-092 quiescent heartbeat cycles are REE's candidate for this gate, but the biological evidence for gated (rather than spontaneous) moral replay is thin.

## Confidence reasoning

High source quality, directly relevant to replay-DMN coupling. Confidence moderated by the gap between spatial replay and moral simulation, and the absence of gating evidence.
