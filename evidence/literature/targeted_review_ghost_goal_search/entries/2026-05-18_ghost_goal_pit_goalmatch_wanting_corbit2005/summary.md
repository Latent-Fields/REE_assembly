# Corbit & Balleine 2005 / 2011 — the substrate of cue-driven invigoration (PIT)

Pavlovian-instrumental transfer is, almost embarrassingly, a direct experimental model of the ghost-goal bank's core operation. The animal learns a Pavlovian cue-outcome relation and, separately, an instrumental action. Then a cue is presented in extinction and you ask: does the cue invigorate the action? That is exactly what MECH-293 does — present an internally retrieved cue (a matched ghost anchor) and let it energise a separately stored policy. So the question of whether our `wanting x goal_match` term is biologically structured the right way is, for once, a question with a clean experimental answer.

The answer from Corbit and Balleine is that there are *two* such operations, not one, and they double-dissociate. In the 2005 study, basolateral amygdala lesions abolished *outcome-specific* PIT — the cue's ability to selectively boost the action that earns *that cue's particular predicted outcome* — while sparing *general* PIT. Central amygdala lesions did the opposite: they abolished general PIT — the cue's nonspecific power to energise whatever instrumental response is available — while leaving the specific form intact. In 2011 they pushed the dissociation one synapse downstream and found it preserved: nucleus accumbens shell carries outcome-specific transfer, core carries general transfer, and reversible muscimol inactivation *at test* reproduced the lesion pattern — meaning these structures are required at the moment of retrieval and expression, not merely during learning.

Read against our score, this is almost too on-the-nose. The BLA -> NAc-shell channel is `goal_match`: "this cue corresponds to this specific stored goal, so re-probe *that* goal" — the targeted probe MECH-293 is supposed to issue. The CeA -> NAc-core channel is the general `wanting`/arousal gain: a value-nonspecific energisation. The discrete, addressable thing the specific channel indexes into is ARC-060's bank. As supporting evidence for the architecture existing in biology, it is about as strong as the strand gets.

The caveat is the same one Flagel raised, now anatomically proven rather than inferred: these are *separable* systems with *independent* failure modes, and a single multiplicative `wanting x goal_match` collapses them. The biology predicts you can lose the specific channel and keep generic drive — a state where matched cues no longer re-probe their own deferred goals but the agent is still diffusely activated (clinically, this rhymes with undirected agitation). Or you can lose the general channel and keep specific matching — accurate retrieval with no vigor to act on it. And because expression depends on the retrieval-time channel being online, a perfectly well-encoded ghost entry can be *stored but un-actionable* if the invigoration channel is unavailable when it is queried. None of those three states is representable in a scalar product. I take the honest conclusion to be that the term structure of `ghost_priority` should be two additive, independently-gateable channels, not a product — and that the validation battery should be able to induce each of the three dissociated failure states deliberately.
