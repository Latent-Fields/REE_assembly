# Barch & Dowd 2010 -- losing goal maintenance collapses agency without erasing liking

## What the paper did

Barch and Dowd reviewed the schizophrenia motivation literature to explain why
patients with negative symptoms fail to initiate and sustain goal-directed
behavior. Their central proposal is that the problem is not primarily a failure
of pleasure at contact, but a failure to maintain and use internal goal
representations.

## Key findings relevant to ghost-goal search

The decisive result is architectural: intact liking does not guarantee
goal-directed behavior. When sustained goal representations fail, behavior
collapses into passivity even if local hedonic evaluation is largely intact.

For ghost-goal search, this means a good agent cannot rely only on local terrain
signals. It needs some preserved representation of what it is trying to get to,
including when the direct route is blocked or temporarily unavailable.

## Translation to REE

This paper anchors the case for MECH-230 plus ARC-060. REE's current wanting
field helps with approach when a local gradient exists, but blocked or deferred
goals need a discrete preserved trace if the system is not to collapse into
short-horizon passivity. The ghost-goal bank is one concrete way to preserve
goal-directedness through local invalidation.

## Limitations and caveats

This is a clinical review, not a hippocampal planning paper. It supports the
need for persistent goal representations, but does not by itself specify how
they should be stored or queried.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence 0.80. The clinical dissociation is a strong existence proof that
goal maintenance matters independently of local pleasure or threat avoidance.
The translation to ghost-goal search is conceptual but straightforward.
