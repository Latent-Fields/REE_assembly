# Berkowitz 1989 -- blocked goals remain affectively live

## What the paper did

Berkowitz reformulated the frustration-aggression hypothesis, arguing that
blocked goal attainment produces negative affect and can bias subsequent
behavior. The important point is not the aggression claim itself, but the fact
that blockage leaves a live motivational and affective residue.

## Key findings relevant to ghost-goal search

A blocked goal is not equivalent to a vanished goal. The path closure remains
behaviorally active. In computational terms, the unresolved goal path carries
continued bias and pressure rather than disappearing cleanly from the system.

This is close to the phenomenology the user described as "ghosts of goals":
goals that are not currently achievable but remain salient enough to attract
future search.

## Translation to REE

REE should not literalize the aggression part of the model. The useful
translation is narrower: anchor invalidation should not erase the motivational
identity of the blocked path. Instead, the blocked path should remain as a
preserved unresolved trace that can later be probed, rerouted, or demoted.

That is the direct architectural argument for SD-039 and MECH-292.

## Limitations and caveats

This is social-psychological and affective rather than hippocampal. It says
that blocked goals stay live, but not how a neural planner should preserve or
query them.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence 0.69. The mechanism mismatch is real, but the directional support is
strong: blocked pathways leave continued motivational pressure rather than
simply vanishing.
