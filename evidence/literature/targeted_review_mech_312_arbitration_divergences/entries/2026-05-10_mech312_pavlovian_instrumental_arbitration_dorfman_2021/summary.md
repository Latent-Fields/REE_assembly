# Gershman, Guitart-Masip & Cavanagh 2021 — multi-variable dynamic arbitration with controllability

The Gershman, Guitart-Masip & Cavanagh 2021 PLOS Computational Biology paper builds on Dorfman & Gershman's prior model of Pavlovian-instrumental arbitration to test the claim that the balance between these two control modes is dynamically updated within a single session, modulated not only by per-channel reliability (the standard model-based vs model-free arbitration variable) but additionally by an inferred controllability variable — the agent's estimate of how much its actions actually influence outcomes. The authors re-analyse two prior datasets (one EEG, one fMRI, both go/no-go tasks) using the controllability-modulated arbitration model and find converging neural signatures: when controllability is inferred to be low, Pavlovian biases dominate the action policy; when high, instrumental control wins out. The controllability inference is itself a Bayesian latent-state estimate that is distinct from per-channel reliability and operates in parallel.

This entry's relevance to REE's MECH-312 multi-variable arbitration question is in two parts. The supporting half: Gershman et al 2021 demonstrate that biological arbitration already does what REE's MECH-312 needs to do — combine multiple modulating variables (per-channel reliability + a controllability-style additional signal) into dynamic arbitration weights that update within-session as evidence accumulates. The structural premise that arbitration is multi-variable and dynamically updated, rather than uncertainty-only and quasi-static, is biologically supported. This is also the cleanest existing-literature evidence so far that the arbitration substrate handles MORE than two competing channels modulated by MORE than one variable — Pavlovian + instrumental + controllability is a 2-channel, 2-variable case, but the architecture clearly extends.

The complicating half is that the Pavlovian-vs-instrumental case is a special case of arbitration in which one channel (Pavlovian) is largely hard-wired and the other (instrumental) is learned. This is structurally different from REE's MECH-312, which has multiple LEARNED channels (uncertainty-weighted + practice-mature + diversity-driven, per the Pull 1 R4 + Pull 2 R4 outputs) competing in parallel. Whether the controllability-style modulation REE could borrow from this paper would generalise to all-learned-channel arbitration is not directly answered. The likeliest extension is that controllability becomes a per-channel agency-confidence signal — each channel carries an estimate of how reliably its outputs translate into intended outcomes, and arbitration weights are modulated by these per-channel confidence signals in addition to per-channel reliability and the global modulators (V_s, practice maturity, etc.).

For the R4 verdict in the synthesis, this entry is the strongest existing-literature evidence that multi-variable, multi-channel, dynamic arbitration is biologically real — supporting Pull 4 R4's recommendation to use a multi-variable combination rule. Together with Kool/Gershman/Cushman 2017 (cost-benefit, separately entry'd), this pair recommends a MULTIPLICATIVE-GATE combination rule with the following structure: arbitration weights for each channel are products of (a) per-channel reliability (Daw 2005, Lee 2014 lineage), (b) per-channel cost (Kool 2017), (c) per-channel agency/controllability (Gershman 2021), (d) global modulators including V_s freshness for the current region (this pull D3) and practice maturity (Pull 2 R4). The product form ensures any one factor can effectively suppress a channel.

For the R5 cluster-architecture decision, the multiple-modulator picture this entry surfaces argues against a single monolithic MECH-312. The cleaner architecture is MECH-312 as the parent claim ("multi-variable multi-channel arbitration in BG-cortical loops") with sub-MECHs for the major modulating-variable categories: MECH-312a uncertainty/reliability weighting, MECH-312b practice/maturity weighting, MECH-312c affective-stream modulation (this pull D2), MECH-312d V_s freshness modulation (this pull D3), and possibly MECH-312e controllability/agency modulation (this pull D4 extension). This makes each modulator independently testable while keeping the parent claim coherent.
