# Behrens et al. 2007 — global volatility tracking in ACC, the closest analog to V_s

The Behrens, Woolrich, Walton & Rushworth 2007 Nature Neuroscience paper is the canonical demonstration that humans optimally track environmental volatility — the rate at which reward contingencies are changing — and use the volatility estimate to modulate their learning rate. Subjects performed a two-armed bandit task in which the reward probabilities switched periodically; behaviourally, learning rate increased during volatile blocks and decreased during stable blocks, in close agreement with a Bayesian model that treats volatility as a hidden state to be inferred. Functional MRI showed that BOLD signal in anterior cingulate cortex (ACC) tracked the model's volatility estimate, and across-subject variation in this signal predicted across-subject variation in learning-rate adjustment.

This is the closest existing-literature analog to REE's V_s machinery (MECH-269 verisimilitude scalar, MECH-284 broadcast invalidation, MECH-287 staleness propagation). REE proposes that each region of the world model carries a freshness signal indicating how trustworthy the agent's current model of that region is, and that this signal modulates rule-arbitration weights for rules indexed in that region (a stale region's rules get downweighted). Behrens 2007 has the same fundamental architecture: a substrate-level signal that tracks model-trustworthiness and modulates downstream weight updates, with ACC as the documented neural locus. The structural premise — "the brain runs a metalearning signal about how trustworthy current beliefs are, and routes that signal into weight modulation" — is established.

The genuine REE divergence is on two axes: scope and function. On scope, Behrens 2007 estimates a single global volatility level for the task as a whole, whereas REE's V_s is per-region: each region of the agent's world model carries its own freshness scalar. The literature does not yet have a clean per-region analog. The closest neighbour is Bouton-style contextual renewal (separately entry'd in this pull), which demonstrates context-dependent retrieval of extinguished associations — but Bouton treats context categorically (renewal/no-renewal) rather than as a continuous freshness scalar that modulates ongoing trust. The per-region continuous-freshness reading is REE-novel. On function, Behrens modulates LEARNING RATE on a single value estimate (how fast should I update my beliefs given this trial?), whereas REE's V_s modulates RULE TRUST in arbitration (should this rule even be applied to choose my next action in this region?). These are related but functionally distinct: Behrens is "how fast to learn given new evidence", REE is "how much to trust the existing learned thing for current action selection". Both depend on a freshness estimate, but the routing is different.

For the R3 verdict in this pull's synthesis, the right characterisation is PARTIAL-OVERLAP: the substrate-level freshness signal and its routing-into-weight-modulation is well-established (Behrens 2007 + the considerable follow-up literature on hierarchical Bayesian inference in ACC), but the per-region scope and the rule-trust function are REE-specific extensions. This is a weaker form of "genuine novelty" than e.g. MECH-094 simulation-mode write-gating, where the literature provides only the substrate without the function. For V_s, the literature provides both the substrate AND a recognisable functional cousin (learning-rate modulation), and the REE-specific contribution is in the precise scope and routing rather than in the existence of the mechanism class.

Two additional notes on caveats. First, the Behrens framework is normatively Bayesian and assumes the agent maintains a posterior over a hidden volatility state. REE's V_s is more pragmatic and has not yet committed to a normative Bayesian formulation; this is a degree of freedom REE substrate design will have to take a position on. Second, Behrens-style ACC volatility signals have been shown in many follow-up studies (Behrens & Bachmann 2017 review; Soltani & Izquierdo 2019 review) to interact with prefrontal regions in ways that make ACC look more like a volatility-broadcaster than a volatility-modulator-of-anything-specific — consistent with REE's architectural framing where V_s is a substrate-level signal that propagates broadly rather than being local to one rule channel. Confidence is set to 0.60 (mixed) because the entry is the closest analog REE can point to and substantially supports MECH-269's structural premise, while the per-region and rule-trust extensions remain genuinely REE-specific.
