# Murray & Rudebeck 2018 — parallel OFC streams for affective and discriminative value

Murray & Rudebeck's 2018 Nature Reviews Neuroscience review consolidates a decade of macaque lesion and electrophysiology work into a coherent map of how primate ventral prefrontal cortex partitions value-guided choice. The key result is a functional subdivision: medial OFC (areas 14, 11, 13) handles value-comparison, central OFC encodes specific outcome identity, and lateral OFC + ventrolateral PFC handle credit assignment. Crucially, the amygdala is not an interchangeable value-coder but supplies a specifically-affective component — its lesions selectively impair affective responses to previously-rewarded stimuli without abolishing identity-driven choice. The IT-cortex / rhinal / OFC route, by contrast, supplies the sensory-property channel: which thing is which, what its previously-associated outcome was. The amygdala-OFC system is therefore documented as parallel-but-interacting streams that supply distinct content (affective valence via amygdala, sensory-identity-and-association via the IT-OFC route, comparison via medial OFC) to downstream choice machinery.

This is the cleanest biological precedent for REE's SD-010 / SD-011 split. REE proposes that nociceptive content reaches downstream arbitration via two structurally distinct streams: a sensory-discriminative harm stream (z_harm_s, SD-010) carrying the "what is happening, where, with what features" content, and an affective harm stream (z_harm_a, SD-011) carrying the "how aversive" content. MECH-312 then proposes that these streams are weighted distinctly into rule-arbitration. The Murray-Rudebeck framework is structurally exactly that: two parallel streams carrying complementary content into a downstream comparator. The biological precedent is on the reward side rather than the harm side, but the amygdala's role in coding aversive valence (LeDoux's classical work) extrapolates the affective-stream framing cleanly to harm-relevant content. SD-010/011 are therefore biologically well-anchored — this is not a REE-novel architectural commitment.

The genuine REE divergence is downstream of where Murray & Rudebeck stop. The review documents that the streams exist and that they carry distinct content; it does NOT specify an algorithmic combination rule for how they jointly determine choice or, more relevantly for REE, how they jointly modulate the weights on competing rule-application channels. The literature stops at "amygdala carries affective signals into the OFC system" and does not advance to "and these signals modulate which behavioural rule wins arbitration as a function of (e.g.) the affective load of the local situation". REE's MECH-312-d (or whatever sub-MECH ends up carrying the affective-stream-modulation content) is therefore a literature-extension claim: the structural premise (two streams) is well-supported, but the functional consequence REE proposes (these streams modulate rule-arbitration weights distinctly, with affective-stream weight increasing in high-arousal situations) is REE-specific and would need experimental support.

There are two transfer risks worth being explicit about. First, the Murray-Rudebeck literature is overwhelmingly macaque, and the medial-vs-lateral OFC dissociation is partially reproducible in rodents but with weaker functional crispness. REE substrate experiments will have to take a position on whether the V3 substrate models the macaque-style separation or a more graded rodent-style version. Second, the literature concerns reward-guided choice between options that differ in identity and value. REE's harm-avoidance applications are an extrapolation, and although the amygdala's affective-coding role generalises across valence, the specific OFC subdivisions documented for reward have less direct evidence on the harm side. These are not refutations — they are caveats on how strongly REE can lean on this literature for its specific harm-stream architectural commitment.

The R2 verdict for this entry is GENUINE-DIVERGENCE-PARTIALLY-OVERLAPS. The dual-stream structural premise has strong biological support (this is not REE-novel). But the specific functional claim REE makes — that these streams differentially modulate rule-arbitration weights — is genuinely a REE-specific extension that goes beyond what the Murray-Rudebeck literature documents. So the R3 / R5 cluster-architecture decision should reflect this asymmetry: the SD-010/011 split should NOT be advertised as a REE-novel divergence (it is a literature-anchored architectural choice), but the affective-stream-modulation-of-arbitration claim (which would live as a MECH-312 sub-claim if MECH-312 splits, or as a documented modulator if it stays unitary) IS a REE-specific extension and should be flagged accordingly in the cluster registration.
