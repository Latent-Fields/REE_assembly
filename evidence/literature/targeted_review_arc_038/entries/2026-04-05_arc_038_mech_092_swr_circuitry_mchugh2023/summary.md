# Summary: He, Guan & McHugh (2023)

**Citation:** He H, Guan H, McHugh TJ. "The expanded circuitry of hippocampal ripples and replay." *Neuroscience Research* 189:13-19. DOI: [10.1016/j.neures.2022.12.010](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neures.2022.12.010)

## What the paper did

Review of rodent hippocampal physiology examining the circuitry underlying SWR generation and replay, with particular attention to recent findings expanding beyond the canonical CA3->CA1 model. Covers the role of CA2 as an additional circuit influence on SWR generation and information content.

## Key findings

1. **SWRs during slow-wave sleep and quiet wakefulness organize place cell replay** for memory consolidation and offline planning -- both states are SWR-active offline processing modes.
2. **CA3 recurrent excitatory dynamics** are the canonical primary generator of SWRs.
3. **CA2 region additionally influences SWR generation and content** in a task- and state-dependent manner -- a newer finding expanding the canonical model.
4. Disrupting SWRs impairs learning (consistent with Ego-Stengel & Wilson).
5. The SWR circuitry is not a single homogeneous generator but a state-dependent multi-circuit system.

## Relevance to ARC-038 and MECH-092

**ARC-038 (waking consolidation mode -- distinct state):** The task- and state-dependent nature of CA2's contribution directly supports the claim that waking-quiescence SWR replay is a distinct operational mode. If circuit contributions change based on behavioral state (active vs. quiet), then the content and function of replay changes too -- supporting ARC-038's claim of a distinct waking-consolidation mode vs. goal-directed forward sweeps.

**MECH-092 (quiescent E3 heartbeat trigger):** The state-dependency of SWR generation (quiet wakefulness as a distinct trigger condition) provides mechanistic grounding for why the E3 heartbeat quiescence specifically enables the consolidation mode. CA3 recurrent dynamics = the substrate for E3 HippocampalModule replay; the quiescent-state gate is what allows CA2/CA3 to drive the consolidation SWR rather than the active-exploration theta mode.

## Limitations for REE mapping

- CA2's role is an emerging (2022) finding, not yet consolidated consensus
- The paper reviews circuitry rather than directly testing the goal-directed vs. consolidation distinction
- V3 HippocampalModule currently lacks CA3-equivalent recurrent connectivity and CA2-equivalent state-gating circuit
- Transfer from full hippocampal circuitry to single-module E3 architecture requires architectural decisions not yet made
