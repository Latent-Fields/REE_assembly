# Salamone & Correa (2012) — The mysterious motivational functions of mesolimbic dopamine

*According to PubMed. Neuron 76(3):470-85. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2012.10.021) · PMID 23141060 · PMC4450094.*

## What the paper did

This is the review that did the unglamorous, necessary work of telling the field what mesolimbic dopamine is *not*. Salamone and Correa assemble decades of pharmacological, lesion, and microdialysis evidence to argue against the lazy shorthand of "dopamine = reward." Accumbens dopamine, they show, does not mediate primary food motivation or appetite — hungry, dopamine-depleted animals still like food and will eat it if it is easy to obtain. What dopamine does is supply the *activational* dimension of motivation: behavioural activation, the willingness to exert effort, approach, and the maintenance of effortful engagement over time. Crucially, the activational and directional components dissociate under dopaminergic manipulation.

## Why it matters for MECH-320

MECH-320's careful boundary-drawing in `claims.yaml` — distinct from SD-012 (deficit-keyed drive), from MECH-216 (target-conditioned wanting), from MECH-313 (LC-NE noise), from MECH-330 (boredom/valence) — is exactly the distinction this review insists on. The claim's `v_t` is a *target-free activational* term: it raises the propensity to act and penalises passivity regardless of which goal, if any, is live. Salamone & Correa give that term its biological name. The `w_action`/`w_passive` contrast is the activational dimension; it is not appetite, not hedonics, not goal selection. The review is, in effect, the substrate-identity half of MECH-320's grounding, with Niv (2007) supplying the formal coupling and Hamid (2016) the dynamic measurement.

## Limitations and the honest caveat

A review is an argument about a literature, not a measurement of the mechanism, so I weight it for *identity* rather than *quantity*. It does not test that the activational signal tracks an average reward rate (that is Niv/Hamid); it tells us what kind of signal we are entitled to call it. And its central caution cuts both ways: if a future REE analysis let `v_t` drift into encoding reward magnitude or goal value, this paper would become a *weakening* citation, because it documents precisely that dissociation. That is why I have logged the appetite/reward boundary as a failure signature rather than burying it — it is the edge along which the mapping could fail. Transfer risk is non-trivial: this is rodent pharmacology, and REE's vigor scalar is an algorithmic quantity, so "mesolimbic dopamine" is an analogy with teeth, not an anatomical identity.
