# Eastwood, Frischen, Fenske & Smilek 2012 — The Unengaged Mind: Defining Boredom in Terms of Attention

[DOI](https://doi.org/10.1177/1745691612456044) · PMID 26168505 · *Perspectives on Psychological Science* 7(5):482-495

## What the paper argues

A theoretical synthesis paper that pulls together psychodynamic, existential, arousal, and cognitive accounts of boredom into one definitional frame: boredom is "the aversive experience of wanting, but being unable, to engage in satisfying activity." The authors locate the construct in attention — the bored individual experiences attention as un-occupiable by what is currently available. Three signature features fall out of this definition: subjective time slows; the situation, not the self, is felt as the cause; and the want-to-engage-but-unable loop is itself the aversive content. The paper is the canonical modern definitional anchor; nearly every subsequent neuroscience and clinical paper on boredom cites it as the construct definition.

## Why this matters for ARC-067

This is the load-bearing R1 paper for the functional-emotion-engagement-search reading. ARC-067's architectural commitment — that prolonged low-engagement is itself aversive — is essentially the computational restatement of Eastwood's "wanting-to-engage-but-unable" loop. The agent has the capacity for engagement (high energy, working substrates), but the integrated engagement-rate scalar is below threshold; the gap between capacity-to-engage and actual-engagement is what accumulates as the aversive. Eastwood's three-part signature — time-slowing, situation-attribution, want-engage-fail — gives a clean target shape for the child MECH design: a slow engagement-rate integrator that produces aversive valence routed through the affective harm stream so the agent registers the state as "about something wrong" rather than internal-only.

The paper does not commit to the underlying mechanism (it is definitional, not mechanistic), and that ambiguity is exactly where the next two papers — Westgate & Wilson 2018 (attentional MAC) and van Tilburg & Igou 2017 (functional emotion) — divide. The R1 verdict adjudicates between those two readings; Eastwood is the shared anchor that makes the dispute well-posed.

## Limitations and confidence

The paper is theoretical synthesis, not empirical work. The construct is human-introspective and ARC-067 is computational; the functional shape transfers cleanly but the qualia framing does not. There is an important computational ambiguity in "wanting" — it could be agentive in the MECH-216 predictive-wanting sense (target-conditioned) or non-agentive (a pre-target activation pressure). ARC-067 takes the non-agentive reading; Eastwood does not arbitrate. Confidence aggregate 0.78 — high source quality and high mapping fidelity, modest transfer risk for the qualia-to-computation jump.

## Failure signature it would falsify

An REE substrate that produces no action-pressure under sustained low-engagement in the well-fed-safe-familiar-no-z_goal regime fails the Eastwood definitional test directly. The agent must show some behavioural marker of "wanting to engage and being unable" — exploratory action density rising over the no-engagement interval, or some equivalent — for the substrate to count as instantiating boredom in the Eastwood sense.
