# Summary: Stachenfeld, Botvinick & Gershman (2017) — The Hippocampus as a Predictive Map

## What the paper did

Stachenfeld, Botvinick, and Gershman start from a simple observation that ought to unsettle the standard story about place cells: place fields are not purely geometric. They warp around barriers, skew in the direction of travel on linear tracks, expand near reward locations, and reorganise when the animal's policy changes. The traditional cognitive map metaphor — hippocampus as a GPS — cannot explain these effects. The authors ask instead: what representation would be optimal for an agent trying to maximise future reward in a spatial environment? They show, using the formalism of reinforcement learning, that the answer is the successor representation (SR): each state is encoded not by its Euclidean coordinates but by a discounted sum of expected future state occupancies under the agent's current policy. The key mathematical move is the decomposition of the value function into an SR component (pure transition structure) and a reward weight vector — a decomposition that allows rapid recomputation of values when reward distributions change without having to relearn the state representation from scratch.

## Key findings

The paper then demonstrates that this SR account reproduces a striking range of empirical phenomena. Asymmetric place field skewing on linear tracks falls out naturally from the directional statistics of the agent's movement policy. The expansion of place fields near reward locations follows from the increased dwell time (and therefore inflated expected future occupancy) near rewarding states. The community structure of hippocampal pattern similarity mirrors the graph topology of successor states — states in the same navigational community are represented more similarly, even when Euclidean distance would predict otherwise. In the entorhinal cortex, the authors argue that grid cells encode a low-dimensional basis set for the SR, providing a compact factorisation from which place cell representations can be reconstructed. An fMRI reanalysis (Garvert et al.) shows that hippocampal similarity in a non-spatial relational task tracks successor-state proximity, not feature similarity — extending the framework beyond the spatial domain.

## REE translation

ARC-007 claims that the hippocampus stores and replays paths through residue-field terrain — the accumulated causal/harm footprint of actions in the environment. Stachenfeld et al. provide the theoretical architecture that makes this claim neurobiologically coherent. If hippocampal place cells encode the SR, then the topology of the map they constitute is not Euclidean but is shaped by whatever structure governs state transitions under the agent's policy. When a residue field creates high-salience (high-harm) regions that the agent systematically avoids or approaches, the SR distorts around those regions in precisely the way that place fields distort around barriers and reward locations. The residue field, in this framing, is not external to the hippocampal map — it is one of the causal factors that shapes the policy, which in turn shapes the SR, which constitutes the map. ARC-007's claim follows as a corollary rather than an additional postulate.

## Limitations and caveats

The honest difficulty here is the indirection. The SR is policy-sensitive: it encodes what the agent actually does, not what it ought to do. A residue field shapes the map only insofar as it shapes behaviour. If an agent has not yet learned the harm topology of a novel environment — which is precisely when you want the map to encode that topology for prospective planning — the SR will not yet reflect it. This is a genuine asymmetry between the SR account and what REE needs: REE's residue field is a prospective, normative harm landscape that should constrain planning even before experience has shaped policy. The SR is retrospective by construction. There is also the question of temporal resolution: the SR is estimated over repeated transitions, but REE's residue field may accumulate and dissipate within a single episode. Whether hippocampal SR mechanisms operate at that resolution is not addressed.

## Confidence reasoning

The paper earns high confidence (0.82) as supporting evidence for ARC-007 for three reasons: it is the single most cited theoretical account of why hippocampal maps encode more than geometry; it directly predicts value-topology distortion of hippocampal representations; and it provides a mathematically precise mechanism (SR + reward weight decomposition) that maps cleanly onto REE's distinction between structural path knowledge and salience weighting. The reduction from 1.0 reflects the policy-mediated indirection and the temporal resolution question. This is strong theoretical support, not direct experimental confirmation.
