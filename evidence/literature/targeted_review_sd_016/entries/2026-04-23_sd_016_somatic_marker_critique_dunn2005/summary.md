# Summary: Dunn et al. 2005 — Somatic Marker Hypothesis: Critical Evaluation

**Source:** Dunn, Dalgleish & Lawrence (2005). *The somatic marker hypothesis: a critical evaluation.* Neuroscience and Biobehavioral Reviews 30(2): 239–271. [DOI: 10.1016/j.neubiorev.2005.07.001](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neubiorev.2005.07.001)

## What the paper did

Dunn and colleagues wrote a comprehensive critical review of the somatic marker hypothesis (SMH) as articulated by Damasio and the Iowa group. The review examined the Iowa Gambling Task (IGT) evidence base, the psychophysiological data on anticipatory SCRs, lesion and neuroimaging data, and pharmacological studies. The critique is organised around three challenges: (1) cognitive penetrability — that participants may consciously learn the IGT deck rules and produce SCRs via explicit cognition rather than somatic signalling; (2) psychophysiological ambiguity — that anticipatory SCRs may reflect arousal, uncertainty, or task engagement rather than somatic markers per se; (3) insufficiency of causal evidence — that peripheral somatic feedback has not been demonstrated to *cause* vmPFC-mediated decision improvements rather than being merely correlated with them.

## Key findings

The review is nuanced rather than dismissive. It does not challenge the empirical fact that vmPFC lesion patients are impaired on the IGT and fail to generate anticipatory SCRs. What it challenges is the specific mechanistic story — that peripheral body-state feedback (somatic markers) is the causal pathway through which vmPFC guides decisions. The review argues that the same IGT performance patterns could be explained by: (a) explicit probability-learning with intact vmPFC providing cognitive scaffolding; (b) vmPFC representing outcome statistics without requiring somatic feedback; or (c) uncertainty-driven arousal modulating task performance via prefrontal arousal pathways.

The review closes not by rejecting the SMH but by calling for more causal experiments (e.g. peripheral autonomic blockade combined with vmPFC lesions) and more precise specification of the neural mechanisms.

## Limitations and caveats

This is a theoretical review, not an experimental paper. It does not produce new data and cannot be assigned a strong empirical evidence direction for SD-016 specifically. It is included as a governance caution — a record that the biological framework SD-016 invokes is contested, even if the specific empirical dissociation (vmPFC controls anticipatory signalling) is not itself refuted by the critique.
