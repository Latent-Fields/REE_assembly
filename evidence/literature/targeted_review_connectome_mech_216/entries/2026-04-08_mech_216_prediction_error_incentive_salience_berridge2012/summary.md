# Berridge 2012: From prediction error to incentive salience

Berridge's review is the canonical articulation of the incentive salience hypothesis --
the proposal that mesolimbic dopamine does not merely signal prediction errors for
learning but dynamically generates motivational "wanting" by combining cached Pavlovian
associations with current physiological state. The paper is comprehensive, drawing on
decades of rodent behavioural pharmacology, lesion studies, and human imaging to argue
that wanting is computed afresh each time a cue is re-encountered: the learned
association provides the template, the neurobiological state provides the gain.

The central equation, formalised in Zhang et al. 2009, is V_tilde(s) = r_t x kappa,
where r_t is the cached associative value and kappa is the current physiological state
multiplier. This is directly relevant to MECH-216: E1 schema activations that predict
resource encounters are the REE analog of cached CS-UCS associations (r_t), and the
SD-012 homeostatic drive modulation is the analog of kappa. The claim that wanting is
generated at cue re-encounter -- not retrieved from storage -- maps onto the proposal
that E1 schema activation seeds VALENCE_WANTING at the current z_world position, not
at a remembered resource position.

The paper provides particularly striking evidence from salt appetite experiments: rats
that have never previously experienced sodium depletion while a salt cue was present
nonetheless show immediate wanting for that cue upon their first experience of sodium
depletion. The learned CS-UCS association (salt cue predicts salt) was formed during
satiation; the wanting is generated de novo by the kappa-modulated recomputation at
re-encounter under new physiological conditions. This demonstrates that wanting is not
a learned response but a dynamically computed motivational attribution -- exactly the
mechanism MECH-216 proposes for E1 schema-conditioned wanting.

The mapping caveat is that Berridge's wanting can dissociate entirely from hedonic
liking and from cognitive expectation. MECH-216 does not yet model this dissociation:
in REE, wanting is grounded in resource benefit, and there is no "wanting without
liking" pathway. The biological mechanism also requires mesolimbic dopamine as the
substrate for wanting generation, whereas REE uses the serotonin module and residue
field. These are architectural differences that limit transfer fidelity, but the core
computational logic -- learned prediction x state = motivation -- is preserved. The
paper strongly supports the plausibility of MECH-216's schema-conditioned wanting
mechanism as a computational abstraction of incentive salience.
