# Contingency Learning from Accidental Action -- Keren-Portnoy et al. (2021)

## What the paper does

Keren-Portnoy et al. tested whether 6-month-old infants would spontaneously increase their vocalizations when doing so produced a contingent visual reward (colorful shapes on an iPad), with no social element. In a 5-minute session, 83% of infants in the contingent condition increased vocalization frequency from the first to second half of the session, compared to 47% in the non-contingent control group. The paper frames this as the "Did I just do that?" moment: the infant recognizing that its own output caused an environmental change, and the resulting increase in the producing action as the first observable precursor to intentional goal-directed behavior.

## Key findings relevant to REE

The central finding is that infants detect and act on action-outcome contingencies rapidly and without social mediation. This sits within the mobile paradigm tradition (Rovee-Collier 1969; Watson 1972) which showed infants as young as 2-3 months increase kicking rates when tied to a mobile, detecting the contingency within minutes. The Keren-Portnoy extension is notable because it generalises across vocal actions without social feedback -- closer in spirit to REE's non-social grid-world training environment.

The speed of learning has substrate implications. If the REE infant stage has sufficient benefit contact frequency, the accidental-contact-to-repetition cycle should bootstrap a non-trivial set of goal-associated action patterns within relatively few episodes.

## How this maps to REE

MECH-189 posits high-salience benefit contacts are written to persistent ContextMemory as super-ordinal goal anchors. Keren-Portnoy et al. provide behavioral evidence for the precursor: an accidental action encounters a positive outcome, and the infant rapidly increases that action. In REE terms, this is when a trajectory vector gets tagged with VALENCE_WANTING before deliberate goal pursuit begins. INV-073's exploration epoch requirement is confirmed: contingency is discovered through spontaneous exploration, not deliberate pursuit.

## Limitations and caveats

The study measures vocalization rates, not internal affective states. The valence change upon discovery is inferred, not measured directly. We cannot determine whether the contingency discovery generates a VALENCE_WANTING signal independently of VALENCE_LIKING -- though Berridge's wanting/liking dissociation predicts they should be separable. The contingency is single-action-to-single-outcome; REE needs multi-action affordance repertoires.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence 0.73 reflects a well-powered peer-reviewed study with a clean design in the leading infancy journal. Mapping fidelity is good at the mechanism level (accidental contact -> contingency discovery -> increased rate) but incomplete at the valence level. Transfer from infant vocal contingency to grid-world benefit contact is indirect but the principle is domain-general.
