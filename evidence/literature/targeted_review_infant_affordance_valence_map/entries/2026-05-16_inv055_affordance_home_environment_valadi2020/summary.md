# Affordance Diversity and Developmental Repertoire Breadth -- Valadi et al. (2020)

## What the paper does

Valadi, Gabbard, and Hooshyari investigated whether home environment affordance richness predicts cognitive, social, and communication outcomes in children 18-42 months, using the validated Affordances in the Home Environment for Motor Development - Infant Scale (AHEMD-IS). This instrument measures four dimensions: physical space, variety of stimulation, fine-motor toys, and gross-motor toys. The study recruited 251 families and assessed outcomes using the Ages and Stages Inventories.

## Key findings relevant to REE

Home environment affordance richness significantly predicted personal-social, problem-solving, and communication skills -- not just motor outcomes. The effect generalised beyond the motor domain, suggesting that the richness of the exploration environment during early development has downstream consequences for the breadth of competencies acquired. This is ecologically significant: the paper demonstrates that environmental affordance diversity translates to diverse developmental outcomes across domains.

The AHEMD-IS physical space dimension and variety of stimulation dimension were most predictive. The variety of stimulation dimension is notable as the only one not significantly modulated by SES, suggesting it reflects an independently relevant environmental factor.

## How this maps to REE

INV-055 requires the infant stage to produce not just a motor behavioral repertoire but also conceptual substrates for object-schema formation and social pattern recognition. Valadi et al. show that affordance diversity in the training environment produces diverse downstream competencies across domains. For REE, this supports designing the infant substrate with diverse object types (MECH-296 multi-resource variety), varied layouts, and surface diversity. The AHEMD-IS dimensions map to REE substrate parameters: physical space = grid-world size and topology, variety of stimulation = object type diversity, gross-motor toys = action-affordance richness.

## Limitations and caveats

Cross-sectional design cannot establish causal direction: more capable children may elicit richer affordance provision from parents, reversing the arrow. SES is a significant confounder for most dimensions. The age range (18-42 months) is older than the critical infant window for REE's pre-deliberate-planning stage. The parental self-report measurement method introduces additional uncertainty.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence 0.65 reflects a moderate-quality empirical study (N=251, validated instruments, but cross-sectional and self-report) with moderate mapping fidelity: the correlation between environment richness and repertoire breadth is established but causality and critical-window timing are not. Transfer risk is moderate-high given the older age range and social family context versus grid-world agent.
