# Danjo, Toyoizumi & Fujisawa 2018 — Spatial Representations of Self and Other in the Hippocampus

**Source:** Science, [DOI 10.1126/science.aao3898](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aao3898) (PubMed PMID 29326273). According to PubMed.

## What the paper did

A companion paper to Omer 2018, in rats. Long-Evans observer rats watched demonstrator rats run an observational T-maze (the observer needed to track the demonstrator's choice to retrieve reward), while the authors recorded single units in dorsal CA1 of the observer.

## Key findings relevant to the SD-011 generalization

Two complementary results to Omer 2018:

1. *Joint and discrete coding.* CA1 pyramidal cells jointly and discretely encoded the spatial location of both self and other.
2. *Shared receptive fields.* A subset of cells had *identical* receptive fields for self-position and other-position. These are the most striking — the same cell fires when the rat itself is at location X *or* when the demonstrator is at location X, abstracting over the agent identity.

The shared-receptive-field result is the strongest version of the social-channel claim. It argues that what the hippocampus tracks is not "self at location X" but something closer to "an agent at location X," with self and other as two instances of the more general primitive.

## REE translation

For the SD-011 generalization, this is the second confirmation (across species) that social/conspecific position has privileged map representation. Beyond that, the shared-receptive-field finding is architecturally intriguing: it suggests that the privileged channel is not "social as a separate stream" but rather "the spatial map natively supports multiple agents." This dovetails with REE's planning architecture (E2 simulates trajectories — but whose? the agent's own, or also a partner's?) and with the residue-field formalism if extended to multi-agent traces.

## Limitations and caveats

Observational T-maze is heavily constrained: rats are head-restrained, the demonstrator's trajectory is fixed, and only two agents are present. The shared-receptive-field cells are a subset, not the bulk of the recorded population, so the strong claim is about *some* cells, not the whole CA1. Whether free interaction with multiple conspecifics produces the same coding pattern is open.

## Confidence

0.80 — Science paper, replicates Omer 2018 in a different species and paradigm, careful single-unit recording. Confidence bounded by the paradigm constraints and the subset nature of the shared-field cells.
