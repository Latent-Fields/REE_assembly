# Lansink, Goltstein, Lankelma, McNaughton, and Pennartz (2009) -- Hippocampus leads ventral striatum in replay of place-reward information

**PLoS Biology 7(8):e1000173 | DOI: 10.1371/journal.pbio.1000173 | PMID: 19688032**

## What the paper did

Lansink and colleagues recorded simultaneously from large ensembles of hippocampal CA1 place cells and ventral striatum neurons in freely moving rats during waking exploration (on a circular track with a fixed reward location) and subsequent slow-wave sleep. Using cross-regional reactivation analysis, they asked whether hippocampal place cells and reward-related ventral striatal neurons co-reactivated during sleep in a way that preserved the temporal relationships observed during waking. They specifically tested whether replay was stronger for hippocampal-striatal cell pairs that had been conjunctively active at the reward location during waking.

## Key findings for SD-014

The central finding is that hippocampal-striatal co-reactivation during sleep was significantly stronger than chance, and was dominated by cell pairs where the hippocampal place cell had fired at the reward location while the striatal cell was reward-selective during waking. Two further findings are important for SD-014: first, replay was temporally ordered -- hippocampal cells fired before their striatal partners, suggesting that the spatial tag in the hippocampal node retrieves the reward signal rather than being driven by it; second, the replay was not merely a reflection of overall activity correlation but specifically linked to the spatial-reward contingency experienced during waking.

## REE mapping: hippocampal nodes as the retrieval cue for valence consolidation

SD-014 proposes that hippocampal map nodes store valence components (w, l, h, s) that are updated incrementally and consolidated during offline replay. The Lansink et al. result provides direct evidence for the consolidation side of this claim. The hippocampus-leads-striatum ordering is particularly informative: it suggests that during SWR replay, the spatial map node is the initiating signal that drives reactivation of associated reward information -- precisely the write-mechanism SD-014 requires. If the striatum led the hippocampus, one would infer that reward signals drive map reactivation rather than the map driving reward updating; the observed opposite ordering supports SD-014's architecture where valence is stored at the node level and consolidated by the node firing.

The study also establishes that replay is not uniform across the map: it is preferentially organised around reward-location cell pairs. This selective reactivation is the empirical signature of what SD-014 formalises as the valence-weighted replay prioritisation rule: high-valence nodes generate stronger or more frequent replay signatures.

## Limitations

The paper measures replay as binary co-reactivation of place-reward cell pairs, not as ordered sequences with decoded spatial trajectories. It does not distinguish which component of valence (wanting vs. liking vs. harm vs. surprise) the ventral striatal reward cells encode. The study is restricted to sleep replay; awake SWR replay, which is increasingly seen as the more planning-relevant mechanism, is not examined. The study also records from dorsal hippocampus and lateral/medial ventral striatum, leaving open whether the same dynamics hold at the ventral hippocampus-medial shell interface that is more relevant to drive-state modulation.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence is 0.78. This is an important mechanistic paper establishing that hippocampal replay coordinates specifically with reward-encoding striatal neurons during sleep, and that the hippocampal node leads temporally. These two facts together support the SD-014 consolidation mechanism. The main limitation is that the paper cannot dissect the w/l/h/s components of the valence vector or show that drive-state modulates which pairs are replayed. It is best read as establishing the structural coupling (hippocampal map nodes link to mesolimbic reward signals during offline phases) that SD-014's valence update rules require.
