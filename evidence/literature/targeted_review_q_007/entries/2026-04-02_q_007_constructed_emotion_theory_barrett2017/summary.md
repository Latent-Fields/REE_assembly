# Barrett (2017) — The Theory of Constructed Emotion

## What the paper does

Barrett presents the Theory of Constructed Emotion (TCE), proposing that emotions are not discrete natural kinds generated by dedicated neural circuits but are instead constructed from domain-general processes: interoceptive prediction (core affect comprising valence and arousal), sensory prediction, and conceptual categorization. The brain uses active inference to construct emotional episodes, with precision-weighted prediction errors driving belief updating. Emotion categories (anger, fear, etc.) are conceptual overlays on continuous interoceptive-exteroceptive prediction, not carved-at-the-joints biological categories.

## Relevance to Q-007

Q-007 asks whether universal emotional expressions map to stable control-channel regimes in z_beta. Barrett's TCE provides a nuanced answer:

**Supporting aspect:** Core affect (valence and arousal) is acknowledged as a genuine, continuous neural signal tied to interoceptive prediction. This aligns with z_beta as a dimensional affective latent — there is a real underlying signal to track.

**Weakening aspect:** The theory predicts no stable discrete regimes corresponding to basic emotion categories. If Q-007 expects z_beta to show distinct attractor states for "anger," "fear," "happiness," the TCE says this is biologically implausible — those categories are constructed, not wired.

**Mixed overall:** The theory suggests that Q-007 should be reframed. Rather than asking whether discrete regimes exist, the question should be whether z_beta exhibits dimensional structure (valence x arousal gradients) that correlates with E3 precision dynamics. This is a more tractable and biologically plausible formulation.

## Implications for REE architecture

The active inference framing in the TCE aligns well with REE's precision-weighting architecture. Barrett's model predicts that interoceptive prediction errors (analogous to z_beta deviations from homeostatic setpoint) would modulate precision globally — which is exactly what MECH-025 and ARC-016 implement. The disagreement is about whether the modulation is categorical (regime) or continuous (gradient).

For REE's design, this suggests z_beta should be implemented as a continuous space with potential for learned clustering rather than hardcoded discrete states.
