# Summary: Boureau & Dayan 2011 -- Opponency Revisited: Competition and Cooperation Between Dopamine and Serotonin

**Entry:** 2026-04-09_inv_052_opponency_da_5ht_boureau2011
**Claim tested:** INV-052
**Evidence direction:** supports

## What the paper did

Boureau and Dayan set out to resolve a long-standing puzzle: if dopamine and serotonin are "opponent" systems, opponent along what axis? Prior accounts treated the opponency as valence-based (dopamine = reward, serotonin = punishment), but this generates predictions that do not match the pharmacological evidence. The paper constructs a two-dimensional framework: valence (reward-punishment) and action (invigoration-inhibition). Crucially, serotonin's role in this framework is not simply "anti-dopamine" -- its function depends on threat proximity, behavioural controllability, and the opportunity structure of the environment. The most important novel formulation for INV-052 is their characterisation of tonic serotonin as encoding the "opportunity benefit for the passage of time, leading to quiescence."

## Key findings

The opportunity-benefit framing is the paper's central contribution for our purposes. Tonic serotonin, in Boureau and Dayan's model, sets a baseline expectation that waiting and persisting has positive value -- separate from any phasic reward signal. This is not about inhibiting approach in response to punishment; it is about maintaining a positive prior over the value of continued engagement with the environment. In harm-rich environments, this prior is exactly what prevents the benefit terrain from collapsing: the tonic floor says "continued pursuit of goals has value even in the presence of harm," which is structurally equivalent to what MECH-186 requires.

The paper also demonstrates that the dopamine-serotonin relationship is not monotonically opponent: at different points in the invigoration-inhibition-valence space, the two systems cooperate rather than compete. This is important because it means the tonic 5-HT floor is not simply "opposing" benefit signalling -- it is operating in a partially orthogonal regulatory space that dopamine does not cover.

## Translation to INV-052

The "opportunity benefit for time" formulation is the closest published statement to MECH-186's tonic floor concept. Where the Dayan-Huys papers establish that a tonic floor is *necessary* (via asymmetry and failure-mode arguments), Boureau and Dayan provide a positive characterisation of what that floor *does*: it maintains the a priori value of continued goal-directed engagement. In REE terms, the terrain stage (MECH-186) is supported because the floor preserves a positive gradient over the latent benefit space even when local harm signals dominate. The transduction stage (MECH-187) is indirectly supported because the floor sets the baseline from which VALENCE_WANTING seeding gain is computed: without the floor, seeding gain approaches zero as the punishment baseline dominates.

The maintenance stage (MECH-188) receives the least direct support from this paper, but the quiescence framing is relevant: quiescent persistence requires that goal representations be maintained across time, which is the maintenance stage's function.

## Limitations

The paper models tonic 5-HT as regulating invigoration-inhibition, not directly terrain representation. REE's terrain is a spatial/latent construct; Boureau and Dayan's framework is action-oriented. The translation from "opportunity benefit for time" to "spatial benefit gradient maintenance" requires an inferential step -- these are compatible but not identical concepts. The paper also does not distinguish between the three INV-052 stages, and the quiescence framing could be read as opposing approach rather than supporting it, which creates a surface-level tension with benefit orientation maintenance.
