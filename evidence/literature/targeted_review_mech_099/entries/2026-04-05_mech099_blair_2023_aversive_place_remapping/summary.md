# Blair et al. 2023 -- Hippocampal Place Cell Remapping Marks Remembered Aversive Events

**Source:** Blair GJ, Guo C, Wang S, Fanselow MS, Golshani P, Aharoni D, Blair HT. "Hippocampal place cell remapping occurs with memory storage of aversive experiences." *eLife* 12:e80661, 2023. DOI: [10.7554/eLife.80661](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.80661). PMID: 37466236

**Claim evidenced:** MECH-099

---

## What the paper did

Blair et al. used wide-field miniscope calcium imaging to track hundreds of CA1 place cells in mice across sessions in a familiar environment. In one session, mice received unexpected foot shocks at specific locations. Researchers tracked whether place cells remapped (changed their firing field locations) following the shock, and whether remapping was selective for remembered vs forgotten shock experiences. They also analysed whether remapping cells were preferentially recruited into SWR replay events during post-shock rest.

## Key findings relevant to REE

Foot shocks caused CA1 place cells to remap significantly more than in unshocked control sessions. Crucially, remapping was selective: cells remapped significantly more following remembered shocks than forgotten ones, establishing a direct link between harm-associated place cell reorganisation and memory storage. Remapping cells were preferentially recruited into SWR replay events during the post-shock rest period, at higher rates than stable (non-remapping) cells, despite having similar firing rates during navigation.

## Translation to REE

MECH-099 claims the residue field encodes the history of where harm occurred, maintained by the E3 hippocampal system. Blair et al. provide the most direct existing evidence for this claim: the hippocampal spatial map is literally reorganised at the location where harm was encountered, and this reorganisation is (a) gated by memory storage (only remembered harms remap), and (b) fed preferentially into the SWR consolidation stream.

The memory-gated remapping directly implements the selective write property that MECH-099 requires: harm events that are remembered update the residue field; forgotten events do not. This is not a passive sensory trace -- it is an active, memory-dependent map update.

The SWR recruitment of remapping cells closes the loop to SD-017: the harm-location-specific spatial signal enters the offline consolidation pipeline, which SD-017 claims is necessary for hippocampal attribution mapping to converge. These two claims are thus directly linked by this finding: MECH-099's harm map update feeds into SD-017's necessary consolidation stream.

## Limitations and caveats

The paradigm involves a single shock session, not multi-episode accumulation. MECH-099 specifically emphasises accumulated harm history. Whether repeated encounters with harm at the same location progressively strengthen the spatial representation (as implied by accumulation) is not tested. The study uses contextual fear conditioning, which involves a strong one-time aversive event; residue field accumulation in REE would involve weaker, repeated harm encounters whose cumulative effect builds the field. The spatial scale is a small chamber -- whether the same remapping mechanism supports the multi-zone residue field geometry MECH-099 requires is an open question.
