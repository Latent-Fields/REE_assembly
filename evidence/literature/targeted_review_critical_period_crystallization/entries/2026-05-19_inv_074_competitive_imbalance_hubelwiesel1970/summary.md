# Hubel & Wiesel (1970) — The period of susceptibility to unilateral eye closure in kittens

*According to PubMed. J Physiol 206(2):419-36. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1113/jphysiol.1970.sp009022) · PMID 5498493 · PMC1348655.*

## What the paper did

This is the experiment that gave the critical period its empirical body. Hubel and Wiesel sutured one eyelid shut in kittens at different ages and for different durations, then recorded from striate cortex and asked how many cells the deprived eye could still drive. The result is one of the cleanest dose-by-timing curves in neuroscience: susceptibility appears abruptly near the start of the fourth week, stays high through weeks six to eight, and is gone by the end of the third month. Inside that window, three to four days of closure is enough to collapse the deprived eye's cortical influence — cells drivable by that eye fall from roughly 85% to about 7%. Outside it, a *year* of closure in an adult cat does nothing measurable. And recovery, once a window is missed, is meagre: even five years of subsequent eye opening produced only limited cortical return.

## Why it matters for INV-074

INV-074 asserts that a model-building agent under Hebbian-equivalent learning will converge to monostrategy — its under-driven pathway permanently suppressed — unless a time-bounded plasticity asymmetry lets competing circuits establish competitive weight before winner-take-all dynamics close the option space. Hubel & Wiesel are the biological prototype of every clause in that sentence. The window is real and sharply bounded; deprivation inside it is near-permanent; the same deprivation outside it is inert; and recovery requires *active competitive rebalancing* (closing the strong eye), not mere re-exposure. That last point is the one I want to underline, because it is easy to miss: the mechanism is competition, not disuse. A REE analogue that suppressed diversity by simple decay rather than by active dominant-pathway competition would not reproduce this phenomenology, and INV-074's framing as a *competitive* special case (its dependence on INV-075) is exactly right on this evidence.

## Limitations and the honest caveat

One sensory system, one species, one manipulation. INV-074 claims universality, and a single developmental paradigm — however foundational — cannot carry "universal" by itself. What it can do is establish that the phenomenon is real, causal, and time-bounded in a biological substrate; the substrate-independence then has to come from convergence with the artificial-network evidence (Achille, Rovere & Soatto 2019, filed alongside this). The analogy from "deprived eye" to "minority diversity circuit" is a mapping of competitive dynamics, not anatomy, and I have logged the strong-irreversibility result as a failure signature precisely because it cuts against any hope that a late, large diversity signal could rescue a missed window — INV-074 says it cannot, and Hubel & Wiesel agree, uncomfortably.

0.84, `supports`. Source quality is as high as the field has. Mapping fidelity is strong for the window-and-permanence logic and weaker for the explicitly REE-flavoured "monostrategy" gloss.
