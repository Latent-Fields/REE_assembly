# Fagiolini & Hensch (2000) — Inhibitory threshold for critical-period activation in primary visual cortex

*According to PubMed. Nature 404(6774):183-6. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/35004582) · PMID 10724170.*

## What the paper did

Hubel & Wiesel told us the window exists. Fagiolini & Hensch told us what opens it — and showed it could be opened on command. They worked with mice lacking GAD65, an isoform of the GABA-synthetic enzyme, and found these animals never spontaneously enter their ocular-dominance critical period: the potential for plasticity simply lies dormant. A single benzodiazepine treatment, boosting GABAergic transmission, unmasked the plasticity — at any age. Conversely, enhancing GABA transmission early in life caused the window to be spent prematurely, leaving the adult permanently insensitive to monocular deprivation, just like a normal wild-type mouse that has already passed through its critical period. The conclusion is sharp: a threshold level of intracortical inhibition triggers, once in life, an experience-dependent window for circuit consolidation.

## Why it matters for INV-074

INV-074's load-bearing phrase is "unless a time-bounded plasticity asymmetry allows competing diversity circuits to establish competitive weight." That presupposes the asymmetry is a *thing that gets triggered* — not a passive consequence of accumulating training. Fagiolini & Hensch are the causal demonstration that this is so: the window is gated by a controllable threshold, can be forced open pharmacologically at any age, and can be closed early by pushing the threshold prematurely. This is the strongest licence in the biological literature for treating REE's MECH-333 open phase as a discrete, parameterised trigger rather than an emergent artefact of optimisation dynamics. It also sharpens INV-074 in a way worth stating plainly: the danger is not only a *missed* window but a *prematurely spent* one — over-early closure forecloses the diversity circuits just as surely as never opening.

## Limitations and the honest caveat

The trigger they identified is a concrete neurochemical quantity — intracortical GABA, the excitation/inhibition balance. REE has no GABA and no E/I balance in any literal sense, so this entry does not transfer a mechanism; it transfers a *structure*: "a controllable scalar threshold opens a one-shot plasticity window, and the timing of that threshold-crossing is itself consequential." That abstraction is clean and the paper supports it strongly, but I have kept mapping fidelity below the source quality to mark the gap. And note the scope: this is evidence for *opening* (INV-074, MECH-333). It says nothing direct about *closure as active locking* — that is Pizzorusso and Morishita, in the same folder.
