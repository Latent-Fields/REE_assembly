# Active interoceptive inference and the emotional brain

**Seth & Friston (2016) -- Phil Trans R Soc B -- DOI: 10.1098/rstb.2016.0007 -- PMID: 28080966**

## What the paper did

Seth and Friston present a comprehensive theoretical synthesis connecting interoception -- the brain's perception and regulation of internal bodily states -- to the free energy principle and active Bayesian inference. The paper argues that interoception should not be understood as passive readout of visceral signals but as the inversion of a hierarchical generative model: the brain maintains predictions about internal milieu states (cardiac, gastric, respiratory, immunological) and updates these predictions via ascending interoceptive signals. What we experience as embodied selfhood -- the felt sense of being a body from the inside -- is proposed to emerge from the joint hierarchical content of this predictive model across interoceptive, proprioceptive, and exteroceptive channels. The paper contextualises this framework within active inference and examines implications for emotion, neuromodulation as precision-weighting, and clinical conditions including depression and autism.

## Key findings relevant to MECH-113

Two claims are central. First, the authors argue that maintaining the fidelity of the interoceptive generative model -- keeping predictions accurate and residuals small -- constitutes the maintenance of selfhood. When this model degrades (predictions chronically wrong, residuals large) the agent cannot sustain a stable self-representation. The Markov blanket formulation is explicit: internal states minimise free energy (surprise) about self-generated sensory states, and this minimisation IS the self-organising process. Second, the paper links this framework to psychiatric phenomenology: depressive emotional numbing maps to an interoceptive model that has become insensitive to sensory context, generating the same predictions regardless of input. The self-model is not absent but it has become rigid and miscalibrated.

## REE translation

MECH-113 claims the agent needs a self-maintenance error signal monitoring internal latent coherence independently of external harm signals. Seth and Friston provide precisely the theoretical grounding for why this is necessary: without monitoring the fidelity of its self-generative model, the agent cannot detect silent degradation of z_self. The Markov blanket framing and the D_eff coherence framing converge: maintaining z_self fidelity (keeping the self-model prediction accurate) IS maintaining the blanket. The MECH-113 self-maintenance loss -- penalising high D_eff, high self-prediction error -- is the computational analog of the interoceptive prediction error signal that, in this framework, constitutes interoceptive distress. This grounding is not merely metaphorical: it specifies what kind of error signal is warranted and what its failure modes should look like.

## Limitations and caveats

The transfer risk here is genuine and should not be minimised. Seth and Friston's framework is grounded in visceral and autonomic interoception -- cardiac baroreceptors, gastric mechanoreceptors, respiratory signals -- signals that are biologically ancient and tightly coupled to survival-relevant homeostatic loops. z_self in REE is a latent representation learned by an LSTM over motor-sensory prediction errors in a grid-world abstraction. The mathematical structure (Bayesian inference over self-generated states) is preserved in principle, but the specific signal modalities, the precision-weighting mechanisms (neuromodulation -> attention in REE), and the temporal dynamics are all different. The theoretical framework supports MECH-113's existence claim but does not constrain its implementation choices directly. A second caveat: the paper is a theoretical review integrating heterogeneous empirical evidence. The primary empirical supports are the rubber hand illusion paradigm (body ownership), cardiac interoception experiments, and psychiatric case descriptions -- all at the level of whole-organism phenomenology, not latent self-model geometry.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence is placed at 0.80. The source quality is high (Phil Trans R Soc B, Seth and Friston are the originating authorities on this framework, the paper has >1000 citations). The mapping fidelity is strong because MECH-113 was designed with this framing in mind -- the convergence is not post-hoc. Transfer risk is the main limiter: the jump from biological interoceptive inference to artificial LSTM latent self-monitoring is architecturally substantial, and it is genuinely unclear whether D_eff as implemented in REE captures the Markov blanket properties described here. The paper provides strong theoretical motivation and a coherent failure-mode taxonomy, but empirical confirmation in the REE substrate remains the necessary next step.
