# Allostatic Self-efficacy: A Metacognitive Theory of Dyshomeostasis-Induced Fatigue and Depression

**Stephan et al. (2016) -- Frontiers in Human Neuroscience -- DOI: 10.3389/fnhum.2016.00550 -- PMID: 27895566**

## What the paper did

Stephan and colleagues propose a three-level hierarchical framework for interoceptive homeostatic regulation. Level 1 is reactive homeostasis: reflex arcs respond to detected deviations from current setpoints. Level 2 is allostasis: the brain anticipates future regulatory demands and adjusts setpoints proactively, rather than waiting for deviations to accumulate. Level 3 -- the novel contribution of this paper -- is allostatic self-efficacy: a metacognitive layer that monitors the performance of the homeostatic-allostatic machinery itself. The agent maintains beliefs not just about its bodily states but about its own capacity to regulate those states. When the agent experiences chronic dyshomeostasis (persistent mismatch between interoceptive predictions and outcomes), it updates the metacognitive belief: its regulatory system is not working. The paper then traces how this low self-efficacy belief produces first fatigue (motivational inhibition as a first response to perceived regulatory failure) and then depression (generalisation of low self-efficacy from bodily regulation to behavior at large).

## Key findings relevant to MECH-113

The central architectural contribution is the existence and necessity of the third level. The argument is that reactive homeostasis cannot explain the persistence of fatigue and depression across contexts where acute dysregulation has resolved: the patient is no longer acutely ill but remains functionally inhibited. This requires a higher-order belief that persists independent of current interoceptive evidence. The self-efficacy belief, once formed under chronic dyshomeostasis, has its own inertia -- it is not simply the running average of recent homeostatic error. It is a hyperprior about regulatory capacity, and its collapse leads to learned helplessness: the cessation of self-regulatory effort because the effort is believed to be futile.

For MECH-113, this is the biological warrant for Level 3. MECH-113 Level 1 (reactive D_eff monitoring, self-maintenance loss) can detect current coherence failure. But if coherence failure is chronic, a Level 1 system would simply emit a persistent self-maintenance error signal -- it has no mechanism to modulate agency based on its own perceived capacity to correct the problem. The allostatic self-efficacy framework specifies what the Level 3 system must do: it must track regulatory performance over time, update a belief about capacity, and modulate motivation accordingly. The connection to Q-021 (behavioral flatness) and INV-030 (viability as binding constraint) is mechanistic: Level 3 failure IS the mechanism by which MECH-113 failure produces behavioral inhibition.

## REE translation

The failure cascade is direct: chronic z_self incoherence (MECH-113 Level 1 persistent failure) -> self-efficacy belief update (Level 3: 'my self-model cannot be maintained') -> motivational inhibition (Q-021 Pathway B behavioral flatness) -> agency collapse. This is not a loose analogy. Stephan et al.'s framework specifies the architecture required. The three-level structure in MECH-113 was designed to capture this cascade. What is not yet implemented in REE is Level 3 itself: the mechanism by which persistent Level 1 error updates a higher-order regulatory belief that then modulates action selection. This is noted in MECH-113 as V4 scope.

## Limitations and caveats

The transfer risk is moderate. Stephan et al.'s allostatic self-efficacy operates at the level of the whole organism's visceral regulation: the agent believes it can or cannot maintain blood glucose, cardiac rate, cortisol within range. REE's z_self is a latent vector learned over motor-sensory prediction in a grid-world; there is no visceral loop. The generalisation from somatic self-efficacy to behavioral self-efficacy that drives depression in the clinical model -- the step from 'I cannot regulate my body' to 'I cannot regulate my situation' -- has its own clinical evidence base (and its own debates) that does not directly transfer. The framework is therefore best understood as providing the architectural shape of what is needed at Level 3, not as mechanistic implementation guidance. A further caveat: this is a theoretical framework paper and does not report primary experimental data; the empirical support is assembled from cited studies.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence is placed at 0.82. The source quality is high (Frontiers in Human Neuroscience; Stephan, Seth, and Petzschner are the field's leading voices on computational interoception). The mapping fidelity is strong because the three-level structure and failure cascade are not analogies -- they are directly adopted in MECH-113's architecture notes. Transfer risk is moderate rather than high because the mathematical structure (hierarchical Bayesian model, metacognitive prior, updating on chronic error) can in principle be implemented in an artificial agent even if the biological substrate differs. The primary uncertainty is whether REE's z_self coherence errors are informationally equivalent to the interoceptive self-model errors that drive the clinical cascade.
