# Targeted Review: ARC-045 -- Bidirectional Hippocampus-Neocortex Flow for Attribution Convergence

**Claim:** ARC-045 -- Bidirectional hippocampus-neocortex information flow is architecturally necessary for attribution mapping convergence: the SWS-direction (hippocampus to cortex) installs context schema structure; waking and REM-direction reactivations fill those structures with causal attribution estimates. Maps onto the generative/recognition architecture of hierarchical Bayesian models.

## Research Question

Is there evidence that both directions of hippocampal-neocortical information flow are required for attribution mapping convergence, and that this bidirectionality maps onto the generative/recognition duality of hierarchical Bayesian models?

## Key Questions Addressed

1. Direct electrophysiological evidence for bidirectional hippocampus-neocortex flow during NREM SWRs -- YES (Hou 2024, RSC-CA1-RSC circuit with directional asymmetry)
2. Computational theory grounding the architectural necessity of bidirectionality (generative/recognition duality) -- YES (Friston 2005, hierarchical free-energy minimisation)
3. NREM-REM cycling as the temporal implementation of iterative bidirectional convergence -- YES (Langille 2019)

## Summary Assessment

All 3 entries support ARC-045. The evidence package covers:
- Direct electrophysiological demonstration of bidirectionality (RSC -> CA1 pre-SWR; CA1 -> RSC post-SWR) with directional asymmetry that maps onto generative/recognition duality (Hou 2024)
- Computational theory grounding the architectural necessity of bidirectionality in hierarchical Bayesian systems (Friston 2005)
- NREM-REM cycling as iterative convergence implementation, with directional specificity between phases (Langille 2019)

**Main gap remaining:** No paper directly tests whether blocking one direction (hippocampus-to-neocortex OR neocortex-to-hippocampus) specifically impairs attribution convergence. The necessity of bidirectionality is supported by convergent structural and theoretical evidence but not by a direct causal manipulation of directionality.
