# Guilhemsang & Mallet (2023) — Arkypallidal neurons in basal ganglia circuits: unveiling novel pallidostriatal loops?

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.conb.2023.102814); PMID 38016260).

## What the source covers

A focused review of Arkypallidal neurons, a subpopulation of GABAergic neurons in the external globus pallidus (GPe) that project back to the dorsal striatum. This is structurally distinct from the classic Prototypic GPe neurons that project downstream to subthalamic nucleus and other BG nuclei. The Arkypallidal-to-striatum projection was identified in the early 2010s and has since been characterised as one of the largest extrinsic GABA inputs to dorsal striatum.

## Key points

The Arkypallidal projection forms an intrinsic feedback loop within the basal ganglia: striatum → GPe (Arkypallidal subset) → striatum. This is functionally distinct from the conventional direct/indirect pathway dichotomy in two ways. First, it provides feedback inhibition rather than feedforward selection. Second, the Arkypallidal output is differentially modulated by the direct- and indirect-pathway striatal projection neurons, suggesting it may act as a balancing mechanism between selection and inhibition. The review speculates on roles in action execution vs inhibition, with the proximal hypothesis being that Arkypallidal recruitment biases toward action cancellation.

## Mapping to Q-019

Q-019's existing 14-entry literature review canvasses the question "are the three cortico-striatal loops anatomically distinct or convergent" through papers spanning Alexander, Haber, Frank/O'Reilly, the recent Ambrosi 2022 / Scott 2024 / Zheng 2024 / Yu 2021 entries, and others. None of these address the intra-loop feedback architecture. Guilhemsang's review introduces that dimension. The architectural debate in Q-019 is not just about whether the three loops are parallel or convergent — it is also about whether each loop has internal feedback structure that shapes its temporal dynamics (commit vs cancel, sustain vs release).

For REE specifically, this matters at the E3 commit-boundary level. If each BG loop has internal feedback inhibition that biases toward cancellation, then the commit boundary is partly enforced by Arkypallidal-style feedback within the relevant loop. ARC-021's three-loop framing would need to specify, for each loop, whether such internal feedback exists and what computational role it plays. Yu 2021's DMS habit-acquisition work (already in the review) is a candidate locus for the Arkypallidal contribution to motor-loop dynamics; the analog within the thought loop and the sensorium loop is not yet characterised.

## Caveats

Functional role of Arkypallidal neurons is still being characterised; the action-execution-vs-inhibition story is the leading hypothesis but not settled. The review focuses on dorsal striatum (motor loop in Q-019's terminology) and the implications for the other two loops are by extension. The Arkypallidal projection's existence is well-established; the higher-level claim that it should restructure Q-019's architectural framing is my interpretive translation, not the authors'.
