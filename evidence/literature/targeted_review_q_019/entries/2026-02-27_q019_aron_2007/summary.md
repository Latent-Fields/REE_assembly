# Aron et al. (2007) — Triangulating a Cognitive Control Network (IFC-STN-preSMA)

**Q-019 relevance: strongest anatomical evidence for a dedicated action commitment gate**

Aron et al. (2007) used diffusion-weighted MRI tractography combined with fMRI to identify and anatomically validate a three-node right-hemisphere control network: right inferior frontal cortex (IFC), presupplementary motor area (preSMA), and subthalamic nucleus (STN). White matter connections between all three nodes were confirmed in 7–9 of 10 subjects.

**Key mechanisms:**
- **Hyperdirect pathway**: IFC → STN (bypasses striatum). STN → GPi excitation → thalamocortical suppression. This is the "stop/brake" circuit for motor commitment, faster than canonical striatal routes.
- **Go pathway (canonical)**: Premotor cortex → Striatum → GPi → Thalamus → Motor cortex (action initiation).
- **Conflict monitoring**: preSMA detects competing response tendencies and recruits IFC/STN braking. Individual differences in preSMA activation specifically predict conflict-induced slowing (not outright stopping).
- **Stopping**: Right IFC and STN activation correlates with stop-signal reaction time across subjects (r=0.53), not preSMA.

**Three-gate mapping for Q-019:**
- IFC-STN hyperdirect pathway → **action commitment gate** substrate: dedicated fast-acting circuit for preventing irreversible commitment during conflict
- STN → GPi suppression → **commit boundary enforcement mechanism**: gate arrests motor execution
- preSMA conflict detection → **pre-commit error channel**: signals conflict before commitment, recruits gate authority

**Critical architectural implication for REE**: The action commitment gate uses a distinct BG pathway (IFC-STN-GPi, hyperdirect) compared to the cognitive/thought-loop gates (SNr-based disinhibition in PBWM). This anatomical separation supports the Q-019 claim that the three gates are distinct circuits, not one circuit with different parameters.
