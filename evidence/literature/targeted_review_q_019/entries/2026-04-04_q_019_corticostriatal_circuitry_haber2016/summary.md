# Summary: Haber 2016 — Corticostriatal Circuitry Review

**Source:** Haber SN. "Corticostriatal circuitry." *Dialogues in Clinical Neuroscience*, 2016, 18(1):7-21. DOI: [10.31887/DCNS.2016.18.1/shaber](https://doi.org/10.31887/DCNS.2016.18.1/shaber)

**Evidence direction for Q-019:** mixed (strongest support for three-gate distinctness from modern human data, combined with explicit characterisation of interface convergence zones)

---

This is the most useful single review for Q-019 because it synthesises three decades of primate tract-tracing with modern human fMRI and diffusion tractography. The result is the clearest available statement of what the anatomy actually shows: topographic parallel organisation is real and confirmed in humans, and functional territory boundaries in the striatum (ventral/limbic, caudate/cognitive, putamen/sensorimotor) are replicable across methods and species. But interactions between functional territories are extensive, concentrated at interface regions, and relevant to goal-directed behavior.

The three-domain partition of human striatum is confirmed across multiple imaging modalities. The ventral striatum, centred on the nucleus accumbens and extending into the ventromedial caudate, receives the strongest input from orbital and medial prefrontal cortex, anterior ACC, hippocampus, and amygdala -- the limbic complement. The caudate nucleus receives primarily from DLPFC, lateral OFC, and anterior cingulate -- the cognitive/associative complement. The putamen receives from sensorimotor and premotor cortex -- the motor complement. These are not merely statistical tendencies; they reflect the underlying topographic gradient of corticostriatal axon density. The REE three-gate partition maps onto these three striatal zones with high fidelity.

The interface zones are described as specific anatomical regions where the boundaries between functional territories become graded. The transition between ventral striatum and caudate body (the limbic-cognitive interface) is where goal and motivational signals interact with trajectory planning. The transition between caudate and putamen (the cognitive-motor interface) is where planned actions are handed off to motor execution. These transitions are not random; they correspond anatomically to the REE concept of gate handoff -- the point at which the thought loop passes a committed trajectory to the action commitment loop. The anatomy provides not just confirmation that the gates are distinct but a sketch of where and how the transitions occur.

For Q-019 the implications are clear. The question as posed may underestimate the complexity: the three gates are anatomically supported as the primary organising principle, but the interface zones are real and functionally important. A single-gate model would not predict the systematic topographic gradient within the human striatum. A pure three-gate model with hermetically sealed circuits would not predict the interface convergence zones or the dopaminergic spiral. The correct architectural inference is probably that the REE three-gate model is anatomically warranted, with the addition that the interface regions must be modelled if the system is to correctly handle the handoff from harm/goal evaluation in the limbic loop to trajectory evaluation in the cognitive loop. The 2016 Haber review is the strongest modern evidence base for this combined picture.
