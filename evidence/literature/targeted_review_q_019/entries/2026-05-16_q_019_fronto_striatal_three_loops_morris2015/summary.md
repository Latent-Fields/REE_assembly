# Morris et al. 2015 — Fronto-striatal organization: Defining functional and microstructural substrates of behavioural flexibility

**DOI:** [10.1016/j.cortex.2015.11.004](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cortex.2015.11.004) | *Cortex*, 2015 | **Claim:** Q-019

---

## What the paper did

Morris and colleagues mapped frontal cortical projections to the striatum in 59 healthy adults using a high-sensitivity multi-echo resting-state fMRI sequence combined with NODDI (neurite orientation dispersion and density imaging) microstructure MRI. Three behavioral tasks were also administered: the Cambridge Gambling Task (goal-directed model-based learning), probabilistic reversal learning, and attentional shifting. The goal was to establish both the functional architecture and the tissue-level microstructural substrates of the parallel fronto-striatal circuits.

## Key findings

Three anatomically and functionally distinct circuits emerged clearly from both the connectivity and the microstructure data. The limbic circuit linked vmPFC, VS, and VTA — functional connectivity here predicted goal-directed, model-based behaviour on the gambling task, and probabilistic reversal learning correlated specifically with lateral OFC to VS connectivity. The sensorimotor circuit linked SMA, putamen, and SNc — connectivity here predicted model-free habitual responding. The associative/cognitive circuit linked lateral PFC and caudate. Attentional shifting correlated with dlPFC to VS connectivity, consistent with an associative role in executive control of what inputs reach the limbic gate.

NODDI microstructure confirmed the dissociation at tissue level: mOFC neurite density correlated with model-based performance (goal-directed circuit), while putamen neurite complexity correlated with model-free performance (habit circuit). These microstructural correlates are highly specific and not explained by volumetric or grey-matter density differences alone.

## How findings translate to REE

Q-019 asks whether the three cortico-striatal loops are anatomically distinct or convergent at a single gate. Morris et al. 2015 provide the clearest human-level answer in the direction of anatomical and functional distinctness. The limbic circuit (vmPFC-VS-VTA) maps onto REE's sensorium loop — the circuit that gates what motivationally salient information the system processes. The associative circuit (lateral PFC-caudate) maps onto REE's thought loop, governing trajectory planning and the E3 complex's evaluative operations. The sensorimotor circuit (SMA-putamen-SNc) maps onto REE's action commitment loop, with the model-free habit association directly paralleling REE's automaticity gradient: expert performance migrates to this loop (lower overhead), and harm residue would be expected to re-engage the associative and limbic circuits to bring automated sequences back under deliberate control.

The behavioral dissociation is the key finding for Q-019: if a single unified BG gate implemented all three functions, the connectivity patterns should load onto a common factor. They do not — each circuit predicts a distinct behavioral phenotype that is statistically independent of the others.

## Limitations and caveats

The principal limitation for Q-019 purposes is that resting-state fMRI connectivity captures the existence and strength of the circuits, not the gating operations implemented within each. Q-019 is ultimately a question about what computational role each loop plays, not just that separate connectivity patterns exist. The model-based/model-free dichotomy, while anatomically homologous to REE's thought/action commitment distinction, is not identical: model-based inference in humans involves frontal evaluation of learned world models, not necessarily the E3 complex's hippocampal map navigation. Transfer risk is moderate-low given these are human data and Q-019 is framed for a human-like system.

## Confidence: 0.79 — supports Q-019 option (B)

Strong human neuroimaging evidence that three functionally and microstructurally distinct fronto-striatal circuits exist with non-overlapping behavioral correlates. The behavioral dissociation directly contradicts the unified-selector prediction. The main caveat is that connectivity evidence does not directly demonstrate the gating operations, which REE's three-loop model specifies in mechanistic detail.
