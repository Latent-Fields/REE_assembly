# Crick (1984) — The Searchlight Hypothesis (Thalamic Reticular Nucleus)

**Q-019 relevance: foundational support for thalamic sensorium gate; structural template for three-gate architecture**

*Bibliographic note: Original plan cited PMID 6584887 (incorrect, resolves to unrelated rat thymosin paper). Correct PMID is 6589612. DOI confirmed.*

Crick's 1984 searchlight hypothesis proposes that the thalamic reticular nucleus (TRN) controls selective attention by producing burst firing in a targeted subset of thalamic relay neurons while suppressing all others via GABAergic inhibition. The TRN is positioned as a "gateway controller" receiving collateral input from both thalamocortical and corticothalamic pathways.

**Key mechanisms:**
- **Push-pull gating**: The TRN activates a targeted thalamic channel (attended stimulus) while inhibiting all others. This is spatially and temporally precise, not a global gain change.
- **Burst firing**: Selected thalamic neurons fire in rapid bursts, amplifying signal detectability in the corresponding cortical region and acting as a "wake-up call" for environmental change.
- **Malsburg synapses**: Rapidly modifiable connections creating transient cell assemblies — including "vertical assemblies" uniting neurons across cortical hierarchy levels — that are the neural expression of bound attentional content.
- **Dynamics**: Assemblies are transient and reform with each attentional shift, making the searchlight a dynamic gate rather than a fixed routing switch.

**Three-gate mapping for Q-019:**
- TRN push-pull gating → **sensorium gate physical substrate**: TRN selects which sensory/sensorimotor stream gains access to cortical processing — this is the biological implementation of the sensorium loop's function.
- TRN burst mechanism → **timing and selectivity of gate opening**: burst dynamics explain how the gate can be fast and specific.
- Malsburg synapse / transient vertical assemblies → **thought loop binding mechanism**: rapidly reconfigurable assemblies uniting E1 predictions, E3 evaluations, and affective residue into candidate trajectories.
- Push-pull mutual exclusion → **simulation/execution mutual exclusion**: when action commitment gate fires, it should amplify the action-execution thalamic channel and suppress the simulation (thought-loop) channel — TRN push-pull provides the structural mechanism.

**Mapping caveat**: 1984 paper predates modern BG-thalamic and oscillatory frameworks. Searchlight specifically addresses sensory thalamic channels; extension to non-sensory nuclei (MD, VA, VL serving cognitive loops) requires additional claims. Malsburg synapse concept has evolved.
