# Wikenheiser & Redish 2015 — Hippocampal theta sequences reflect current goals

According to PubMed, Wikenheiser & Redish (2015, [Nat Neurosci 18:289-94](https://doi.org/10.1038/nn.3909)) recorded hippocampal CA1 single-unit and LFP activity in rats running a value-guided spatial decision-making task with multiple paths leading to proximal and distal goals. Theta-cycle place-cell sequences are well-established to represent time-compressed forward-projected trajectories through space; what this paper tested was whether the *content* of those sequences depends on what the animal is currently trying to do.

The headline finding has a clean structure. The look-ahead distance of theta-cycle sequences scaled with the distance to the current goal: longer look-ahead on journeys to distant goals than on journeys to proximal goals. Sequence content was predictive of the animal's destination — decoding the theta sequence allowed prediction of which goal the rat would visit next. Critically, the look-ahead distance was not a stable feature of the animal but varied moment-by-moment with the current goal. On arrival at goals, look-ahead became uniform regardless of where the journey began, suggesting that the goal-conditional scaling resets at goal completion (a finding incidentally relevant to SD-034's closure operator: post-completion the prospective representation collapses back to a uniform distance).

For MECH-267 (HippocampalModule.propose_trajectories conditioned on `operating_mode` in addition to world_state), this is direct empirical evidence that hippocampal trajectory content is conditioned on the agent's current goal hierarchy. The architectural reading is straightforward: the same hippocampal substrate, in the same physical environment, produces *different* prospective trajectory content depending on what the animal is trying to achieve. Translated to V3's HippocampalModule, this supports the claim that proposal content should depend on whether the agent is in a long-horizon planning mode (long look-ahead, distal-goal-projection trajectories) versus a short-horizon habit mode (short look-ahead, proximal-action trajectories) versus a default-mode-replay state (consolidation-relevant content rather than action-relevant). The mode-conditioning that MECH-267 specifies is the natural architectural reading of the goal-distance scaling Wikenheiser & Redish observed.

I want to flag two distinctions that constrain the transfer to MECH-267. The first is the conditioning-signal distinction: the conditioning signal in Wikenheiser & Redish is goal *distance*, not operating *mode*. These are related but not the same. Goal-distance variation can occur within a single operating mode — the same animal in habit mode running to goal-distance-2 versus goal-distance-5 would still show the look-ahead scaling without any mode-state shift. The paper therefore supports goal-conditioning of hippocampal sequences but does not directly support mode-conditioning. The natural composition is that operating_mode shapes the goal hierarchy (which goals are active, with what relative priority), the goal hierarchy shapes the goal-distance representation, and the goal-distance shapes look-ahead. The implementation question for V3 HippocampalModule is whether operating_mode enters this chain at the goal-hierarchy step (modulating which goals are active) or at the look-ahead step (modulating the horizon parameter directly). The paper does not discriminate.

The second distinction is timescale. Theta sequences are within-cycle hippocampal phenomena at roughly 120 ms scale. The V3 HippocampalModule.propose_trajectories operates at the trial-level proposal-generation scale (one proposal cycle per agent decision). If the goal-conditioning observed at the theta-sequence scale aggregates to a corresponding shift at the trial-level proposal scale, then MECH-267 inherits the support directly. If instead the conditioning is a sub-trial phenomenon that washes out at the proposal-scale aggregation, the support is at the wrong timescale. The paper provides evidence at the theta-cycle scale and does not directly test the trial-level aggregation. For V3 this is acceptable — the architectural commitment that hippocampal proposals are goal-conditioned is well-grounded — but it should be flagged that the timescale at which the conditioning operates may need to inform the V3 implementation (e.g., whether each proposal cycle generates a single fixed-look-ahead set of candidates, or whether the cycle samples across look-ahead distances proportional to goal-distance).

A third caveat is the species- / domain-transfer risk. The task is rat spatial navigation; REE operates over a more abstract action space (sensory predictions, movement primitives, residue-field navigation). Generalising from rat goal-conditioned theta sequences to REE's goal-conditioned trajectory proposals requires a transfer step. The structure-of-content analogy holds — the hippocampus represents what comes next, conditioned on what the agent is trying to achieve, and the look-ahead is goal-distance-modulated — but the implementation specifics (theta-cycle organisation, place-cell sequence decoding) are spatial-navigation-specific.

Confidence components: source quality high at 0.90 (Nat Neurosci 2015, Redish lab, primary empirical demonstration of goal-conditioned theta sequences, well-cited as canonical for hippocampal goal-projection at the theta scale). Mapping fidelity good at 0.75 — goal-conditioned look-ahead distance is the strongest direct empirical signature available for MECH-267-style conditioning, with the goal-distance vs operating-mode caveat. Transfer risk is moderate at 0.30 because of the timescale and species-domain distinctions. Net direction is **supports** MECH-267, with the implementation-level recommendation that operating_mode should modulate look-ahead distance / horizon parameters of the proposal mechanism.

## MECH-267 synthesis with the new entries

The MECH-267 review now spans five entries: Pfeiffer & Foster 2013 (goal-directed forward replay before maze traversal), Tambini 2019 (reactivation bias toward task-relevant memories), Olafsdottir 2018 (replay review including state-dependent content), Mattar & Daw 2018 (prioritized memory access as the normative framework), and Wikenheiser & Redish 2015 (theta-sequence look-ahead scales with goal distance). Combined synthesis: the architectural commitment that hippocampal proposals are context-conditioned is biologically very well grounded across multiple measurement modalities (single-unit, LFP/theta sequences, replay decoding, computational modelling).

The implementation questions that remain for V3 HippocampalModule are: (a) at what timescale does the conditioning operate (within-cycle vs trial-level vs across-trial); (b) does operating_mode enter as a horizon parameter (look-ahead distance modulation), as a value-function selection (which goals are active), or as a proposal-generation policy (which proposal mechanism is used in which mode); (c) how is the goal-conditioning related to the Mattar & Daw need/gain decomposition. Rather than committing to one of these for V3 implementation, MECH-267's V3 form can keep the conditioning interface narrow (operating_mode passed as a kwarg to propose_trajectories, modulating either look-ahead horizon or proposal-policy selection) and let downstream V4 elaboration formalise the deeper question. Combined: MECH-267 is a strong V3 commitment, with the mechanism well-grounded across multiple substrates and timescales, and the V4 architectural question being how to formalise the conditioning interface.
