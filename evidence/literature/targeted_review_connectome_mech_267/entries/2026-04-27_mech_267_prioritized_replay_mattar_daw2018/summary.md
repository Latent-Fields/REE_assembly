# Mattar & Daw 2018 — Prioritized memory access explains planning and hippocampal replay

According to PubMed, Mattar & Daw (2018, [Nat Neurosci 21:1609-1617](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41593-018-0232-z)) develop the prioritized-memory-access account of hippocampal replay. Their normative theory predicts which memories an agent should access at each moment to optimise future decision quality, formalising replay content as a function of two utility components: **need** (the probability of visiting a particular state in the near future, given current goals and policy) and **gain** (the expected improvement in future choice quality from updating that state's value). The total utility of replaying a state is the product of need and gain, and replay content at any moment should be ordered by this utility. The model unifies seemingly disparate findings under a single framework — planning replay (forward-directed sequences toward goals), learning replay (recently-experienced trajectories), consolidation replay (offline reactivation during quiescent states) — and explicitly notes that the mechanism whose dysfunction may underlie pathologies like rumination and craving (high gain in counterfactually-relevant content but low decision-improvement payoff).

For MECH-267 (HippocampalModule.propose_trajectories conditioned on `operating_mode` in addition to world_state), this is among the strongest direct architectural support available in the modern literature. The core claim of MECH-267 is that the same hippocampal substrate produces *different* proposal content depending on the current operating mode — planning mode generates forward-directed goal-projected proposals, habit mode generates short familiar trajectories, default-mode-replay generates consolidation-relevant content. Mattar & Daw 2018 provides the mechanism: the prioritization function is itself conditioned on the agent's current task / behavioural context (need depends on goal-conditional state-visit probabilities; gain depends on which value-function entries are most uncertain). When the operating mode changes, the relevant goal hierarchy changes, the need/gain function changes, and the prioritized-replay content changes. Mode-conditioned proposals are not a special-case extension of HippocampalModule; they are the canonical mode of operation.

The implication for SD-033 governance is significant. If MECH-267 is biologically grounded by prioritized memory access, then the mode-conditioning is not a knob to be added — it is a property the hippocampal substrate already has. The V3 implementation question is whether the existing HippocampalModule.propose_trajectories machinery actually exposes mode-conditioning at the right interface. The current candidate-CEM proposal mechanism is largely world-state-driven (z_world latent + e1_prior + action_bias). Operating_mode would need to enter either as an additional conditioning signal at proposal generation, or implicitly through mode-modulated changes to the value-flat ranking after proposal. Mattar & Daw suggests the conditioning is at the *prioritization* step — which in REE corresponds to value-flat ranking — rather than at trajectory generation per se.

I want to flag two caveats. The first is a mapping caveat: Mattar & Daw frame replay conditioning in terms of **need and gain** — both task-state-level value-related variables, computed from a value function over states. MECH-267 specifies conditioning on `operating_mode` — an architectural-mode signal from the SalienceCoordinator. These are not the same variable, even though they are related (operating_mode shapes the agent's current goals which shape need/gain). The paper supports the architectural commitment that hippocampal proposals are context-conditioned but does not directly demonstrate conditioning on operating-mode-level variables specifically. The implementation question is how operating_mode enters the proposal computation — does it modulate the value function consulted, modulate the proposal-generation policy, or modulate which substrates participate in proposal generation? The paper does not discriminate, and the V3 implementation needs to make this choice explicit.

The second caveat is the substrate-localisation question. Mattar & Daw's normative model is tested against rodent hippocampal replay data, but the model itself is substrate-agnostic — it predicts what replay *should* be under the assumption that the brain optimises future decision quality. The strict empirical claim that hippocampus *implements* the prioritization is supported but not fully demonstrated; alternative substrates (PFC, cortico-hippocampal interactions, basal ganglia value updating) could also realise the optimisation. If hippocampus is one component of a distributed proposal-generation system rather than the canonical locus, MECH-267's substrate commitment may be too narrow. For V3 this is fine — HippocampalModule is the V3 commitment — but for V4 elaboration, distributed-substrate proposal generation deserves consideration.

A third implication of the paper that does not directly affect MECH-267 but is worth surfacing for the SD-033 governance plan: the rumination / craving framing. Mattar & Daw note that prioritized replay can become pathological when it gets stuck on counterfactually-relevant content (gain stays high in regret/loss-related trajectories) without producing decision improvement. This connects to OCD's intrusive-repetitive-thought signature in the SD-033 governance plan and to MECH-292's `goal_match_floor` rumination guard (the design doc explicitly cites Mattar & Daw 2018 for the rumination concern). MECH-267's mode-conditioning provides a natural defensive mechanism: if mode-state-shift updates the prioritization function, getting stuck on a single state-content cluster requires also being stuck on the operating mode that makes that content relevant. SD-033's governance machinery (closure operator + asymmetric hysteresis + dACC saturation) jointly ensure that mode-state-shift is achievable, which in turn ensures that prioritized-replay content can re-prioritize on mode changes.

Confidence components: source quality is very high at 0.92 (Nat Neurosci 2018, Daw lab, foundational paper for prioritized-memory-access, deeply cited as the modern unifying framework for hippocampal replay). Mapping fidelity is good at 0.78 — the architectural commitment that hippocampal proposals are context-conditioned is directly supported, with the caveat that the conditioning variable (need/gain vs operating_mode) is not exactly aligned with MECH-267's spec. Transfer risk is moderate at 0.30 because the substrate may be distributed across hippocampus + cortex rather than hippocampus alone. Net direction is **supports** MECH-267's architectural commitment, with the implementation-level question being how operating_mode enters the proposal computation.
