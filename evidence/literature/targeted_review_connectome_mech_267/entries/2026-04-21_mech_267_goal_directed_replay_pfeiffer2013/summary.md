# Pfeiffer and Foster 2013 — Goal-directed hippocampal trajectory sequences (MECH-267)

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/nature12112)), Pfeiffer and Foster demonstrate in rat hippocampal recordings that place-cell sequences generated before goal-directed navigation depict trajectories from the animal's current location toward a known goal. These brief "preplay" sequences predict immediate future behaviour — and do so even when the specific start-goal combination is novel, ruling out simple template-matching to past experience. The finding extends the interpretation of hippocampal replay: these sequences are not just retrospective recapitulations of past paths; they also support a prospective, goal-directed trajectory-finding mechanism that operates at moments when memory retrieval is required.

This is the strongest entry I can find for MECH-267 at the first-level architectural claim. MECH-267 asserts that HippocampalModule.propose_trajectories should condition on operating_mode in addition to world_state — that proposal content is task-dependent, not world-state-only. Pfeiffer and Foster show exactly this phenomenon in rodent hippocampus at the goal-condition level: the same world state (the animal's current location) produces different trajectory proposals depending on the current goal. The REE mapping is then that "operating_mode" in the MECH-267 sense includes the current goal state as one component; when the goal shifts, proposal content shifts.

The specific translation to REE's four-mode taxonomy (external_task, internal_planning, internal_replay, offline_consolidation) is where the mapping becomes looser. Pfeiffer and Foster's paradigm is active, awake, goal-directed behaviour — an external_task-mode context in REE terms. They do not test whether the same hippocampus in the same animal produces different trajectory content under, say, offline replay periods. Other work in the field (Olafsdottir et al. 2018 review; see separate entry) addresses the broader mode-dependent-content question.

The paper's strongest contribution to MECH-267 is the novelty-of-trajectory result. When the start-goal combination is novel, the hippocampal sequences still depict trajectories from the current location toward the goal — so the proposals are not just memory recalls of past traversals. They are generative. That is exactly what MECH-267 needs for internal_planning mode: the proposals must be able to construct counterfactual / branching trajectories that have not been previously executed. Pfeiffer and Foster do not classify their result as internal_planning-mode per se, but the generative-trajectory finding is the kind of phenomenon that internal_planning mode in REE would need to support.

The honest caveat is that this is goal-conditioning rather than mode-conditioning. Pfeiffer and Foster dissociate "proposals depend only on world state" from "proposals depend on world state and current goal"; they do not dissociate "proposals depend on goal" from "proposals depend on operating-mode vector". For the MECH-267 architectural claim, the former dissociation is necessary but not sufficient; the latter requires evidence from paradigms that vary operating mode independent of goal.

Confidence is set at 0.82. Source quality is very high (Nature, canonical replay paper). Mapping fidelity is strong at the first-mode-conditioning level; looser for the full four-mode taxonomy. Transfer risk is moderate: rodent spatial navigation to REE's gridworld trajectories carries some abstraction cost, but the underlying phenomenon (context-dependent proposal generation) is architecturally general. This is a supporting entry, not a decisive one — it establishes the biological reality of state-and-goal-conditioned hippocampal proposals and places the mode-conditioning extension on plausible ground.
