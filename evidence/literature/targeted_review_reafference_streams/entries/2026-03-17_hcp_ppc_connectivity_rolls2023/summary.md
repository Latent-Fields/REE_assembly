# Literature Summary: 2026-03-17_hcp_ppc_connectivity_rolls2023

- Scope: HCP 7T effective connectivity of posterior parietal cortex across 21 regions and 360 cortical areas.
- Claim linkage:
  - `MECH-098` (encoder reafference cancellation — premotor→PPC efference copy pathway confirmed)
  - `MECH-095` (TPJ agency comparator — TPJ connectivity to premotor confirmed via SLF)
  - `MECH-099` (three-pathway architecture — VIP and intraparietal connectivity to premotor/frontal areas)
- Direction: supports
- Confidence: 0.82
- Key finding: From n=171 HCP participants at 7T: SLF I/II/III are the white matter highways connecting premotor cortex to PPC. Premotor area 6 (PMd) connects with superior parietal via medial SLF; PMv connects with anterior IPL via arcuate/SLF III. VIP (ventral intraparietal, peripersonal space) has direct connections to premotor cortex. The effective connectivity analysis confirms that the premotor→parietal pathway carries information in the functionally relevant direction for motor-to-sensory efference copy.
- REE relevance: Anatomical grounding for the efference copy pathway to parietal (MECH-098). The SLF is the white matter tract that would carry z_self predicted change to the encoder's world-head processing stage. VIP connectivity to premotor also grounds MECH-097 (peripersonal space) — VIP is an anatomically confirmed site for self-world spatial interaction.
- Caveat: Effective connectivity is functionally inferred from structural tractography. Direct labelling of these connections as "efference copy" requires functional inference layered on anatomy.
