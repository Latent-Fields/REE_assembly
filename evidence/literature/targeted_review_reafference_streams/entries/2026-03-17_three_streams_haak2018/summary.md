# Literature Summary: 2026-03-17_three_streams_haak2018

- Scope: Connectome-level triple dissociation of human visual pathways from HCP resting-state data.
- Claim linkage:
  - `MECH-099` (three-pathway visual architecture — direct connectome confirmation)
  - `MECH-069` (error signal incommensurability — three pathways have distinct white matter tracts, anatomically grounding the incommensurability claim)
  - `MECH-096` (dual-stream encoder — needs update to three-stream encoder)
  - `ARC-017` (sensory stream tags — SELF_SENSORY/WORLD/AGENCY tags map to dorsal/ventral/lateral)
- Direction: supports
- Confidence: 0.86
- Key finding: Data-driven analysis of 22 visual areas from n=470 HCP participants shows a statistically significant triple dissociation, not the classical binary dorsal/ventral split:
  - **Dorsal** (IPS/occipitoparietal): spatial processing, visually guided action, self-motion
  - **Ventral** (VO/PHC): object identity, content, sustained representation
  - **Lateral/third** (MT→MST/FST→posterior STS): dynamic motion, biological motion, social/agency
- The third stream is confirmed by non-human primate tracer studies and terminates near TPJ (posterior STS / right TPJ). This stream is specifically the one that processes biological motion and agency signals — directly relevant to MECH-095.
- REE relevance: The three pathways are anatomically distinct with distinct white matter tracts. This provides direct structural evidence that MECH-069's three incommensurable error channels are not an arbitrary choice but reflect distinct biological processing streams. MECH-096 should be updated from "dual-stream encoder" to "three-stream encoder."
- Caveat: MT projects to both STS (lateral stream) and TEO (ventral), so the streams are not hermetically sealed. Active debate on clean stream assignment for MT-recipient areas.
