# Literature Summary: 2026-03-17_mstd_reafference_gu2008

- Scope: MSTd congruent/incongruent neuron populations as a biological reafference cancellation mechanism.
- Claim linkage:
  - `MECH-098` (encoder reafference cancellation — MSTd is the biological instantiation)
  - `MECH-095` (TPJ agency comparator — MSTd feeds the lateral stream that terminates at TPJ)
  - `MECH-099` (three-pathway visual architecture — MSTd is the entry point of the lateral stream)
- Direction: supports
- Confidence: 0.88
- Key finding: MSTd contains two neuron populations — congruent (visual and vestibular heading preferences aligned: expected reafference from self-motion) and incongruent (preferences opposite: exafference indicating genuine world change). These implement causal inference about whether visual motion is self-generated or world-generated. MSTd receives visual optic flow, vestibular inertial signals, and efference copy from premotor cortex simultaneously.
- REE relevance: This is exactly the computation MECH-098 requires at the encoder level — using predicted self-motion (efference copy / E2_self prediction) to filter out perspective-shift components from z_world, leaving only genuine world-state changes. The congruent/incongruent split maps directly to the perspective-shift vs genuine-causal-footprint separation needed for SD-003 attribution.
- Caveat: Macaque data; human MSTd is less well characterised at single-unit level. Functional homology is strongly supported by fMRI and behaviour.
