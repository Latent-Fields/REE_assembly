# Bailey, Goldman, Bello, Chohan, Jeong, Winiger, Chun, Schipani, Kalmbach, Cheer, Balsam & Simpson (2018) — An interaction between 5-HT2C signalling and DA enhances goal-directed vigor and persistence in mice

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1523/JNEUROSCI.2088-17.2018); PMID 29367407).

## What the paper did

The 5-HT2C-selective ligand SB242084 was administered to mice during quantitative goal-directed work tasks designed to dissociate effort, reward value, and generalised motor activity. Striatal DA was measured with both fast scan cyclic voltammetry (phasic, NAc) and microdialysis (tonic, NAc and DMS). The behavioural read-outs are vigor (rate of work) and persistence (time on task) under varying work demands.

## Key findings

SB242084 increased vigor and persistence specifically when work demands were high; trivial work conditions showed no effect. Phasic DA in NAc, the canonical reward-coding signal, was unchanged. Tonic DA in NAc was unchanged. Tonic DA in DMS was potentiated. The behavioural change tracked the DMS DA change, not the NAc phasic signal. The authors take care to dissociate this from generalised activity (no change), reward value (no change), and motor effects (no change). The clean read is: 5-HT2C signalling, via a direct cross-talk to the DA system, gates the *willingness to work hard* without changing how much the reward is wanted.

## Mapping to MECH-187

This is the cleanest direct mechanism for MECH-187 in the recent literature. MECH-187 asserts that serotonin modulates the gain on dopaminergic incentive salience, converting SD-012's hard threshold into a graded transduction. Bailey supplies the receptor (5-HT2C), the mechanism (cross-talk to DA), the locus (striatum), and the behavioural signature (graded-vigor effect specifically when demands warrant it).

The translation needs care on locus. MECH-187's current statement maps the mechanism to NAc, in line with the Berridge incentive-salience tradition. Bailey's DA potentiation is in DMS, not NAc. Two readings are possible. The first is that MECH-187 should be extended to cover DMS as a relevant gain-modulation locus — the underlying claim (5-HT modulates DA-mediated motivational gain) survives; only the locus widens. The second is that MECH-187 covers the effort/vigor dimension specifically, while the reward-value-coding NAc-shell signal is governed by a separate pathway. Either reading preserves MECH-187's core; both are consistent with the EXQ-252 PASS where bidirectional z_goal seeding gain was confirmed (the seeding gain is closer to effort/vigor than to reward-value coding in the REE substrate).

For V4, the substrate question matters. If MECH-187 is wired at DMS, the implementation should pull from the dorsal striatal loops (action-vigor); if it is wired at NAc shell, it should pull from the limbic loop (incentive-salience proper). Bailey leans toward the former but does not exclude both.

## Caveats

Mouse model; 5-HT2C-selective pharmacology rather than endogenous-tone manipulation. The work-demands task is a particular operationalisation of effort; other effort tasks (progressive ratio, force production) would be useful triangulation. The locus question is unresolved.

## Confidence reasoning

I land at 0.82. Source quality is high — J Neurosci, Cheer/Simpson collaboration, three converging methods, careful behavioural dissociation. Mapping fidelity is good but the locus question keeps it from going higher. Transfer risk is the standard rodent-to-human caveat for striatal gain mechanisms; reduced by the fact that the same SB242084 manipulation has clinical-translational interest.
