# Browne, Abela, Chu, Li, Ji, Lambe & Fletcher (2018) — DRN 5-HT neurons inhibit operant responding via inputs to VTA but not NAc

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41386-018-0271-x); PMID 30420603).

## What the paper did

TPH2-Cre mice received optogenetic stimulation of dorsal raphe 5-HT neurons during operant responding for a sucrose reward. The optogenetic stimulation was either at the somatic level (DRN) or at terminal level in two downstream targets — ventral tegmental area (VTA) or nucleus accumbens (NAc). Some experiments combined the optogenetic manipulation with a low-dose SSRI (citalopram) to elevate ambient 5-HT tone alongside phasic release.

## Key findings

DRN 5-HT stimulation alone enhanced downstream 5-HT release but was not sufficient to inhibit operant responding. Combining the stimulation with low-dose citalopram synergistically reduced responding. The same synergistic effect was reproduced when optogenetic stimulation was confined to 5-HT terminals in VTA. It was *not* reproduced when stimulation was confined to 5-HT terminals in NAc. The interpretation the authors offer is that DRN 5-HT exerts its inhibitory influence on motivated behaviour primarily through a direct interaction with the mesolimbic DA system at the level of VTA (where DA cell bodies live), not at the NAc target where DA terminates.

## Mapping to MECH-187

This paper complicates rather than confirms MECH-187's NAc-locus framing — and the complication is informative. MECH-187 in its current statement maps the 5-HT modulation of DA-mediated incentive salience to NAc. Browne shows that, for one important behavioural axis (operant responding for sucrose), the relevant 5-HT signal is upstream at VTA, where it gates DA cell firing, not at NAc where it would otherwise gate DA-modulated EPSCs.

Two readings are compatible with this. (1) MECH-187 should be re-located to VTA: the gain mechanism is at the source (DA cell firing modulation) rather than the target (NAc EPSC modulation). This conflicts with the Christoffel 2021 NAc-locus evidence in this same review, which showed clear 5-HT effects on NAc EPSCs. (2) Both loci are operative, with different behavioural axes preferentially recruiting one or the other. Browne's task is operant responding (effort/work axis); Christoffel's behavioural axes are cocaine CPP and MDMA sociability (reward attribution / social). MECH-187 may need to specify *which gain-modulation mechanism* governs *which behavioural axis*.

The SSRI co-requirement is itself a constraint on MECH-187. Phasic 5-HT release alone was not sufficient — tonic 5-HT had to be elevated alongside it. That suggests the gain mechanism in vivo depends on a particular ratio of phasic to tonic 5-HT signalling, which is a finer-grained constraint than MECH-187's current statement. EXQ-252 used a global gain manipulation and so cannot speak to this; future experiments should consider phasic-vs-tonic separation.

## Caveats

The behavioural axis is operant responding for sucrose, a single operationalisation of motivation. The SSRI co-requirement complicates simple optogenetic interpretation. The paper does not measure DA directly during the optogenetic manipulation, so the inferred 5-HT-on-DA mechanism is reconstructed from the behavioural pattern rather than directly observed.
