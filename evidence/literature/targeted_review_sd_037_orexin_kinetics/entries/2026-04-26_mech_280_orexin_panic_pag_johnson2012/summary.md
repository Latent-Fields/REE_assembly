# Johnson et al. 2012 — orexin in stress and panic, and the PAG modulation it produces

## What the paper did

Johnson and colleagues review the orexin / hypocretin literature on stress and anxiety/panic states. They synthesise evidence from anatomical tracing, pharmacological injection, optogenetic manipulation (where then-available), and clinical pharmacology of orexin antagonists, with an emphasis on the panic phenotype and its CO2 / sodium-lactate provocation models. The PAG figures prominently because it is the brainstem locus where orexin's effect on defensive arbitration is most clearly bidirectional.

## Key findings relevant to MECH-280

Two findings matter for the MECH-280 prediction. First, orexin signalling at the PAG is bidirectional and subregion-specific: orexin in the dorsolateral / dorsomedial PAG promotes panic-class active responses (escape, vocalisation, autonomic activation), while orexin in the ventrolateral PAG is permissive of escape over freeze. The unified statement "orexin raises freeze threshold" is therefore an averaging across two functional axes. Second, the recruitment is graded and can be pharmacologically dissociated: orexin antagonists selectively block panic-class active responses while leaving baseline freezing intact, which is consistent with a multiplicative gain on freeze threshold rather than a hard switch. The graded, pharmacologically dissociable shape is supportive of MECH-280's multiplicative formulation; the bidirectionality at the subcolumn level is a challenge to its single-scalar collapse.

## How this translates to REE

MECH-280's prediction maps onto the ventrolateral-PAG / escape-permissive branch of the orexin-PAG system. The dorsolateral / dorsomedial branch (panic-promoting active coping) is a separate axis: under MECH-280's current formulation, both an active-foraging-under-hunger and a panic-class freeze-disruption would map to the same theta_freeze elevation, because MECH-280 cannot distinguish goal-directed active coping from panic. This is the principal validity caveat the claim's evidence_quality_note already names ("PAG subcolumn substrate not implemented in V3"). For V3 the simplification is acceptable; the validation factorial should not be expected to reproduce the panic-versus-foraging dissociation.

## Limitations and caveats

This is a review rather than a primary experimental paper; its evidence is integrated across many primary sources of varying methodological strength. Two specific caveats matter for MECH-280: (a) the bidirectional PAG modulation is a real architectural distinction that MECH-280 currently elides, and (b) much of the strongest orexin-PAG evidence comes from the panic / CO2 paradigm, which is not the metabolic-demand paradigm MECH-280 is built for. The transfer from panic-class active coping to foraging-under-threat is non-trivial; the two share an active-coping output mode but differ in their drive antecedents.
