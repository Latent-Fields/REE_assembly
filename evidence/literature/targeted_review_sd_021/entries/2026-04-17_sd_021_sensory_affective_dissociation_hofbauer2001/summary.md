# Hofbauer et al. 2001 -- Cortical representation of the sensory dimension of pain

## Source
Hofbauer RK, Rainville P, Duncan GH, Bushnell MC. *Journal of Neurophysiology* 86(1):402-11. DOI: 10.1152/jn.2001.86.1.402

## What the paper did

This paper is one half of a deliberate two-study program by the same research group. Rainville and colleagues (1997, Science) had previously shown that hypnotic suggestions directed at pain unpleasantness produced specific changes in anterior cingulate cortex (ACC) activity without altering perceived intensity or somatosensory cortex activity. The present study asked the complementary question: what happens to cortical activity when hypnotic suggestions specifically target pain intensity (the sensory-discriminative dimension) rather than unpleasantness? Ten volunteers underwent PET scanning during four conditions -- alert control, hypnosis control, suggestions for increased pain intensity, and suggestions for decreased pain intensity -- while noxious heat was applied to the hand. The result was a clean dissociation: modulation of perceived pain intensity was associated with changes in primary somatosensory cortex (S1) activity. The ACC, which had changed in Rainville 1997, did not change here when only intensity was targeted.

## Key findings for SD-021

The double dissociation this creates is the most direct empirical anchor for the z_harm_s / z_harm_a distinction in SD-021. Hofbauer 2001, taken with Rainville 1997, establishes that pain has at least two neurally separable dimensions: a sensory-discriminative dimension (intensity, localization) represented in S1 and modifiable by top-down signals without disturbing the affective dimension; and an affective-motivational dimension (unpleasantness) represented in the ACC and modifiable without disturbing the sensory-discriminative dimension. These are not mere psychological labels -- they have distinct neural substrates that can be independently up- or down-regulated by top-down cognitive control. This is the human neuroscience grounding for SD-021's assertion that commitment attenuates z_harm_s without suppressing z_harm_a.

## REE mapping

SD-021 posits a selective gate: E3 commitment reduces z_harm_s PE precision (spinal sensory-discriminative input attenuation via PAG-RVM-dorsal horn) while leaving z_harm_a (C-fiber affective-motivational signal) intact. Hofbauer 2001 demonstrates that top-down modulation can be directed selectively at the sensory-discriminative dimension. The cortical substrate (S1) is not the same as the spinal locus (dorsal horn) where SD-021 places the gate, but S1 activity is an upstream correlate of ascending sensory-discriminative input. If top-down signals can selectively modulate the cortical representation of pain intensity independently of pain unpleasantness, the architecture exists for selective precision reduction at every level of the ascending pathway, including at the spinal entry gate proposed in SD-021.

The affective channel (z_harm_a) remaining intact under commitment is critical: without it, the agent would have no aversive motivation to monitor harm accumulation and exit the committed trajectory if costs exceed expectations. Hofbauer 2001 shows empirically that the ACC -- the substrate for the affective-motivational signal -- is not suppressed when only the sensory dimension is modulated. This is exactly the architectural requirement SD-021 makes.

## Limitations

The top-down mechanism here is hypnotic suggestion, which operates through expectation and attentional redirection. E3 commitment in REE operates through a different mechanism: resolved trajectory planning with a specific planning horizon. Whether the selectivity seen with hypnosis (S1 modulated, ACC stable) generalizes to commitment-gated mechanisms is inferred rather than demonstrated. Additionally, S1 modulation is not the same as spinal dorsal horn modulation; the paper does not address the PAG-RVM pathway directly. The sample is small (10 participants) and the companion paper (Rainville 1997) used a separate sample, so the double dissociation is across studies rather than within subjects.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence is 0.87. The double dissociation design is the most powerful evidence structure for functional independence, and the S1/ACC dissociation is extremely clean and replicable across the two-study program. The transfer risk is moderate because mechanism (hypnosis vs commitment) and measurement level (cortical representation vs spinal PE precision) both differ from the precise claim in SD-021, but the functional architecture demonstrated here is precisely what SD-021 requires.
