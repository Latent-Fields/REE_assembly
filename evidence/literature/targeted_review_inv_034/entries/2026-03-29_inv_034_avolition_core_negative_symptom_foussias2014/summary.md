# Strauss, Bartolomeo, and Harvey (2021) -- Avolition as the core negative symptom in schizophrenia

**Claim tested:** INV-034 (goal maintenance is a necessary co-condition for ethical agency alongside harm-avoidance)

## What the paper did

Strauss et al. 2021 set out to argue for a specific architectural claim about negative symptoms in schizophrenia: that avolition -- reduced motivated, goal-directed behavior -- is the core negative symptom from which other negative symptoms derive or with which they correlate, and that understanding avolition is the key to developing effective pharmacological treatments. The paper distinguishes two main subdomain clusters: amotivation (avolition and asociality) and diminished expression (poverty of speech, flat affect). Their argument is that the amotivation cluster, particularly avolition, drives functional disability, while diminished expression is less predictive of real-world outcomes. They review evidence from behavioral studies, neuroimaging, and treatment trials to support this hierarchy.

## Key findings relevant to INV-034

The paper's key finding for INV-034 is that avolition -- the impairment in goal-directed initiation and persistence -- is the most important predictor of functional outcome in schizophrenia. Patients with primary avolition fail to pursue goals, initiate activities, and sustain effort over time. This is not because they are globally cognitively impaired or globally anhedonic; the amotivation profile can occur with relatively preserved cognitive function and preserved consummatory pleasure. It occurs because the circuit that generates and maintains goal representations (VTA-NAc-PFC) is disrupted.

Critically for INV-034, the paper emphasises that patients with primary avolition retain harm-avoidance capacities. They are not reckless. They do not seek out dangerous situations or act impulsively. Their harm-avoidance systems are functional. Yet their agency is profoundly impaired because they cannot pursue goals. This is the clinical demonstration of INV-034's philosophical claim made concrete: harm-avoidance minus goal maintenance equals passivity, not ethical agency.

## REE translation

INV-034 states that an agent that only avoids harm but cannot sustain prospective goal-directed motivation cannot exercise genuine agency. Strauss et al. 2021 provide the strongest clinical evidence for this claim's empirical plausibility in real biological systems. The avolition-as-core-symptom argument maps to the architectural claim that goal maintenance is not a luxury add-on to an otherwise functional harm-avoidance system -- it is a necessary prerequisite for agency itself. The paper also provides useful phenomenological grounding: avolitional patients are not in a state of paralysis due to excessive fear (which would suggest harm-avoidance overdrive) but in a state of motivational vacuum (no approach signal to drive commitment). The difference matters architecturally: excessive harm-avoidance could be corrected by lowering harm weights; absent goal maintenance requires a separate architectural solution -- which is exactly what MECH-112 and MECH-116 provide in REE.

## Limitations and caveats

The paper is a review of treatment-oriented research, which means its primary concern is pharmacological targets rather than computational mechanism. The specific mechanism of avolition (is it goal representation failure, effort discounting, prediction error failure, or PFC-NAc connectivity failure?) remains debated, and the paper does not adjudicate this debate definitively. Also, avolition in schizophrenia may involve dopamine dysregulation at the level of incentive salience encoding (too little wanting signal), which is a specific biological mechanism that differs from the absence of a training signal in REE. These mechanisms produce similar behavioral profiles but through different computational routes.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence is 0.72. The paper provides strong clinical evidence that avolition (absent goal pursuit) is the core disability in schizophrenia negative symptoms, and that harm-avoidance can be intact while agency collapses. This directly supports INV-034's claim. Confidence is moderate because the paper is focused on clinical treatment rather than computational mechanism, and the inference from clinical avolition to architectural necessity requires philosophical bridging.
