# Barch and Dowd (2010) -- Goal representations and motivational drive in schizophrenia

**Claim tested:** INV-034 (goal maintenance is a necessary co-condition for ethical agency alongside harm-avoidance)

## What the paper did

Barch and Dowd reviewed the neuropsychological literature on negative symptoms in schizophrenia specifically to develop a framework explaining why patients fail to initiate and sustain goal-directed behavior despite largely intact hedonic capacity. Their contribution is to articulate the prefrontal-striatal goal representation circuit as the key mediator: it is not that patients cannot experience pleasure, but that they cannot translate anticipated pleasure into sustained goal pursuit. The review covers neuroimaging studies of reward anticipation, behavioral studies of effort allocation and reinforcement learning, and pharmacological studies of dopaminergic modulation, synthesising them into a unified account of motivational drive failure in schizophrenia.

## Key findings relevant to INV-034

The central clinical finding is that schizophrenia patients with negative symptoms have dissociable impairments: they show intact consummatory pleasure (liking) but impaired anticipatory pleasure and goal-directed initiation (wanting). This is the most important empirical fact for INV-034: it demonstrates that a system can have intact harm-avoidance circuitry (patients do not engage in dangerous or reckless behavior -- they are often hypervigilant, not hypovigillant) and intact hedonic evaluation at contact, yet show profoundly reduced agency -- they do not make decisions, initiate actions, or sustain goal pursuit.

The prefrontal-striatal account proposes that goal representations maintained in dorsolateral PFC and communicated via ventral PFC projections to NAc are what generate motivational drive. Without this goal maintenance circuit, the agent has no sustained internal representation of where it is going -- it can evaluate harm locally but cannot commit to a direction. This maps precisely to INV-034's claim: harm-avoidance alone (intact but directionless) is not agency.

## REE translation

INV-034's philosophical claim is that genuine ethical agency requires both harm-avoidance and goal-directed approach -- a system with only the former is a risk manager, not an agent. Barch and Dowd provide the clinical existence proof. The schizophrenia negative-symptom patient is, in a clinical sense, a demonstration of what happens when the goal-maintenance circuit fails in a system with otherwise intact harm-avoidance: avolition, passivity, failure to initiate, inability to sustain purposive behavior over time. This is not trivially remediable by adding more harm-avoidance signal -- no amount of fear signal would restore goal-directed agency in these patients. Only interventions targeting the wanting/goal-maintenance pathway can restore motivational drive.

For REE, this means that the D1/D2 architecture (ARC-030) requires both signals: D1 (goal-approach drive, wanting, z_goal) and D2 (harm-avoidance, NoGo). An agent with no D1 signal -- trained only on harm -- can only commit by default when harm is absent. The schizophrenia negative-symptom case shows that this architecture fails to produce agency. The patients are not committing by default when harm is absent; they are simply not committing at all, because there is nothing to commit toward.

## Limitations and caveats

The wanting/liking dissociation in schizophrenia, while well-documented, is not the only mechanism contributing to negative symptoms. Cognitive dysregulation, formal thought disorder, social cognition deficits, and medication effects all contribute. The specific claim that prefrontal goal representation failure is the proximal mechanism of avolition is a hypothesis with good supporting evidence but not definitively established. Also, translating from clinical motivation failure to the philosophical claim about necessary conditions for ethical agency requires a step that the paper does not take -- Barch and Dowd are not making claims about agency theory, and the inference from their findings to INV-034's claim requires additional philosophical argument.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence is 0.78. The clinical analog is strong -- the schizophrenia negative symptom profile is a compelling existence proof for INV-034's claim. The confidence is moderate rather than high because the paper is a review (not primary data) and because the inference from clinical motivation failure to the necessity of goal maintenance for ethical agency requires philosophical bridging beyond the paper's own claims.
