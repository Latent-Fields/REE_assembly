# Davidson & Wolpert (2005): Forward Models Are Widespread

This is the architectural-scaffold paper for the SD-047 lit-pull rather
than load-bearing direct evidence. Wolpert and colleagues had by 2005
established a strong empirical case that internal predictive models —
forward models of arm dynamics, of object dynamics, of postural state —
are not localised to a single motor circuit but are accessed pervasively
across oculomotor, skeletomotor, perceptual, postural, and even mental-
imagery systems. The review summarises that case.

For SD-047 the relevance is structural rather than detailed. MECH-095
agency-detection and MECH-098 reafference cancellation both depend on
forward-model machinery: a comparator that asks what *would* have
happened given the agent's actual action, then subtracts that from
what *did* happen, can only do its job if there is a forward model to
provide the counterfactual. Davidson and Wolpert establish that such
machinery is the rule rather than the exception in biology. This means
that REE's commitment to `E2_harm_s(z_t, a_actual)` and the
counterfactual_forward gap as the primary metric for agency detection
is consistent with how the brain actually does this kind of
computation, rather than being an idiosyncratic engineering choice.

Where this paper does not help: it is silent on the env-side question
that SD-047 raises. The biology of forward-model access does not
directly tell us anything about what statistics the *input* to those
forward models needs to have for them to learn. For that, Sawtell 2010
and Asai 2016 are the load-bearing references. Davidson and Wolpert
sit underneath the whole stack as the architectural commitment that
forward-model-based comparators are the right kind of mechanism to
worry about in the first place.

I rate this confidence ~0.70 — it supports SD-047 indirectly by
establishing that the comparator architecture SD-047 serves is
biologically grounded, but it does not bear directly on the question
of whether richer env causation is necessary for those comparators to
converge. Useful as part of a coherent set, not as a single
load-bearing citation.
