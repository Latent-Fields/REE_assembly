# INV-054 lit notes -- cross-references

## HDD (Huys, Daw & Dayan 2015) -- attractor-topology convergence

The canonical decision-theoretic account of depression, catalogued under INV-053
at `targeted_review_inv_053/entries/2026-04-18_inv_053_decision_theoretic_depression_huys2015/`,
is structurally relevant to INV-054 but lives in the INV-053 directory because
its primary mapping is to the substrate-collapse claim.

The INV-054-relevant observation: HDD's self-perpetuation spiral and INV-054's
depression-attractor maintenance loop **converge on the same attractor shape
via different mechanics**.

- HDD: pessimistic evaluation of decision variables <- action outcomes confirm
  the pessimistic prior <- further pessimistic evaluation. Self-sustaining
  through action-selection feedback over preserved goals.
- INV-054: benefit_exposure below SD-012 seeding threshold -> no z_goal
  seeding -> no benefit-oriented exploration -> benefit_exposure stays low.
  Self-sustaining through exploration starvation with no goal representation
  to repair.

Same topology (self-reinforcing attractor around a depressed state), different
substrate (decision-variable layer vs motivational-substrate layer).

This is worth an explicit treatment in the REE paper: "same attractor, different
mechanism" is a stronger framing than either account alone and locates INV-054
in the computational-psychiatry map without collapsing it onto HDD.

See the full discussion in the Huys 2015 summary under INV-053.
