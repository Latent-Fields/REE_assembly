# Sarafyazd & Jazayeri 2019 -- Hierarchical reasoning by neural circuits in frontal cortex

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aav8911), PMID 31097640), Sarafyazd & Jazayeri design a primate behavioural task that cleanly dissociates two causes of negative outcomes: the monkey might be making errors because the stimulus is ambiguous and the judgement is hard (within-rule error), or because the stimulus-response contingency rule has covertly switched on the experimenter's side (rule-switch error). The monkey can do well on the task only if it performs the hierarchical inference -- decide whether to keep the current rule and accept that judgements are noisy, or to switch to the alternative rule because the contingency has changed. Recording from dorsomedial frontal cortex (DMFC) and anterior cingulate cortex (ACC), they find both regions encode evidence supporting a rule-switch interpretation, but ACC functions downstream of DMFC and specifically encodes the rule-switch INFERENCE -- the categorical conclusion that the rule has changed, distinct from accumulating evidence about the underlying stimulus statistics.

For MECH-318 this is the strongest primate-empirical anchor in the cluster. The other entries (Wang 2018 meta-RL framework; Duan 2016 RL² architecture; Botvinick 2019 fast-and-slow review) are theoretical / computational anchors. Sarafyazd & Jazayeri 2019 is the load-bearing primate electrophysiology that shows the rule-state inference is actually happening in frontal cortex in a temporally-extended, hierarchically-organised way. The architectural signature -- evidence accumulation in DMFC then causal-source attribution in ACC -- maps onto REE's existing SD-033a + ARC-062 cluster relatively cleanly.

The mapping that holds up: ARC-062 GatedPolicy is the per-tick rule-context discriminator (DMFC-analog evidence accumulation); SD-033a LateralPFCAnalog's rule_state buffer is the rule-state representation (ACC-analog causal-source attribution). The architectural sequencing reading is that the discriminator output gets aggregated over multiple ticks into the rule_state buffer via the planned Phase 3 GAP-C wiring, which is exactly the wiring that gives the rule_state buffer its content-is-rule-state function. The Sarafyazd-Jazayeri biology supports treating ARC-062 + SD-033a as a two-component sequential rule-inference cluster rather than as parallel substrates.

The substrate-design implication: the within-V3 portion of MECH-318 (W1+W3+W4 covered by SD-033a + ARC-062 + planned Phase 3 wiring) is potentially sufficient as the V3-tractable substrate for rule-state inference. The Phase 3 GAP-C wiring (closing the loop between the ARC-062 discriminator and the SD-033a rule_state buffer) is the architectural move that gives the within-V3 substrate its hierarchical-inference signature. Sarafyazd-Jazayeri provides the empirical reason to think this is the right architectural commitment: the biology does sequential hierarchical inference, not parallel evidence-and-decision.

The empirical-signature reading for V3 validation: the multi-rule-context substrate that V3-EXQ-543c-successor will need should support a paradigm analogous to Sarafyazd-Jazayeri's design -- negative outcomes that can be attributed to within-rule errors vs covert rule-switches. An REE agent on such a substrate should display:
- **Differential response to rule-switch evidence**: the agent should switch policies after evidence accumulates that the rule has changed, but should not switch in response to within-rule errors of similar surface magnitude.
- **Hierarchical signature**: the ARC-062 discriminator should show per-tick activity reflecting accumulated evidence; the SD-033a rule_state buffer should show category-jump behaviour at the rule-switch detection threshold.
- **Failure mode signatures**: with the ARC-062 component intact but SD-033a uninvolved, the agent should accumulate evidence but never act on it (under-switching). With SD-033a intact but ARC-062 uninvolved, the agent should switch erratically on any negative outcome (over-switching). The two ablations should produce dissociable failure modes.

These are falsifiable predictions on a multi-rule-context substrate that V3 does not yet have. The substrate-readiness work the Q-044 synthesis already named is the same enrichment MECH-318 validation needs.

The mapping caveat I would not want to bury: Sarafyazd & Jazayeri's evidence is in vivo single-unit recording in macaque during a specific behavioural paradigm (two-alternative stimulus judgement with covert contingency switches). REE's MECH-318 has to be measured behaviourally on a multi-rule-context substrate that does not yet exist in V3. The substrate-identification claim (DMFC + ACC as the relevant frontal regions) maps to REE's existing SD-033a + ARC-062 cluster relatively cleanly, but the empirical signatures the monkey paradigm produces (specific firing patterns, lesion-effects, etc.) may or may not transfer to REE's substrate-level analog measurements. The behavioural-action-distribution signature is what REE actually has access to, and it is a step removed from the neural signature.

I rate this confidence 0.84. Source quality is high (Science, primate single-unit is the strongest empirical evidence class for rule-state inference). Mapping fidelity is strong for the substrate-identification claim that frontal cortex does hierarchical rule-inference. Transfer risk is moderate because the behavioural-signature measurement on REE has to be at the behavioural level rather than the neural level. This is the primary empirical anchor for the cluster -- the reason to think the meta-RL framework Wang 2018 anchors theoretically is actually instantiated in primate brains.
