# Burgess, Veitch, de Lacy Costello & Shallice 2000 — Cognitive and Neuroanatomical Correlates of Multitasking

## What the paper does

Burgess and colleagues test 60 patients with circumscribed cerebral lesions and 60 age- and IQ-matched controls on a naturalistic multitasking procedure — one that tries to capture what actually breaks down when patients with frontal injuries fail in everyday life, rather than what a single rule-switching task probes. Structural equation modelling over task performance yields three primary cognitive constructs supporting multitasking: retrospective memory, prospective memory, and planning. The latter two are shown to depend on the first. The lesion-behaviour mapping then dissociates across prefrontal sub-regions.

## Key findings relevant to SD-033e

The most load-bearing finding for REE's frontopolar substrate is the dissociation: rule-breaking and task-switching failures are specifically associated with lesions affecting the medial and polar aspects of Brodmann's areas 8, 9, and especially 10. Planning deficits track the right dorsolateral PFC; retrospective-memory deficits track posterior cingulate and forceps major regions. The frontopolar/medial-polar region is not doing rule-holding or planning. It is doing something else — coordinating rules, maintaining pending intentions, switching between subtasks.

This is the data pattern that forced Burgess to develop the gateway hypothesis seven years later. For present purposes it is what justifies SD-033e as a substrate distinct from SD-033a (lateral-PFC-analog rule-holding). SD-033a lesion analogues would predict failure to maintain the active rule. What this paper's BA 10 patients show is the opposite — they can hold the individual rule but cannot manage the super-ordinate task structure that coordinates multiple rules over time.

## How this translates into REE

For SD-033e, this paper is the empirical foundation that justifies registering the claim at all. Without this kind of dissociation, SD-033e would be redundant with SD-033a. With it, the substrate split is biologically licensed and predicts a specific failure mode: SD-033e ablation should selectively damage nested-task coordination and suspended-intention maintenance while sparing within-rule execution.

For the MECH-261 operating_mode design: the Burgess 2000 data make the "external_task" mode (where SD-033a's held rule drives behaviour) clearly dissociable from the mode that coordinates nested operations. V3 does not need to implement the second mode, but the fact that lesions dissociate these functions in humans is what makes the "reserve a mode name for V4" design call load-bearing rather than speculative.

## Limitations and caveats

The 2000-era lesion localisation is coarse. "Medial and polar BA 8/9/10" is a large region; the paper cannot cleanly isolate BA 10. The constructs identified by SEM are also psychological constructs, not mechanism accounts — the paper tells you what is broken, not what the intact substrate is computing. Read alongside Burgess 2007 (gateway-hypothesis refinement) and Mansouri 2015 (primate selective-lesion replication at much finer localisation) to close these gaps.

## Confidence reasoning

Source quality high — large N, matched controls, SEM analysis, ecological task, well-cited. Mapping fidelity good — the dissociation directly supports the SD-033a/SD-033e substrate split REE is proposing. Transfer risk modest — human patient data is exactly the target population, but the localisation is imprecise. Net confidence 0.78.
