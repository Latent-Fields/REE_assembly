# Boorman, Behrens, Woolrich & Rushworth 2009 — Frontopolar Cortex and the Evidence in Favour of Alternative Courses of Action

## What the paper does

Boorman and colleagues set up a three-alternative decision task in fMRI and fit trial-by-trial choices with a Bayesian reinforcement-learning model that simultaneously tracks the value of the option the subject chose AND the value of each option they could have chosen but did not. Because the model-fit is trial-by-trial, they can look for brain regions whose activity scales with chosen-option value versus counterfactual-option value on any given decision. The payoff is a clean double dissociation: vmPFC tracks chosen-option value; lateral frontopolar cortex (lFPC) tracks the reward-based evidence favouring the best counterfactual option. A distributed network including dorsomedial frontal cortex and posteromedial cortex joins lFPC on the counterfactual side. Individual variation in lFPC activity predicts how readily a participant switches to a profitable alternative on later trials — the signal is causally relevant to the switch decision, not just correlated with it.

## Key findings relevant to SD-033e

This is the single sharpest mechanistic entry in the V4-prep lit-pull. It reframes what SD-033e's primary function is. Koechlin & Summerfield 2007 framed frontopolar function as tree-search branching — holding a pending task while executing a current one. That framing is not wrong, but it is narrow. Boorman's data argue for a more specific and more measurable computational role: the frontopolar-analog substrate computes the relative value of options that were not taken, and this counterfactual-value signal drives the switch-to-alternative decision.

For REE this is load-bearing because it grounds a specific mechanism claim, not just a substrate claim. SD-033e on its own says "there is a substrate here"; Boorman tells you what the substrate computes. That motivates a new mechanism claim (proposed MECH-264 in the Prong D synthesis) that names frontopolar counterfactual-value tracking explicitly and distinguishes it from vmPFC-analog (SD-033c) chosen-value tracking. The two substrates are doing distinct computations over related but non-identical representations.

## How this translates into REE

Three concrete implications:

- **Register a new MECH claim for frontopolar counterfactual-value tracking** (proposed MECH-264). The claim: SD-033e's principal computational function is to maintain, in parallel with SD-033c's chosen-option value, an estimate of the value of unchosen alternatives, and to signal switches when the counterfactual estimate exceeds the chosen estimate by a threshold-sensitive margin. This is cleaner, more testable, and more V4-appropriate than "branching".

- **SD-033c vs SD-033e is a genuine computational dissociation**, not just a naming convenience. vmPFC-analog tracks chosen value; frontopolar-analog tracks counterfactual value. This dissociation is what makes the cluster non-redundant and justifies keeping SD-033e as a separate substrate rather than folding it into SD-033a or SD-033c.

- **Mode-name reconsideration.** "deliberative_branching" (Koechlin framing) misses what Boorman's data show is the measurable frontopolar function. A better reserved V4 mode name would be "counterfactual_deliberation" or "parallel_goal_deliberation" — see Prong D synthesis for the full proposal.

## Limitations and caveats

The Boorman paradigm uses monetary-reward decisions. Whether the same frontopolar machinery computes counterfactual value over morally-laden alternatives — which is what REE ultimately needs for V4 ethical reasoning — is an extrapolation. The neuroeconomic literature broadly supports the extrapolation: similar networks appear in moral-decision fMRI and in social-decision tasks. But the mapping from reward-counterfactual to moral-counterfactual deliberation is an assumption REE should flag rather than assume silently.

## Confidence reasoning

Source quality very high — Neuron, Rushworth lab, model-based fMRI with explicit Bayesian trial-by-trial value extraction. Mapping fidelity high — counterfactual-value tracking gives SD-033e a specific and testable computational role. Transfer risk modest-to-high — domain is reward, V4 target is ethical deliberation, and the bridge is plausible but not empirically nailed down for REE's specific use case. Net confidence 0.85.
