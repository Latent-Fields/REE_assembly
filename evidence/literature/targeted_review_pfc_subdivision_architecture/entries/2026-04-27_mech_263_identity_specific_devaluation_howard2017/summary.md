# Howard & Kahnt (2017) -- Identity-Specific OFC Reward Representations Modulated by Selective Devaluation

## What the paper did

Hungry human participants were scanned with fMRI while making choices between high- and low-intensity versions of two appetitive food odours, matched in initial value. They were then fed a meal corresponding to one of the two odours -- selective devaluation. After the meal, pattern-based fMRI was used to ask whether the OFC representation of each odour identity changed selectively for the sated versus the non-sated food, and whether OFC-vmPFC functional connectivity predicted the resulting choice behaviour.

## Key findings relevant to MECH-263

Behaviourally, participants switched their choices toward the low-intensity version of the sated odour but retained their preference for the high-intensity version of the non-sated odour -- a sensory-specific devaluation effect. Neurally, identity-specific pattern representations in lateral posterior OFC were altered after the meal for the sated food but were retained for the non-sated counterpart. This is a clean dissociation: OFC was not encoding a uniform "food reward" signal, it was encoding outcome identity in a way that updated selectively when one identity's value changed. Functional connectivity between this OFC region and ventromedial PFC predicted individual differences in satiety-driven choice. The authors interpret this as evidence for "a mechanism by which devaluation modulates a cognitive map of expected reward in OFC and thereby alters general value signals in vmPFC to guide goal-directed behavior."

## How this maps to REE

MECH-263 names devaluation sensitivity as one of two load-bearing criteria for SD-033b: "if the value of outcome o changes while the state-action -> outcome mapping does not, behaviour changes appropriately because the outcome representation itself is structured." It also names a falsification condition: if SD-033b "encodes scalar value without discriminating between same-value / different-outcome-identity states," then the OFC/vmPFC dissociation collapses and SD-033b should be merged with SD-033c. Howard & Kahnt provide direct human-fMRI evidence on both fronts. Lateral posterior OFC encodes outcome identity that is dissociable from value. Devaluation selectively updates the identity-specific representation. And OFC-vmPFC connectivity predicts behaviour, which supports the architectural picture in which SD-033b's structured outcome information is read out by SD-033c's scalar value circuit to drive choice.

## Limitations and caveats

fMRI pattern-similarity analysis is one inferential step removed from single-unit codes -- "identity-specific" here means dissociable BOLD patterns rather than identity-coding neurons. The paradigm is narrow: appetitive food odours under selective satiety. Transfer to non-appetitive outcomes (harm prediction in REE, abstract task-state outcomes) is plausible but not directly tested. The behavioural-neural correlation is between subjects, n is moderate. The species transfer is short (human -> REE) but the modality transfer (BOLD -> latent representation in E2) is longer than for the rat single-unit work.

## Confidence reasoning

I have set this at 0.82. Source quality is high (J Neurosci, well-designed selective-devaluation paradigm, pattern-based decoding). Mapping fidelity is high -- the paper tests exactly the devaluation-sensitivity criterion MECH-263 names. Transfer risk is low on species but moderate on modality (BOLD -> latent code). The reason it is not 0.85+ is the narrow appetitive-only paradigm; the reason it is not lower is that this is human evidence, which the rat papers cannot directly provide.
