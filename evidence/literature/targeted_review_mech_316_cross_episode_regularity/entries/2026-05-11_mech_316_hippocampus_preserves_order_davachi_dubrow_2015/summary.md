# Davachi & DuBrow 2015 -- Hippocampus, prediction, context, order

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2014.12.004), PMID 25600586), Davachi & DuBrow synthesise about a decade of Davachi-lab fMRI and behavioural work into a single thesis: the hippocampus is not a passive episode-storage warehouse but an active substrate that performs prediction-based, context-dependent order-preserving operations on the input stream. Three specific findings hold the synthesis together. First, when a prediction error occurs during encoding -- something violates the agent's expectation about what should come next -- the hippocampus signals an event boundary, and temporal-order memory for items spanning the boundary is specifically impaired. Second, context-dependence modulates which items get bound into the same hippocampal trace; the same item presented in two different contexts is encoded into separate representational frames even when surface features are matched. Third, co-occurring items, when statistically reliable, acquire shared hippocampal representations through what is recognisable as statistical learning rather than episodic memorisation. The story is one of an active substrate that carves trajectory into events and extracts the statistical structure of what occurs within each.

For MECH-316 the relevance is direct. The user's brief named this paper -- or a paper like it -- as the empirical-anchor primary for the cross-episode regularity extraction substrate. The operations Davachi & DuBrow describe are the operations MECH-316 needs the substrate to perform. Where Schapiro 2017 (already in Pull 1) provided the computational-modelling anchor for hippocampal complementary-learning-systems, Davachi & DuBrow 2015 provides the empirical anchor: the behavioural and fMRI signatures that a hippocampal-style cross-episode regularity-extraction substrate should reproduce. The two are complementary load-bearing pieces of the same architectural commitment. A V3 substrate that wants to claim it instantiates MECH-316 has to deliver on both -- the modelling story that says hippocampus has a regularity-extraction pathway distinct from the episode-encoding pathway (Schapiro), and the empirical-signature story that says the regularity-extraction operations are prediction-error-modulated and context-segmented (Davachi & DuBrow).

The mapping caveat I would not want to bury comes in two parts. First, the venue. The user's brief mentioned Davachi 2015 J Neurosci as the empirical-anchor primary; the closest existing Davachi 2015 review on hippocampal statistical/order learning is in Trends in Cognitive Sciences, not Journal of Neuroscience. I have used the TICS review as the closest Davachi-tradition 2015 anchor; substantive content matches the brief's intent. The other Davachi 2015 candidates in J Neurosci are primary empirical papers on narrower questions, none of which is a review of hippocampal statistical learning per se. The substantive content is what the brief intended. Second, the paradigm transfer. Davachi & DuBrow's evidence comes from human fMRI on temporal-order memory tasks (subjects view sequences of objects, are later asked which came first). REE's MECH-316 has to operate on a different input substrate -- the agent's own behavioural-trajectory record, where the agent is generating the sequence by acting rather than passively viewing it. The principle that hippocampus extracts cross-episode regularities transfers; the specific operational signatures may differ on trajectory-record input.

What does failure look like? If a candidate MECH-316 substrate is built without prediction-error gating, temporal-order memory should be unaffected at would-be event boundaries -- which is the opposite of what Davachi & DuBrow's literature predicts. If the substrate is built without context-segmentation, the agent should bind items across context-boundaries that humans clearly separate. If the substrate is built without a statistical-learning operation distinct from episodic memorisation, co-occurrence-based generalisation should not emerge. These are falsifiable signatures, and any V3 instantiation of MECH-316 should be expected to reproduce at least some of them on a trajectory-record analog.

I rate this confidence 0.74. Source quality is high (TICS, Davachi is the established hippocampal-statistical-learning lead). Mapping fidelity is good for the substrate-level claim. Transfer risk is moderate because the experimental paradigms (human-fMRI temporal-order memory tasks) are one step removed from REE's behavioural-trajectory regularity-extraction. Confidence is held below 0.80 because of the venue mismatch with the brief's specification (TICS rather than J Neurosci) and because this is a review rather than a primary empirical paper -- the load-bearing empirical work is in the Davachi-lab primary papers it synthesises, not in the review itself. As a Davachi-tradition 2015 anchor for the empirical substrate of hippocampal statistical learning, however, this is the closest fit available and the substantive support for MECH-316 is real.
