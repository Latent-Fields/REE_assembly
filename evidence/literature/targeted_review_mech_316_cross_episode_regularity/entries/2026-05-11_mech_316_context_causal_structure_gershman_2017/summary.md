# Gershman 2017 -- Context-dependent learning and causal structure

According to PubMed ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.3758/s13423-016-1110-x), PMID 27418259), Gershman returns to the question that has been quietly animating the latent-cause literature since 2010: what is context actually doing, computationally? The history of the field has produced at least five competing answers -- context as occasion-setter, as configural cue, as modulator of associative weights, as hierarchical structuring variable, as the generative source of observations -- and the experimental literature has supported each at different times. Gershman's 2017 move is to argue that these are not different mechanisms competing for the same explanatory slot. They are different inferences over the same causal-structure space. Whichever context-function emerges in a given behavioural paradigm is the appropriate computation when the corresponding causal structure obtains. The Bayesian inference machinery is one substrate; its outputs are five.

For MECH-316 the relevant operationalisation step is sharper than what Gershman & Niv 2010 alone delivered. The 2010 review establishes the framework: latent-cause inference is the right algorithmic family. The 2017 paper commits to a stronger architectural claim: the inference is selecting among specific structural alternatives, and those alternatives prescribe how the agent should generalise across episodes. An SR-style substrate that smoothly interpolates between regimes cannot reproduce this -- it has no representation of the discrete causal-structure space the inference operates over. A latent-cause substrate that maintains a posterior over hidden causes can; it can also display the right qualitative dissociations between context-functions when different causal-structure interpretations win the posterior race.

The substrate-design relevance for REE: if V3's intended multi-rule-context substrate (the SD-054 extended-with-two-reef-configurations example from MECH-318's falsifier, or the multi-rule-mode environment the Q-044 three-arm ablation eventually needs) requires the agent to switch between qualitatively different generalisation regimes, the substrate underneath MECH-316 has to support inference over the discrete causal-structure space -- not just statistical-regularity readout. That is a stronger architectural commitment than 'add successor-representation'. It is closer to 'add a structured generative model whose hidden variables index regime'. The implementation cost is non-trivial and the verdict on whether REE needs this richness or can get away with the simpler SR flavour has to be made by the substrate-design pass that follows this lit-pull.

The mapping caveat I would not want to bury: Gershman 2017 is anchored in classical conditioning paradigms. The context-cues in renewal, summation, configural learning, and occasion-setting are externally imposed -- the experimenter presents them. The agent's job is to infer how those externally-imposed cues constrain reward expectation. REE's MECH-316 operates on a different input substrate -- the agent's own behavioural-trajectory record -- and the context-cues are partly endogenous (anchor-set boundaries, MECH-269 region labels, even self-generated curriculum). The transfer from 'context-cue inference over a conditioning training history' to 'rule-context inference over a behavioural trajectory record' is a real step. It is plausible, but it has not been empirically tested on the trajectory-record substrate REE actually has.

I rate this confidence 0.76. Source quality is reasonable (Psychon Bull Rev, a respected mid-tier venue; Gershman is the field lead but this is a brief report rather than the definitive treatment). Mapping fidelity is high for the framework-extension claim -- the 2017 paper is genuinely a stronger commitment than 2010. Transfer risk is somewhat elevated because the paper is rooted in Pavlovian conditioning paradigms whose transfer to RL-style behavioural-trajectory inference is one step further than the 2010 review. The combined Gershman 2010 + Gershman 2017 pair is the load-bearing pair for the Bayesian-latent-cause flavour of MECH-316, and the substrate-design pass should treat them as a unit when deciding whether to commit to this implementation flavour.
