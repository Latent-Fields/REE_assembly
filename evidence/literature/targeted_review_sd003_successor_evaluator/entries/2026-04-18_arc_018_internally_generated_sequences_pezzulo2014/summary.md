# Pezzulo, van der Meer, Lansink, Pennartz (2014). Internally generated sequences in learning and executing goal-directed behavior. *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*.

## What the paper does

This is a synthesis, not an experiment, but it is the synthesis that named the problem for the next decade of hippocampal planning research. The authors collect the evidence on three kinds of internally generated sequence in hippocampus -- theta sequences (which sweep forward of the animal's location within each theta cycle during movement), preplay (novel trajectories assembled from place-cell repertoire before the animal has traversed them), and awake replay during brief pauses -- and argue that these are not incidental epiphenomena of memory but the operational substrate of goal-directed behaviour. The claim is stronger than it sounds. They are saying that the hippocampus is continuously generating forward-model rollouts, and that goal-directed action selection in the mammalian brain is literally reading those rollouts off.

## Findings and how they carve the problem

The three sequence types, in their account, do different work. Theta sequences are on-line: the animal is walking, and every ~120 ms a miniature forward sweep probes the space a few body-lengths ahead. Preplay is pre-experience: before the animal has explored an arm, hippocampal cells representing that arm fire in a coherent order that predicts the order the animal will later traverse. Awake replay during stopping points is retrospective or prospective depending on context -- and crucially, Pezzulo and colleagues marshal evidence that the content of these sequences is selected by the downstream value circuit, not just by recency or spatial proximity. The ventral striatum and mPFC co-activate with the sequence, and the selection of which trajectory gets replayed is biased by reward structure and goal identity. So the loop, as they describe it, has the hippocampus proposing candidate futures and the striatal-prefrontal evaluator scoring them -- before action.

## REE mapping

This is the cleanest biological precedent I have seen for the ARC-018 + MECH-033 architecture, which is why it is here as the first evaluator-mode entry. ARC-018 says the hippocampus generates explicit rollouts; MECH-033 says E2 forward-prediction kernels seed those rollouts; the SD-003 successor design needs a forward model whose outputs can be scored for pre-action value. The Pezzulo review gives us every piece of that in biological form: theta sequences are the kernel-chained rollouts, preplay is their combinatorial extension, and the striatum-mPFC readout is the value evaluator. The paper also bears on MECH-102 (violence as terminal error correction) in a softer way -- if the normal mode of action selection is low-energy, small-sweep rollout-and-score, then the circuit breakdown that produces terminal-error-correction behaviour should show up as failure of this pre-action evaluator loop.

## Where it does not map cleanly

The paper treats the hippocampus-PFC-vStriatum evaluator as one circuit evaluating a single value signal. ARC-033, by contrast, commits REE to a stream-separated architecture: E2_harm_s has to be a distinct forward model from E2.world_forward, operating on a harm-discriminative latent that does not share substrate with world dynamics. Pezzulo and colleagues do not argue for separation; they argue for integration. That is neither support nor weakening for ARC-033 itself -- the review does not test the question -- but it is a caveat worth naming. The biological evaluator in this account is monolithic; REE is proposing a bifurcated one for reasons (SD-010, SD-011) that come from outside this literature.

## Confidence reasoning

Source quality is very high -- TiCS review, senior authors with a decade of work on exactly this question, the kind of paper later empirical work cites as the definitional synthesis. Mapping fidelity to ARC-018 and MECH-033 is good at the functional level (rollout generation, pre-action scoring, hippocampal generation, downstream evaluator). Transfer risk is moderate-to-low for the pre-action-evaluator claim itself, but higher for any claim that requires the evaluator circuit to be stream-separated. Confidence 0.75 -- this is one of the strongest single pieces of supporting literature for the general shape of ARC-018/MECH-033, but it is a review not a primary test, and it does not adjudicate the harm-stream question.
