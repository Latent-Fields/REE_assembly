# Memory signals are temporally dissociated in and across human hippocampus and perirhinal cortex

**Staresina et al. (2012) -- Nature Neuroscience -- DOI: 10.1038/nn.3154 -- PMID: 22751037**

## What the paper did

Benno Staresina and colleagues attacked a methodological problem in the familiarity/recollection literature: conventional fMRI cannot detect temporal differences between perirhinal familiarity signals and hippocampal recollection signals because the BOLD response is too slow. To overcome this, they combined simultaneous fMRI with intracranial EEG recordings from epilepsy patients undergoing presurgical evaluation, plus a separate fMRI-only healthy control cohort. During a recognition memory task with a source retrieval component (remember not just that the item was seen but in what context), the intracranial EEG allowed millisecond-resolution tracking of memory signals across hippocampus and perirhinal cortex. The critical finding: standard fMRI analysis showed broadly overlapping roles for the two regions. But the EEG time courses revealed a clear temporal dissociation: perirhinal signals emerged earlier, consistent with a rapid familiarity-based match-strength computation, while hippocampal signals followed with a delay, consistent with the additional time required for contextual pattern completion.

## Key findings relevant to MECH-118

Three findings extend the Yonelinas/Aggleton dual-process framework. First, the temporal dissociation confirms that familiarity and recollection are not merely computed by different regions -- they operate on different timescales. This implies genuinely distinct computational processes, not just different neural read-outs of the same computation. Second, the simultaneous recording design allows within-trial tracking of signal evolution, showing that perirhinal signals predict familiarity-based recognition even before hippocampal signals differentiate hits from misses. Third, the finding that conventional fMRI would have missed this dissociation is a methodological lesson: some distinctions that appear subtle or absent at low temporal resolution are substantial at higher resolution. For MECH-118, this motivates the hypothesis that D_eff and Hopfield familiarity, which appear to be computed simultaneously at each step in the current REE implementation, might need to operate at different integration timescales to achieve full informational independence.

## REE translation

The primary REE relevance is the addition of a temporal dimension to the spatial/informational dissociation. MECH-118 claims D_eff and Hopfield familiarity are informationally distinct; Yonelinas 2001 provides the theoretical framework; Aggleton 2005 provides the substrate dissociation. Staresina 2012 adds: these signals not only arise from different substrates but emerge at different times in the retrieval process. In REE, this translates to a prediction: if D_eff and Hopfield stability are to be genuinely independent monitoring streams, they may need different temporal integration windows -- Hopfield stability computed over a shorter window (most recent z_self samples), D_eff computed over a longer window (participation ratio across a broader sample). This would constitute a temporal dissociation in the REE architecture analogous to the perirhinal/hippocampal temporal dissociation in biology. This prediction is not currently tested in REE and represents a direction for future architectural refinement.

## Limitations and caveats

The intracranial EEG component is the methodological innovation, but it is limited to patients with medication-resistant epilepsy who have electrodes implanted for clinical purposes. These patients have known cortical abnormalities, and the electrode placements are clinically rather than experimentally driven, introducing sampling biases. The overlap between intracranial EEG and fMRI samples is also limited because the studies used different participant populations -- the temporal and spatial dissociations come from different people and are combined inferentially. The claim that familiarity signals arrive before recollection signals is robust across the intracranial EEG data, but the magnitude of the temporal gap and its implications for computational architecture depend on assumptions about signal propagation that the study does not fully address.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence is set at 0.73. Nature Neuroscience and methodological innovation raise source quality substantially above typical fMRI studies. The temporal dissociation is a genuine empirical finding with clear implications for the informational independence claim. Transfer risk is moderate-high because the temporal dissociation is at millisecond resolution in a biological system, while REE operates at step resolution in an artificial system -- the translation requires explicit architectural choices (different integration windows) that are not yet made. This paper is best understood as motivating a specific architectural refinement rather than validating the current implementation.
