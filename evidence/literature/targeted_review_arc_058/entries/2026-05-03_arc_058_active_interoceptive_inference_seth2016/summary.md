# Seth & Friston (2016): Active Interoceptive Inference

This Seth–Friston *Phil Trans R Soc B* review is the canonical
theoretical reference for the interoceptive predictive-coding
framework. The argument is that bodily states are not simply sensed —
they are regulated by autonomic reflexes that are "enslaved" by
descending predictions from deep generative models of the internal
and external milieu. Emotion is reframed as the process of inferring
the causes of internal sensory signals, with precision (uncertainty)
as the key control variable that translates into neuromodulation.

For the ai-cognitive-failure-taxonomy made-feelings / interoceptive-
passivity sub-cluster, this paper is the architectural scaffold rather
than load-bearing direct evidence. It establishes that an interoceptive
comparator exists; that it operates via descending predictions on
internal sensory signals; and that precision-weighting is the lever
modulating its output. Made-feelings phenomena are then naturally
interpreted as failures of self-authorship tagging at the interoceptive
comparator's output level — affective state arrives without the marker
that would identify it as self-generated.

For ARC-058 the contribution is principled rather than decisive. The
review establishes that the interoceptive comparator exists; whether
its forward model shares a trunk with the somatic-discriminative
forward model (the ARC-058 strong reading) or operates with its own
independent substrate (ARC-033 strong reading) is a question Seth &
Friston don't directly adjudicate. They commit to the architecture
without forcing one side of that subsidiary disagreement. The existing
empirical entries in this targeted_review (Wager 2013 NPS, Hofbauer
2001 sensory-affective dissociation, Krishnan 2016 vicarious
dissociation, Woo 2017 SIIPS1) carry more weight on the shared-vs-
separate question; Seth & Friston frames the wider commitment.

For MECH-095 the value is in connecting agency-attribution to the
interoceptive channel architecturally. If the agency-detection
comparator's domain extends to interoceptive predictive coding (as
Lyndon 2026 argues for FND), Seth & Friston's framework is the
substrate that comparator operates on. Made-feelings phenomena would
then be MECH-095-class failures localised to the interoceptive layer
rather than to the motor or sensory layers other passivity-cluster
entries address.
