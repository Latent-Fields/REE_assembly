# Jeganathan & Breakspear (2021): Active Inference on Negative Symptoms

This *Lancet Psychiatry* review applies active inference to the
negative symptoms of schizophrenia — affective blunting, avolition,
asociality — that the predictive-coding literature on positive
symptoms had previously left under-theorised. The argument is that
affective behaviours such as smiling are driven by predictions about
social consequences. Negative symptoms then arise from uncertainty in
social prediction circuits, analogously to how positive symptoms have
been linked to uncertainty in sensory circuits. The review explicitly
draws links between social prediction, facial affect mirroring, and
interoception, and identifies "indistinctness of self-other boundaries"
and the content of paranoid delusions as natural consequences of the
same precision miscalibration affecting social and interoceptive
channels.

For the made-feelings / interoceptive-passivity cluster the relevance
is the bridge between the negative-symptom literature and the
passivity literature. Made-feelings phenomena are usually classified
with positive symptoms (Schneiderian first-rank), but the affective
blunting and self-other-boundary indistinctness Jeganathan and
Breakspear write about are continuous with the made-feelings
phenomenology — they describe an agent whose interoceptive and
affective comparators are operating outside their competence regime,
producing self-other distinction failures on the affective channel.

For ARC-058 the review's contribution is to demonstrate that
precision miscalibration can selectively affect interoceptive and
social channels while leaving sensory-discriminative channels intact.
This pattern is more readily explained by separate-stream architecture
(ARC-033) with stream-specific precision than by fully shared trunk
(ARC-058 strong reading). It does not refute ARC-058 outright — a
shared trunk with stream-specific precision channels is a valid
intermediate position — but it constrains the architectural search
space.

For MECH-095 the contribution is to extend agency-attribution into
the social and interoceptive directions that V3 does not currently
model. If V3-EXQ-506 was substrate-ceiling on the somatic channel,
the made-feelings cluster predicts a similar substrate-ceiling on the
affective and social channels for which an analogous SD candidate
would be needed. SD-047 addresses exteroceptive multi-source
dynamics; an interoceptive-multi-source SD would be the natural
successor for this cluster.

The honest weakness is that the review treats schizophrenia broadly
rather than the passivity cluster specifically, and the bridge from
"negative symptoms include self-other indistinctness" to "made-
feelings cluster needs separate machinery" is interpretive rather
than direct. Empirical work on the framework's predictions (automated
facial-coding of blunted expressiveness, acoustic-pattern analysis
of negative-symptom speech) is described as future direction rather
than completed. This is supports-with-caveats rather than load-bearing.

Confidence reasoning: Lancet Psychiatry venue, well-positioned authors.
Mapping fidelity moderate because the review's focus is broader than
the passivity cluster. Transfer risk moderate because REE V3 does not
yet implement social-prediction circuits directly; the framework's
extensions are partly aspirational. Net confidence ~0.76.
