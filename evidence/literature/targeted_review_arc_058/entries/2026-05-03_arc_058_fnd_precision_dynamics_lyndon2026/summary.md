# Lyndon (2026): Precision Dynamics in Functional Neurological Disorder

This 2026 *Brain* paper offers the cleanest available framework for the
made-feelings / interoceptive-passivity sub-cluster of agency-attribution
failure. Lyndon reframes FND as a disorder of precision control within
predictive coding, organised as a four-level hierarchy (affective →
interoceptive → proprioceptive → spinal). Arousal can transiently
spike precision on an unhelpful expectation, suppress corrective
feedback, and produce motor, sensory, cognitive, or visceral symptoms
that migrate across levels. The clinical hallmark — "loss of agency
without wilfulness or malingering" — is the load-bearing phenomenon
for our purposes.

For ARC-058 the contribution is architectural. The four-level
hierarchy implies that precision can be selectively miscalibrated at
one level while the others operate normally. That is consistent with
ARC-033's separate-streams reading and tensions with the strong reading
of ARC-058's fully-shared trunk. The plausible interpretation is that
the streams are separable in their precision dynamics (so FND can
selectively affect one level) while sharing trunk machinery for
aversive PE encoding (so arousal can recruit them collectively when
generalised). This is the partly-shared / partly-separated reading
that the existing ARC-058 entries (Wager 2013, Hofbauer 2001, Krishnan
2016, Woo 2017) already point toward; Lyndon adds the precision-
dynamics layer that those papers don't directly address.

For MECH-095 the contribution is to extend the agency-attribution
comparator's domain into interoceptive and affective channels. The
made-feelings sub-cluster of passivity phenomena — affective
self-generation arriving without the self-authorship tag — is
structurally agency-attribution failure on a non-motor channel.
Lyndon's framework predicts this should be selectively producible by
precision miscalibration at the affective level specifically, even
when motor and visual agency remain intact. That is a testable
dissociation MECH-095 should reproduce in any architecture that
implements multi-stream comparator output.

For SD-047 the relevance is more indirect. The FND framework supports
the substrate-ceiling principle (precision-related failures depend on
the input distribution the comparator operates over), but Lyndon's
levels are more granular than REE V3 currently implements. SD-047
would address the multi-source environmental dynamics gap; an
analogous SD candidate for interoceptive-source dynamics would be a
natural successor if ARC-058 / MECH-095 work moves toward implementing
made-feelings phenomena directly.

The honest weaknesses are that this is theoretical reframing rather
than new empirical data, and the four-level hierarchy is a conceptual
organisation rather than a measured architecture. *Brain* is a strong
venue, but the paper is closer to a position paper than a primary
research report. Net confidence ~0.80 — high enough to be load-bearing
for the made-feelings cluster but read alongside the empirical anchors
already in the ARC-058 entries.
