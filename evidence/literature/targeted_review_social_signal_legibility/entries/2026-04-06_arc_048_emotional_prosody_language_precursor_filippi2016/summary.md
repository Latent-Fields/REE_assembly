# Filippi (2016) -- Emotional and interactional prosody across animal communication systems: the emergence of language

**Claim tested:** ARC-048 -- Language as high-bandwidth externalization of pre-existing functional states

## What the paper finds

Filippi (2016) reviews evidence from three converging domains that emotional vocalization is the evolutionary precursor to language:

1. **Comparative animal communication:** Emotional and interactional prosody (EIP) -- voice modulation conveying emotional content -- is present across diverse mammalian species. Animals modulate pitch, rhythm, and intensity to communicate emotional states and coordinate social interactions.

2. **Human infant development:** Infant-directed speech (motherese) is prosodically exaggerated, engaging the emotional vocalization system to facilitate language acquisition. Infants are sensitive to emotional prosody before they understand words, and this sensitivity scaffolds word learning.

3. **Music-language connections:** Musical abilities and linguistic prosody share cognitive resources, suggesting both evolved from a common emotional vocalization system (consistent with Darwin's original hypothesis).

## The evolutionary bridge for ARC-048

ARC-048 claims that language is a high-bandwidth externalization channel for functional states that already exist pre-linguistically. Filippi provides the evolutionary bridge: the emotional vocalization system is phylogenetically ancient, ontogenetically primary (infants use it before language), and structurally continuous with linguistic prosody. Language did not emerge de novo -- it added referential precision and grammatical structure to an already-functioning emotional communication channel.

The infant development data is particularly relevant: before a child can say "I hurt," they can cry with an acoustic profile that communicates distress. Before they can say "I want that," they can vocalize with a prosodic contour that communicates desire. Language acquisition is a progressive increase in bandwidth over this pre-existing channel -- exactly ARC-048's thesis.

## The channel vs. content distinction

An important subtlety: Filippi's continuity is at the acoustic channel level (emotional prosody -> linguistic prosody), while ARC-048's continuity is at the content level (pre-linguistic functional states -> linguistic referents). These are complementary perspectives. Filippi shows that the delivery mechanism is continuous; ARC-048 adds that the content is also continuous -- the functional states that pre-linguistic signals encode (pain, wanting, positive arousal) become the referents of early linguistic terms.

## What this means for novelty

The prosody-continuity hypothesis is established in language evolution research (Fitch, Mithen, others have made similar arguments). ARC-048's contribution is the specific REE framing: language is not just an evolution of the prosodic channel but specifically an increase in externalization bandwidth for internal functional states that are the computational primitives of the REE architecture. The language-bootstrap phase of REE development is a novel engineering application of the evolutionary continuity thesis.
