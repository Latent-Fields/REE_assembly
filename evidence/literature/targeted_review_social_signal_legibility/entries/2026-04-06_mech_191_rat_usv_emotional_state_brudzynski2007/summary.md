# Brudzynski (2007) -- Ultrasonic calls of rats as indicator variables of negative or positive states

**Claims tested:** MECH-191 -- Stereotyped signals as causal externalizations; INV-057 -- Functional specificity evidenced by legibility

## What the paper finds

Brudzynski (2007) reviews the neuroscience of rat ultrasonic vocalizations (USVs), demonstrating that two categorically distinct call types -- 22-kHz and 50-kHz -- are produced by two distinct neurochemical systems and encode two distinct emotional valences. 22-kHz calls are invariably produced when the ascending cholinergic system is activated, corresponding to negative affective states (fear, anxiety, pain, social defeat). 50-kHz calls are invariably produced when the ascending dopaminergic system is activated, corresponding to positive affective states (anticipation, play, social approach, mating).

The acoustic characteristics of the two call types are sufficiently distinct to allow unambiguous recognition by conspecifics. The calls serve adaptive social functions: 22-kHz calls signal danger and suppress approach behavior in nearby rats; 50-kHz calls signal safety and promote approach behavior.

## The strongest case for causal externalization

This is arguably the cleanest example in the mammalian literature of MECH-191's core claim. The 22-kHz/50-kHz distinction is not learned, not conventional, not strategic -- it is an invariable neurochemical consequence. Activating the cholinergic system produces 22-kHz calls the way activating a motor neuron produces a muscle contraction. The signal is a physical product of the internal state. This is causal externalization in its purest form.

## Functional specificity enables legibility (INV-057)

Brudzynski notes that the acoustic distinctness of the two call types "allows unambiguous recognition of the emitter's emotional state." This directly supports INV-057: functional specificity (each signal type is causally tied to one neurochemical-emotional state) is what makes the signals legible. Conspecific rats do not need to learn a convention -- the signal structure itself carries the information because it is causally produced by the state it signals.

## Limitations for the dog-human case

The rat USV system operates in ultrasonic frequencies inaudible to unaided humans. It is therefore evidence for the mechanism (causal production enables legibility) but not for the specific cross-species reading that MECH-191 and INV-057 describe in the dog-human case. The relevance is at the principle level: if causal production is sufficient for conspecific legibility in rats, the same principle should apply to audible vocalizations (whines, yelps, growls) that operate in frequency ranges shared by dogs and humans.
