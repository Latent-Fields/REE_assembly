# Summary: Huys et al. (2016) — Interplay of approximate planning strategies

**Claim tested:** MECH-186 (valence_wanting_floor — tonic serotonergic floor on VALENCE_WANTING)  
**Evidence direction:** supports | **Confidence:** 0.62

## What the paper did

Huys and colleagues conducted a large-scale study (n=1,762 online participants) examining individual differences in model-based (goal-directed) versus model-free (habitual) decision-making strategies using a two-step task. They then analysed whether tryptophan depletion (ATD, within a smaller clinical subsample n=38) selectively shifted the balance between planning strategies in a valence-dependent manner. The two-step task distinguishes goal-directed planning from habitual responding via second-stage choice patterns; ATD was administered before the task.

## Key findings relevant to MECH-186

Under tryptophan depletion, goal-directed (model-based) behaviour in the reward condition was impaired — participants relied more on habitual, model-free responding for reward outcomes. Critically, the same ATD procedure promoted rather than impaired goal-directed behaviour in the punishment-avoidance condition. This valence asymmetry — 5-HT depletion degrades planning for benefit while leaving or improving planning for harm avoidance — maps directly onto MECH-186's structure: the serotonergic floor is specifically required to maintain goal terrain legibility for benefit pursuit, while the harm avoidance pathway (MECH-187 / harm stream) operates through a separable mechanism.

## Translation to REE

The finding that ATD impairs model-based reward pursuit but leaves punishment avoidance relatively intact or improved provides converging causal evidence for MECH-186's asymmetric claim. In REE terms: depleting the serotonergic floor collapses VALENCE_WANTING without collapsing VALENCE_HARM_DISCRIMINATIVE. The agent retains harm avoidance capacity (the z_harm pathway is not serotonin-dependent per REE architecture) but loses the goal-directed planning capacity for benefit. This double dissociation is precisely what MECH-186 asserts.

## Limitations and caveats

The online sample (n=1,762) is not the ATD sample — the large N is correlational and addresses individual differences, not the 5-HT manipulation. The ATD subsample (n=38) is modest. The two-step task is a well-validated paradigm for model-based vs model-free distinction, but its mapping to REE's trajectory-planning versus residue-field-reading distinction is not one-to-one. Huys et al.'s interpretation of the punishment result (ATD improves planning for punishment) is debated; alternative accounts include attention reallocation effects. The REE-specific claim about residue field VALENCE_WANTING floor is not directly measurable in this paradigm.
