# Greenhouse-Tucknott et al. 2021 — toward the unity of pathological and exertional fatigue

According to PubMed, [Greenhouse-Tucknott et al. 2021](https://doi.org/10.3758/s13415-021-00958-x) extend the Stephan-Petzschner allostatic-self-efficacy account of pathological fatigue to cover transient (exertional) fatigue on the same predictive-processing substrate. The traditional separation between pathological and exertional fatigue, the authors argue, has obscured a shared generative structure: persistent detection of mismatches between prior interoceptive predictions and ascending sensory evidence — prediction error — signals low evidence for internal generative models, which collapses the agent's sense of mastery over the body and is experienced phenomenologically as fatigue. A loss of certainty in control predictions, in this account, is the common foundation for both timescales.

The relevance to SD-048 is not direct empirical support — the paper is a theoretical extension — but architectural licensing. SD-048 implements fatigue drift as a single AR(1) latent state with episode-level reset (`fatigue_ar_coeff` 0.995, `fatigue_noise_scale` 0.005, `contribution_weight` 0.15). This is one shape of slow body-state drift, parameterised to span episode-length timescales. The Greenhouse-Tucknott unification thesis argues that this is the right architectural commitment: rather than introducing a separate Source 4 for pathological-style chronic drift, a single drift shape with appropriate parameters produces both transient and chronic phenomena. SD-048 makes that choice, and this paper supports it.

The mapping has limits. The paper's empirical anchor is exercise physiology and chronic illness — fatigue as it appears in motor effort and clinical fatigue syndromes, not in harm tracking. SD-048 sits in a different substrate: `z_harm_a` is the affective harm stream, and fatigue drift modulates `harm_obs_a` rather than effort perception per se. The mapping holds at the structural level (slow body-state drift on a non-motor channel produces a comparator-readable signature) but does not transfer the paper's specific claims about effort perception or motor confidence collapse. The paper's pathological fatigue is also multi-episode; SD-048's drift resets each episode. The mapping is over within-episode shape, not the cross-episode trajectory — a relevant gap if REE later wants to test cross-episode allostatic dynamics.

A useful second-order point. The paper treats both pathological and exertional fatigue as expressions of metacognitive low-controllability beliefs, in line with Stephan 2016. SD-048 by itself does not implement that metacognitive layer — it gives the comparator an honest substrate to read from. Whether REE's controller develops Stephan-style allostatic-self-efficacy beliefs from SD-048's substrate is an architectural question downstream of the comparator. ARC-058's HarmForwardTrunk and ARC-033's HarmForwardSeparate sit at the lower-layer comparator; the metacognitive arc would need additional commitments (possibly ARC-016 or MECH-094-territory). Greenhouse-Tucknott et al. support SD-048 as the substrate side of the bargain, not as a fix to the metacognitive question.
