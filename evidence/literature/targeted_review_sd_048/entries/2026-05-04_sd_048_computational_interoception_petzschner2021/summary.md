# Petzschner, Garfinkel, Paulus, Koch & Khalsa 2021 — computational models of interoception and body regulation

According to PubMed, [Petzschner et al. 2021](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tins.2020.09.012) survey the computational landscape that has grown around interoception, body regulation, and allostatic control. Their organising move is to extend classical homeostatic control via reflex arcs into flexible adaptive behaviour that takes interoceptive context, experiences, and expectations into account. The review covers active inference, predictive coding, reinforcement learning, and hierarchical Bayesian frameworks, and articulates the translational-research challenges that come with making these models testable.

For SD-048 the paper functions as the substrate-prerequisite anchor. The kind of computational model the review surveys — interoceptive forward models that integrate context, expectations, and forecasts — only has bite when the substrate it operates over carries body-noise that is not generated by the agent's own actions. Without that, an interoceptive forward model can pass any benchmark by memorising the action-damage coupling. SD-048's three-source design is the substrate-level commitment that closes that loophole. Sources 1, 2, and 3 (autonomic Gaussian, sensitisation Poisson, fatigue AR(1)) make the comparator prove that it has learned structural regularities of body-noise rather than a single coupling. The paper's framework requires that capability; SD-048 is what supplies it in V3.

The mapping is at category level rather than at any specific architectural choice. Petzschner et al. do not adjudicate between active inference, RL, and hierarchical Bayes — and they do not specifically endorse a three-source decomposition over alternatives. ARC-058's HarmForwardTrunk and ARC-033's HarmForwardSeparate are both legitimate instances of the computational-models category the review surveys; SD-048 enables testing them but does not, by itself, choose between them. The paper's relevance therefore is high for the substrate-prerequisite framing — that an honest interoceptive substrate is necessary for any of these models to be testable — and lower for any specific architectural prediction within the family.

A second-order point worth flagging. The review treats the translational-research challenges of interoceptive computational models as live and unsolved: the field has not yet shown that these models work on substrates with the kind of body-noise complexity humans face. SD-048 is a small contribution to that programme — it adds three concurrent stochastic body-state sources of qualitatively different character — but it is also a minimum-viable contribution. The paper's framework would naturally extend to multi-modal interoceptive substrates (cardiac, respiratory, gastrointestinal, thermal) that V3 does not yet have. SD-048 covers the harm-stream slice of that picture; whether the substrate-level translation programme the paper articulates would be substantively advanced by SD-048 alone, or would require a broader interoceptive-substrate enrichment, is an architectural question for the V3 → V4 boundary.
