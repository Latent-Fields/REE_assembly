# Asimakidou, Job & Kilteni 2022 — reduced somatosensory attenuation along the positive schizotypy gradient

According to PubMed, [Asimakidou et al. 2022](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41537-022-00264-6) ran a force-discrimination psychophysics study in 100 healthy adults assessed for schizotypal traits. The classical somatosensory finding — self-generated touch feels weaker than externally generated touch of identical intensity, the comparator-driven sensory attenuation that motor-prediction circuits produce — was used to track individual variation in attenuation magnitude and perceptual precision. Higher positive-dimension schizotypy was associated with reduced attenuation and reduced precision of self-generated touch, both as a continuous gradient and under categorical schizotypy splitting. Effects were specific to the positive dimension and not seen for negative or disorganised schizotypy.

For SD-048 the relevance is to the architectural prediction inherited from Asai 2016 about the SD-048 4-arm noise sweep. The SD doc commits to an inverted-U comparator-competence profile across the four arms (`interoceptive_noise_scale` 0 / 0.25 / 1.0 / 4.0), with the predicted peak at ARM_2. That commitment rests on the broader Asai-style claim that comparator competence is non-monotonic in input-noise amplitude. Asimakidou et al. do not directly test the inverted-U; what they do test, and confirm, is the continuous-gradient corollary — that comparator competence is a structural feature of the comparator's parameterisation and varies smoothly across healthy individuals along the schizotypy axis. The cleanest reading is that SD-048's 4-arm sweep is the substrate-level analogue of varying the comparator's effective input-noise / precision regime, and the gradient signature predicted by Asai-style accounts has been shown to hold continuously in healthy people.

The mapping has a real cross-level limitation that I want to call out plainly. The paper studies somatosensory attenuation — motor-to-tactile reafference cancellation, Level 1 of the reafference comparator family. SD-048 is at Level 2 (interoceptive reafference cancellation). The cross-level transfer rests on an architectural assumption that comparator-competence parametrisation has the same structural shape across reafference levels (motor-sensory, motor-interoceptive, interoceptive-interoceptive). That assumption is not provable from somatosensory data alone; transferring the continuous-gradient finding to interoception is a principled but real leap. The mapping is structural rather than direct, which lowers fidelity.

A second feature worth flagging. The Asimakidou et al. result distinguishes positive-dimension schizotypy from negative and disorganised dimensions. That dimension-specificity is informative for REE's broader claim cluster: in MECH-094 territory, the tag-loss / commit-channel-suppression mechanism is conceptually closest to the positive-dimension picture. SD-048 is not directly downstream of MECH-094, but the result lends weight to the broader REE commitment that positive-symptom phenomena have a comparator-competence signature distinct from negative-symptom phenomena. The paper does not test that distinction at the interoceptive level — only at the somatosensory one — but the dimension-specificity is suggestive.

Confidence assigned at 0.74. Source quality is moderate-high (n=100, well-established psychophysics task, Kilteni group at Karolinska, Schizophrenia (Heidelberg) journal). Mapping fidelity is moderate because the paper studies somatosensory rather than interoceptive attenuation — the cross-level inheritance is principled but introduces transfer risk. The continuous-gradient finding is the strongest available analogue for SD-048's 4-arm noise-level sweep prediction at the substrate level.
