# Stephan et al. 2016 — Allostatic self-efficacy and dyshomeostasis-induced fatigue

According to PubMed, [Stephan et al. 2016](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnhum.2016.00550) propose a hierarchical Bayesian framework in which interoception is the inversion of a generative model of viscerosensory inputs. Dyshomeostasis is then formally definable: it is chronically enhanced surprise about bodily signals, equivalently, low evidence for the brain's model of bodily states. Allostasis is a change in the priors that set those bodily-state targets. A metacognitive layer above this monitors the performance of the interoceptive-allostatic circuit and updates beliefs about the brain's capacity to regulate the body — allostatic self-efficacy. Fatigue, in this account, is the early adaptive response to dyshomeostasis (the body-side cousin of sickness behaviour). Depression is what happens when chronic dyshomeostasis collapses allostatic self-efficacy into a generalised low-controllability belief — the framework folds the learned-helplessness literature into the predictive-coding picture.

For SD-048 the relevance is at the architectural level rather than at any particular experimental measurement. Before SD-048, the V3 substrate had no agent-independent body-state variance: `z_harm_a` only changed when the agent contacted hazards. A trivial forward model passes such a substrate by memorising action-damage coupling rather than by learning anything resembling a comparator that separates self-caused from body-noise-caused interoceptive change. Stephan et al.'s claim that interoception is the inversion of a generative model of viscerosensory inputs only has bite when there are viscerosensory inputs that the agent did not generate. SD-048's three-source design — autonomic Gaussian, sensitisation Poisson, fatigue AR(1) — adds exactly that. Of the three sources, fatigue drift maps most directly onto Stephan et al.'s 'chronic dyshomeostasis': a slow latent state that persists independently of what the agent is doing right now, accumulates across an episode, and constitutes a body-side cost that the comparator must learn to attribute correctly.

The framework is principled but not specific about generative shape. Stephan et al. do not commit to AR(1) drift, Poisson bursts, or i.i.d. Gaussian noise; the metacognitive theory predicts only that chronic dyshomeostasis has an interoceptive signature, not which shape that signature takes. SD-048's three-source decomposition is therefore consistent with — but not derived from — this paper. The mapping caveat is that other body-noise shapes would also satisfy Stephan et al.'s account; the SD doc draws its specific shapes from biological analogs (HRV / inflammation / allostatic load) and from the meta-design rule that three qualitatively different temporal characters force structural learning rather than single-pattern filtering.

There is one mapping risk worth flagging. Stephan et al.'s framework is hierarchical: the metacognitive layer reads the interoceptive-allostatic circuit's performance and forms beliefs about the controller. ARC-058's HarmForwardTrunk and ARC-033's HarmForwardSeparate both sit inside the lower layer of the Stephan hierarchy — they are interoceptive comparator architectures, not metacognitive ones. SD-048's substrate enables Level 2 reafference cancellation; it does not by itself make the metacognitive layer testable. The full Stephan-style story would require an additional commitment, possibly something like the ARC-016 / MECH-094 cluster reading comparator stability into a controller-confidence signal. SD-048 is necessary, but not sufficient, for the metacognitive arc.

Confidence assigned at 0.85 — the source quality is high (TNU group, with Seth, Fleming, and Petzschner as coauthors; Frontiers in Human Neuroscience), mapping fidelity is strong for SD-048's fatigue-source framing and the broader Level 2 comparator question, and transfer risk is low for the architectural commitment. The component that pulls confidence down is the under-specification of generative shape — the framework supports the architectural commitment without forcing the AR(1) choice. Load-bearing for the SD-048 design rationale on Source 3 (fatigue drift) and for the broader claim that an honest interoceptive comparator test requires agent-independent body-state variance.
