# Nakazawa et al. (2002), *Requirement for hippocampal CA3 NMDA receptors in associative memory recall* — MECH-338

## What the study did

Nakazawa and colleagues generated a conditional-knockout mouse in which the NMDA-receptor NR1 subunit is deleted specifically from CA3 pyramidal cells of the adult hippocampus, leaving the rest of the brain intact. The recurrent collateral network of CA3 had long been the theorist's candidate for *pattern completion* — recovering a complete stored representation from a partial or degraded input — but causal evidence was missing. The knockout supplied it. The mutant mice were essentially normal when the full set of spatial cues was available: spatial reference memory was intact and CA1 place fields were normal. The deficit appeared selectively when retrieval had to proceed from an *incomplete* cue set — recall was impaired, and CA1 place-field stability degraded specifically under partial-cue conditions. The dissociation is clean: encoding and full-cue recall preserved, partial-cue completion broken, with the lesion localised to the recurrent CA3 substrate.

## Why it matters for the claim

**MECH-338** is the "select" face of the ARC-063 field: a cue is matched against the context tags a CandidateRule carries and, on a match, the candidate is promoted toward rollout-eligibility. The open mechanistic question is *how* a cue that need not be the rule's content can nonetheless reactivate the full candidate. Nakazawa et al. supply the biological answer in the form of a structural analogy: cue-driven retrieval of a context-tagged candidate is, at the substrate level, pattern completion — a partial cue (a reinstated environmental feature, an interoceptive state, a caregiver signal) drives attractor-like recurrent dynamics that restore the complete stored representation. This does three useful things for the cluster. It gives MECH-338 a concrete, causally-established substrate shape. It explains *why* an incidental co-encoded cue suffices (the Godden & Baddeley phenomenon, however fragile, is exactly what a completion network predicts). And it makes explicit MECH-338's stated dependence on **MECH-269**: a context must be *discriminably represented* before a partial cue can complete *to* it — if the representation is monostrategic/degenerate, there is no distinct attractor for the cue to fall into.

## Limitations and honest caveats

This is rodent hippocampal spatial memory, not rule retrieval. The mapping from "completing a stored memory pattern from a partial cue" to "a cue addresses a context-tagged CandidateRule" is a substrate-plausibility analogy at the level of mechanism *shape* — it argues the MECH-338 operation should be attractor/completion-like rather than a flat feature lookup — not a behavioural or representational identity. REE implements no CA3 module and this entry does not propose one; it grounds the plausibility and the qualitative form of the mechanism, nothing more. The transfer risk is the standard rodent-to-architecture one and is recorded as such.

Confidence 0.78. Source quality is very high — a decisive, well-replicated, region-specific causal genetics result in a top venue, and the canonical demonstration of hippocampal pattern completion. Mapping fidelity is good: the partial-cue completion dissociation is structurally the MECH-338 mechanism and even motivates the MECH-269 dependence.
