# Spelke & Kinzler (2007), *Core knowledge* — counter-entry, ARC-077

## What the paper argues

Spelke and Kinzler synthesise a large infant, comparative, and cross-cultural literature into the core-knowledge thesis: human cognition is founded on a small number of innate, domain-specific systems — for representing objects and their mechanics, agents and their goal-directed actions, number, and space, plus a likely fifth system for social partners. Each system has deep phylogenetic and ontogenetic roots, is demonstrable in infancy, and continues to shape adult cognition. The central claim that matters here is that a substantial amount of *structured representational content is endogenous* — it is brought to experience by maturation, not extracted from it and not, in particular, supplied by social scaffolding.

## Why it is the genuine counterweight, and exactly what it bounds

This is the principled endogenous-maturational counter-evidence the GAP-L brief specifically asked to be checked. ARC-077 asserts that the candidate-rule hypothesis space is populated from *outside* the agent by caregivers — the most direct architectural answer to MECH-309. Spelke & Kinzler establish that this cannot be the *whole* story. Some of the most rule-apprehension-relevant structure of all — the representation of agents as having goals and acting to achieve them — is, on their evidence, endogenous and early, present before and independent of social scaffolding. Logged honestly, this does two things to the cluster. It bounds ARC-077's scope: the claim must not be read as "all candidate-rule structure is socially sourced." And it adds a structural qualification to MECH-337: social population does not write into a blank field; it writes into a field already shaped by endogenous core priors, and what scaffolding acts *on* is partly given.

Crucially, this *sharpens* ARC-077 rather than removing it, and it is consistent with how the claim is actually registered. ARC-077 is explicitly one of three rule-sources — peer to ARC-062 (top-down) and ARC-064 (bottom-up), upstream-fed by ARC-065 — not a monopoly account of where rule structure comes from. Spelke & Kinzler is counter-evidence to an over-strong reading of the slot, not to the slot as written. I have flagged in the mapping caveat that treating this as a refutation of the socially-scaffolded pathway would be a misuse.

## Limitations and honest caveats

Two. First, the paper does not engage REE or rule-apprehension at all; the scope bound it imposes is *inferential*, drawn by me from a general nativist synthesis, not stated by the authors — mapping fidelity is moderate for that reason. Second, intellectual honesty cuts both ways: core knowledge is itself a contested position, with substantial constructivist and developmental-systems critiques. So the bound this entry imposes is a *recognised and serious* position, not a settled fact — which is the right epistemic status for a counterweight in a structural-slot lit-pull.

## Confidence reasoning

Confidence 0.66, direction mixed. Source quality is high — a landmark, heavily-cited synthesis. Mapping fidelity is moderate because the scope bound is inferential rather than stated, and transfer risk is the usual moderate developmental-to-architecture gap. The figure encodes a principled, scope-limiting (not refuting) contribution: it tempers the cluster's lit_conf and forces the explicit qualification that social population operates on an endogenously-structured field. As mixed counter-evidence it pulls the aggregate down rather than up, which is the honest and intended effect.
