# Heyes (2016), *Born pupils? Natural pedagogy and cultural pedagogy* — counter-entry, MECH-337 / ARC-077

## What the paper argues

Heyes takes the natural-pedagogy hypothesis apart component by component. For each putative element of the receptive package — sensitivity to eye contact, contingency detection, infant-directed speech, gaze cuing, rational imitation — she asks two separate questions: is it a reliable feature of infant behaviour, and is it a *genetic adaptation specifically for teaching*? Her conclusion is that the evidence does not, for any component, establish the second. Human receptivity to being taught, she argues, is better explained as the product of *non-specific* genetic adaptations for social bonding and social learning, heavily elaborated by culture itself ("cultural pedagogy"), rather than an innate, dedicated "natural pedagogy" instinct. This is the principal published skeptical counterweight to the Csibra & Gergely position included earlier in this pull.

## Why it is logged as counter-evidence, and what it actually bounds

This entry is recorded with `evidence_direction: mixed`, and the distinction it forces is the intellectually important part of the whole pull. Heyes does **not** dispute that scaffolded teaching transmits cognitively opaque content — she accepts the *phenomenon*. What she disputes is that the receptive machinery is innate and dedicated. So she is counter-evidence to a *strong framing* of MECH-337 (one that treats the ostensive-population channel as a pre-wired primitive the agent simply has), not to the architectural slot itself. ARC-077 only requires that the functional channel *exist* — that an external agent can ostensively populate context-bound candidate rules; it does not require that channel to be innate.

Read correctly, Heyes is actually *useful* to the REE position rather than threatening to it. If receptivity to teaching is itself constructed — built developmentally out of social-bonding and associative-learning substrates — then the right way to model MECH-337 is *not* as a free innate primitive but as a learned/constructed channel. That conclusion reinforces, rather than undermines, GAP-L's second hard gate: the caregiver/teacher-agent substrate must be *built*, not assumed, and ARC-077/MECH-337/MECH-338 are correctly held as structural slots only. The failure signatures in the record capture both edges: a substrate that hard-codes an innate ostensive channel inherits an unsupported assumption; and the developmental status of the channel (constructed, not given) is itself a design constraint.

## Limitations and the over-reading risk

The single largest interpretive risk is over-extending this paper into "social scaffolding does not populate rules." It does not say that and the mapping caveat states so explicitly. Heyes' target is the nativist, dedicated-adaptation thesis; the transmission effect is common ground between her and Csibra & Gergely. The entry is included precisely so the governance trail carries the bound, not so the bound can be mistaken for a refutation.
