# Wood, Bruner & Ross (1976), *The role of tutoring in problem solving* — ARC-077 / MECH-337

## What the paper did

Wood, Bruner and Ross observed three-, four-, and five-year-old children individually tutored by an adult on a three-dimensional wooden block construction task (assembling a pyramid). From the tutoring interactions they abstracted the concept that gave the field its vocabulary: *scaffolding*. A scaffold is the process by which a more competent agent enables a learner to solve a task that would be beyond the learner's unassisted effort, by controlling those elements of the task that are initially out of the learner's reach so that the learner can concentrate on, and master, the elements that are in range. They identified six concrete tutoring functions: recruitment, reduction in degrees of freedom, direction maintenance, marking critical features, frustration control, and demonstration. The paper is the canonical operationalization of Vygotsky's zone of proximal development — the band between what a learner can do alone and what they can do with help.

## Why it matters for the claim

This paper is load-bearing for two REE commitments at once. For **ARC-077** it grounds the central architectural assertion: rule structure is populated *from outside* the agent, specifically in a zone the agent cannot reach unaided. That is precisely what makes ARC-077 a genuine third rule-source, peer to ARC-062 (top-down gradient pressure) and ARC-064 (bottom-up self-extraction), rather than a relabelling of either — the regularities a scaffold delivers are by construction the ones that lie *above* what bottom-up extraction or unaided gradient descent would produce. For **MECH-337** the six tutoring functions are an unusually clean decomposition of the "in" face: "marking critical features" and "reduction in degrees of freedom" are exactly the operations by which a context-tagged candidate regularity is written into the field. And the ZPD does useful architectural work as a *bound*: it specifies which candidates are populatable — not the trivially-already-extractable (which bottom-up would get anyway), not the unlearnable, but the in-between band that needs an external source.

## Limitations and honest caveats

Three caveats. First, this is a small-sample, foundational observational study from 1976, not a powered modern experiment; its authority is historical and conceptual rather than statistical. Second, the contingency point matters for any future substrate: effective scaffolding in the paper is *titrated* to the learner's state and *withdrawn* as competence grows. A REE caregiver channel modelled as a fixed, non-contingent injection would not reproduce the tutoring gain — I have recorded this as a failure signature so the substrate work inherits it. Third, a genuine historical caveat: Vygotsky never used the word "scaffolding" and Wood et al. did not themselves tie the construct explicitly to the ZPD; that synthesis was made by later authors (Bruner 1985; Cazden 1979). I treat ZPD + scaffolding as the standard composite the field now uses, but flag the attribution rather than asserting it as original to this paper.
