# Tulving & Thomson (1973), *Encoding specificity and retrieval processes in episodic memory* — MECH-338

## What the paper established

Tulving and Thomson articulated and tested the *encoding specificity principle*: the specific operations performed on a stimulus at the moment of encoding determine what is stored, and what is stored in turn determines which retrieval cues will later be effective. The striking experimental consequence — demonstrated in the recognition-failure-of-recallable-words paradigm — is that a cue's effectiveness is not a function of its raw associative strength but of its overlap with the trace *as it was encoded*. A normally strong pre-experimental associate can fail to retrieve a word that a weak cue, present and encoded alongside the target, successfully recovers. Cue strength per se is not what governs access; match to the encoding context is. This is one of the most robustly replicated principles in the memory literature and is the theoretical parent of context-dependent and state-dependent recall.

## Why it matters for the claim

This is the formal core of **MECH-338**, the "select" face of the ARC-063 CandidateRule field. MECH-338 says: a cue — environmental, interoceptive, a caregiver signal, a reinstated context — is matched against the context tags a CandidateRule was given *at population time* (by MECH-337), and matching candidates are promoted toward rollout-eligibility. Encoding specificity is precisely the principle that makes this mechanism coherent: a rule scaffolded in context C is retrievable later if and only if the present cue overlaps the encoded context C. It also explains, at a mechanistic level, *why context-bound population is the precondition that makes cued retrieval pay off* — and it sharpens ARC-063's falsifiable prediction (a): because the cue, not just the current observation, addresses the rule, the discriminative cut can legitimately be a function of episode or agent history rather than of present input. The second failure signature is the architecturally important one: a population mechanism that wrote a candidate *without* binding it to its scaffolding context would leave nothing for a later cue to match — context-free population structurally breaks cued retrieval. That is a direct constraint on how MECH-337 must write.

## Limitations and honest caveats

The paper concerns episodic memory for word lists in adult humans. The REE object is not an episodic recollection but a context-tagged candidate *rule* feeding a rollout-eligibility gate. The principle transfers cleanly at the level of mechanism — cue-to-encoded-context match governs access — but MECH-338's specific gating story (the ARC-063 Tolerance-Principle availability gate still makes the final eligibility decision; MECH-338 only supplies the cue-addressed *candidate set* it operates over) is a REE construct on which a 1973 verbal-memory study is necessarily silent. I have kept the mapping at the mechanism level and flagged the gate as REE-specific.
