# Tomasello, Carpenter, Call, Behne & Moll (2005), *Understanding and sharing intentions* — ARC-077 / MECH-337

## What the paper argues

This Behavioral and Brain Sciences target article proposes that the decisive difference between human and other primate cognition is *shared intentionality*: the capacity and, critically, the *motivation* to participate with others in collaborative activities built around jointly held goals and intentions. The authors argue this requires more than powerful intention-reading — it requires a species-unique drive to share psychological states and species-unique representational formats for doing so. Developmentally, they trace shared intentionality emerging gradually across the first roughly fourteen months of life as two pathways intertwine: a general great-ape line of understanding others as animate, goal-directed agents, and a human-specific motivation to share emotions, experience and activities (manifesting as joint attention, social referencing, and later role-reversal). The pay-off is that this machinery is what scaffolds linguistic symbols, social norms, and social institutions — the paradigmatic externally-sourced, context-bound rule systems.

## Why it matters for the claim

The mapping here is deliberately to the *precondition* rather than the mechanism. For **ARC-077**, the paper grounds why a socially-scaffolded rule-source must be its own architectural pathway: conventions and norms — exactly the kind of context-bound rules the cluster is about — are acquired *through* the shared-intentionality channel. They are neither invented by the agent (the MECH-309 diagnostic) nor compressed out of solo behaviour (ARC-064 bottom-up); they require an agent motivated and equipped to take up structure another agent is offering. For **MECH-337**, joint attention and social referencing are specifically the channel over which Csibra & Gergely's ostensive marking *operates*: they are what makes a caregiver's mark addressable to the learner in the first place, and they are why a populated candidate is inherently bound to its context — it enters bound to the shared-attention episode in which it was marked.

## Limitations and honest caveats

This is the lowest-mapping-fidelity entry in the supporting set, by design and stated honestly. It evidences the *enabling condition* for ARC-077/MECH-337 — the social-cognitive substrate that makes scaffolding possible at all — not the population write that MECH-337 specifies. REE also has no second-agent substrate, so "shared intentionality" functions here as an architectural motivation, not an implementable specification; this is one of the two independent GAP-L gates and the lit-pull does not discharge it. The fourteen-month developmental-emergence timeline is human-ontogenetic and is not transferred as a schedule — only as evidence that this is a substantive, separable capacity rather than a free by-product of general learning, which is the point relevant to whether ARC-077 deserves its own slot.
