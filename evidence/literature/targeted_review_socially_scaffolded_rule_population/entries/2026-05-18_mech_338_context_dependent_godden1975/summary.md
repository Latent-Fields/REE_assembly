# Godden & Baddeley (1975), *Context-dependent memory in two natural environments* — MECH-338

## What the study did

Godden and Baddeley ran the now-canonical diving experiment. Trained divers learned auditorily presented word lists either on land or roughly six metres underwater, then four minutes later attempted free recall either in the same environment or in the other one, with every diver completing all four learn/test combinations. The original result was a large environmental context effect: recall was substantially better when the recall environment matched the learning environment. Environmental context at encoding, in other words, behaves as a retrieval cue — the same principle Tulving & Thomson had formalised, but demonstrated here with a gross, real-world, content-irrelevant context (where you physically were) rather than a semantic associate.

## Why it matters for the claim

This is the concrete environmental instantiation of **MECH-338**'s "select" face. MECH-338 holds that a CandidateRule populated in context C becomes accessible again when C, or a learned cue for C, recurs. Godden & Baddeley matters because it shows the cue need not be any part of the rule's *content* — a co-encoded context feature that is entirely incidental to what was learned is sufficient to gate later access. That is exactly what "context-bound" is meant to denote in MECH-337/338: the candidate enters bound to the episode it was scaffolded in, and an incidental cue from that episode can later address it. As an architectural illustration it is vivid and on-point.

## Limitations and honest caveats — this entry is deliberately weighted down

The honest reason this entry's confidence is low (0.58) is not the domain gap, though that exists (adult verbal free recall is not candidate-rule retrieval). It is a robustness problem. A 2021 pre-registered, higher-powered replication published in *Royal Society Open Science* (8:200724) did **not** reproduce the same-context advantage. The specific environmental-context-dependent-recall *phenomenon* is fragile and may be considerably smaller, or more condition-dependent, than the 1975 study suggested. I have recorded the original effect as a supporting failure signature and the replication failure as an explicit contra failure signature in the same record, so the governance trail carries both.

The architectural consequence is the important part: MECH-338 must **not** rest its weight on the strength of the environmental-context phenomenon. Its grounding is secured elsewhere in this pull — on the general encoding-specificity principle (Tulving & Thomson 1973, robust and theory-defining) and on the neural substrate that implements partial-cue completion (Nakazawa et al. 2002, CA3 NMDA pattern completion). Godden & Baddeley is retained for the concrete instantiation and, frankly, to log the contra-evidence transparently rather than quietly omit a fragile classic.
