# Csibra & Gergely (2009), *Natural pedagogy* — MECH-337 / ARC-077

## What the paper argues

Csibra and Gergely propose that human communication is not merely a channel for exchanging episodic information but a system specifically adapted to transmit *generic*, kind-relevant knowledge across individuals. The receptive side of this system is in place very early: infants are sensitive to ostensive signals — eye contact, infant-directed speech, contingent reactivity, referential gaze shifts — that flag "this is addressed to you," they develop referential expectations in those ostensive contexts, and, crucially, they interpret what is then communicated as *generalizable* and *kind-relevant* rather than as a one-off fact about the here-and-now. The pay-off the authors emphasise is the social transmission of *cognitively opaque* content: knowledge whose function or rationale cannot be reconstructed by watching alone (why a tool is used a certain way, what an arbitrary convention is for). Observational learning is, by their argument, structurally insufficient for this class; an ostensive teaching channel is required.

## Why it matters for the claim

This is the most direct biological grounding available for **MECH-337**, the "in" face of the ARC-063 CandidateRule field. The REE slot posits that a candidate regularity the agent could not have invented alone is *written into* its hypothesis space from outside, by a caregiver who marks it, and that the candidate enters the field *already bound to the scaffolding context*. Csibra & Gergely supply exactly that mechanism in the biology: ostensive marking is the act of population, and the infant's bias to read marked content as generic-and-kind-relevant is precisely "this enters as a rule, not an episode." At the parent level (**ARC-077**) the paper grounds the architectural commitment that the candidate-rule hypothesis space is populated *from outside the agent* — which is the most direct architectural answer to MECH-309's diagnostic that a gradient/Bayesian learner does not invent the hypothesis space it updates over. The cognitively-opaque-content argument is also a clean falsifier framing: a substrate restricted to bottom-up self-extraction (ARC-064 alone) should fail on exactly the content class the paper identifies.

## Limitations and honest caveats

Two boundaries matter. First, this is a theoretical synthesis, not a single decisive experiment; its strength is the convergence of the infant literature it organises, not one critical result. Second — and this is the load-bearing caveat — the paper's stronger claim that natural pedagogy is an *innate, dedicated* adaptation is genuinely contested; Heyes (2016, included as a counter-entry in this same review) argues the receptivity is built from non-specific adaptations for social bonding and social learning. For REE this is not damaging: the slot needs the *functional channel* to exist (an external ostensive source can populate context-bound candidate rules), not for that channel to be innately specified. I have therefore mapped only the functional necessity and explicitly dropped the innateness sub-claim at the mapping boundary.
