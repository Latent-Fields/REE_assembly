# Summary: Schoenbaum, Saddoris & Stalnaker (2007) -- OFC in Reversal Learning and the Encoding of Outcome Expectancies

**Entry:** 2026-04-04_mech_153_ofc_outcome_expectancy_schoenbaum2007
**Claim tested:** MECH-153 -- E1 ContextMemory requires supervised context-labeling objective for differentiated representations
**Evidence direction:** supports | **Confidence:** 0.73

---

Schoenbaum, Saddoris and Stalnaker are asking why orbitofrontal cortex lesions spare sensory processing and even basic associative learning while selectively eliminating the flexible, outcome-dependent adjustment of behavior. Their answer, marshalled from a substantial body of single-cell recording work in rodents and primate lesion studies, is that OFC is doing something specific and irreplaceable: it is encoding *outcome expectancies* -- predictive cue-value associations that carry the identity of the anticipated outcome, not merely its presence. Crucially, this encoding is not formed through mere cue exposure or through prediction error feedback alone; it requires the outcome-labeled training signal provided by cue-outcome pairing. Rats with OFC lesions can learn the cue statistics (which stimulus predicts reward delivery) but cannot form the value-indexed representation that allows the system to anticipate the specific quality of the upcoming outcome and update that anticipation when contingencies reverse.

This is not an incidental parallel to MECH-153: it is the same logical structure. E1 ContextMemory, trained exclusively on world-model prediction error, receives the sensory trajectory of state transitions near and far from hazards. This is the computational analog of cue exposure: the LSTM sees the statistics of what states look like in each context region. What the world-model objective does not provide is the cue-outcome pairing -- the labeled signal that says "this context is hazard-proximate and the relevant outcome dimension is harm contact frequency." The EXQ-181b result (cosine_sim(mean_prior_A, mean_prior_B)=0.9999 across all seeds) is the LSTM analog of Schoenbaum's OFC-lesioned subjects: the sensory statistics are processed and encoded, but the outcome-indexed context partition does not form. The supervised context-labeling objective MECH-153 requires is the training equivalent of cue-outcome pairing: it provides the gradient that teaches the memory system which distinction matters, not merely what the states look like.

Schoenbaum's account also contains a subtle point that bears on MECH-153 implementation. OFC encoding of outcome expectancies depends on input from basolateral amygdala -- specifically, the amygdala provides the value signal that OFC uses to construct the outcome-expectancy representation. The architecture is not self-contained: outcome encoding requires a labeled error signal from a structure that has already processed the outcome. In REE terms, this supports the design choice of deriving the supervised context label from hazard_gradient_view.max() -- an internally computed signal that corresponds to the outcome-relevant feature of the context -- rather than expecting the ContextMemory module to infer context identity purely from z_world statistics. The amygdala-OFC circuit illustrates why outcome-labeled input to the memory encoding layer is architecturally necessary, not merely convenient.

Two caveats limit confident mapping. OFC is a prefrontal structure with Hebbian synaptic modification and neuromodulatory gating operating over timescales of days to weeks -- a very different mechanism from the LSTM gradient updates that shape ContextMemory over hundreds of episodes. The encoding failure in OFC-lesioned subjects is also expressed as a deficit in flexible reversal, a behavioral measure, whereas MECH-153's failure criterion is cosine similarity between context memory vectors. The logical structure is homologous, but the mechanistic underpinning differs enough that we should treat the OFC evidence as converging biological support for the principle rather than a mechanistic account of the LSTM failure mode.

Confidence 0.73: the OFC outcome-expectancy literature provides strong biological grounding for the claim that outcome-labeled training is required for discriminated context representations. The transfer from prefrontal single-cell recordings to LSTM context-memory encoding is a real epistemic gap, but the functional parallel is direct enough to constitute substantive supporting evidence.
