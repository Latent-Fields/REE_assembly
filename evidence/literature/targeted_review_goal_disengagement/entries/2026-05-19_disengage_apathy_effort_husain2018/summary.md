# Husain & Roiser 2018 — neuroscience of apathy and anhedonia

The first two strands characterise the disengagement trigger and the pathology of its *absence*. This strand does the opposite job: it characterises the pathology of the trigger firing too readily, so the eventual REE abandon trigger can be bounded between two clinical poles rather than pointed at one. Husain and Roiser's transdiagnostic synthesis is the definitive statement of the over-disengagement pole.

Their reconceptualisation is the useful part. Apathy and anhedonia are not deficits in hedonic *experience*; they are disorders of motivated, goal-directed action — specifically a breakdown in effort-based decision-making for reward. The brain continuously asks whether the anticipated reward of a goal justifies its effort cost, across a network spanning ventral striatum, ventromedial/anterior cingulate cortex, and mesolimbic dopamine. Apathy is what happens when that computation is biased — either reward sensitivity is blunted or effort cost is overweighted — so the agent stops initiating and maintaining goals. Crucially, this dissociates from consummatory pleasure: a goal can be abandoned while its outcome is still valued.

For ARC-078's C3 this is a bracketing constraint, and it is why the abandon trigger cannot be the obvious thing. The obvious thing is "abandon when effort got high or value got low." Husain and Roiser show that *is* the apathy phenotype. An abandon trigger keyed on a bare effort-cost or low-instantaneous-value threshold does not approximate adaptive disengagement; it reproduces a transdiagnostic disorder. The trigger has to be bounded *below* — protected from firing on cost/value alone — as much as it has to be bounded *above* to avoid the rumination pole. Two well-characterised clinical failure modes sit on either side of the band the trigger must occupy.

The caveat sharpens rather than weakens this. In biology the effort-cost computation is value-relative and continuously recomputed against fluctuating internal state — fatigue, dopamine tone, recent reward history. Porting it into an artificial abandon trigger as a static threshold would not just be a lossy approximation; it would instantiate exactly the pathological bias the review describes (a fixed, mis-set cost weight that disengages globally rather than context-appropriately). I have scored this supports with high source quality — it is a Nature Reviews Neuroscience synthesis with the mechanism explicitly framed — and the transfer caveat is itself a design instruction: whatever the abandon trigger is, it must not be a static effort-cost cutoff.
