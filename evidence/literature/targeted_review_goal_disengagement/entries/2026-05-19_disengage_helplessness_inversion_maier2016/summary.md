# Maier & Seligman 2016 — learned helplessness at fifty

This is the entry that should change the eventual abandon claim, so I want to state its argument carefully rather than fold it into the others. Maier and Seligman, revisiting fifty years of their own theory with the benefit of the circuit work, conclude that the original theory had the polarity backwards — and the corrected polarity is exactly the thing ARC-078's C3 needs to know.

The classic story was: organisms exposed to uncontrollable aversive events *learn* that they are helpless, and that learned expectation produces passivity. The circuit work says no. Passivity in the face of prolonged adversity is the *unlearned default*, mediated by serotonergic neurons in the dorsal raphe nucleus that, under sustained aversive stimulation, inhibit active escape. What is *learned* is control: a ventromedial/prelimbic prefrontal circuit detects controllability and projects onto the dorsal raphe to *shut the default off*. In their own words, there is nothing in the brain selectively turned on by a lack of control — only something that turns the default off when control is present. And a prior experience of control induces lasting plasticity in that vmPFC→DRN pathway, so the circuit proactively inhibits the default even under later uncontrollable stress.

Apply that to the reframe and the design inverts. The implicit picture in ARC-078's C3 — and in the whole "explicit abandon path" framing — is an action-default system to which we add an abandon operation. Maier and Seligman say the biologically faithful architecture is the other way round: *disengagement is the default, and persistence is the gated, evidence-requiring operation.* The thing that needs an explicit mechanism is not abandonment; it is the control/efficacy-detection gate that *inhibits* abandonment. And the sting in the tail is decisive for the trigger question: prolonged adversity and repeated failure are *precisely the condition that produces pathological default passivity*. A REE abandon trigger keyed on repeated failure or accumulated effort-cost would not approximate adaptive disengagement — it would directly instantiate learned helplessness. This doubly confirms the never-from-accumulated-hopelessness constraint from the cue-system pull and extends it: never-from-repeated-failure, never-from-raw-effort-cost either.

I have scored this supports with high confidence because it is a canonical Psychological Review theory revision charted at circuit level and it answers the strand's central question directly — what the disengage default is, and what gates it. The honest caveat is the polarity-transfer one: this is rodent optogenetic and pharmacological circuit work generalised into a human theory, and the "default" it describes is an *aversive-context* default; reward-context goal pursuit may not share the identical polarity. So I would not import the vmPFC→DRN circuit. What I would carry into the abandon Q-claim, as the load-bearing structural finding of this whole pull, is the reframe itself: design the gate on *persistence/efficacy detection*, let disengagement be what happens in its absence, and treat any trigger that fires *from* repeated failure as a known route to the apathy/helplessness pole rather than a route to adaptive letting-go.
