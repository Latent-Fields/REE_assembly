# Gillan 2021 — the habit hypothesis of OCD and compulsive disorders

Koster gives the rumination face of failure-to-disengage; Gillan gives the compulsive one, and it is worth having both because they triangulate the same architectural point from different clinical phenomenology. The user, as a psychiatrist, will care that the OCD mapping is mechanistically precise rather than the loose "compulsions are over-strong habits" story — and Gillan's review is precisely the correction of that loose story.

The naive account says compulsions are habits that have grown too strong. Gillan's synthesis of cross-species, imaging, and computational work argues the reverse causal structure: enhanced habitual responding in OCD is a *consequence* of a deficit in the goal-directed control system, not a primary strengthening of habit. More specifically, the goal-directed deficit is framed as a failure to construct or consult an internal action-outcome model — without an adequate model, behaviour cannot be re-evaluated against current goals and defaults to stimulus-triggered responding. The compulsion is what is left when the system that would license *stopping* a no-longer-warranted action is impaired.

For ARC-078's C3 this is supporting evidence with a precise target. It says failure-to-disengage is not "persistence dialled too high"; it is the impairment of the *override/re-evaluation* pathway — the very thing a deliberate abandon mechanism would have to engage. The design reading is that the REE abandon mechanism should attach to the model-evaluation/override pathway (the function that can determine an action is no longer worth continuing and halt it), not be implemented as a habit-suppression term or as a downward adjustment of a persistence weight. That keeps it consistent with Koster: in both literatures the failure is an absent override, not an excess of value.

The caveat is the same class as Koster's and I have weighted it the same way. The goal-directed/habit framework is a model of *action selection* — model-based versus model-free control — and Gillan herself flags that human habit measurement is unreliable and contested. So the defensible transfer is "the model that would justify abandoning the action is absent or not consulted," not the literal "habits override goals," and the level of description (action selection) is one step below goal-bank commitment revocation. I have set mapping fidelity to reflect that the *structural* claim transfers well while the mechanistic identity does not, and the eventual abandon Q-claim should cite this for the necessity-and-locus of the override, not as a ready-made circuit to import.
