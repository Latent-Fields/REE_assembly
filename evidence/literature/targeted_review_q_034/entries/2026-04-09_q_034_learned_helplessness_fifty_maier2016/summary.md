# Learned Helplessness at Fifty: Insights from Neuroscience
**Maier & Seligman (2016) — Psychological Review**

Fifty years after the original learned helplessness experiments, Maier and Seligman return to correct a foundational error in the original theory. The 1967 account held that animals learned their responses were ineffective — a cognitive account of passive learning. The 2016 revision argues the opposite: passivity following prolonged aversive events is the default, unlearned response, mediated by constitutive serotonergic activation of the dorsal raphe nucleus (DRN). What organisms learn, when they can, is control. The detection of response-outcome contingency via the ventromedial prefrontal cortex (vmPFC, specifically the prelimbic region) actively suppresses the DRN default-passivity circuit. Identical shock intensity — escapable vs inescapable — produces radically divergent outcomes because only the former allows contingency detection.

The relevance to Q-034 is direct at the level of principle, if not mechanism. Q-034's core prediction is that hazard intensity is not the threshold parameter for the depression attractor — the hazard/resource ratio is. Maier and Seligman provide fifty years of evidence for exactly this proposition under a different operationalisation: the same stressor, the same tissue-level nociception, produces lasting anhedonia, anxiety sensitisation, and passive defeat only when the agent cannot access effective coping responses. When resources (effective actions) are available, the organism escapes the aversive state and the DRN serotonergic cascade is suppressed by vmPFC top-down inhibition. The biological threshold is not how much shock occurs, but whether the agent's action repertoire includes a means of terminating it.

The neurobiological mechanism identified — vmPFC detection of contingency → GABAergic inhibition of DRN 5-HT neurons → prevention of lasting behavioural depression — maps plausibly onto REE's E3 goal-maintenance module (INV-034). When resource density is sufficient for E3 to find trajectories that achieve harm-reduction goals, sustained aversive signal accumulation is prevented and the INV-053 depression attractor is not entered. When resource density is insufficient, E3 cannot satisfy the harm-reduction goal, the harm signal accumulates without offset, and the attractor transition occurs. The correspondence is structural, not mechanistic — REE does not implement a DRN or rely on 5-HT gating, but the functional logic is the same.

The key limitation for Q-034 purposes is that controllability is a binary categorical manipulation, not a parametric resource-density gradient. Q-034 requires a threshold in a continuous ratio — the point at which benefit_exposure falls low enough relative to harm_exposure to tip the system into the attractor. The Maier/Seligman paradigm cannot provide this threshold because it does not vary resource access parametrically. A second limitation is that the mechanism is primarily serotonergic; Q-034 and INV-053 are framed around mesolimbic dopamine attractors, and the connection between DRN 5-HT and NAc dopamine dynamics requires an additional mechanistic bridge not provided here. Confidence is set at 0.67: strong conceptual support for the principle (resource access determines threshold), moderate mapping fidelity due to operationalisation mismatch, and moderate transfer risk.
