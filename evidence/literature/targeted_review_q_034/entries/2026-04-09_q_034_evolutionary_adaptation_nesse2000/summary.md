# Summary: Nesse (2000) -- Is depression an adaptation?

**Entry:** 2026-04-09_q_034_evolutionary_adaptation_nesse2000
**Claim tested:** Q-034
**Evidence direction:** supports

## What the study did

Nesse (2000) is a theoretical paper asking whether low mood and depression might be adaptive responses shaped by natural selection, rather than simply malfunctions to be suppressed. The paper surveys the conditions under which low mood is found across human cultures and in animal models, and proposes that the affective down-regulation of drive and motivation is strategically appropriate in situations where continued effort carries high cost and low probability of success.

## Key findings

The paper's central argument for Q-034 is the specificity of the trigger conditions. Nesse does not argue that all harm triggers depression -- rather, the specific conditions are: (1) situations where the organism is trapped with no escape, (2) situations involving the absence of a crucial resource or viable plan, and (3) situations where continued effort toward a blocked goal would waste resources. Critically, the same harm level WITHOUT the resource deprivation component should not trigger the full depressive response -- harm is a necessary but not sufficient condition. The organism that faces equivalent harm but retains access to resources, viable plans, and escape routes should NOT down-regulate its drive to the same degree. This is a structural statement of the Q-034 ratio claim: the depression trigger is the conjunction of harm and resource absence, not harm alone.

## REE translation

The evolutionary framework provides the strongest theoretical grounding for why the ratio matters. If depression were triggered by absolute harm intensity rather than the ratio, organisms under high-harm conditions with available resources would be maladaptively inhibited at exactly the moments when resource exploitation is both feasible and high-value. Evolution should have selected against a pure harm-threshold trigger in favour of a ratio-based trigger: only down-regulate drive when harm is high AND resources are insufficient to change the situation. REE's Q-034 is asking whether the computational architecture instantiates this evolutionary logic. The Nesse paper provides the evolutionary rationale for why the ratio should be the key parameter.

## Limitations and caveats

This is a theoretical paper with no original empirical data. The evolutionary argument is compelling at the level of function but does not specify the mechanism. The concept of "crucial resource" is much broader than REE's operationalisation of benefit_exposure density: Nesse includes social support, escape routes, viable alternative goals, and cognitive resources -- a richer and less operationally precise construct. The mapping from "situation with no viable plan" to "low num_resources in CausalGridWorldV2" is a simplification. The paper does not use attractor state language or specify a computational threshold function.
