# Li et al. 2022 -- Bidirectional napping/dementia relationship in 1401 older adults

**Claims:** MECH-171
**Direction:** supports (0.76)
**Source:** Alzheimer's & Dementia, DOI: 10.1002/alz.12636, PMID: 35297533

## What the paper shows

Based on articles retrieved from PubMed: Li et al. (Harvard Medical School/Brigham and Women's Hospital) followed 1401 Rush Memory and Aging Project participants for up to 14 years with annual cognitive testing and objective actigraphy. Key findings:

- **Direction 1 (sleep disruption -> AD):** Longer and more frequent daytime naps at baseline were associated with higher risk of incident Alzheimer's dementia; more excessive daytime napping predicted worse cognition one year later.
- **Direction 2 (AD -> sleep disruption):** AD progression more than doubled the annual increase in nap duration and frequency; worse cognition one year predicted more excessive napping the following year.
- Both directions were confirmed in cross-lagged longitudinal analysis, supporting bidirectionality or shared pathophysiological mechanisms.

## REE relevance

**MECH-171** (vicious cycle) predicts that attribution failure from pipeline disruption produces sleep architecture changes (fragmented night sleep, increased daytime napping as compensation), which in turn fail to maintain the pipeline, accelerating failure further. Early intervention should be disproportionately effective because breaking the cycle early prevents compounding.

This paper provides the largest longitudinal empirical demonstration of the bidirectional relationship, with objective measurement (actigraphy) and both directions confirmed within the same cohort. The 14-year follow-up covers the relevant MCI->AD progression timescale.

The "AD more than doubles annual nap increases" finding is the clearest quantitative support for the attribution failure -> sleep disruption arm of the cycle.

## Limitations for REE

Napping is a downstream proxy of disrupted sleep architecture; the paper does not measure NREM/REM phase quality or specific pipeline stage integrity. Observational cohort -- causal inference is limited. The mechanism is not specified (the bidirectionality could reflect shared pathology rather than a causal cycle).

## Key finding for evidence direction

AD progression more than doubling the annual increase in nap duration/frequency -- while excess napping at any time point predicts worse cognition a year later -- is the clearest longitudinal demonstration of the MECH-171 vicious cycle mechanism in a large, objectively measured human cohort.
