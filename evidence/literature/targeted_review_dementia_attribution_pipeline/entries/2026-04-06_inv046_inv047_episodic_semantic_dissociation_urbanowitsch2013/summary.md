# Urbanowitsch et al. 2013 -- Episodic ABM impaired in MCI; semantic spared until moderate AD

**Claims:** INV-046, INV-047
**Direction:** supports (0.74)
**Source:** Frontiers in Behavioral Neuroscience, DOI: 10.3389/fnbeh.2013.00194, PMID: 24339804

## What the paper shows

Based on articles retrieved from PubMed: Urbanowitsch et al. assessed autobiographical memory (ABM) using the E-AGI instrument across groups of MCI patients, early AD patients, and moderate AD patients (and healthy elderly controls). Key findings:

- **Episodic ABM** (richness of detail, event-specific recall) was already impaired in MCI -- the earliest detectable clinical stage.
- **Semantic ABM** (general facts about one's life, personal semantic knowledge) was relatively preserved until moderate AD stages.
- A recency effect (better recall of recent vs. remote autobiographical memories) was present in healthy elderly but vanished in AD patients.
- The dissociation between episodic and semantic ABM was not accounted for by education, gender, or physiological aging.

## REE relevance

**INV-046** (dementia is computationally a contextual attribution failure) is supported by the episodic impairment profile. Episodic recall requires contextual binding -- retrieving the specific spatiotemporal and affective context of an event, which depends on the offline precision-recalibration and schema-tagging pipeline stages. Semantic knowledge (general facts about one's life) is less dependent on contextual binding and more on distributed neocortical storage. The pattern -- episodic fails first, semantic spared -- is exactly the prediction of a contextual attribution failure rather than a flat storage failure.

**INV-047** (staged decline mirrors reverse pipeline dependency order) gains direct support from the episodic-before-semantic dissociation. In the pipeline ordering, precision recalibration (Phase 4, REM) is the final stage and the one most critical for episodic-contextual binding. If Phase 4 fails first (as the reverse-ordering hypothesis predicts), episodic memory should deteriorate before semantic, which is what this paper shows.

## Limitations for REE

The paper does not test pipeline phase integrity or sleep architecture. The dissociation is described at the symptom level; the mechanism is not addressed. Single-centre study with moderate samples. Does not cover the procedural memory sparing component (MECH-172).

## Key finding for evidence direction

The earliest detectable ABM deficit in MCI is specifically episodic, not semantic -- this is the symptom signature predicted by attributing AD to progressive offline pipeline failure beginning with the precision-recalibration phase.
