# Van Egroo et al. 2019 -- Sleep-wake disruption is bidirectionally linked to all AD hallmarks

**Claims:** MECH-171, MECH-168, MECH-169
**Direction:** supports (0.77)
**Source:** Sleep, DOI: 10.1093/sleep/zsz017, PMID: 30649520

## What the paper shows

Based on articles retrieved from PubMed: Van Egroo et al. (University of Liege) provide a focused review of the relationship between sleep-wake disruption and the three major AD pathological hallmarks: (1) Abeta accumulation, (2) tau pathology, and (3) neurodegeneration. For each hallmark, they review evidence from animal models and human studies and argue that the relationship is likely bidirectional:

- Sleep disruption worsens Abeta accumulation (via reduced glymphatic clearance and increased production).
- Abeta pathology disrupts sleep architecture (particularly SWS and slow oscillation power).
- Similar bidirectional patterns hold for tau pathology and for neurodegeneration markers.

The review explicitly focuses on preclinical AD stages, arguing sleep-wake disruption can be identified as a modifiable risk factor before clinical dementia onset.

## REE relevance

**MECH-171** (vicious cycle: attribution failure -> sleep disruption -> accelerated pipeline failure) gets its empirical backbone from this review. The bidirectionality of all three pathological hallmarks with sleep disruption establishes the cycle operates across multiple substrate layers -- not just amyloid. Each disruption arm has now been documented at the preclinical stage, consistent with early intervention being disproportionately effective (the second half of MECH-171's claim).

**MECH-168** (staged pipeline failure maps to MCI->early->middle->late AD) is supported by the preclinical-stage focus -- sleep disruption predicts subsequent cognitive decline and AD conversion, consistent with the earliest pipeline stages failing before clinical symptoms.

**MECH-169** (glymphatic + pipeline are complementary) is reinforced by the three-hallmark framing: glymphatic clearance accounts for amyloid accumulation; the pipeline disruption account is needed to explain why specific cognitive domains fail in a specific order. The review does not make this distinction but provides the substrate evidence.

## Limitations for REE

Narrative review; cannot assign causal directionality quantitatively. Does not address attribution pipeline staging or symptom profile shape. Pre-dates several important longitudinal intervention studies.

## Key finding for evidence direction

The explicit argument that all three AD hallmarks have bidirectional relationships with sleep-wake disruption, and that this is detectable at the preclinical stage, provides the most comprehensive empirical support for MECH-171's vicious cycle prediction.
