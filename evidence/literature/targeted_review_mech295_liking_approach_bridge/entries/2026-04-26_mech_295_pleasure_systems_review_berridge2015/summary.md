# Berridge & Kringelbach 2015 -- Pleasure systems in the brain

**Claim under review:** MECH-295 -- liking-stream activation is a necessary intermediate between drive amplification (SD-012) and approach-cue selection.

**Tag:** (a) direct support for liking-required-for-drive-to-approach -- as architectural articulation rather than primary data.

## What the paper did

This is a 2015 Neuron review by Berridge and Kringelbach -- the most comprehensive single statement of the pleasure-systems architecture in the recent literature. It consolidates two decades of work into a unified picture: liking is generated by a distributed network of hedonic hotspots (NAc shell rostrodorsal, ventral pallidum posterior, parabrachial, OFC mid-anterior) that are anatomically discrete, pharmacologically gated by mu-opioid / endocannabinoid / orexin signalling, and -- crucially -- functionally upstream of motivated approach. The review articulates the position that wanting can occur without liking, but when it does, the resulting motivation is impoverished, addiction-like, or directionless: it lacks the cue-targeting that liking-shaped incentive learning supplies.

## Why it matters for MECH-295

This is the closest available theoretical articulation of MECH-295's necessity claim. The authors are explicit that liking signals are not passive afterglows of consummatory contact; they are active inputs to the motivational decision system. The cue's incentive value is shaped by previous liking experience (the Dickinson & Balleine 1994 incentive-learning logic) and updated each time the experienced liking changes (the Smith Berridge Aldridge 2011 mechanism). Drive amplification on its own can elevate the wanting signal -- but the wanting signal can only target a specific cue and produce committed approach if the cue's incentive value has been shaped by liking-stream activation.

For MECH-295 this is the architectural specification we should hold ourselves to. The EXQ-483 failure -- override fires, drive raised, approach_commit = 0.0 -- is the predicted signature of a system that has the wanting circuit but lacks the liking bridge. The review names this failure mode in clinical language (anhedonic depression, addiction-like wanting without enjoyment) and ties it to specific architectural dissociations.

## Limitations

This is a review, not primary data. The strong necessity claim that MECH-295 makes -- "no liking bridge, no committed approach" -- is articulated here as a general architectural principle of brain reward systems, but the experimental backing for the necessity reading lives in the primary papers the review cites (Pecina & Berridge 2005, Smith Berridge Aldridge 2011, the dopamine depletion data, the DAT-knockdown data). For governance purposes the review's confidence should be weighted as consolidation/synthesis rather than independent primary evidence.

The review also folds OFC pleasure coding (Kringelbach 2005, O'Doherty 2001) into the network. This raises a useful open question for MECH-295: should the REE liking-stream be specifically tied to subcortical hotspot signals (faster, more visceral, contact-driven) or generalised to include the OFC pleasure code (slower, value-laden, prospective)? Both are part of the biological pleasure system; the choice has implications for what gets implemented as z_liking in V3.
