# Castro & Berridge 2014 -- Liking vs wanting hotspot mapping review

**Claim under review:** MECH-295 -- liking-stream activation is a necessary intermediate between drive amplification (SD-012) and approach-cue selection.

**Tag:** (b) liking encoding -- substrate-level evidence supporting a discrete liking generator.

## What the paper did

This is a 2014 Castro & Berridge review consolidating roughly a decade of work on the neuroanatomy of food liking and wanting. The headline finding is that hedonic generators ("hotspots") are spatially small and anatomically discrete, while wanting/incentive generators are larger and partially overlapping but not coextensive. Two hotspots are mapped with high confidence: the rostrodorsal NAc shell (~1 mm cubed) and a posterior VP region. Mu-opioid agonists in either hotspot amplify orofacial liking; outside the hotspots they amplify intake (wanting) without amplifying liking. There are also "coldspots" where mu-opioid action suppresses liking. Multiple neuromodulatory systems (mu-opioid, kappa-opioid, delta-opioid, anandamide, orexin) gate the hotspots.

## Why it matters for MECH-295

MECH-295 commits to a specific architectural shape: the liking-stream is a real, separable signal that carries the cue-side pull required to convert drive amplification into approach. Without liking-stream activation, drive-amplified goal seeding produces a passive goal latent and approach_commit stays at 0.0 (the EXQ-483 failure signature). For this to be biologically defensible, liking has to live somewhere -- specifically, somewhere distinct from the wanting/drive substrate. Castro & Berridge 2014 is the consolidated anatomical case that it does. The hotspots are not just sub-regions of NAc shell; they are pharmacologically and behaviourally distinct generators that can be selectively activated or suppressed.

## Limitations

The paper is a review, not primary data, so its evidential weight is summative rather than independent. The hotspot data it consolidates is rat orofacial taste-reactivity at receipt -- consummatory liking. The bridge to PRE-contact approach selection (which is MECH-295's actual prediction target via approach_commit) requires the incentive-learning logic of Dickinson & Balleine 1994: experienced hedonic value retroactively shapes wanting/approach-readiness toward the cue that predicted it. That bridge is theoretical infrastructure on the REE side, not data in this paper.

The strong-necessity experiment for MECH-295 (mu-opioid antagonist in the rostrodorsal hotspot, plus a hunger manipulation, with the prediction that drive-amplified approach to a learned food cue collapses) is referenced in this kind of literature as something that should follow from the hotspot mapping, but the direct experiment is not delivered here.
