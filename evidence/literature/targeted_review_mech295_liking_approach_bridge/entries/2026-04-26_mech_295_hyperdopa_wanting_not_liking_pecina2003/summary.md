# Pecina, Cagniard, Berridge & Aldridge 2003 -- Hyperdopaminergic mice: more wanting, same liking

**Claim under review:** MECH-295 -- liking-stream activation is a necessary intermediate between drive amplification (SD-012) and approach-cue selection.

**Tag:** (c) wanting/liking dissociation background.

## What the paper did

DAT-knockdown mice carry chronically elevated synaptic dopamine. Pecina and colleagues asked the obvious question: does this make sucrose more pleasurable, or does it just make the mice more eager? The answer is the second. The mutants approached sucrose more vigorously, consumed it faster, and worked harder for it instrumentally -- all consistent with elevated wanting. But their orofacial taste-reactivity to sucrose was indistinguishable from wild-type. The hedonic impact at receipt was unchanged. Tonic dopamine elevation amplifies the motivational pursuit signal selectively.

## Why it matters for MECH-295

There are two readings of how this paper bears on MECH-295, and the choice matters.

The naive strong reading of MECH-295 -- "elevated approach requires elevated liking" -- would predict that DAT-knockdown mice should show elevated liking alongside elevated wanting. They do not. On that reading the paper weakens the claim.

The more precise reading of MECH-295 -- "liking-stream activation is the necessary bridge that propagates drive amplification into cue-side approach pull; when drive is raised AND the liking bridge is present at baseline, approach proceeds" -- treats the liking-stream as a structural connector, not as a level that must rise in lockstep with wanting. On this reading the paper is consistent: DAT-knockdown amplifies the bridge's downstream output (wanting) without needing to push the upstream input (liking). The bridge is intact; it can be driven harder.

The EXQ-483 failure mode -- override fires, drive raised, approach_commit = 0.0 -- is much better explained by a broken or unwired bridge than by insufficient liking level. Pecina 2003 indicates that under intact wiring, drive can amplify approach without raising liking; what it does not test is whether removing the bridge causes drive-amplified approach to collapse. That is the symmetric experiment MECH-295 actually predicts.

## Limitations

This is a one-tailed dissociation: push wanting up, watch liking fail to follow. The cleanest test of MECH-295 is the opposite manipulation: knock out liking (mu-opioid antagonist in the hot spot, or hot-spot lesion) plus a hunger / drive manipulation, and ask whether cue-driven approach to a learned food site collapses. That experiment exists in fragments across the Berridge group's work but not in this single paper.

So this is calibration evidence for the wanting/liking dissociation background that MECH-295 inherits. It is not direct evidence for the necessity claim. I have classed it tag (c).

## Confidence reasoning

Source quality high (J Neurosci, primary data, clean dissociation). Mapping fidelity low-moderate (the experimental direction is opposite to what MECH-295's necessity claim asks). Transfer risk moderate. Aggregate 0.70. Direction: mixed -- on the strong reading it weakens MECH-295; on the precise reading it is compatible. Documented honestly as mixed so future readers can recheck the architectural commitment.
