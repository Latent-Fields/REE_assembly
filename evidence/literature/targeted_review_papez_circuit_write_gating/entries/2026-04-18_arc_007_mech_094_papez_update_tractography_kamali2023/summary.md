# Kamali et al. 2023 — The Cortico-Limbo-Thalamo-Cortical Circuits: An Update to the Original Papez Circuit

**PubMed:** 37148369 | **DOI:** [10.1007/s10548-023-00955-y](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10548-023-00955-y) | **Venue:** Brain Topography, 36(3), 371-389.

*Source: PubMed.*

## What the paper does

A tractography-driven review of the human limbic system. The authors use
diffusion-weighted imaging evidence accumulated since the original 1937 Papez
proposal to remap the anatomy: the Papez circuit as Papez drew it — a loop
through cingulate, entorhinal, parahippocampal, hippocampus, hypothalamus,
thalamus — is structurally incomplete. Modern tractography adds the
prefrontal/orbitofrontal cortex, septum, amygdala, and anterior temporal
lobes to the core limbic network, and shows that the interconnections are
multiply-reciprocal rather than a serial loop.

## Findings relevant to REE

Two threads matter for the claims under investigation.

First, for ARC-007. The hippocampal map-navigation claim assumes the
hippocampal system is wired into a distributed architecture for trajectory
proposal and terrain reading. Kamali et al. show that this is exactly the
architecture the tractography reveals — multiple reciprocal fibre bundles
connecting hippocampus to cingulate, to anterior thalamus, to entorhinal
cortex, with additional prefrontal and orbitofrontal input. ARC-007 is
well-matched to this biology.

Second, for MECH-094. The hypothesis_tag write gate was tentatively located
(per Schnider 2003) in the orbitofrontal reality filter with cingulate
mediation. The updated circuit makes that mapping tighter: OFC is now an
explicit component of the limbic network, not a separate executive adjunct.
The pathway from OFC through cingulate to hippocampal encoding machinery is
structurally supported by the diffusion data.

## Mapping and translation to REE

The source claim is structural, not functional. The REE translation is
therefore a correspondence, not an inference: the pathways needed for MECH-094
top-down tag delivery and ARC-007 multi-node trajectory proposal exist in the
human brain. What the paper does not do is demonstrate that any specific
pathway carries a write-gating signal, or that different action modes
(habitual / planned / simulated / sleep) use different subsets of these
pathways. The action-mode specificity is still gap territory.

## Caveats and limitations

Tractography resolves structural connectivity but not direction-of-information-
flow or signal content. A pathway from OFC to hippocampus does not, by itself,
tell us whether OFC writes tags to hippocampus or vice versa. The paper is a
review, not primary data, so confidence is capped at the quality of the
underlying imaging literature. Human anatomy is the right target for REE
mapping, so transfer risk is low on that axis; but review-of-imaging is one
further degree of abstraction from the circuit dynamics the claims need.

## Confidence reasoning

0.7. High mapping fidelity because the claim is architectural and the review
explicitly addresses the anatomy. Moderate source quality because tractography
reviews do not themselves produce mechanism. Low transfer risk because the
target is human anatomy. The entry supports but does not prove; it anchors
the network-level topology the existing synthesis assumed.
