# Lavallé et al. 2020 — Source Monitoring in Schizophrenia: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

**PubMed:** 32406366 | **DOI:** [10.1192/j.eurpsy.2020.48](https://doi.org/10.1192/j.eurpsy.2020.48) | **Venue:** European Psychiatry, 63(1), e38.

*Source: PubMed.*

## What the paper does

A systematic review and meta-analysis of source-monitoring task
performance in schizophrenia. Source monitoring is the behavioural
paradigm that tests a subject's ability to identify the source of
remembered content — was it said by A or B, was it thought or spoken,
was it imagined or actually perceived? The meta-analysis aggregates
effect sizes across three sub-types — external, internal, and reality
monitoring — and finds systematic deficits in schizophrenia, with
reality monitoring most robustly impaired. The typical failure pattern
is external attribution of internally-generated content, which is
mechanistically continuous with hallucinatory experience.

## Findings relevant to REE

This entry addresses the gap the memory note flagged: MECH-094 has
been lumping confabulation, psychosis, and hallucination together as
"tag loss" phenomena, but they are dissociable clinical phenotypes
that need separate mechanistic treatment. Lavallé et al. provide the
clinical operationalisation of that dissociation for the
hallucination arm.

The critical observation is that schizophrenia source-monitoring
deficits are not uniform. The meta-analysis finds the largest effect
size for reality monitoring — confusing imagination with perception.
That is a specific pattern: the content is generated (so the write
is happening), but the source-tag is systematically inverted. It is
not the Korsakoff pattern, where the content is confabulated but
there is no tag on it at all. It is a tag-mis-delivery pattern.

## Mapping and translation to REE

MECH-094 needs refinement to carry the clinical dissociation. Two
distinct failure modes map onto the same general architecture:

**Tag-loss (Korsakoff, confabulation):** write occurs, hypothesis_tag
is absent. The system cannot distinguish simulated from actual
experience because the marker is missing. Lesion substrate: Papez
relay (mammillary, anterior/mediodorsal thalamus).

**Tag-misassignment (schizophrenia, hallucination):** write occurs,
hypothesis_tag is delivered but with inverted source. Internally-
generated content is marked external. Lesion substrate: not yet
fully localised in the connectome literature; candidates include
prefrontal-temporal dysconnectivity rather than primary Papez
damage.

These are mechanistically distinct. They are also probably
anatomically distinct. Treating them as one failure mode in MECH-094
obscures the structure that the clinical data provides.

## Caveats and limitations

Source-monitoring tasks are behavioural, not mechanistic. The paper
does not identify a circuit-level substrate for the sub-component
dissociation. The interpretation that external / internal / reality
monitoring maps onto separable sub-components of the write-gate is
theoretical extrapolation — the paper supports the component
separation behaviourally, but whether those components are implemented
by separable sub-circuits is a further step.

Schizophrenia as a diagnostic category is heterogeneous. The meta-
analytic effects average across patient populations with varying
symptom profiles. The mapping to MECH-094 is cleanest for patients
with prominent positive symptoms; the source-monitoring deficit in
predominantly negative-symptom presentations may have a different
mechanistic signature.

## Confidence reasoning

0.75. Meta-analytic source quality is high. Mapping to MECH-094 is
strong behaviourally — the clinical dissociation between
confabulation-pattern and hallucination-pattern source-monitoring
failure is exactly the dissociation MECH-094 needs. Transfer risk is
low because human clinical data is the correct target. The main
uncertainty is circuit-level: the substrate for tag-misassignment
(vs Korsakoff's tag-loss) is not directly established by this
paper and will need its own targeted pull.
