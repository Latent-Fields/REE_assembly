# Arts, Walvoort & Kessels 2017 — Korsakoff's Syndrome: A Critical Review

**PubMed:** 29225466 | **DOI:** [10.2147/NDT.S130078](https://doi.org/10.2147/NDT.S130078) | **Venue:** Neuropsychiatric Disease and Treatment, 13, 2875-2890.

*Source: PubMed.*

## What the paper does

A clinical review of Korsakoff's syndrome — the chronic neuropsychiatric
picture that follows untreated Wernicke encephalopathy, usually in the
context of alcohol-use disorder and thiamine deficiency. Arts et al.
cover lesion distribution, neuropsychological profile, differential
diagnosis, and management. The core anatomical finding is stable
across the literature they review: Korsakoff preferentially damages
the Papez-circuit relay — mammillary bodies, mediodorsal and anterior
thalamic nuclei, fornix — while relatively sparing hippocampal
formation itself. The core clinical finding is equally specific:
severe anterograde amnesia with confabulation and executive
dysfunction, against preserved procedural memory and largely
preserved remote memory.

## Findings relevant to REE

This is the clinical complement to the connectome evidence. MECH-094
predicts that severing the hypothesis-tag delivery pathway should
produce a specific phenotype: the patient retains content but loses
the marker that distinguishes true from simulated / confabulated
memories. The patient will generate reconstructions confidently, and
those reconstructions will not be flagged as untagged. Korsakoff
matches this prediction on both axes.

The lesion pattern also tightens the anatomical claim. Confabulation
in Korsakoff correlates with damage to the Papez relay, not with
hippocampal lesion per se. That is evidence that the write-gate /
tag-delivery apparatus sits in the relay, not in the storage site —
a structural prediction of the reality-filter model (Schnider 2003)
that previously rested on OFC lesion data alone.

## Mapping and translation to REE

Translated into REE terms: Korsakoff is the phenotype of a broken
MECH-094 tag-delivery pathway in the presence of intact storage
substrate. Content writes still occur (the patient produces
reconstructions) but they are unmarked — there is no hypothesis_tag
available to distinguish retrieved from confabulated / simulated
output. The clinical behaviour matches what the REE write-gate
architecture predicts.

There is an important qualification. Korsakoff confabulation is
predominantly spontaneous / provoked in type — the patient confabulates
in response to questions or in free speech. Schnider 2003's OFC
reality-filter failures produce a slightly different phenotype
(behaviorally spontaneous confabulation with acting-on-confabulations).
These may tap distinct arms of the write-gate: the relay arm
(Papez / Korsakoff) vs the reality-filter arm (OFC / anterior limbic).
The clinical dissociation is itself evidence that the write-gating
system has multiple components, not one.

## Caveats and limitations

Alcohol-related Korsakoff comes bundled with diffuse cortical atrophy,
cerebellar damage, and broader executive dysfunction. The pure-Papez
attribution of confabulation is complicated by this co-morbidity. Even
so, the localisation claim is well-supported across the literature
the review aggregates. The review is a secondary source; primary
evidence is clinical and neuropsychological, not circuit-level.

The mechanism-level mapping to MECH-094 is an inference. The paper
documents lesion-phenotype correlation, not a direct measurement of a
tag-delivery signal. REE treats this as strong circumstantial evidence
rather than mechanistic proof.

## Confidence reasoning

0.75. High source quality (authoritative clinical review), high mapping
fidelity for the lesion-phenotype claim (Papez damage produces
exactly the reality-monitoring failure MECH-094 predicts), low transfer
risk (human clinical data is the canonical target). The entry provides
the clinical grounding for the MECH-094 hypothesis that the connectome
reviews (Bubb 2017, Kamali 2023) and the rodent anatomy (Dillingham
2015) cannot supply on their own.
