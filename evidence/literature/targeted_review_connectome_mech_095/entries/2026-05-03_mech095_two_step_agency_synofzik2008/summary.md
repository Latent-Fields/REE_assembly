# Synofzik, Vosgerau, Newen (2008): Two-Step Account of Agency

This 2008 *Consciousness and Cognition* review is the most-cited
theoretical extension of Frith's comparator account, and it sits in
the lit-pull as the qualifying voice rather than the supporting one.
The argument is that the simple comparator model — efference copy +
feedback comparison producing a single agency signal — fails on two
fronts. First, it conflates a non-conceptual *feeling* of agency
(arising from a multifactorial weighting of sensory, motor, and
intentional indicators) with a conceptual *judgement* of agency
(produced by downstream conceptual modules). Second, even within the
feeling-of-agency stage, the simple comparator account doesn't
explain agency-for-thoughts, where there is no obvious efference copy
to compare against.

The framework is generative for REE because it suggests MECH-095
should be read not as a complete agency-detection mechanism but as
the multifactorial-weighting stage feeding a separate downstream
attribution-judgment process. The counterfactual_forward gap on
E2_harm_s would be one input among several (sensory prediction,
intentional prior, contextual weighting); the judgement-of-agency
stage that produces explicit self/other tags is architecturally
separate. SD-047 substrate enrichment provides the input distribution
the multifactorial weighting stage needs to learn — it does not by
itself solve the downstream tag-production problem.

This is the kind of distinction that becomes important when V3-EXQ-506
successors run. If SD-047 fixes the substrate-ceiling on MECH-095's
counterfactual_forward gap but leaves explicit agency *judgements*
(downstream consumers of MECH-095's output) still failing, the
Synofzik framework predicts exactly that pattern — the weighting
stage was the substrate-ceiling problem, but the judgment stage is a
different architectural gap. That would route the next substrate
SD candidate toward the judgment-stage rather than further refinement
of the weighting stage.

The mixed direction reflects the framework's genuine ambivalence:
it supports the general architectural commitment to agency-detection
comparators while challenging the strong reading that a single
comparator suffices. For REE V3 work in 2026, the practical
implication is: do not assume that fixing MECH-095 substrate-ceiling
necessarily produces clean agency-judgment output; the judgment stage
may need its own architectural attention.

Confidence reasoning: high-quality theoretical review in a respected
venue, but no new empirical data — the framework is influential but
speculative for REE-style implementations specifically. Mapping
fidelity is moderate because whether MECH-095 *should* be split into
weighting-stage and judgment-stage components is an architectural
question, not yet settled. Net confidence ~0.72 — a useful
qualifying voice in the lit-pull set rather than load-bearing.
