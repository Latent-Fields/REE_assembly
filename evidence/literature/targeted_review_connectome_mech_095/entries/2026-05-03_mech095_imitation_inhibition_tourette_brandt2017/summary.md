# Brandt et al. (2017): Compensatory Imitation Inhibition in Tourette Children

This 2017 paper examined fifteen children with Tourette syndrome and
fifteen age-matched controls on a standard imitation inhibition
paradigm. The headline result is unintuitive: children with Tourette
do not show the elevated automatic-imitation interference effect one
might expect from clinical echopraxia. They show a *smaller*
interference effect than controls, but a slower overall reaction time.
Brandt and colleagues interpret this as evidence that Tourette
children actively compensate for their elevated automatic-imitation
tendencies, achieving normal-looking interference effects at the cost
of slower responses overall.

For MECH-095 the architecturally interesting finding is the cost-of-
compensation signature. Echo-phenomena-prone systems do not
necessarily produce florid echopraxia under controlled paradigms,
because additional inhibitory machinery can be recruited. The
parametric implication is that the comparator's failure mode is
graded, not binary: a partially failed MECH-095 can be supported by
extra inhibitory effort, and that effort should be measurable as
slower or more variable processing.

For SD-047 substrate enrichment specifically, the implication is
constructive: a working MECH-095 + enriched substrate should produce
not just clean self-other gating but should also exhibit graded
processing cost when the input distribution is hard (e.g. high
multi-source noise levels). A flat reaction-time profile across noise
levels would be implausible — the human comparator pays for its
gating under challenge. This adds an extra signature to look for in
the SD-047 validation experiment beyond the C1-C3 PASS/FAIL pattern.

The honest weaknesses are sample size (N=15 + N=15 is small) and the
developmental angle (Tourette is largely a paediatric population; the
mapping to a non-developmental REE V3 implementation has some transfer
risk). The result is also one of several in the imitation-inhibition
literature, not a load-bearing isolated finding. Including it here
adds nuance to the action-passivity story rather than carrying decisive
weight.

Confidence reasoning: source quality moderate (small N, *J
Neuropsychology*). Mapping fidelity is high because the imitation
inhibition paradigm operationalises automatic-imitation control
directly. Transfer risk is elevated by the developmental angle.
Net confidence ~0.71.
