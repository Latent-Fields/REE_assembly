# Ganos et al. (2015): Obsessional Slowness Revisited

This 2015 paper from the Ganos / Bhatia movement-disorders unit at
Queen Square is a clinical case series plus systematic review of the
controversial syndrome called "obsessional slowness." Three primary
cases were reviewed in detail, plus 77 published cases. The authors'
conclusion is that the syndrome is heterogeneous — covering OCD with
prominent motor slowness, catatonic presentations, and at least one
autism-spectrum case — and that it should not be retained as a
primary diagnostic entity.

For the lit-pull's purposes, the relevant finding is that echo-
phenomena (echopraxia and echolalia) co-occur in this cluster
alongside motor poverty, bizarre postures, mannerisms, oculogyric
tics, and obsessive-compulsive symptoms. Echo-phenomena rarely appear
in isolation. They live in a wider motor-control dysregulation
cluster.

For MECH-095 this is a useful scope-constraining entry. The strong
reading "MECH-095 substrate ceiling lifted by SD-047 will produce
clean self-other gating" should be tempered by the clinical
observation that motor-channel echo-phenomena tend to cluster with
adjacent motor-control failures (in REE terms: commitment_
dysregulation, threshold miscalibration on motor commitment gates,
possibly residue-blindness). A clean MECH-095 fix that did not
address these adjacent failures would still leave the Ganos cluster
intact. The negative prediction is informative: SD-047 should be
expected to lift agency-attribution function specifically, not the
broader motor-control phenomenology that echo-phenomena tend to be
embedded in.

The honest weakness is that case series are descriptive rather than
empirical paradigm work, and the lift into REE architectural language
is interpretive. The mixed direction reflects two reads of the data:
the entry supports MECH-095 as one mechanism in the cluster while
challenging the strong reading that fixing only MECH-095 would
address the wider syndrome. Sample is small and heterogeneous.

For SD-047 implementation, this entry is the cleanest case for
maintaining modest claims about what substrate enrichment will and
will not fix. SD-047 lifts MECH-095's substrate ceiling for the
agency-attribution function specifically. It does not, by itself,
address commitment dysregulation, residue blindness, or motor-
threshold miscalibration. Those are separate substrate / architectural
problems with their own claim IDs.

Confidence reasoning: case series + systematic review at a moderate-
tier journal = lower source quality than paradigm studies. Mapping
fidelity is moderate: descriptive cluster maps onto multiple REE
failure modes, not just MECH-095. Transfer risk elevated because
clinical phenomenology categories don't map one-to-one to architectural
mechanisms. Net confidence ~0.62 — useful as scope-constraining
context.
