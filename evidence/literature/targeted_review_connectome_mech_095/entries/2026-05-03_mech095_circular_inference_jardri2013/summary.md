# Jardri & Deneve (2013): Circular Inferences in Schizophrenia

This 2013 *Brain* paper from Jardri and Deneve is the computational
companion to the Nassar 2021 empirical entry. The argument is that
excitation/inhibition imbalance in hierarchical Bayesian inference
networks produces a pathological dynamics they name "circular belief
propagation": bottom-up sensory information and top-down predictions
reverberate, so each gets counted multiple times. A single substrate-
level mechanism (E/I imbalance) then produces four superficially
distinct phenomena: hallucinations (sensory information acquires
prior-like authority), overconfidence on probabilistic choices,
unshakable causal beliefs about unrelated events, and — paradoxically —
immunity to certain perceptual illusions.

The relevance to the symmetric-over/under-attribution diagnostic
signature pull is the architectural principle. One substrate
pathology produces multiple opposite-direction surface phenomena
because the inference machinery the substrate supports has multiple
consumers, each of which exhibits the imbalance differently. The
hallucination case looks like over-attribution; the immunity-to-
illusions case looks like under-attribution; both are manifestations
of the same underlying E/I problem. This is exactly the architectural
shape Asai 2016 documented for agency attribution and Nassar 2021
documented for belief updating.

For MECH-095 and SD-047 the constructive implication is that
agency-detection failures should be expected to manifest bidirectionally
across paradigms, and substrate enrichment alone may not address all
manifestations. If MECH-095's failure has an E/I-like origin in REE
(precision miscalibration on the comparator output rather than
substrate poverty alone), then SD-047 multi-source enrichment will fix
the substrate-poverty component while leaving precision-side
miscalibration intact. The diagnostic move is to look for residual
bidirectional patterns even after substrate enrichment — those would
indicate the precision side needs separate attention.

For the broader ai-cognitive-failure-taxonomy this paper is the
strongest single demonstration of the principle that a substrate
pathology can produce internally consistent but surface-paradoxical
behaviour. It generalises the Asai signature beyond agency to
hallucinations, beliefs, and perceptual illusions. Monitoring for
bidirectional surface patterns becomes a generic diagnostic across
multiple comparator failure modes in the taxonomy.

The honest weakness is that Jardri & Deneve is a computational
modeling paper rather than direct empirical demonstration, and
circular-inference is one of several competing computational accounts
of schizophrenia. Alternative frameworks (overly-precise priors,
aberrant salience, all-or-nothing updating per Nassar 2021) make
overlapping but not identical predictions. The circular-inference
specific E/I-imbalance mapping into REE V3's particular architecture
is analogical rather than literal.
