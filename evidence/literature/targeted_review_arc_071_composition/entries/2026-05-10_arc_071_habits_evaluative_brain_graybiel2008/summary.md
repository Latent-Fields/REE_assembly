# Graybiel 2008 -- Habits, rituals, and the evaluative brain

The decade-after follow-up to Graybiel 1998. The architectural commitment is unchanged -- striatal recoding chunks action sequences into performance units -- but the framing is substantially upgraded. Chunking is now positioned as the output of an evaluative-brain process: circuits in the limbic and associative striatum evaluate actions; circuits in the sensorimotor striatum chunk evaluated-as-good actions into habits. The two-step structure (evaluate, then chunk) matters for ARC-071's outcome-consistency requirement.

The most useful contribution for ARC-071 is the parameterisation of the chunking process. Graybiel 2008 reviews the empirical work that landed in the decade after 1998 -- striatal recording during overtraining, the Smith-style task-bracketing signature (high firing at chunk onset and offset, suppressed during the middle), the development of pharmacological tools to dissociate habit-from-goal-directed control. The chunking signature is robust across mammalian species. The parametric range is broad: chunks form over hundreds to thousands of repetitions in rodents; the corresponding figure in humans is task-dependent but typically in the same range.

The clinical extensions matter for ARC-071 less for the architectural commitment and more for the pathology cross-references the slot's notes already speculate about. Graybiel positions OCD, addiction, and ritualised behaviour as failures-of-evaluation in the chunking circuit -- chunks form, but on sequences the evaluative circuit should not have endorsed. This is a much sharper framing than the slot's pre-registered "OCD as ARC-071-weakened" speculation, which assumes the chunking machinery itself is impaired. Graybiel 2008 suggests the better framing is "the evaluative gate upstream of ARC-071 is impaired, so ARC-071 chunks the wrong sequences". Worth flagging in the synthesis as an upstream-gate refinement of the speculative pathology mappings.

Graybiel 2008 is silent on the real-vs-imagined distinction (R6). The substrate-level claim is that chunking happens in the sensorimotor striatum during S-R learning, which is by construction execution-side, but the paper does not directly test whether replay or imagined practice can produce a chunking signature. R6 needs other papers (Albouy 2013 hippocampal-striatal sleep replay) to settle.
