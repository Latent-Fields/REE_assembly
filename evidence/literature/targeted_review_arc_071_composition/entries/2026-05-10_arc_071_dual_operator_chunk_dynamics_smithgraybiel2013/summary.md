# Smith & Graybiel 2013 -- A dual operator view of habitual behavior

The empirical paper that closes the chunk-level mapping Yin & Knowlton 2006 left implicit. Smith & Graybiel record from DLS and infralimbic cortex (IL) during T-maze overtraining in rats, demonstrating that the two regions develop chunk-related activity patterns on different time courses and serve as dual operators in habit crystallisation. Selective optogenetic disruption of IL during overtraining prevents habit formation -- causal manipulation, not just correlational evidence.

The findings load directly onto two of the synthesis verdicts. R3 (transition mechanism for MECH-163): Smith & Graybiel supplies the causal evidence that disrupting one component of the chunking machinery (IL) prevents the planned->habitual transition. This means the transition is not just a passive consequence of repetition but an active machinery, with cortical AND striatal components. ARC-071's R3 verdict therefore comes back CONFIRMED at the causal level -- ARC-071 IS the missing transition mechanism in MECH-163, with substrate located across striatum and infralimbic cortex.

R5 (outcome-consistency threshold for chunk formation/dissolution): Smith & Graybiel show chunks form, dissolve, and reform across overtraining timescales. Chunks form when the action sequence stabilises with consistent outcomes; chunks dissolve when context changes or outcome value shifts (though the dissolution timescale is slower than the formation timescale). This supports an outcome-variance threshold below which chunks de-form. ARC-071 implementation must include both formation and dissolution machinery -- not just an accumulator that adds chunks but a maintenance process that can remove them. The slot's notes mention chunk dissolution implicitly via cross-reference to ARC-070; the synthesis should pin the dissolution-vs-decomposition relation explicitly (dissolution = chunk removed from the proposal pool entirely; decomposition = chunk re-segmented into sub-elements, original chunk still exists).

Two failure signatures matter for ARC-071 implementation. First, IL disruption prevents chunking -- a pure-striatal ARC-071 implementation will fail to chunk. The substrate must include a cortical (vmPFC-analog in REE; cross-link to INV-037 / INV-038) component. Second, the striatal task-bracketing pattern is reward-devaluation-INSENSITIVE while the IL pattern remains reward-sensitive late in overtraining -- the two operators have different evaluation properties. ARC-071 implementation must distinguish the chunk-EXECUTION pattern (devaluation-insensitive, DLS-analog) from the chunk-MAINTENANCE pattern (devaluation-sensitive, IL-analog). This is a substantive architectural detail the slot's pre-registration does not yet capture.

The caveat is that the optogenetic disruption was applied to IL, not to DLS -- the paper does not directly test whether disrupting striatum alone (with IL intact) would prevent chunking. Mapping ARC-071's substrate to JUST striatum-analog therefore underspecifies; the substrate is striatum-PLUS-IL-analog. The IL homology in REE candidates is vmPFC, with INV-037 (stored / active distinction) and INV-038 (EVR pattern) as substrate hooks.
