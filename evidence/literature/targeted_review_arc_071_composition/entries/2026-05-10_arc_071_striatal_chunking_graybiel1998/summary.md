# Graybiel 1998 -- The basal ganglia and chunking of action repertoires

The foundational reference for the chunking-of-action-repertoires construct. Graybiel proposes that the striatum, through gradual S-R learning, recodes cortically-derived information into chunked units that can be implemented as single performance units. The recoding is slow, requires repetition, and (in humans) can occur without conscious awareness.

The architecturally-load-bearing claim is the recoding direction. Cortex sends a sequence of primitives; the striatum compresses that sequence into a unit such that downstream selection treats the chunk as a single move. The chunked unit retains its sub-elements implicitly (the motor sequence still runs as a sequence) but the unit of selection at the basal ganglia level is the chunk, not its parts. This is exactly the architectural shape ARC-071 commits to: a sequence of policy primitives accumulated into a single primitive that the rule-apprehension layer (ARC-062) and the diversity-generation layer (ARC-065) operate on atomically.

Three R-verdicts depend on this paper. R1 (trigger primary): Graybiel anchors the canonical pattern -- repetition + outcome consistency through S-R coupling. R2 (substrate locus): the striatum is named explicitly as the chunking site, with the implicit cortico-striatal loop providing the input. R3 (transition mechanism for MECH-163): Graybiel does not name MECH-163's dual-systems architecture but the recoding direction is exactly the planned-into-habitual content pump that R3 needs. Whether ARC-071 IS the transition mechanism is therefore biologically warranted at the substrate level; the explicit two-systems framing comes from Yin & Knowlton 2006 and Smith & Graybiel 2013.

The caveat is that Graybiel 1998 is a theoretical review rather than a primary experimental paper. The empirical support comes from subsequent striatal recording work (Jog 1999; Barnes 2005; Smith & Graybiel 2013) and the chunking-as-task-bracketing signature. The 1998 paper proposes the construct; later work provides the parametric data. For ARC-071 lit grounding, this paper anchors the architectural commitment; later papers settle the parameterisation (R5 outcome-consistency thresholds) and the substrate detail (R6 real-vs-imagined gating).
