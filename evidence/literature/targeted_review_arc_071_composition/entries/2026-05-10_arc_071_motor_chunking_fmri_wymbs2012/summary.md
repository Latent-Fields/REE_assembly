# Wymbs, Bassett, Mucha, Porter, Grafton 2012 -- Differential recruitment of putamen and frontoparietal cortex during motor chunking

The cleanest human-substrate paper for the multi-substrate verdict (R2) on ARC-071. Wymbs et al. ran a 3-day longitudinal fMRI study with healthy subjects performing a cued motor sequence production task, applying dynamic network analysis to identify chunking structure trial-wise. The headline finding splits chunking into two processes with substrate-distinct attribution: the putamen supports concatenation (chunking IN -- forming motor-motor associations between elements); frontoparietal cortex supports segmentation (chunking OUT -- parsing contiguous elements into shorter sets).

This paper is the load-bearing R2 evidence for the family-level concatenation / segmentation distinction. ARC-071 instantiates concatenation; ARC-070 instantiates segmentation. Wymbs et al. supplies the substrate non-redundancy directly: ARC-071's substrate is putamen-analog (DLS-equivalent in REE), ARC-070's substrate is frontoparietal-analog (vmPFC + parietal in REE). The two processes are not symmetric inverses sharing a single substrate; they are substrate-distinct machinery operating at different phases of skill acquisition.

The phase-asymmetry is also relevant. Segmentation (chunking OUT) happens more early in learning, when the agent is still parsing the sequence into manageable parts; concatenation (chunking IN) happens more late, when stable sub-sequences crystallise into single units. This matches the family doc's pre-registered fast-decomposition vs slow-composition framing -- ARC-070 is the faster, simulation-side, early-learning operation; ARC-071 is the slower, execution-side, late-learning operation. The temporal asymmetry is empirically grounded.

The substrate attribution has a sharp implication for ARC-071's R2 verdict. The claim "ARC-071 substrate locus is dorsolateral striatum (Yin & Knowlton 2006)" is correct but incomplete. The full picture is: ARC-071 substrate is striatum (putamen/DLS-analog) for the motor-motor association formation, with cortical (frontoparietal) involvement during early-phase parsing. The Smith & Graybiel 2013 evidence adds infralimbic cortex as a third component required for the maintenance of formed chunks. ARC-071 is therefore phase-dependent multi-substrate machinery -- the R2 verdict should report this rather than nominate a single substrate.

The caveat is that Wymbs operationalises chunking through fMRI BOLD + dynamic network analysis -- this is a network-level signature, not a direct measure of compose/decompose operations on a representational object. The mapping requires assuming the BOLD signature reflects the underlying chunking machinery that ARC-071 instantiates. Also, Wymbs is purely behavioural-motor; the cognitive chunking dimension (Graybiel 2008's broader scope) is not tested.

Confidence assigned 0.79. Source quality 0.85 (Neuron, 3-day longitudinal human fMRI with novel dynamic network analysis). Mapping fidelity 0.80 -- direct on substrate distinction; the BOLD-to-mechanism step is the only small concession. Transfer risk 0.15 -- human data, no species-translation needed.
