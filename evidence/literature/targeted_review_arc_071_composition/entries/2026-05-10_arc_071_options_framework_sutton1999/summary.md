# Sutton, Precup, Singh 1999 -- Between MDPs and semi-MDPs: Options framework

The cleanest formal analog of policy composition. Sutton, Precup, and Singh introduce the options framework, where an option is a closed-loop policy for taking action over a period of time, defined by three fields: a policy (the action-selection rule), an initiation set (the contexts in which the option can be invoked), and a termination condition (when the option completes). Options can be used interchangeably with primitive actions in planning and learning, and a set of options defined over an MDP constitutes a semi-Markov decision process.

For ARC-071, this paper supplies the formal vocabulary. ARC-071's chunked primitive is the REE-substrate analog of an option, and the three-field structure maps directly: the chunked-policy is the sequence itself, the initiation set is the context conditions under which the chunk fires, the termination condition is the completion signal. The options-framework interchangeability claim supports ARC-071's design choice that chunked primitives go into the same candidate pool as un-chunked primitives -- ARC-007 hippocampal proposals select from a mixed-grain set, not from grain-segregated pools.

The architectural implication is sharp. ARC-071's chunked primitive cannot be just a sequence; it must carry context-keyed initiation conditions and a completion signal. This is a substantive detail beyond the slot's pre-registered "accumulate sequence into a single primitive" framing. Without these fields, ARC-071 chunks will mis-fire in the wrong contexts (no initiation guard) or fail to terminate cleanly (no completion signal). The synthesis should add this as a load-bearing architectural detail for the child-MECH design pass.

The semi-MDP framing matters too. ARC-071's chunked-primitive execution takes variable time -- a chunk fires as a unit but the underlying motor sequence still unfolds over its natural duration. The semi-MDP formalism handles variable-duration actions cleanly without breaking the rest of the agent's planning machinery. ARC-071 implementation can adopt this directly: the chunk is selected as one move at the planning layer, but the execution layer plays out the underlying sequence in time.

The dominant caveat is that the options framework is formal not biological. It constrains the SHAPE of ARC-071 chunks but does not constrain the biological MECHANISM by which they form. Sutton 1999 is silent on the options-discovery problem (which has spawned a substantial sub-literature -- Stolle & Precup 2002, McGovern & Barto 2001 bottleneck-state subgoal discovery, etc.). ARC-071's biological-trigger commitment (repetition + outcome consistency from Graybiel 1998) is the biology-side answer to the discovery problem; the formal framework does not constrain it.

The second important caveat is that the value-side machinery in the options framework (Q-learning over options) does NOT translate to ARC-071. ARC-007 strict in REE preserves value-flat hippocampal proposals -- proposals do not carry value heads, value is computed downstream by E3 evaluation. ARC-071 chunks must remain value-flat at the proposal layer. The "options interchangeable with primitive actions" claim survives this constraint at the SELECTION layer (E3 evaluates options and primitives the same way) but breaks at the OPTIONS-VALUE-LEARNING layer (REE does not learn Q-values over options the way SMDP-Q-learning does). The synthesis should pin this distinction to avoid a Sutton-1999-shaped implementation that contaminates ARC-007 strict.
