# Yin & Knowlton 2006 -- The role of the basal ganglia in habit formation

The canonical reference for the dual-systems architecture in the basal ganglia. Yin & Knowlton synthesise the rodent lesion and outcome-devaluation literature into a framework where instrumental behaviour begins as goal-directed (DMS-mediated, sensitive to outcome value and action-outcome contingency) and gradually becomes habitual (DLS-mediated, impervious-to-devaluation, stimulus-cued). The transition happens with extended training, and the two systems operate in parallel with some degree of interdependency rather than as a clean either/or switch.

This paper is the load-bearing biological grounding for R3 in the ARC-071 synthesis. R3 asks whether ARC-071 IS the missing transition mechanism that MECH-163 dual_goal_directed_systems presupposes but does not specify. The Yin & Knowlton review supports the framing: MECH-163 names the dual systems (planned + habitual); biology grounds those systems in DMS / DLS substrates; ARC-071 names the chunk-level machinery by which content moves from one to the other. Without ARC-071 the dual systems in MECH-163 are static configurations; with ARC-071 the division of labour shifts continuously with experience -- which is exactly the rodent-lesion + overtraining picture this review synthesises.

The biology supplies three sharp testable predictions for ARC-071 implementation. First, DLS lesions preserve goal-directed control after overtraining -- an REE agent with ARC-071 disabled should fall back cleanly to MECH-163 planned-system control without breakdown. Second, the habit signature is impervious-to-outcome-devaluation -- ARC-071-formed chunks should be selectable on context-cue-match even when the chunked-sequence's outcome value drops. Third, DMS and DLS coexist during transition (parallel, interdependent), which directly supports ARC-071's design choice that the original primitive sequence remains available alongside the chunked unit.

The caveat is that Yin & Knowlton frame the transition at the SYSTEM level (one striatal subdivision becomes more dominant) rather than at the CHUNK level (specific sequences get chunked into specific units). ARC-071's commitment is the chunk-level claim; this review supplies the system-level scaffold within which the chunk-level mechanism becomes biologically plausible. The downstream lit is needed to firm up the chunk-level grain: Smith & Graybiel 2013 supplies the chunk-dynamics evidence; Wymbs 2012 supplies the multi-substrate-during-chunking evidence.
