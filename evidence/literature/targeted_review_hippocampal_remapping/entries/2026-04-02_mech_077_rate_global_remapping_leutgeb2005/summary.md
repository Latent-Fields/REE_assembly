# Leutgeb et al. (2005) — Rate Remapping vs. Global Remapping: Two Modes of Hippocampal Geometry Change

**Claims tested:** MECH-077, MECH-076
**Evidence direction:** supports | **Confidence:** 0.78

---

## What the paper did

Leutgeb and colleagues (Moser lab, Bergen) recorded from hippocampal CA3 and CA1 neural ensembles in rats placed in two conditions: (1) the same recording chamber in the same room (varying only visual cues on the walls — context manipulation with stable place), and (2) identical chambers in two different rooms (stable visual cues but different place context). These two manipulations were designed to dissociate spatial remapping from contextual remapping.

The results identified two separable forms of hippocampal population change. When context varied but place was stable (condition 1), cells changed their firing rates while maintaining their spatial field locations — rate remapping. When place varied but cues were stable (condition 2), both firing locations and rates changed, producing statistically independent ensemble activity patterns — global remapping. These two modes are separable and encode independent aspects of episodic memory: the spatial code (where) and the contextual/episodic code (what kind of experience).

## Key findings for MECH-076 and MECH-077

The rate remapping / global remapping distinction provides the mechanistic substrate for two REE claims:

**MECH-076 (residue as structural map deformation):** Residue accumulation from harm exposure is analogous to rate remapping. The topological arrangement of the viability map (which trajectories exist, which nodes are connected) remains stable, but the activity weights — the attractor basin depths — change. A trajectory through a harm-associated state zone is not removed from the map; its rate-analogue (attractor depth / retrieval probability) is modulated. This predicts that harm-exposed trajectories remain topologically accessible in the map but are retrieved with altered weighting — consistent with REE's no-erasure principle and MECH-092's note that replay preserves path-dependent curvature.

**MECH-077 (therapeutic remapping):** Genuine therapeutic change — not just reweighting of existing representations but reorganization of the map — requires a global remapping event. In the hippocampal literature, global remapping requires a sufficiently different context. In MECH-077 terms, therapeutic interventions that work by providing a new context (exposure therapy, EMDR context reinstatement, major life transition) can trigger global remapping in which the harm-trajectory nodes are reorganized into a new geometric structure. This is not erasure (which REE prohibits) but reorganization: the trajectory history is preserved but its topological relationship to other trajectories changes.

## REE translation: a diagnostic prediction

The rate/global distinction generates a testable clinical prediction: exposure-based therapies that work by reducing the arousal weight of existing trauma representations (desensitisation) should produce rate remapping — the nodes remain, their attractor depth decreases. Context-shift therapies (EMDR, which establishes a new safe context as the retrieval frame; context reinstatement; place-based memory reconsolidation) should produce global remapping — genuine topological reorganization. These two outcomes would produce different patterns of hippocampal geometry, detectable in principle via fMRI adaptation or pattern similarity analysis.

## Limitations

Spatial navigation contexts; the mapping from spatial context change to abstract harm-state context change requires inference. The threshold for global vs. rate remapping (what makes a context change 'global') is not well-specified — it depends on the degree of place change and contextual novelty in ways that may not translate directly to abstract state spaces. The therapeutic mapping (rate remapping = desensitisation, global remapping = context-shift therapy) is a theoretical inference, not a tested prediction.
