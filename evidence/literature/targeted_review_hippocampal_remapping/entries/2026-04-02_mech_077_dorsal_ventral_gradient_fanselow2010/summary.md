# Fanselow & Dong (2010) — Dorsal/Ventral Gradient: Anatomical Constraint on MECH-077 Therapeutic Remapping

**Claims tested:** MECH-077, MECH-076
**Evidence direction:** supports | **Confidence:** 0.75

---

## What the paper did

Fanselow and Dong synthesised behavioral, anatomical, and gene expression studies to address a persistent question: are the dorsal and ventral hippocampus functionally different structures, or is the hippocampus functionally homogeneous? Their answer, drawn from decades of lesion studies, tract-tracing, and the Allen Brain Atlas gene expression data, was that hippocampus is best described as three compartments along a functional gradient: dorsal (cognitive, spatial precision), ventral (stress, emotion, motivational integration), and intermediate (transitional).

The connectivity data drove the functional interpretation. Dorsal hippocampus receives dense input from sensory and posterior parietal cortex; it projects to retrosplenial, cingulate, and prefrontal areas involved in spatial navigation. Ventral hippocampus is connected to amygdala (BLA and central), hypothalamus, nucleus accumbens, anterior cingulate, and prefrontal areas involved in anxiety and affect. The intermediate zone bridges these two connectivity profiles. Gene expression follows the same gradient — nearly one third of hippocampal genes show graded dorsal-to-ventral expression changes.

## Key findings for MECH-076 and MECH-077

**MECH-076 (residue as structural map deformation):** The dorsal/ventral gradient provides the anatomical substrate for understanding where harm-related residue accumulates. Harm events (threat, aversive outcomes) activate BLA, which projects primarily to ventral and intermediate hippocampus — consistent with harm-trajectory residue being a ventral HPC phenomenon. The cognitive/spatial substrate of the viability map (which trajectories exist, which paths are navigable) is dorsal; the affective weighting of those trajectories (the deformation in MECH-076 terms) is ventral-intermediate.

**MECH-077 (therapeutic remapping):** Therapeutic change must therefore occur in ventral/intermediate HPC, not just dorsal. Rate remapping of the spatial structure (Leutgeb et al.) is primarily a dorsal phenomenon; the emotionally weighted global remapping that MECH-077 requires must reorganize ventral HPC assemblies that have been warped by harm-associated BLA input. This is consistent with evidence that therapeutic interventions for fear and trauma work via ventral HPC — ventral HPC lesions in rodents impair contextual fear extinction (one of the primary therapeutic analogs for MECH-077's claim).

For human translation: in humans, the anterior hippocampus is functionally analogous to rodent ventral hippocampus (connected to amygdala, anterior cingulate, vmPFC — the affective circuit) while posterior hippocampus is analogous to rodent dorsal (connected to retrosplenial/parietal, precise spatial coding). Therapeutic remapping events should therefore produce anterior hippocampal reorganization in human imaging studies.

## REE implications: a diagnostic prediction

The dorsal/ventral distinction sharpens REE's testable predictions for MECH-077 remapping. Interventions that reduce harm-trajectory residue (desensitization, EMDR, context-shift therapy) should produce detectable changes in ventral/anterior hippocampal geometry — measurable in principle via fMRI pattern similarity analysis or neural adaptation paradigms. Purely cognitive restructuring approaches that reframe beliefs without direct trauma exposure would not be expected to produce ventral HPC remapping, which REE would predict corresponds to superficial rather than deep therapeutic change.

## Limitations

The dorsal/ventral distinction is reviewed for its behavioral and anatomical correlates, not for remapping dynamics specifically. Whether remapping dynamics actually differ along the dorsal/ventral axis (i.e., whether global remapping thresholds are lower in ventral than dorsal HPC) has not been directly tested. The rodent→human anterior/posterior re-labeling is well-supported but introduces a small amount of translational uncertainty. The paper's core claim is about functional organization, not therapeutic mechanisms — the application to MECH-077 is a theoretical inference that requires its own empirical test.
