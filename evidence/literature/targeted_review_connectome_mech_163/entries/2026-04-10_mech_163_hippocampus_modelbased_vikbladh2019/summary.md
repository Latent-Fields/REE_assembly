## Paper

Vikbladh OM, Meager MR, King J, Blackmon K, Devinsky O, Shohamy D, Burgess N, Daw ND. "Hippocampal Contributions to Model-Based Planning and Spatial Memory." *Neuron* 102(3):683-693, 2019. DOI: 10.1016/j.neuron.2019.02.014 (PMID: 30871859)

## Core Finding

Epilepsy patients treated with unilateral anterior temporal lobectomy including hippocampal resection showed impaired performance on two measures: (1) model-based planning (two-step Markov decision task) and (2) spatial place memory. Critically, the magnitude of model-based planning impairment correlated with right hippocampal lesion size, controlling for overall lesion size. In healthy controls, planning and place memory performance covaried significantly; this relationship was attenuated in the patient group -- consistent with both functions sharing the hippocampus as a common substrate in the healthy brain.

## Relevance to MECH-163

MECH-163 claims a hippocampally-planned system is required for model-based behavior. Vikbladh et al. provide direct human causal evidence: hippocampal damage causally impairs model-based planning in proportion to lesion size. This bridges the rodent lesion evidence (Miller et al. 2017, muscimol) to human cognition without cross-species transfer risk. The covariation between planning and spatial memory in healthy participants also supports REE's view that hippocampal place-map mechanisms are the computational substrate for model-based benefit accumulation across contexts.

The inclusion of Nathaniel Daw as senior author ensures the model-based/model-free behavioral dissociation is rigorously applied using the same framework as Daw et al. 2005 (already in MECH-163's evidence base).

## Limitations for MECH-163 Mapping

- The two-step Markov task tests contingency learning over 200+ trials; not equivalent to long-horizon prospective planning in novel environments.
- Unilateral lesions reduce causal inference strength compared to bilateral hippocampal inactivation.
- Confound: temporal lobe structures adjacent to hippocampus are also affected by surgery.
- Does not test prosocial, novel-context, or long-horizon benefit-accumulation aspects of MECH-163.
- Does not address the VTA/ventral striatum or SNc/dorsal striatum components of the dual-system claim.

## Methodological Quality

Human lesion-behavior correlation with good sample sizes for this patient population. Two-task battery (planning + place memory) allows within-subject dissociation. Lesion size quantification adds dose-response rigor. Published in Neuron, strong collaborative team (NYU, Columbia, UCL).
