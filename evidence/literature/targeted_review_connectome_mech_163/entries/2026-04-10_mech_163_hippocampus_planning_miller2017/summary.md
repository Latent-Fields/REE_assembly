# Summary: Miller, Botvinick & Brody 2017 -- Dorsal Hippocampus Contributes to Model-Based Planning

**Entry ID:** 2026-04-10_mech_163_hippocampus_planning_miller2017
**Claim tested:** MECH-163
**Direction:** supports
**Confidence:** 0.87

## Paper

Miller KJ, Botvinick MM, Brody CD. "Dorsal hippocampus contributes to model-based planning." *Nature Neuroscience* 20(9):1269-1276, 2017. DOI: 10.1038/nn.4613 (PMID: 28758995)

## Core Finding

Muscimol inactivation of dorsal hippocampus in rats selectively impairs model-based (goal-directed) planning behavior while leaving habitual (model-free) responding intact. The authors developed a novel rat task producing many planning trials per session -- enabling formal algorithmic behavioral analysis to dissociate model-based from model-free strategy use. This provides the first unambiguous causal evidence that dorsal hippocampus is necessary for planning, resolving a long-running controversy caused by earlier insensitive task designs.

## Relevance to MECH-163

MECH-163 claims a hippocampally-planned system (VTA/ventral-striatum + PFC) is required for model-based behavior in novel contexts and long-horizon benefit accumulation, in contrast to the habit system (SNc/dorsal-striatum). Miller et al. provide the critical causal evidence for the hippocampal node of this claim: dorsal hippocampus is necessary -- not merely correlated -- for model-based planning. Prior correlational and computational evidence (Daw 2005, Balleine & O'Doherty 2010, Dolan & Dayan 2013) left open whether hippocampus was playing a primary or secondary role. This paper closes that gap with a clean lesion-based dissociation.

## Limitations for MECH-163 Mapping

- The rat task is spatial navigation; translation to prosocial planning and long-horizon benefit accumulation is inferential.
- Does not directly test VTA/ventral striatum involvement or contrast these with SNc/dorsal striatum (the other half of MECH-163).
- Demonstrates necessity for planning in a practiced navigation context; the "novel context" emphasis in MECH-163 is partially supported by the task design but not the primary manipulation.

## Methodological Quality

Combines gold-standard causal intervention (muscimol inactivation) with formal behavioral model fitting. The task design innovation (high-trial-per-session planning behavior in rodents) is a significant methodological contribution. Published in Nature Neuroscience, high citation impact.
